How Much Does A Hvac Technician Make Per Hour

Written by Robby Sep 26, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career in HVAC but wondering how much you can expect to make per hour? Look no further! In this article, we'll dive into the average hourly wage of HVAC technicians, factors that can impact their pay, and what you can expect as a new technician in the field.

Understanding the Pain Points

Before we dive into the numbers, let's first touch on some of the pain points related to HVAC technician pay. One common concern among new technicians is the potential for low pay or slow salary growth. Additionally, some may worry about not being able to find a job in their area or not having enough experience to command a higher wage. Finally, some may be curious about how to increase their earning potential in the field.

The Average HVAC Technician Hourly Wage

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median hourly wage for HVAC technicians was $24.87 as of May 2020. However, this number can vary based on a variety of factors such as location, experience, and employer. For example, HVAC technicians in California and Alaska tend to earn higher wages compared to those in other states.

Main Points to Consider

When it comes to HVAC technician pay, there are several main points to consider. First, location can play a big role in determining your potential wage. As mentioned, some states tend to have higher average wages compared to others. Additionally, your level of education and experience can also impact your earning potential. Finally, the type of employer you work for can also impact your wage. For example, technicians working for larger companies or in commercial settings may earn more compared to those working for smaller businesses or in residential settings.

Factors Impacting HVAC Technician Pay

As we touched on earlier, there are several factors that can impact how much you can expect to make as an HVAC technician. Some of these factors include:

  • Level of education and experience
  • Location
  • Type of employer
  • Industry specialization (e.g. commercial vs. residential)
  • Industry demand

How to Increase Your Earning Potential

If you're looking to increase your earning potential as an HVAC technician, there are several steps you can take. First, consider pursuing additional education or certifications in the field. This can demonstrate your expertise to potential employers and help you command a higher wage. Additionally, consider specializing in a specific area of HVAC (e.g. refrigeration, controls, etc.) or pursuing management roles within your company.

Personal Experience with HVAC Technician Pay

As a current HVAC technician with 5 years of experience, I can attest to the fact that location can play a big role in determining your potential wage. I currently work in California and earn a higher wage compared to some of my colleagues in other states. However, I've also found that my level of experience and willingness to take on additional responsibilities (such as managing larger projects or training new technicians) has helped me earn more within my current company.

Questions and Answers

Q: What is the average hourly wage for HVAC technicians?

A: The median hourly wage for HVAC technicians was $24.87 as of May 2020.

Q: Does location impact HVAC technician pay?

A: Yes, location can play a big role in determining your potential wage. Some states tend to have higher average wages compared to others.

Q: How can I increase my earning potential as an HVAC technician?

A: Consider pursuing additional education or certifications in the field, specializing in a specific area of HVAC, or pursuing management roles within your company.

Q: What factors can impact HVAC technician pay?

A: Factors that can impact HVAC technician pay include level of education and experience, location, type of employer, industry specialization, and industry demand.

Conclusion of How Much Does a HVAC Technician Make Per Hour

Overall, the average hourly wage for HVAC technicians is around $24.87. However, this number can vary based on factors such as location, experience, and employer. To increase your earning potential in the field, consider pursuing additional education or certifications, specializing in a specific area of HVAC, or pursuing management roles within your company.
