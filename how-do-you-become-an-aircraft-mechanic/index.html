How Do You Become An Aircraft Mechanic

Written by Bardi Oct 06, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Working as an aircraft mechanic can be an exciting and rewarding career. If you're interested in aviation and have a passion for fixing things, then becoming an aircraft mechanic might be the perfect career for you. In this blog post, we'll explore what it takes to become an aircraft mechanic and how you can get started on this career path.

The Pain Points of Becoming an Aircraft Mechanic

One of the biggest challenges of becoming an aircraft mechanic is the educational and training requirements. Aircraft mechanics need to have a thorough understanding of aircraft systems, engines, and avionics, which requires specialized education and training. Additionally, there are strict safety regulations and standards that must be followed when working on aircraft, so attention to detail and adherence to safety protocols is crucial.

Answering the Target of How Do You Become an Aircraft Mechanic

The first step to becoming an aircraft mechanic is to complete a certified training program. These programs typically take 18-24 months to complete and cover topics such as aircraft systems, engines, and avionics. After completing the training program, you'll need to pass a series of exams to become certified by the Federal Aviation Administration (FAA).

Once you're certified, you can start working as an aircraft mechanic. However, many employers prefer to hire mechanics with on-the-job experience, so it's a good idea to get an internship or apprenticeship to gain hands-on experience. You can also continue your education and training to specialize in a specific area of aircraft maintenance, such as avionics or engines.

Summary of the Main Points

In summary, becoming an aircraft mechanic involves completing a certified training program, passing FAA exams, and gaining on-the-job experience. It's also important to have a strong attention to detail and a commitment to safety. Specializing in a specific area of aircraft maintenance can also be beneficial for career advancement.

Education and Training Requirements

Most aircraft mechanics start their careers by completing a certified training program. These programs are offered by aviation schools, community colleges, and vocational schools. The training programs typically take 18-24 months to complete and cover topics such as aircraft systems, engines, and avionics.

After completing the training program, you'll need to pass a series of FAA exams to become certified. These exams cover topics such as airframe structures, aircraft systems, and powerplant systems. Once you're certified, you can start working as an aircraft mechanic.

On-the-Job Experience

Many employers prefer to hire aircraft mechanics with on-the-job experience. You can gain experience by completing an internship or apprenticeship with an airline or maintenance facility. During your internship or apprenticeship, you'll work alongside experienced mechanics and gain hands-on experience with aircraft maintenance and repair.

Additionally, continuing education and training can help you specialize in a specific area of aircraft maintenance, such as avionics or engines. Specialization can lead to higher-paying jobs and increased opportunities for career advancement.

Adherence to Safety Protocols

Adherence to safety protocols is crucial for aircraft mechanics. Aircraft maintenance and repair is a high-risk job, and any mistakes or oversights can have serious consequences. Mechanics must follow strict safety regulations and standards to ensure the safety of themselves, their colleagues, and the passengers who rely on the aircraft they maintain.

Attention to Detail

Aircraft mechanics must have a strong attention to detail. Aircraft are complex machines with many interconnected systems, and mechanics must be able to diagnose and repair problems with precision and accuracy. Attention to detail is also important when following safety protocols and regulations.

Question and Answer

Q: What kind of training is required to become an aircraft mechanic?

A: To become an aircraft mechanic, you'll need to complete a certified training program that covers aircraft systems, engines, and avionics. These programs typically take 18-24 months to complete.

Q: How do I become certified by the FAA?

A: To become certified by the FAA, you'll need to pass a series of exams that cover topics such as airframe structures, aircraft systems, and powerplant systems.

Q: Do I need on-the-job experience to become an aircraft mechanic?

A: While on-the-job experience isn't required to become an aircraft mechanic, many employers prefer to hire mechanics with experience. Completing an internship or apprenticeship can be a great way to gain hands-on experience.

Q: Is specialization important for aircraft mechanics?

A: Specializing in a specific area of aircraft maintenance, such as avionics or engines, can lead to higher-paying jobs and increased opportunities for career advancement.

Conclusion

Becoming an aircraft mechanic requires specialized education and training, as well as a commitment to safety and attention to detail. Completing a certified training program, passing FAA exams, gaining on-the-job experience, and specializing in a specific area of aircraft maintenance can all be important for career advancement in this exciting and rewarding field.
