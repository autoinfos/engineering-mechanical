How Much Does A Truck Driver Make In New Zealand

Written by Petter May 25, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you considering a career as a truck driver in New Zealand? Do you wonder how much you can earn as a truck driver in this country? If you are looking for answers to these questions, then you are in the right place. In this post, we will give you all the information you need about how much a truck driver can make in New Zealand.

Truck driving is an essential job in New Zealand, and it is a challenging career that requires a lot of skills and experience. However, it is also a well-paying job, and it can provide a comfortable living for you and your family.

The average salary for a truck driver in New Zealand is around NZD 25 per hour, which is equivalent to NZD 50,000 per year. However, this salary can vary depending on several factors such as experience, location, and the type of truck you are driving.

In general, truck drivers who have more experience and drive larger vehicles such as heavy trucks and tankers can earn more than those who drive smaller vehicles such as vans and light trucks. Additionally, truck drivers who work in remote areas or transport hazardous materials can also earn more than those who work in urban areas and transport regular goods.

Personal Experience with Truck Driving in New Zealand

I have been working as a truck driver in New Zealand for 10 years, and I can say that it is a rewarding career. I started as a van driver, and I gradually worked my way up to become a heavy truck driver. I have driven all over the country, from the North Island to the South Island, and I have transported all kinds of goods, from food to construction materials.

My current salary is around NZD 30 per hour, which is equivalent to NZD 60,000 per year. I work for a company that specializes in transporting hazardous materials, and I have to follow strict safety procedures and regulations. However, I enjoy the challenge of this job, and I am proud of what I do.

Advantages and Disadvantages of Truck Driving in New Zealand

Truck driving in New Zealand has its advantages and disadvantages. One of the main advantages is the salary, which can provide a decent living for you and your family. Additionally, truck driving can be a flexible job, and you can choose to work for a company or be self-employed.

On the other hand, truck driving can also be a challenging job, and it requires a lot of skills and experience. You have to be able to drive for long hours, sometimes in difficult weather conditions, and you have to be able to handle the stress of transporting valuable goods.

How to Become a Truck Driver in New Zealand

If you are interested in becoming a truck driver in New Zealand, there are several steps you need to follow. First, you need to obtain a Class 2 driver's license, which allows you to drive medium-sized trucks. Then, you can work your way up to a Class 5 driver's license, which allows you to drive heavy trucks and tankers.

You also need to have good driving skills, be familiar with New Zealand roads and traffic rules, and have a good understanding of safety procedures and regulations. Additionally, you need to have a clean driving record and be able to pass a medical and drug test.

How to Find Truck Driving Jobs in New Zealand

If you are looking for truck driving jobs in New Zealand, there are several ways to find them. You can search online job boards, contact trucking companies directly, or work with a recruiting agency. Additionally, you can network with other truck drivers and attend industry events and job fairs.

Conclusion of How Much Does a Truck Driver Make in New Zealand

Truck driving is a well-paying and rewarding career in New Zealand. The average salary for a truck driver is around NZD 25 per hour, but this can vary depending on several factors such as experience, location, and the type of truck you are driving. If you have the skills and experience to become a truck driver in New Zealand, then it can provide you with a comfortable living and a challenging career.
