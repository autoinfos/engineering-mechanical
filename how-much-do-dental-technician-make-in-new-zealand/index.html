How Much Do Dental Technicians Make In New Zealand

Written by William Aug 08, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you considering a career as a dental technician in New Zealand? If so, you may be wondering how much you can expect to make in this field. Salary is an important consideration for anyone entering a new profession, and it can be difficult to find accurate information online. In this post, we'll explore how much dental technicians make in New Zealand and what factors can affect their earnings.

Dental technician jobs can be highly rewarding, but they also come with their fair share of challenges. One of the biggest concerns for many people considering this career path is salary. After all, you want to make sure that your hard work and dedication will be adequately compensated. While there's no one-size-fits-all answer to the question of how much dental technicians make in New Zealand, we can provide some general guidance.

The median salary for dental technicians in New Zealand is around $46,000 per year. However, this can vary significantly depending on a number of factors. For example, dental technicians with more experience or advanced qualifications may be able to command higher salaries. Additionally, the location and type of practice can also affect earnings. Dental technicians working in larger cities or for larger practices may earn more than those in smaller towns or rural areas.

In summary, dental technicians in New Zealand can expect to make around $46,000 per year, although this can vary depending on a number of factors. To learn more about what affects dental technician salaries in New Zealand, keep reading.

Factors That Affect Dental Technician Salaries in New Zealand

As we mentioned earlier, there are a number of factors that can impact how much dental technicians make in New Zealand. Here are a few things to consider:

Experience and Qualifications

Like many professions, dental technicians with more experience or additional qualifications may be able to command higher salaries. For example, someone who has been working in the field for 10 years may earn more than someone who is just starting out. Similarly, a dental technician with a master's degree in dental technology may be able to earn more than someone with only a bachelor's degree.

Type of Practice

The type of practice you work for can also affect your earnings. For example, dental technicians who work for larger practices or dental labs may be able to earn more than those who work for smaller practices. Additionally, dental technicians who specialize in certain areas (such as orthodontics or prosthodontics) may be able to earn more than general dental technicians.

Personal Experience with Dental Technician Salaries in New Zealand

As a dental technician myself, I can tell you that salaries in this field can vary widely. When I first started out, I was making around $40,000 per year, but as I gained more experience and advanced my skills, my salary increased. Today, I make around $60,000 per year, but I know colleagues who make more or less depending on their individual circumstances.

Education and Training for Dental Technicians in New Zealand

To become a dental technician in New Zealand, you'll generally need to complete a diploma in dental technology. This program typically takes two years to complete and covers topics like dental anatomy, materials science, and dental laboratory procedures. After completing your diploma, you may be able to work as a dental technician or continue on to further education (such as a bachelor's or master's degree). Additional training or certifications can also help you advance in your career and potentially earn more money.

Job Outlook for Dental Technicians in New Zealand

The job outlook for dental technicians in New Zealand is generally positive. According to the New Zealand Ministry of Business, Innovation, and Employment, employment in the dental technology field is expected to grow moderately over the next few years. This means that there should be opportunities for new graduates and experienced dental technicians alike.

Questions and Answers

Q: Is dental technology a good career choice in New Zealand?

A: That depends on your individual interests and goals, but dental technology can be a highly rewarding career for those who enjoy working with their hands and have an eye for detail.

Q: What are some of the challenges of working as a dental technician in New Zealand?

A: Some of the challenges of this profession include working long hours, dealing with difficult or demanding patients, and staying up-to-date with new technologies and techniques.

Q: What are some of the benefits of working as a dental technician in New Zealand?

A: Some of the benefits of this profession include the opportunity to work in a hands-on, creative field, the ability to help improve people's oral health and appearance, and the potential for career growth and advancement.

Q: How can I find dental technician jobs in New Zealand?

A: There are a number of job search websites that list dental technician positions in New Zealand, such as Seek, Indeed, and Trade Me Jobs. You can also check with local dental practices or labs to see if they have any openings.

Conclusion of How Much Do Dental Technicians Make in New Zealand

In conclusion, dental technicians in New Zealand can expect to make around $46,000 per year, although this can vary depending on factors like experience, qualifications, location, and type of practice. While salary is an important consideration, it's also important to remember that dental technology can be a highly rewarding field with opportunities for growth and advancement. If you're interested in pursuing a career in dental technology, consider completing a diploma in dental technology and exploring job opportunities in your area.
