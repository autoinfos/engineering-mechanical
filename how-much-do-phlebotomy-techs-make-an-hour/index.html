How Much Do Phlebotomy Techs Make An Hour

Written by Luffy Sep 26, 2023 · 3 min read

Are you considering a career as a phlebotomy technician? If so, one of the questions you may have is how much do phlebotomy techs make an hour? It's important to know what kind of salary you can expect in this field before making any decisions.

When it comes to phlebotomy techs, pay can vary based on a number of factors. These may include location, employer, education and experience level. It's also worth noting that phlebotomy techs may be paid hourly or on a salary basis.

On average, phlebotomy techs make around $16 - $20 an hour. However, this can vary depending on the factors mentioned above. For example, those working in metropolitan areas may make more than those in rural areas. Similarly, those with more experience or education may be able to command a higher salary.

In summary, phlebotomy techs can expect to make around $16 - $20 an hour, but this can vary based on several factors.

What Are the Factors That Affect Phlebotomy Tech Pay?

When it comes to how much do phlebotomy techs make an hour, there are several factors that can affect pay. These may include:

Location:

Those working in metropolitan areas may make more than those in rural areas.

Employer:

Different employers may offer different pay rates for phlebotomy techs.

Education and Experience:

Those with more education or experience may be able to command a higher salary.

Shifts:

Phlebotomy techs who work overnight or weekend shifts may make more than those who work traditional hours.

These are just a few of the factors that can affect how much do phlebotomy techs make an hour.

My Experience with Phlebotomy Tech Pay

As a former phlebotomy tech, I can speak to the pay in this field. When I first started out, I was making around $15 an hour. However, as I gained more experience and education, my pay increased to around $18 an hour. I also found that working for a larger hospital system tended to pay more than working for a smaller clinic.

How Can You Increase Your Phlebotomy Tech Pay?

If you're interested in increasing your phlebotomy tech pay, there are a few things you can do. These may include:

Further Education:

Consider pursuing additional education or certifications in phlebotomy or a related field.

Gain Experience:

The more experience you have, the more you may be able to command in terms of pay.

Look for Higher-Paying Employers:

Consider working for a larger hospital system or medical center, as these tend to offer higher pay for phlebotomy techs.

Question and Answer

Q: Is the pay for phlebotomy techs the same across the country?

A: No, pay can vary based on location, employer, education and experience level.

Q: Do phlebotomy techs typically make more on a salary or hourly basis?

A: It varies, but many phlebotomy techs are paid hourly.

Q: Can further education increase my pay as a phlebotomy tech?

A: Yes, pursuing additional education or certifications may result in a higher pay rate.

Q: What is the average hourly pay for phlebotomy techs?

A: On average, phlebotomy techs make around $16 - $20 an hour.

Conclusion of How Much Do Phlebotomy Techs Make an Hour

In conclusion, the pay for phlebotomy techs can vary based on a number of factors. On average, phlebotomy techs can expect to make around $16 - $20 an hour. However, those with more education or experience may be able to command a higher salary. If you're interested in pursuing a career in phlebotomy, it's important to consider these factors when thinking about how much do phlebotomy techs make an hour.
