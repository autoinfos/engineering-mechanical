Factory Technician Job Description What You Need To Know

Written by Christine Aug 21, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you someone who enjoys working with your hands, solving problems, and keeping busy? If so, a career as a factory technician may be the perfect fit for you. As a factory technician, you'll be responsible for ensuring that machinery and equipment are running smoothly, diagnosing and fixing issues, and performing routine maintenance. It's a challenging and rewarding job that requires a unique set of skills and knowledge.

However, as with any job, there are some pain points that come with being a factory technician. For starters, the work can be physically demanding, as you may be required to lift heavy objects, climb ladders, and work in uncomfortable positions. Additionally, the job can be stressful, as you'll often be working under tight deadlines and dealing with unexpected issues that need to be resolved quickly.

The target of a factory technician job description is someone who has a strong mechanical aptitude, excellent problem-solving skills, and the ability to work well under pressure. You'll need to be able to read schematics and diagrams, have a basic understanding of electrical systems, and be comfortable using a variety of hand and power tools. Additionally, you'll need to be able to communicate effectively with other members of your team and be willing to work irregular hours if necessary.

In summary, being a factory technician is a challenging and rewarding career that requires a unique set of skills and knowledge. If you're someone who enjoys working with your hands, solving problems, and keeping busy, it may be the perfect job for you.

What Does a Factory Technician Do?

As mentioned earlier, a factory technician is responsible for ensuring that machinery and equipment are running smoothly. This involves diagnosing and fixing issues, performing routine maintenance, and keeping detailed records of all work performed. A factory technician may work on a variety of different types of equipment, including conveyor systems, motors, pumps, and more.

My personal experience as a factory technician involved working on a large-scale assembly line that produced automotive parts. My job was to keep the machines running smoothly and to fix any issues that arose during the production process. It was a fast-paced and demanding job, but I found it to be incredibly fulfilling.

What Skills Do You Need to Be a Factory Technician?

As mentioned earlier, a factory technician needs to have a strong mechanical aptitude, excellent problem-solving skills, and the ability to work well under pressure. Additionally, you'll need to be able to read schematics and diagrams, have a basic understanding of electrical systems, and be comfortable using a variety of hand and power tools. Communication skills are also important, as you'll need to be able to effectively communicate with other members of your team.

What Education or Training Do You Need to Be a Factory Technician?

While a formal education is not always required to become a factory technician, having a degree or certificate in a related field can be helpful. Additionally, many employers prefer to hire candidates who have completed an apprenticeship or on-the-job training program. These programs provide hands-on experience and can help you develop the skills you need to be successful in this career.

How Much Money Can You Make as a Factory Technician?

The salary of a factory technician can vary depending on a variety of factors, including your level of experience, the type of equipment you'll be working on, and your location. However, according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for industrial machinery mechanics, which includes factory technicians, was $54,920 as of May 2020.
