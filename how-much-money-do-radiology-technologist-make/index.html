How Much Money Do Radiology Technologists Make

Written by Oliver Sep 11, 2023 · 3 min read

Are you considering a career as a radiology technologist but unsure about the pay? Or maybe you're already a radiology technologist and wondering if you're making enough money? Money is an important factor in choosing a career, and it's essential to know how much you can expect to earn before making a decision.

The average salary for radiology technologists varies depending on factors such as location, experience, education, and certification. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for radiologic and MRI technologists was $62,280 as of May 2020. However, this can range from $42,320 to $97,460 or more, depending on the aforementioned factors.

In summary, radiology technologists can earn a good salary, with the median annual wage being $62,280. However, the salary can vary depending on factors such as location, experience, education, and certification.

What Affects the Salary of Radiology Technologists?

My personal experience as a radiology technologist has taught me that several factors can influence your salary. Firstly, your location plays a significant role in determining your salary. If you work in a rural area, you may earn less compared to someone working in a metropolitan city. Secondly, your level of education and experience can also impact your salary. For example, if you have an associate's degree in radiology technology, you may earn less than someone with a bachelor's degree. Lastly, certification can also affect your salary. If you have additional certifications, such as mammography or CT, you may earn more.

How Can Radiology Technologists Increase Their Salary?

One way to increase your salary as a radiology technologist is to pursue additional certifications. As mentioned earlier, having additional certifications can increase your earning potential. Another way to increase your salary is to advance your education. Pursuing a bachelor's or master's degree in radiology technology can open up opportunities for higher-paying positions such as management or education.

What is the Job Outlook for Radiology Technologists?

The job outlook for radiology technologists is promising. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, employment of radiologic and MRI technologists is projected to grow 9 percent from 2020 to 2030, which is faster than the average for all occupations. This growth is due to an aging population, which will require more diagnostic imaging services.

How Do Radiology Technologists Compare to Other Healthcare Professions?

When it comes to salary, radiology technologists fare well compared to other healthcare professions. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for registered nurses as of May 2020 was $75,330, while dental hygienists earned a median annual wage of $77,090. Physician assistants earned a median annual wage of $115,390. Radiology technologists, with a median annual wage of $62,280, compare favorably to these healthcare professions.

Conclusion of How Much Money Do Radiology Technologists Make

Overall, radiology technology is a rewarding and well-paying profession. While salary can vary depending on location, education, experience, and certification, radiology technologists can earn a good income. Pursuing additional certifications and education can increase earning potential, and the job outlook for radiology technologists is promising.
