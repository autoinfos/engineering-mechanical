How Much Does An Ase Certified Diesel Mechanic Make

Written by Robby Aug 11, 2023 · 5 min read

If you are considering a career as an ASE certified diesel mechanic, one of the most important factors to consider is how much you can expect to make. In this article, we will explore the earning potential of an ASE certified diesel mechanic and what factors can affect your salary.

As with any profession, there are certain pain points that can impact how much an ASE certified diesel mechanic can make. These may include the location of your job, your level of experience, and the type of employer you work for. Additionally, the level of certification you hold can also play a role in determining your salary.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for diesel service technicians and mechanics was $50,200 as of May 2020. The lowest 10 percent earned less than $32,920, while the highest 10 percent earned more than $76,350. Keep in mind that this data reflects the earnings of all diesel mechanics, not just those who are ASE certified.

In general, ASE certified diesel mechanics can expect to make more than those who are not certified. This is because certification demonstrates a higher level of skill and knowledge, which can be attractive to employers. Additionally, those who hold multiple certifications may be able to command higher salaries.

In summary, an ASE certified diesel mechanic can earn a median annual wage of $50,200, with the potential to earn more based on location, experience, employer, and level of certification.

Factors That Impact How Much ASE Certified Diesel Mechanics Make

One of the biggest factors that can impact how much an ASE certified diesel mechanic makes is their location. Mechanics who work in areas with a high cost of living may be able to command higher salaries than those who work in areas with a lower cost of living. Additionally, mechanics who work in urban areas may be able to earn more than those who work in rural areas.

Experience is another important factor to consider when it comes to earning potential. Mechanics who have been working in the field for a longer period of time may be able to earn more than those who are just starting out. Additionally, those who have experience working with specific types of engines or vehicles may be able to earn more as well.

The type of employer you work for can also impact your salary as an ASE certified diesel mechanic. Mechanics who work for large companies or government agencies may be able to earn more than those who work for smaller companies. Additionally, those who work in industries where there is a high demand for diesel mechanics, such as the transportation or construction industry, may be able to earn more as well.

Advantages of Being an ASE Certified Diesel Mechanic

Becoming an ASE certified diesel mechanic can offer a number of advantages over those who are not certified. For one, certification demonstrates a higher level of skill and knowledge, which can be attractive to employers. Additionally, those who are certified may be able to command higher salaries and have more opportunities for advancement within their field.

Another advantage of being ASE certified is that it can help to boost your credibility with customers. Customers who know that their mechanic is certified may be more likely to trust their expertise and feel more confident in their ability to repair their vehicle.

How to Become an ASE Certified Diesel Mechanic

If you are interested in becoming an ASE certified diesel mechanic, there are a few steps you will need to take. First, you will need to complete a diesel technology program at a vocational school, community college, or trade school. These programs typically take one to two years to complete and cover topics such as diesel engine repair, brake systems, and electrical systems.

Once you have completed your training, you will need to gain some experience working in the field before you can take the ASE certification exam. The amount of experience required will depend on the specific certification you are pursuing.

After you have met the experience requirement, you can take the ASE certification exam. The exam consists of multiple-choice questions and covers topics such as engine repair, brakes, and suspension and steering. You will need to pass the exam in order to become certified.

Question and Answer

Q: How much can an ASE certified diesel mechanic make?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for diesel service technicians and mechanics was $50,200 as of May 2020. However, the earnings of an ASE certified diesel mechanic can vary based on location, experience, and employer.

Q: Why is becoming ASE certified important for diesel mechanics?

A: Becoming ASE certified demonstrates a higher level of skill and knowledge, which can be attractive to employers and can help diesel mechanics to earn higher salaries. Additionally, certification can help to boost a mechanic's credibility with customers.

Q: What is the process for becoming an ASE certified diesel mechanic?

A: To become an ASE certified diesel mechanic, you will need to complete a diesel technology program, gain experience in the field, and pass the ASE certification exam.

Q: What factors can impact how much an ASE certified diesel mechanic makes?

A: Location, experience, employer, and level of certification can all impact how much an ASE certified diesel mechanic can make.

Conclusion

ASE certified diesel mechanics can expect to earn a median annual wage of $50,200, with the potential to earn more based on location, experience, employer, and level of certification. Becoming certified can offer a number of advantages, including the ability to command higher salaries and boost your credibility with customers. If you are interested in pursuing a career as an ASE certified diesel mechanic, be sure to research the training requirements, experience requirements, and certification process in your area.
