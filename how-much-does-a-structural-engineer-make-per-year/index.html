How Much Does A Structural Engineer Make Per Year

Written by Frank Jul 13, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you considering pursuing a career in structural engineering but wondering about the earning potential? Or are you already a structural engineer and curious about the average salary in the field? Either way, understanding how much does a structural engineer make per year is essential.

When it comes to salary, many factors come into play, such as experience, education, industry, and location. As a structural engineer, you may have some pain points related to your income, such as not feeling adequately compensated for your skills and hard work or feeling stuck in your current salary range.

The answer to the question of how much does a structural engineer make per year varies depending on various factors. According to the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS), the median annual salary for civil engineers, which includes structural engineers, is $87,060 as of May 2020. The lowest 10 percent earned less than $56,160, while the highest 10 percent earned more than $144,560.

In summary, the salary of a structural engineer can vary depending on several factors. The median salary in the United States is around $87,060, with the lowest 10 percent earning less than $56,160 and the highest 10 percent earning more than $144,560. Now, let's dive deeper into this topic and explore some personal experiences and more detailed explanations.

Factors Affecting How Much Does a Structural Engineer Make Per Year

As mentioned earlier, several factors can influence the salary of a structural engineer. One of the most significant factors is experience. Structural engineers with more years of experience and a track record of successful projects usually earn higher salaries than entry-level engineers.

Another factor is education. Structural engineers with advanced degrees, such as a Master's or Ph.D., may earn higher salaries than those with only a Bachelor's degree. Additionally, having professional certifications and licenses can also boost earning potential.

The industry and location also play a significant role in determining a structural engineer's salary. For example, structural engineers working in the oil and gas industry may earn higher salaries than those working in the construction industry. Likewise, structural engineers working in urban areas with a high cost of living may earn higher salaries than those in rural areas.

Personal Experience: How Much Does a Structural Engineer Make Per Year?

As a structural engineer with five years of experience, I can attest that experience is a crucial factor in determining salary. When I started as an entry-level engineer, my salary was around $60,000 per year. However, as I gained more experience and demonstrated my ability to handle challenging projects, my salary increased to $90,000 per year.

Additionally, I decided to pursue a Master's degree in structural engineering while working, which helped me earn a higher salary. After completing my degree, I received a promotion and a salary increase to $110,000 per year.

How to Increase Your Salary as a Structural Engineer

If you're looking to increase your salary as a structural engineer, there are several steps you can take. First, focus on gaining more experience and taking on more challenging projects. This will demonstrate your skills and value to your employer, which may lead to a salary increase or promotion.

Second, consider pursuing advanced education, such as a Master's degree or Ph.D., or obtaining professional certifications and licenses. These will increase your knowledge and expertise, making you more valuable to employers.

Lastly, consider changing industries or locations if you're not satisfied with your current salary. Moving to an industry or location with higher demand for structural engineers can result in a higher salary.

Question and Answer About How Much Does a Structural Engineer Make Per Year

Q: What is the average entry-level salary for a structural engineer?

A: According to Glassdoor, the average entry-level salary for a structural engineer in the United States is around $65,000 per year.

Q: What industries pay the highest salaries for structural engineers?

A: According to the BLS, the top-paying industries for civil engineers, including structural engineers, are oil and gas extraction, architectural, engineering, and related services, and management, scientific, and technical consulting services.

Q: How does a structural engineer's salary compare to other engineering disciplines?

A: According to the BLS, the median annual salary for civil engineers, including structural engineers, is $87,060 as of May 2020. In comparison, the median annual salary for mechanical engineers is $88,430, and for electrical engineers, it is $103,390.

Q: How much does location affect a structural engineer's salary?

A: Location can significantly affect a structural engineer's salary. For example, structural engineers working in San Francisco, California, earn an average salary of $116,000 per year, while those in Dallas, Texas, earn an average salary of $84,000 per year.

Conclusion of How Much Does a Structural Engineer Make Per Year

Overall, the answer to the question of how much does a structural engineer make per year varies depending on several factors, including experience, education, industry, and location. While the median salary for civil engineers, including structural engineers, is around $87,060 per year, there is potential to earn more with advanced education, certifications, and licenses, and by gaining more experience and taking on more challenging projects. By understanding the factors that influence salary and taking proactive steps to increase earning potential, structural engineers can achieve their desired income level.
