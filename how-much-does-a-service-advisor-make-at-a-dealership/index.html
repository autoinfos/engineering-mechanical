How Much Does A Service Advisor Make At A Dealership

Written by Robby Nov 12, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you considering a career as a service advisor at a dealership? One of the biggest questions you may have is how much you can expect to make in this role. It's important to know what the industry standard is and what factors can affect your salary. In this blog post, we will explore the topic of how much does a service advisor make at a dealership and provide you with the information you need to make an informed decision.

As with any career, there are both pros and cons to being a service advisor at a dealership. One of the main pain points for many people is the salary. It can be challenging to know what to expect in terms of pay, especially if you're new to the industry. However, it's important to remember that there are many factors that can impact your salary, including your location, the dealership you work for, and your level of experience.

So, how much does a service advisor make at a dealership? According to Glassdoor, the national average salary for a service advisor in the United States is around $44,000 per year. However, this number can vary depending on your location and the dealership you work for. Some service advisors may earn more than this, while others may earn less.

In summary, how much does a service advisor make at a dealership can vary depending on several factors, including your location, the dealership you work for, and your level of experience. However, the national average salary for a service advisor is around $44,000 per year.

Factors That Affect a Service Advisor's Salary

One of the biggest factors that can impact a service advisor's salary is their location. For example, service advisors who work in major cities may earn more than those who work in rural areas. This is because the cost of living is generally higher in cities, so employers may offer higher salaries to offset these expenses.

The dealership you work for can also impact your salary. Some dealerships may offer higher salaries than others, depending on their size and the services they offer. Additionally, your level of experience can also play a significant role in your salary. Service advisors who have been in the industry for several years and have a proven track record of success may be offered higher salaries than those who are just starting.

My Experience as a Service Advisor

As a former service advisor, I can tell you that the salary can vary greatly depending on where you work. In my experience, I earned around $45,000 per year, which was slightly above the national average. However, I worked for a large dealership in a major city, which likely contributed to my higher salary.

One thing to keep in mind is that a service advisor's salary is not always fixed. Many dealerships offer commission-based pay, which means that your salary can increase if you meet certain sales goals. This can be a great way to earn more money and advance in your career.

How to Become a Service Advisor

If you're interested in becoming a service advisor, there are several steps you can take to get started. First, you'll need to have a high school diploma or equivalent. Many dealerships also prefer candidates who have some college education or vocational training in automotive technology.

Next, you'll need to gain experience in the automotive industry. This can be done through internships, apprenticeships, or entry-level positions. Once you have some experience under your belt, you can begin applying for service advisor positions at dealerships in your area.

Additional Skills and Qualifications

To be successful as a service advisor, there are several skills and qualifications you should have. These include:

  • Excellent communication skills
  • Strong customer service skills
  • Knowledge of automotive repair and maintenance
  • Ability to work in a fast-paced environment
  • Attention to detail and accuracy

Tips for Negotiating Your Salary

If you're offered a job as a service advisor, it's important to negotiate your salary to ensure that you're being paid fairly. Here are a few tips to keep in mind:

  • Research the average salary for service advisors in your area
  • Highlight your experience and qualifications during the negotiation process
  • Be willing to negotiate on other aspects of the job, such as benefits or work schedule
  • Consider asking for commission-based pay to increase your earning potential

Conclusion of How Much Does a Service Advisor Make at a Dealership

While the salary for a service advisor at a dealership can vary depending on several factors, the national average is around $44,000 per year. However, it's important to remember that your salary can be impacted by your location, the dealership you work for, and your level of experience. By gaining experience in the automotive industry and developing the necessary skills and qualifications, you can increase your earning potential and advance in your career.

Question and Answer

Q: How much can I expect to make as a service advisor in a rural area?

A: The salary for a service advisor in a rural area may be lower than the national average, which is around $44,000 per year. However, this can vary depending on the dealership you work for and your level of experience.

Q: Is commission-based pay common for service advisors?

A: Yes, many dealerships offer commission-based pay for service advisors. This can be a great way to increase your earning potential and advance in your career.

Q: What skills do I need to be a successful service advisor?

A: To be successful as a service advisor, you should have excellent communication and customer service skills, knowledge of automotive repair and maintenance, and the ability to work in a fast-paced environment.

Q: How can I negotiate my salary as a service advisor?

A: To negotiate your salary as a service advisor, research the average salary in your area, highlight your experience and qualifications, be willing to negotiate on other aspects of the job, and consider asking for commission-based pay.
