How Much Do Stage Technicians Get Paid

Written by Christine Jul 28, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Working behind-the-scenes as a stage technician can be a rewarding and exciting job. However, it's not always clear how much these hardworking professionals get paid. If you're considering a career in stage technology, you might be wondering about the average salary and whether or not it's a viable option for your future.

While being a stage technician can be fulfilling, it's not uncommon for professionals in this field to feel underpaid. This is especially true for those just starting out, who may be struggling to make ends meet while working long hours.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for stage technicians was $46,510 in May 2020. This means that half of all stage technicians earned less than this amount, while the other half earned more. However, it's important to note that salaries can vary widely depending on a number of factors, such as experience, location, and type of employer.

In order to command a higher salary as a stage technician, it's important to gain as much experience as possible. This can include working on larger productions or gaining expertise in a particular area of stage technology. Building a strong network of industry contacts can also be helpful in securing higher-paying jobs.

Personal Experience with Stage Technician Salaries

As a former stage technician myself, I can attest to the fact that salaries in this field can be somewhat inconsistent. While I was lucky enough to work on some high-paying gigs, I also had to take on lower-paying jobs in order to make ends meet. It's definitely possible to earn a living as a stage technician, but it's important to be realistic about the potential earnings.

Factors That Affect Stage Technician Salaries

As mentioned earlier, there are a number of factors that can impact how much a stage technician gets paid. Some of the most important factors include:

Experience

As with any job, experience is key when it comes to earning a higher salary. Stage technicians who have been working in the industry for a longer period of time are likely to earn more than those who are just starting out.

Location

Where you work can also have a big impact on your salary as a stage technician. Those who work in major metropolitan areas or in entertainment hubs like Los Angeles or New York City are likely to earn more than those who work in smaller cities or towns.

Working as a Freelance Stage Technician

Many stage technicians work as freelancers rather than being employed by a single company. While this can provide more flexibility and potentially higher earnings, it also comes with its own set of challenges. Freelancers often have to hustle to find work and may not have the same level of job security as those who are employed full-time.

Question and Answer

Q: Can stage technicians make a good living?

A: Yes, it's possible to make a good living as a stage technician. However, salaries can vary widely depending on a number of factors, and it may take time to build up the necessary experience to command a higher salary.

Q: What is the average salary for a stage technician?

A: The median annual wage for stage technicians was $46,510 in May 2020, according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics. However, salaries can vary widely depending on a number of factors.

Q: Is it better to work as a freelance stage technician or be employed full-time?

A: This depends on your personal preferences and financial situation. Freelancers may have more flexibility and potentially higher earnings, but full-time employees may have more job security and benefits.

Q: How can I increase my earning potential as a stage technician?

A: Gaining as much experience as possible and building a strong network of industry contacts can help you command a higher salary. It's also important to specialize in a particular area of stage technology and to be willing to take on larger and more complex productions.

Conclusion of How Much Do Stage Technicians Get Paid

While being a stage technician can be a rewarding career, salaries in this field can be somewhat inconsistent. However, with the right experience and networking skills, it's possible to earn a good living and even command high-paying jobs. Whether you choose to work as a freelancer or be employed full-time, it's important to be realistic about the potential earnings and to continue building your skills and experience over time.
