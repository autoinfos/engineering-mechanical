How Much Do Ford Lube Techs Make

Written by Luffy May 25, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Have you ever wondered how much Ford lube techs make? Well, you're not alone. Many people are curious about the earning potential of a lube tech. In this blog post, we'll explore the average salary of Ford lube techs and what factors can impact their pay.

Working as a lube tech can be demanding, and one of the biggest concerns is the pay. Most lube techs earn an hourly wage, which can be lower than other automotive jobs. Additionally, some lube techs may not receive benefits like health insurance or paid time off. These factors can make it challenging to make ends meet, especially if you're just starting in the industry.

The average hourly pay for a Ford lube tech is around $15-$17 per hour. However, this can vary depending on a few key factors. For example, more experienced technicians may earn a higher wage, and those who work in busier dealerships may earn more in tips. Additionally, some dealerships may offer benefits like health insurance or retirement plans, which can impact your overall compensation.

My Experience as a Ford Lube Tech

I started working as a lube tech at a Ford dealership a few years ago. At first, the pay wasn't great, but I was able to work my way up and earn a higher wage. One of the things that helped me was taking additional training courses and getting certified in different areas. This allowed me to take on more responsibilities and work on higher-paying jobs.

Overall, I think being a lube tech can be a good career choice if you're willing to put in the time and effort to advance. While the pay may not be great at first, there is room for growth, and you can learn a lot about the automotive industry.

Factors That Impact Pay

Several factors can impact how much a Ford lube tech makes. One of the most significant is experience. More experienced techs may be able to command a higher wage, especially if they have additional certifications or training. Additionally, those who work in busy dealerships may earn more in tips, which can significantly impact their overall pay.

Another factor that can impact pay is location. Lube techs who work in high-cost-of-living areas may earn more than those who work in more rural areas. Additionally, dealerships in larger cities may have more business, which can translate to higher pay for their employees.

Certifications and Training

As mentioned earlier, certifications and training can be vital for lube techs who want to earn a higher wage. Many dealerships offer training programs and certification courses for their employees. These courses can cover everything from basic automotive maintenance to more advanced topics like engine repair or transmission work.

By taking additional courses and earning certifications, lube techs can demonstrate their expertise to their employer and customers. This can lead to higher pay and more job opportunities down the road.

Negotiating Pay

If you're a lube tech and feel like you're not being paid what you're worth, it's essential to speak up. Many dealerships are willing to negotiate pay or offer additional benefits like health insurance or paid time off. However, you need to be willing to ask and make a case for why you deserve more compensation.

Conclusion of How Much Do Ford Lube Techs Make

In conclusion, the average pay for a Ford lube tech is around $15-$17 per hour. However, several factors can impact how much you earn, including experience, location, and certifications. While the pay may not be great at first, there is room for growth in the industry, and taking additional training courses can help you earn a higher wage.

Question and Answer

Q: Can lube techs earn bonuses or commissions?

A: Some dealerships may offer bonuses or commissions for lube techs who work on higher-paying jobs or sell additional services to customers.

Q: What benefits do lube techs typically receive?

A: This can vary depending on the dealership, but some lube techs may receive benefits like health insurance or retirement plans.

Q: How long does it take to become a certified lube tech?

A: This can vary depending on the training program, but most certification courses take a few months to complete.

Q: What is the job outlook for lube techs?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, employment of automotive service technicians and mechanics (which includes lube techs) is projected to grow 4 percent from 2019 to 2029, about as fast as the average for all occupations.
