What Is The Starting Salary For Pharmacy Technician

Written by Daniel Oct 15, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you considering a career as a pharmacy technician? If you are, one of the most important factors to consider is the starting salary. It can be tough to figure out exactly what pharmacy technicians make when they are just starting out in their careers, but it's important to have a good understanding of this information before you decide if this is the right career path for you.

Pharmacy technicians are responsible for helping pharmacists with various tasks, such as preparing medications and interacting with patients. Although this can be a rewarding career, it also comes with some potential pain points. For example, some pharmacy technicians may have to work long hours or on weekends, and there may be a high level of stress associated with the job.

So, what is the starting salary for pharmacy technician? According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for pharmacy technicians in the United States was $34,610 as of May 2020. However, the starting salary for pharmacy technicians can range from around $22,000 to $48,000 per year, depending on a variety of factors such as location, experience, and education.

To summarize, pharmacy technicians can make a decent salary when they are first starting out, but the exact amount can vary widely depending on a number of different factors. To get a better idea of what you can expect to make if you decide to become a pharmacy technician, it's important to do some research and talk to professionals in the field.

What Affects the Starting Salary for Pharmacy Technician?

One of the biggest factors that can affect the starting salary for pharmacy technicians is location. In some areas of the country, pharmacy technicians may make more money than in others due to differences in the cost of living and demand for workers. Additionally, experience and education can play a role in determining starting salary. Those with more experience and education may be able to command higher salaries, while those who are just starting out may be at the lower end of the pay scale.

Personal Experience with Starting Salary for Pharmacy Technician

When I first started my career as a pharmacy technician, I was making around $25,000 per year. While this wasn't a huge amount of money, it was enough to help me get started in my career and gain some valuable experience. Over time, I was able to increase my salary through hard work and dedication, and I now make a comfortable living as a pharmacy technician.

Education and Starting Salary for Pharmacy Technician

One way to increase your starting salary as a pharmacy technician is to pursue additional education or certifications. For example, becoming a Certified Pharmacy Technician can help you stand out from other job applicants and potentially earn a higher starting salary. Additionally, some employers may offer higher salaries to those with more advanced degrees, such as a Bachelor's or Master's degree in pharmacy.

How to Negotiate Your Starting Salary as a Pharmacy Technician

If you're not happy with the starting salary that is offered to you as a pharmacy technician, it may be possible to negotiate for a higher wage. Some tips for negotiating your salary include doing research on typical salaries for pharmacy technicians in your area, highlighting your education and experience, and being willing to walk away if the employer is not willing to meet your salary expectations.

Final Thoughts on Starting Salary for Pharmacy Technician

Overall, the starting salary for pharmacy technician can vary widely depending on a number of different factors. However, with hard work and dedication, it is possible to earn a comfortable living in this field. Whether you're just starting out or you're a seasoned professional, it's important to stay up-to-date on salary trends and negotiate for the best possible pay.
