How Much Do Licensed Mechanics Make

Written by Frank May 26, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career as a licensed mechanic but wondering how much you can expect to earn? If so, you're not alone. Many people are curious about the earning potential in this field, and for good reason. After all, you want to ensure that you can earn a comfortable living in any career you pursue.

For those wondering how much do licensed mechanics make, the answer is that it varies depending on a few factors. Some of the key factors that can impact a mechanic's earnings include their level of experience, the type of work they specialize in, and the location where they work.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for automotive service technicians and mechanics was $44,050 as of May 2020. This means that half of all mechanics earned more than this amount, while the other half earned less. The lowest 10 percent of earners in this field made less than $25,620 per year, while the highest 10 percent earned more than $71,940.

In summary, licensed mechanics can expect to earn a median annual wage of $44,050, with the potential to earn more or less depending on their experience, specialization, and location.

What Factors Affect a Mechanic's Earnings?

As mentioned, there are several factors that can impact how much a licensed mechanic earns. Here are a few of the most significant:

Experience

One of the biggest factors that impacts how much a licensed mechanic can earn is their level of experience. Mechanics who have been working in the field for several years and have developed a strong skill set can often command higher wages than those who are just starting out.

Specialization

Another factor that can affect a mechanic's earnings is their area of specialization. For example, a mechanic who specializes in working on high-end luxury vehicles may be able to earn more than one who works on more common models.

Location

The location where a mechanic works can also play a role in their earnings. Mechanics who work in areas with higher costs of living may be able to earn more to compensate, while those in more rural areas may earn less.

Personal Experience with Earnings as a Licensed Mechanic

As a licensed mechanic with 10 years of experience, I can attest to the fact that earnings can vary widely in this field. When I first started out, I was earning around $30,000 per year. However, as I gained more experience and developed my skills, my earnings steadily increased. Now, I earn around $60,000 per year, and I know that there is still room for growth.

How Can You Increase Your Earnings as a Licensed Mechanic?

If you're interested in maximizing your earning potential as a licensed mechanic, there are a few steps you can take:

Specialize in a High-Demand Area

As mentioned, specializing in a high-demand area can help you earn more. Consider focusing on an area that is in high demand, such as electric or hybrid vehicle repair.

Continue Learning and Developing Your Skills

The more skills you have, the more valuable you are as a mechanic. Take advantage of continuing education opportunities and stay up-to-date with the latest technology and repair techniques.

Question and Answer

Q: Do licensed mechanics only work on cars?

A: No, licensed mechanics can work on a wide range of vehicles and machinery, including motorcycles, trucks, boats, and airplanes.

Q: Are there any downsides to working as a licensed mechanic?

A: Like any career, there are pros and cons to working as a licensed mechanic. Some of the potential downsides include exposure to hazardous chemicals and the physical demands of the job.

Q: Can licensed mechanics work for themselves?

A: Yes, many licensed mechanics choose to start their own businesses or work as independent contractors.

Q: Are there any opportunities for advancement in this field?

A: Yes, licensed mechanics can advance to supervisory or management roles with additional education and experience.

Conclusion of How Much Do Licensed Mechanics Make

Overall, licensed mechanics can expect to earn a median annual wage of $44,050, with the potential for higher earnings depending on their experience, specialization, and location. By specializing in high-demand areas, continuing to develop their skills, and exploring opportunities for advancement, mechanics can maximize their earning potential and build a successful career in this field.
