What Are The Subjects In Automotive Engineering

Written by William Oct 15, 2023 · 4 min read

Have you ever wondered what goes into designing and building a car? Are you interested in pursuing a career in the automotive industry? If so, you may want to consider studying automotive engineering. However, before you jump into this field, it's important to understand the subjects involved in automotive engineering and what you can expect to learn.

For many people, the idea of designing and building cars seems like a dream job. However, the reality is that automotive engineering is a complex and challenging field that requires a strong foundation in math and science. If you're not comfortable with these subjects, you may struggle to succeed in this field.

At its core, automotive engineering is all about designing and developing vehicles that are safe, efficient, and reliable. This involves a wide range of subjects, including mechanical engineering, electrical engineering, materials science, and computer science. Some of the key subjects you'll study in automotive engineering programs include:

Vehicle Dynamics

Vehicle dynamics is the study of how vehicles move and respond to different driving conditions. This subject covers topics such as suspension design, steering systems, and tire mechanics. By studying vehicle dynamics, engineers can design cars that handle well and are easy to control.

Powertrain Systems

Powertrain systems are the components of a car that generate and transmit power to the wheels. This includes the engine, transmission, and drivetrain. Engineers who specialize in powertrain systems work to optimize the efficiency and performance of these components, while also minimizing emissions.

Materials Science

Materials science is the study of how different materials behave under different conditions. In automotive engineering, materials science plays a critical role in selecting the right materials for different components, such as the body, chassis, and engine. Engineers must consider factors such as strength, weight, and cost when selecting materials.

Computer-Aided Design

Computer-aided design (CAD) is the use of computer software to create detailed models of parts and systems. In automotive engineering, CAD is used to design everything from individual components to entire vehicles. This allows engineers to test and refine designs before anything is built, saving time and money in the development process.

In conclusion, automotive engineering is a challenging but rewarding field that requires a strong foundation in math and science. By studying subjects such as vehicle dynamics, powertrain systems, materials science, and computer-aided design, you can learn the skills needed to design and develop cars that are safe, efficient, and reliable.

Vehicle Electronics

Vehicle electronics is the study of how electronic systems are used in vehicles. This subject covers topics such as engine management systems, climate control systems, and entertainment systems. By studying vehicle electronics, engineers can design cars that are more comfortable, convenient, and enjoyable to drive.

Automotive Safety

Automotive safety is a critical subject in automotive engineering. This subject covers topics such as crash testing, safety systems, and accident investigation. By studying automotive safety, engineers can design cars that are safer for drivers and passengers.

Vehicle Aerodynamics

Vehicle aerodynamics is the study of how air flows around a vehicle. This subject covers topics such as drag reduction, downforce, and wind noise. By studying vehicle aerodynamics, engineers can design cars that are more fuel-efficient and quieter.

How to Choose an Automotive Engineering Program

If you're interested in studying automotive engineering, it's important to choose a program that meets your needs. Look for programs that offer hands-on experience, access to cutting-edge technology, and opportunities for internships or co-op placements. You may also want to consider the location of the program, as some regions have a strong automotive industry presence.

FAQs About What Are the Subjects in Automotive Engineering

Q: What kind of math do you need for automotive engineering?
A: Automotive engineering requires a strong foundation in calculus, geometry, and trigonometry.

Q: What are some of the challenges of working in automotive engineering?
A: Some of the challenges of working in automotive engineering include managing complex projects, working with tight deadlines, and keeping up with rapidly changing technology.

Q: What kind of jobs can you get with a degree in automotive engineering?
A: Degree in automotive engineering can lead to careers in areas such as design, manufacturing, testing, and research and development.

Q: What are some skills you need to succeed in automotive engineering?
A: Some of the skills you need to succeed in automotive engineering include problem-solving, attention to detail, and the ability to work in a team.

Conclusion of What Are the Subjects in Automotive Engineering

Automotive engineering is a fascinating field that requires a deep understanding of math and science. By studying subjects such as vehicle dynamics, powertrain systems, materials science, and computer-aided design, you can learn the skills needed to design and develop cars that are safe, efficient, and reliable. Whether you're interested in pursuing a career in automotive engineering or simply want to learn more about how cars work, studying the subjects involved in this field can be a rewarding experience.
