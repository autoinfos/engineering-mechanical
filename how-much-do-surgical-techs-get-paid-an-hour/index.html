How Much Do Surgical Techs Get Paid An Hour

Written by Petter May 27, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career as a surgical tech but not sure about the salary? This post will answer your biggest question: how much do surgical techs get paid an hour?

As a surgical tech, you may be worried about the amount of money you will be making. After all, you are responsible for assisting surgeons during surgeries and ensuring that the operating room is safe and clean. You may also be worried about the cost of education and training to become a surgical tech.

On average, surgical techs earn $23 an hour in the United States. However, the salary can vary based on factors such as location, experience, and employer. Some surgical techs may earn as little as $15 an hour, while others may earn as much as $33 an hour.

In addition to the hourly rate, surgical techs may also receive benefits such as health insurance, retirement plans, and paid time off. Some employers may also offer bonuses or incentives for working overtime or on-call shifts.

My Personal Experience

When I first started my career as a surgical tech, I was making $18 an hour. However, as I gained more experience and worked for different employers, my salary increased to $28 an hour. I also received benefits such as health insurance and paid time off. Overall, I am satisfied with the salary and benefits that come with being a surgical tech.

Factors That Affect Salary

As mentioned earlier, the salary of a surgical tech can vary based on several factors. Location is a significant factor, with surgical techs in urban areas typically earning more than those in rural areas. Experience is another factor, with more experienced techs earning higher wages. Employers such as hospitals and outpatient surgery centers may also offer different salaries.

Education and Training

To become a surgical tech, you will need to complete an accredited surgical technology program. These programs typically take one to two years to complete and result in a diploma, certificate, or associate's degree. The cost of these programs can vary, with some community colleges offering programs for as little as $3,000, while other private institutions may charge upwards of $20,000.

How to Increase Your Salary

If you want to increase your salary as a surgical tech, there are several ways to do so. One way is to gain more experience and become an expert in your field. You can also consider pursuing additional certifications, such as the Certified Surgical Technologist (CST) credential. Another way to increase your salary is to negotiate with your employer for a raise or seek employment with a higher-paying employer.

Final Thoughts

Overall, the salary of a surgical tech can vary based on several factors, but the average hourly rate is $23. With the right education, training, and experience, you can increase your salary and enjoy a rewarding career as a surgical tech.

Question and Answer

Q: How much does a surgical tech make per year?

A: On average, surgical techs earn $48,990 per year.

Q: What is the job outlook for surgical techs?

A: The job outlook for surgical techs is positive, with a projected growth rate of 7% from 2019 to 2029.

Q: Do surgical techs work long hours?

A: Surgical techs may work long hours, including nights, weekends, and holidays. Some techs may also be on-call and need to be available at all hours.

Q: How long does it take to become a surgical tech?

A: It takes one to two years to complete an accredited surgical technology program and become a surgical tech.

Conclusion of How Much Do Surgical Techs Get Paid an Hour

In conclusion, the salary of a surgical tech can vary based on location, experience, and employer, but the average hourly rate is $23. With the right education and training, you can enjoy a rewarding career as a surgical tech with a competitive salary and benefits.
