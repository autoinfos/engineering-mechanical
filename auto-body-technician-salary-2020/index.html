Auto Body Technician Salary 2020 The Ultimate Guide

Written by Eveline Sep 23, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you considering a career as an auto body technician? Or are you already in the field and wondering what your earning potential is? Either way, understanding the current salary trends for this profession is crucial. In this guide, we'll take a deep dive into the world of auto body technician salary in 2020, and what factors can affect your pay.

As with any profession, there are certain pain points that come with being an auto body technician. One of the biggest challenges is the physical demands of the job. Repairing and restoring vehicles can be physically taxing, and can lead to burnout or injury over time. Another challenge is the potential for irregular work hours, including evenings and weekends, which can make it difficult to maintain a work-life balance. However, for those who are passionate about cars and enjoy working with their hands, the rewards of this profession can be immense.

So, what can you expect to earn as an auto body technician in 2020? According to data from the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual salary for auto body technicians and painters was $44,550 as of May 2019. However, this number can vary depending on a number of factors, including experience level, location, and type of employer.

In general, auto body technicians who work for dealerships or larger repair shops tend to earn higher salaries than those who work for smaller, independent shops. Additionally, those who have specialized skills or certifications, such as experience working with luxury or high-performance vehicles, may command higher salaries as well.

The Importance of Negotiating Your Salary

When it comes to determining your salary as an auto body technician, it's important to advocate for yourself and negotiate with potential employers. This can involve researching industry standards for your area, highlighting your relevant skills and experience, and being prepared to walk away if the salary offered is too low. Remember, your salary is a reflection of your value and expertise as a professional, so don't be afraid to stand up for yourself.

Personal Experience: Navigating Salary Negotiations

When I first started out as an auto body technician, I was excited to begin my career in a field that I was passionate about. However, as I gained more experience and took on more responsibilities, I realized that I wasn't being compensated fairly for my work. After doing some research and speaking with colleagues in the industry, I decided to negotiate with my employer for a higher salary. While the process was nerve-wracking, it ultimately paid off and I was able to secure a raise that reflected my skills and experience.

Tips for Maximizing Your Salary Potential

So, how can you ensure that you're earning the highest salary possible as an auto body technician? Here are a few tips to keep in mind:

1. Focus on Specializing Your Skills

As mentioned earlier, having specialized skills or certifications can help you command a higher salary. Consider pursuing additional training or certifications in areas like collision repair, welding, or painting to increase your value to potential employers.

2. Consider Working for a Larger Employer

Larger repair shops or dealerships may be able to offer higher salaries due to their larger budgets and increased demand for skilled technicians. Consider expanding your job search to include these types of employers.

Personal Experience: Finding Success with a Larger Employer

After several years of working for smaller, independent repair shops, I decided to take a chance and apply for a position at a larger dealership. While the application process was competitive, I was ultimately offered a position with a higher salary than I had been earning previously. Not only did this increase my earning potential, but it also allowed me to work with a wider variety of vehicles and gain valuable experience.

Exploring the Future of Auto Body Technician Salary

So, what can we expect for the future of auto body technician salary? While it's difficult to predict with certainty, there are a few trends that may impact earning potential in the coming years. For example, as electric and hybrid vehicles become more popular, technicians with specialized training in these areas may be in higher demand and command higher salaries.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the average starting salary for an auto body technician?

A: Starting salaries for auto body technicians can vary widely depending on location and employer. However, the median starting salary for this profession is around $32,000 per year.

Q: What factors can impact my salary as an auto body technician?

A: Factors that can impact your salary include experience level, location, type of employer, and specialized skills or certifications.

Q: Is there room for advancement in the auto body technician field?

A: Yes, there are opportunities for advancement in this field. Experienced technicians may be able to move into management or supervisory roles, or may choose to specialize in a particular area of auto body repair.

Q: Can I negotiate my salary as an auto body technician?

A: Yes, it's important to negotiate your salary as an auto body technician to ensure that you are being compensated fairly for your skills and experience.

Conclusion of Auto Body Technician Salary 2020

While auto body technician salary can vary widely depending on a number of factors, understanding the current trends and advocating for yourself during salary negotiations can help ensure that you're earning the highest salary possible. By focusing on specialized skills, considering larger employers, and staying up-to-date on industry trends, you can maximize your earning potential and find success in this rewarding profession.
