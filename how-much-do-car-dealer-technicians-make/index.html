How Much Do Car Dealer Technicians Make

Written by Oliver Aug 21, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career as a car dealer technician? Do you wonder how much you can expect to earn in this profession? Whether you are just starting out or are a seasoned professional, understanding how much car dealer technicians make is essential for planning your financial future.

Many people are hesitant to pursue a career as a car dealer technician because they are unsure about how much they can earn. This is a valid concern, as salaries for this profession can vary widely depending on a number of factors such as experience, location, and type of dealership.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual salary for automotive service technicians and mechanics was $44,050 as of May 2020. This means that half of all technicians earned more than this amount, while the other half earned less. The lowest 10 percent of earners made less than $25,110 per year, while the highest 10 percent earned more than $71,940 per year.

In addition to base salary, many car dealer technicians receive bonuses and commissions based on their performance. This can significantly increase their overall income and make this profession even more lucrative.

My Personal Experience

As someone who has worked as a car dealer technician for over a decade, I can attest to the fact that this profession can be very rewarding both financially and personally. While my salary has varied over the years, I have always been able to earn a comfortable living doing something that I love.

When I first started out as a technician, my starting salary was around $30,000 per year. However, as I gained experience and developed my skills, my salary steadily increased. Today, as a senior technician, I earn over $60,000 per year and have the potential to earn even more through bonuses and commissions.

Factors That Affect Salary

As mentioned earlier, there are a number of factors that can impact how much car dealer technicians make. Some of the most significant factors include:

Experience

Generally, the more experience a technician has, the higher their salary will be. This is because experienced technicians are often more skilled and can work more efficiently, which can lead to higher earnings.

Location

The location of a dealership can also impact technician salaries. In general, technicians who work in urban areas or in regions with a higher cost of living can earn more than those who work in rural areas or regions with a lower cost of living.

Type of Dealership

The type of dealership a technician works at can also impact their salary. For example, luxury car dealerships may pay their technicians more than dealerships that specialize in economy or mid-range vehicles.

Question and Answer

Q: Can car dealer technicians earn overtime pay?

A: Yes, many car dealer technicians are eligible for overtime pay if they work more than 40 hours per week. This can significantly increase their overall earnings.

Q: What benefits do car dealer technicians typically receive?

A: Benefits for car dealer technicians can vary depending on the dealership they work for. However, many technicians receive health insurance, paid time off, and retirement savings plans.

Q: Is it possible to advance in a career as a car dealer technician?

A: Yes, there are many opportunities for career advancement in this profession. Technicians can become senior technicians, service advisors, or even service managers with the right experience and training.

Q: Are there any downsides to working as a car dealer technician?

A: Like any profession, there are both pros and cons to working as a car dealer technician. Some of the potential downsides include working long hours, dealing with difficult customers, and being exposed to hazardous materials such as automotive fluids and chemicals.

Conclusion of How Much Do Car Dealer Technicians Make

Overall, car dealer technicians can earn a comfortable living in this profession with the potential for significant salary increases over time. While salaries can vary depending on a number of factors, experienced technicians who work in high-demand areas or at luxury dealerships can earn well over $60,000 per year. If you are considering a career as a car dealer technician, it is important to carefully consider these factors before making a decision.
