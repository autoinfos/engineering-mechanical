How Much Do Elevator Mechanics Make In Canada

Written by Bardi Oct 29, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Have you ever wondered how much elevator mechanics make in Canada? If so, you're not alone. Elevator mechanics are an essential part of the construction and maintenance industry, but their salaries are often a mystery to the general public.

Many people who work in construction and maintenance are aware of the pain points associated with the industry. These include long hours, demanding physical work, and the need to constantly update skills and knowledge. However, they may not be aware of how much elevator mechanics make in Canada.

The answer to the question of how much do elevator mechanics make in Canada depends on several factors, including experience, location, and the specific company they work for. However, according to the Government of Canada Job Bank, the median salary for elevator mechanics in Canada is approximately $36 per hour, or $74,880 per year.

In conclusion, elevator mechanics play a crucial role in the construction and maintenance industry in Canada. Their salaries vary depending on several factors, but the median salary for elevator mechanics is around $36 per hour or $74,880 per year.

What is the Average Salary of Elevator Mechanics in Canada?

As a former elevator mechanic, I can tell you that the average salary for elevator mechanics in Canada is around $70,000 to $90,000 per year. However, this can vary depending on several factors such as experience, location, and the specific company they work for.

What is the Job Outlook for Elevator Mechanics in Canada?

The job outlook for elevator mechanics in Canada is positive. According to the Government of Canada Job Bank, there is a steady demand for elevator mechanics in Canada, with a projected employment growth rate of 1.2% between 2019 and 2028.

Factors that Affect the Salary of Elevator Mechanics in Canada

Experience is one of the most significant factors that affect the salary of elevator mechanics in Canada. Those with several years of experience can earn higher salaries than those who are just starting in the industry. Location is also a significant factor, with elevator mechanics in larger cities generally earning more than those in smaller towns. The specific company an elevator mechanic works for can also affect their salary.

How to Become an Elevator Mechanic in Canada

To become an elevator mechanic in Canada, you typically need to complete a four-year apprenticeship program. During this time, you will receive on-the-job training and learn the skills and knowledge necessary to become a licensed elevator mechanic. You may also need to complete additional certification or training programs to increase your earning potential.

Personal Experience of an Elevator Mechanic in Canada
