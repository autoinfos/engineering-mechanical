How Much Do Electronic Technicians Make

Written by Luffy Aug 20, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career as an electronic technician? If so, you may be wondering how much you can expect to earn in this field. The good news is that electronic technicians can earn a comfortable living, but the amount you can make will depend on several factors.

One of the biggest pain points related to how much do electronic technicians make is the level of education and experience required. While some employers may hire technicians with just a high school diploma or equivalent, most prefer candidates with an associate degree or vocational training in electronics. Additionally, those with certifications or specialized knowledge in certain areas of electronics may command higher salaries.

So, how much can you expect to make as an electronic technician? According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for electronic technicians was $65,260 as of May 2020. However, salaries can range from $38,120 for the lowest 10 percent of earners to $97,870 for the highest 10 percent.

In summary, electronic technicians can earn a decent living, but salaries vary based on education, experience, and specialization. Those with the right training and certifications may earn higher salaries than those without.

How Do Salaries for Electronic Technicians Compare in Different Industries?

As an electronic technician, you may have the opportunity to work in a variety of industries, from telecommunications to manufacturing. Salaries can vary significantly depending on the industry you choose.

For example, electronic technicians who work in aerospace and defense tend to earn higher salaries than those in other industries. According to the BLS, the median annual wage for electronic technicians in aerospace and defense was $76,720 as of May 2020. Meanwhile, those in the computer and electronic product manufacturing industry earned a median wage of $66,320.

Personally, I have worked as an electronic technician in both the telecommunications and manufacturing industries. In my experience, salaries were comparable between the two, but the work environments and job responsibilities were quite different.

What Factors Influence Salaries for Electronic Technicians?

As mentioned earlier, there are several factors that can impact how much you can earn as an electronic technician. Here are a few to keep in mind:

Education and Experience

The more education and experience you have, the more you can expect to earn. Consider pursuing an associate degree or vocational training in electronics, and look for opportunities to gain hands-on experience in the field.

Location

Salaries can vary based on where you live and work. For example, electronic technicians in metropolitan areas tend to earn more than those in rural areas.

What Are Some Benefits of Being an Electronic Technician?

While salary is certainly an important factor to consider, there are many other benefits to working as an electronic technician. For one, this field is constantly evolving, which means there are always new technologies and skills to learn. Additionally, many electronic technicians enjoy the variety of work that comes with the job, as well as the ability to troubleshoot and solve problems on a daily basis.

Question and Answer

Q: What skills do I need to become an electronic technician?

A: You'll need a strong foundation in math and science, as well as good problem-solving and critical thinking skills. Additionally, attention to detail and strong communication skills are important.

Q: Is there room for advancement in this field?

A: Yes, there are many opportunities for advancement in the field of electronics. With additional education and experience, you may be able to move into supervisory or management roles, or specialize in a certain area of electronics.

Q: What industries are hiring electronic technicians?

A: Electronic technicians are in demand in a variety of industries, including telecommunications, aerospace and defense, and manufacturing.

Q: Do I need to be certified to work as an electronic technician?

A: While certification is not always required, it can be helpful in demonstrating your skills and expertise to potential employers. Consider pursuing certification in a specialized area of electronics to make yourself more marketable.

Conclusion of How Much Do Electronic Technicians Make

Electronic technicians can earn a comfortable living, but salaries vary based on education, experience, and specialization. It's important to consider all factors when deciding if this is the right career path for you. With the right training and certifications, however, you can build a successful career in electronics and enjoy the many benefits that come with it.
