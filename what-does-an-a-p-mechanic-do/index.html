What Does An A P Mechanic Do A Closer Look

Written by William Aug 09, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you interested in aviation and mechanics? Do you want to pursue a career in the aviation industry? If yes, then you’ve probably heard of the term “A&P mechanic”. But what does an A&P mechanic do exactly? In this article, we’ll break down the job description of an A&P mechanic and give you a closer look at what they do.

Working as an A&P mechanic can be a physically and mentally demanding job. The job requires attention to detail, critical thinking, and problem-solving skills. A&P mechanics are responsible for inspecting, repairing, and maintaining aircraft engines, airframes, and other components. They ensure that the aircraft is in good condition and safe to operate. A&P mechanics work in various settings, including airports, repair stations, and manufacturing facilities.

An A&P mechanic holds an airframe and powerplant certificate issued by the Federal Aviation Administration (FAA). The certification requires passing written and practical exams and completing 1,900 hours of training or work experience. A&P mechanics must also participate in ongoing training to maintain their certification.

In summary, an A&P mechanic inspects, repairs, and maintains aircraft engines, airframes, and other components to ensure that they are in good condition and safe to operate. They hold an airframe and powerplant certificate issued by the FAA and undergo ongoing training to maintain their certification.

What Does an A&P Mechanic Do: A Personal Experience

As an A&P mechanic, I always start my day by reviewing the maintenance schedule of the aircraft I’m responsible for. I conduct a thorough inspection of the aircraft’s engines, airframe, and other components to check for any signs of wear and tear or damage. If I find any issues, I determine the cause and recommend a repair or replacement. I also perform routine maintenance tasks, such as changing oil and filters, cleaning and lubricating parts, and inspecting hydraulic and pneumatic systems.

The Role of an A&P Mechanic in Aviation Safety

As an A&P mechanic, I take pride in my role in ensuring that aircraft are safe to operate. I understand that any mistake or oversight on my part can have serious consequences. That’s why I follow strict safety protocols and guidelines when inspecting, repairing, and maintaining aircraft. I also stay up-to-date with the latest technologies and industry standards to provide the best service possible.

How to Become an A&P Mechanic

To become an A&P mechanic, you need to complete an FAA-approved training program or have at least 30 months of on-the-job training. You also need to pass written and practical exams and meet the experience requirements. Once you have your certification, you can work in various aviation settings, such as airlines, private jet companies, and the military.

The Future of A&P Mechanics in Aviation

The demand for A&P mechanics is expected to grow in the coming years as more aircraft are produced and the existing fleet needs maintenance. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for aircraft mechanics and service technicians was $64,090 in May 2020. This makes it a lucrative career for those interested in aviation and mechanics.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the difference between an airframe mechanic and a powerplant mechanic?
A: An airframe mechanic is responsible for inspecting, repairing, and maintaining the structure, electrical systems, and other components of an aircraft's body. A powerplant mechanic is responsible for inspecting, repairing, and maintaining an aircraft's engines and propellers.

Q: What are the common issues that A&P mechanics encounter when inspecting aircraft?
A: A&P mechanics usually encounter issues such as corrosion, fatigue, cracks, and wear and tear on aircraft components. These issues can affect the safety and performance of the aircraft if not addressed promptly.

Q: What are the work settings for A&P mechanics?
A: A&P mechanics can work at airports, repair stations, manufacturing facilities, and military bases. They can also work for airlines, private jet companies, and other aviation organizations.

Q: Can A&P mechanics work on helicopters?
A: Yes, A&P mechanics can work on helicopters as they hold the same airframe and powerplant certification as fixed-wing aircraft.

Conclusion of What Does an A&P Mechanic Do

A&P mechanics play a crucial role in the aviation industry by ensuring that aircraft are safe to operate. They inspect, repair, and maintain aircraft engines, airframes, and other components to ensure that they are in good condition. If you’re interested in pursuing a career as an A&P mechanic, you need to complete an FAA-approved training program, pass written and practical exams, and meet the experience requirements. It’s a challenging yet rewarding career that offers job security and growth opportunities.
