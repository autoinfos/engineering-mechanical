What Does A Service Porter At A Car Dealership Do

Written by Oliver Nov 09, 2023 · 6 min read

Have you ever wondered what a service porter at a car dealership does? If you are a car owner, chances are that you have interacted with one during your visit to a dealership. While many people may think that the role of a service porter is simply to park cars, there is actually much more to the job than that. In this blog post, we will explore the duties and responsibilities of a service porter at a car dealership.

When you visit a car dealership for maintenance or repairs, you may be asked to interact with a service porter. While this may seem like an inconvenience, service porters play an important role in ensuring that the dealership runs smoothly. They are responsible for a variety of tasks, from moving cars around the lot to assisting customers with their needs. However, some people may find it frustrating to interact with a service porter who may not have the level of technical knowledge as a mechanic or service advisor.

A service porter at a car dealership is responsible for a variety of tasks related to the movement and organization of vehicles. These tasks may include parking cars, moving them around the lot, and bringing them to the service area for maintenance or repairs. Additionally, service porters may be responsible for washing and cleaning cars before they are returned to the customer. They may also assist customers with their needs, such as helping them locate their car or providing basic information about the services offered at the dealership.

In summary, a service porter at a car dealership plays an important role in ensuring that the dealership runs smoothly. They are responsible for a variety of tasks related to the movement and organization of vehicles, as well as assisting customers with their needs. While they may not have the same level of technical knowledge as a mechanic or service advisor, they are an essential part of the team.

What Does a Service Porter at a Car Dealership Do - Explained

As a former service porter at a car dealership, I can say that the job is not as simple as it may seem. While the primary responsibility of a service porter is to move cars around the lot, there are many other tasks that are required to ensure that the dealership runs smoothly. One of the most important tasks is ensuring that the service area is organized and efficient. This means that the porter must be able to locate and move cars quickly and efficiently, as well as communicate effectively with the service advisors and mechanics.

Another important responsibility of a service porter is customer service. While they may not have the same level of technical knowledge as a mechanic or service advisor, they are often the first point of contact for customers when they arrive at the dealership. This means that they must be able to answer basic questions about the services offered, as well as provide assistance with locating cars and other tasks. Additionally, service porters may be responsible for preparing cars for delivery to customers, which involves washing and cleaning them to ensure that they are in top condition.

What Does a Service Porter at a Car Dealership Do - Duties and Responsibilities

As we have discussed, the duties and responsibilities of a service porter at a car dealership are varied and important. Some of the key duties include:

Parking and Moving Cars:

The primary responsibility of a service porter is to move cars around the dealership lot, ensuring that they are in the correct location for maintenance, repairs, or customer delivery.

Cleaning and Maintenance:

Service porters may be responsible for washing and cleaning cars before they are returned to the customer. Additionally, they may be required to perform basic maintenance tasks, such as checking fluid levels and tire pressure.

Assisting Customers:

Service porters are often the first point of contact for customers when they arrive at the dealership. This means that they must be able to answer basic questions about the services offered, as well as provide assistance with locating cars and other tasks.

Preparing Cars for Delivery:

Service porters may be responsible for preparing cars for delivery to customers, which involves washing and cleaning them to ensure that they are in top condition.
