Average Salary For Electronic Engineering Technician Everything You Need To Know

Written by Daniel Oct 20, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you interested in pursuing a career as an electronic engineering technician but wondering about the average salary? Look no further! In this blog post, we will delve into the average salary for electronic engineering technicians and provide you with all the information you need to know.

As with any career, there are both pros and cons to becoming an electronic engineering technician. One aspect that can often be a concern for those interested in the field is the average salary. It is important to have a realistic understanding of what you can expect to earn in this role.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual salary for electronic engineering technicians in the United States is $65,260 as of May 2020. However, this can vary depending on factors such as experience, location, and industry.

In summary, electronic engineering technicians can expect to earn a median annual salary of $65,260. Factors such as experience, location, and industry can impact this figure. Now, let's take a closer look at this topic.

The Importance of Experience

Experience can play a significant role in how much an electronic engineering technician can earn. Those just starting out in the field can expect to earn less than those with several years of experience. In fact, the Bureau of Labor Statistics reports that the bottom 10 percent of earners in this field make less than $39,840, while the top 10 percent earn more than $97,870.

Personally, I have seen firsthand how experience can impact an electronic engineering technician's salary. A friend of mine started in the field with no prior experience and earned around $50,000 per year. However, after just a few years of working and gaining experience, they were able to increase their salary to over $70,000 per year.

The Impact of Location and Industry

Location and industry can also impact an electronic engineering technician's salary. Those working in larger cities or in industries such as aerospace and defense tend to earn more than those working in smaller towns or in industries such as telecommunications.

For example, an electronic engineering technician working in San Francisco, California can expect to earn an average of $79,370 per year, while someone working in Fargo, North Dakota would earn an average of $56,890 per year. This is due to differences in cost of living and demand for skilled workers in different areas.

The Role of Education

While not always required, having a degree in electronics or a related field can increase an electronic engineering technician's salary. Those with an associate's degree can expect to earn more than those with just a high school diploma, while those with a bachelor's degree can earn even more.

In fact, the Bureau of Labor Statistics reports that the median annual salary for electronic engineering technicians with an associate's degree is $63,420, while those with a bachelor's degree can earn a median annual salary of $85,640.

Negotiating Your Salary

When starting a new job as an electronic engineering technician, it is important to negotiate your salary. Many employers are willing to negotiate, and doing so can help you earn more money in the long run.

Before negotiating, it is important to do your research and have a realistic understanding of what you should be earning based on your experience, location, and industry. You can use websites such as Glassdoor or the Bureau of Labor Statistics to gather this information.

Conclusion of Average Salary for Electronic Engineering Technician

Overall, electronic engineering technicians can expect to earn a median annual salary of $65,260. However, factors such as experience, location, industry, and education can impact this figure. By gaining experience, working in higher-paying industries or areas, earning a degree, and negotiating your salary, you can increase your earning potential as an electronic engineering technician.

Question and Answer

Q: Is the demand for electronic engineering technicians increasing?
A: Yes, the Bureau of Labor Statistics reports that employment of electronic engineering technicians is projected to grow 2 percent from 2019 to 2029, which is about as fast as the average for all occupations.

Q: What are some industries that pay electronic engineering technicians well?
A: Aerospace and defense, government, and scientific research and development services are some industries that tend to pay electronic engineering technicians more than others.

Q: Is it possible to become an electronic engineering technician without a degree?
A: Yes, although having a degree can increase your earning potential, it is not always required. Some electronic engineering technicians learn on the job or through vocational programs.

Q: How can I negotiate my salary as an electronic engineering technician?
A: Before negotiating, do your research and have a realistic understanding of what you should be earning based on your experience, location, and industry. Be confident and professional in your approach, and come prepared with examples of your accomplishments and contributions to the company.
