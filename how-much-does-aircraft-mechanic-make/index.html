Aspiring aircraft mechanics often wonder whether the salary of an aircraft mechanic is worth the investment in education and training. So, it's essential to know the earning potential of an aircraft mechanic to determine if it's worth pursuing.

On average, an aircraft mechanic in the United States earns around $65,000 per year. However, this salary can vary based on the location, experience, and certification of the mechanic. Entry-level aircraft mechanics can expect to earn around $40,000 per year, while experienced mechanics can earn up to $100,000 per year.

One of the most significant factors that affect the salary of an aircraft mechanic is location. The demand for mechanics in certain areas can be higher, resulting in higher salaries. For example, aircraft mechanics working in Alaska, Hawaii, and California earn more than the national average.

Experience is another factor that affects the salary of an aircraft mechanic. Entry-level mechanics typically earn less than experienced mechanics. However, with experience comes a higher salary. Experienced aircraft mechanics can earn up to $100,000 per year.

How to Become an Aircraft Mechanic?

Becoming an aircraft mechanic requires significant investment in education and training. You must complete FAA-approved training and obtain certification to work as an aircraft mechanic. Here are the steps to become an aircraft mechanic:

Step 1: Obtain High School Diploma or GED

The first step to becoming an aircraft mechanic is to obtain a high school diploma or GED. Most FAA-approved training programs require a high school diploma or equivalent.

Step 2: Complete FAA-Approved Training Program

After obtaining a high school diploma, you must complete an FAA-approved training program. These programs can take anywhere from 18 months to two years to complete. Upon completion, you will receive an Airframe and Powerplant (A&P) certificate.
