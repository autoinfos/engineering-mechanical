Is Surgical Technology A Good Career

Written by Robby Aug 22, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Have you ever wondered about a career in surgical technology? It can be a highly rewarding and exciting career path, but is it a good one? Let's dive in and find out.

One of the biggest concerns when considering any career is job stability. With surgical technology, there is a high demand for skilled professionals in the healthcare field. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, employment of surgical technologists is projected to grow 7 percent from 2019 to 2029, faster than the average for all occupations. This means that there will likely be a steady need for surgical technologists in the years to come.

So, is surgical technology a good career? The answer is a resounding yes! There are many benefits to pursuing a career in surgical technology, including job security, competitive pay, and the opportunity to work in a fast-paced and exciting environment.

In summary, a career in surgical technology is a great choice for those looking for a stable and rewarding career. With a growing demand for skilled professionals, competitive pay, and an exciting work environment, it's definitely worth considering.

The Benefits of a Career in Surgical Technology

As someone who has worked in surgical technology for several years, I can attest to the many benefits of this career path.

First and foremost, the job security in this field is excellent. As mentioned earlier, there is a high demand for surgical technologists, which means that you're likely to have job opportunities available to you throughout your career.

Additionally, the pay for surgical technologists is quite competitive. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for surgical technologists was $49,710 in May 2019. This is a decent salary for a career that typically requires only an associate's degree.

The Educational Requirements for Surgical Technology

So, what does it take to become a surgical technologist? The educational requirements for this career typically involve completing an accredited surgical technology program, which can last anywhere from 9 months to 2 years depending on the program. After completing your program, you will need to pass a certification exam to become a Certified Surgical Technologist (CST).

How to Get Started in Surgical Technology

If you're interested in pursuing a career in surgical technology, the first step is to research accredited programs in your area. Once you've found a program that interests you, be sure to speak with an admissions counselor to learn more about the program and its requirements.

My Experience in Surgical Technology

As someone who has worked in surgical technology for several years, I can say without a doubt that it's been a great career choice for me. I love the fast-paced environment of the operating room, and I find it incredibly rewarding to be a part of a team that is helping to save lives.

Question and Answer

Q: Is surgical technology a physically demanding career?

A: Yes, surgical technology can be physically demanding, as it involves standing for long periods of time and assisting with patient transfers and positioning.

Q: What skills are important for a career in surgical technology?

A: Some important skills for surgical technologists include attention to detail, strong communication skills, and the ability to work well under pressure.

Q: Can surgical technologists advance in their careers?

A: Yes, there are opportunities for surgical technologists to advance in their careers by pursuing additional education or certifications, or by specializing in a particular area of surgery.

Q: What is the work schedule like for surgical technologists?

A: The work schedule for surgical technologists can vary depending on the facility and the type of surgery being performed. Many surgical technologists work full-time, but there may be opportunities for part-time or on-call work as well.

Conclusion

In conclusion, a career in surgical technology can be an excellent choice for those looking for a stable and rewarding career in healthcare. With a growing demand for skilled professionals, competitive pay, and an exciting work environment, it's definitely worth considering if you're interested in this field.
