How Much Do Mechanics Make In Usa

Written by Petter Aug 14, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Are you considering a career in mechanics? Or perhaps you're already working as a mechanic and wondering if you're being paid fairly? Whatever the case may be, understanding how much mechanics make in the USA is an important factor to consider.

For many people, the salary of a mechanic is a pain point. The fear of not earning enough to support themselves and their families is a real concern. And with the rising cost of living, it's no wonder that people are worried about their income.

So, how much do mechanics make in the USA? According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for automotive service technicians and mechanics was $44,050 as of May 2020. However, this number can vary depending on factors such as location, experience, and skill level.

In summary, mechanics in the USA can earn a median salary of $44,050 per year. However, this number can vary based on various factors.

What Affects a Mechanic's Salary?

Several factors can impact a mechanic's salary, including:

Location: Mechanics in urban areas tend to earn more than those in rural areas due to the higher cost of living.

Experience: Mechanics with more experience will typically earn more than those who are just starting.

Skill Level: Mechanics who have specialized skills, such as working on diesel engines or hybrids, can earn a higher salary.

Personally, I've found that gaining experience in different areas of mechanics has helped me increase my salary over time. By constantly learning and improving my skills, I've been able to take on more challenging jobs and earn a higher wage.

Education and Certification

Education and certification can also impact a mechanic's salary. Many employers prefer to hire mechanics who have completed a formal training program or apprenticeship. Additionally, obtaining certification from organizations such as the National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence (ASE) can increase a mechanic's earning potential.

For me, obtaining ASE certification was a game-changer. It not only helped me increase my salary, but it also gave me the confidence to take on more challenging jobs and further my career.

Conclusion

In conclusion, how much mechanics make in the USA can vary based on several factors such as location, experience, skill level, education, and certification. By constantly improving your skills and gaining experience, you can increase your earning potential and enjoy a fulfilling career as a mechanic.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What is the average hourly wage for mechanics in the USA?

A: The average hourly wage for mechanics in the USA is $21.17 per hour.

Q: Do mechanics make more money working for dealerships or independent repair shops?

A: It depends on the dealership or repair shop. Some dealerships may offer higher salaries, while some independent repair shops may provide more opportunities for bonuses or commissions.

Q: Can becoming ASE certified help me earn more as a mechanic?

A: Yes, obtaining certification from organizations such as ASE can increase a mechanic's earning potential.

Q: What is the job outlook for mechanics in the USA?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, employment of automotive service technicians and mechanics is projected to grow 4 percent from 2019 to 2029, about as fast as the average for all occupations.
