How Much Do Vet Techs Get Paid Per Hour

Written by Oliver Nov 11, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you considering a career as a veterinary technician? If so, you may be wondering how much you can expect to earn. It's important to know the salary range for this profession to make an informed decision about your future career. In this blog post, we'll explore how much vet techs get paid per hour and what factors can affect their earnings.

As a vet tech, you may encounter some challenges when it comes to your salary. The profession is known for its low pay, especially in comparison to other healthcare professions. Additionally, salaries can vary depending on the state, city, and type of facility where you work.

The average hourly wage for vet techs in the United States is around $16.44 per hour, according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics. However, this can vary depending on several factors. For example, vet techs who work in research facilities or specialty clinics may earn more than those who work in general practices or animal shelters.

In summary, how much vet techs get paid per hour can vary depending on several factors, including the state, city, and type of facility where they work. The average hourly wage for vet techs in the United States is around $16.44 per hour, but this can be higher or lower depending on various factors.

Factors That Affect How Much Vet Techs Get Paid Per Hour

One of the biggest factors that can affect how much vet techs get paid per hour is their location. Some states, such as California and New York, have higher costs of living and therefore tend to pay vet techs more than other states. Additionally, vet techs who work in cities or metropolitan areas may earn more than those who work in rural areas.

The type of facility where a vet tech works can also impact their earnings. For example, vet techs who work in research facilities or specialty clinics may earn more than those who work in general practices or animal shelters. Additionally, experience can play a role in how much a vet tech earns. Those who have been working in the field for several years and have advanced certifications or degrees may earn more than those who are just starting out.

My Experience with Vet Tech Salaries

As a former veterinary technician, I can attest to the challenges of low pay in this profession. When I first started working as a vet tech, I earned around $12 per hour. However, as I gained more experience and advanced certifications, my hourly wage increased to around $18 per hour.

Tips for Increasing Your Vet Tech Salary

If you're a vet tech looking to increase your salary, there are several steps you can take. One is to continue your education and earn advanced certifications or degrees. This can show employers that you're committed to the profession and have a higher level of knowledge and skills. Additionally, networking with other professionals in the field can help you learn about job openings and opportunities for advancement.

How to Negotiate Your Vet Tech Salary

If you're starting a new job as a vet tech or looking to negotiate your current salary, there are several strategies you can use. One is to research the average salary range for vet techs in your area and use this information to negotiate with your employer. You can also highlight your experience, certifications, and skills to show why you deserve a higher salary.

Other Careers to Consider in the Animal Health Field

If you're passionate about animal health but concerned about the low pay of vet techs, there are other careers to consider in this field. Some options include veterinary assistants, animal behaviorists, and animal control officers. These careers can offer higher salaries and opportunities for advancement.

Conclusion of How Much Do Vet Techs Get Paid Per Hour

As we've explored in this blog post, how much vet techs get paid per hour can vary depending on several factors. These include the state, city, and type of facility where they work, as well as their experience and certifications. While the profession is known for its low pay, there are steps vet techs can take to increase their earnings and advance their careers.
