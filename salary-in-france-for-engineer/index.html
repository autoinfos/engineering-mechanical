Salary In France For Engineers What You Need To Know

Written by Petter Jul 18, 2023 · 5 min read

France is known for its stunning architecture, rich history, and delicious cuisine. However, if you're an engineer considering a move to France, it's important to understand the country's salary structure. In this blog post, we'll explore the ins and outs of salaries in France for engineers and provide you with the knowledge you need to make an informed decision.

One of the biggest pain points for engineers moving to France is the seemingly low salaries compared to other countries. However, it's important to remember that France has a different cost of living and social benefits system than other countries. Understanding these factors can help you make a more accurate comparison and evaluate whether a salary in France is sufficient for your needs.

The target salary for an engineer in France ranges from €30,000 to €60,000 per year, depending on your experience and field of expertise. The average salary for an engineer in France is around €45,000 per year. While this may seem low compared to other countries, it's important to keep in mind that France has a high quality of life, excellent healthcare, and generous social benefits, including paid vacation time, maternity and paternity leave, and a strong retirement system.

In conclusion, while salaries for engineers in France may seem lower than in other countries, it's essential to consider the country's cost of living and social benefits system. With an average salary of €45,000 per year and a high quality of life, France can be an excellent place to work and live as an engineer.

What is the Target Salary for Engineers in France?

The target salary for an engineer in France varies depending on your field of expertise and level of experience. As a general rule, the average salary for an engineer in France is around €45,000 per year. However, engineers in more specialized fields or with more experience may be able to earn higher salaries, up to €60,000 per year.

Personally, I have found that the salary range for engineers in France is sufficient to live comfortably, especially considering the country's high quality of life and social benefits system. While it may not be as high as other countries, the benefits of living and working in France more than make up for this difference.

What Factors Affect Salaries for Engineers in France?

Several factors can affect salaries for engineers in France, including your field of expertise, level of experience, and location. Generally, engineers in more specialized fields or with more experience can earn higher salaries. Additionally, engineers working in larger cities like Paris or Lyon may earn higher salaries due to the higher cost of living in these areas.

Personally, I have found that networking and building relationships with colleagues and industry professionals can also be a factor in earning a higher salary as an engineer in France. By establishing yourself as an expert in your field and building a strong reputation, you may be able to negotiate a higher salary or secure more lucrative job opportunities.

What is the Cost of Living in France?

The cost of living in France is generally higher than in other countries, particularly when it comes to housing and transportation. However, the country's excellent healthcare system, affordable public transportation, and high quality of life make it a desirable place to live and work.

It's important to factor in the cost of living when evaluating a salary offer for an engineering position in France. While salaries may be lower than in other countries, the benefits of living and working in France can more than make up for this difference.

How Can Engineers Negotiate Higher Salaries in France?

One way for engineers to negotiate higher salaries in France is to establish themselves as experts in their field and build relationships with industry professionals. By networking and building a strong reputation, engineers may be able to secure more lucrative job opportunities or negotiate higher salaries.

Additionally, it's important to research the average salaries for engineers in your field and level of experience in France, as well as the cost of living in your desired location. Armed with this knowledge, you can make an informed decision about the salary offer and negotiate from a position of strength.

Question and Answer

Q: Is the salary for engineers in France sufficient for living expenses?

A: Yes, the salary range for engineers in France is generally sufficient to live comfortably, especially considering the country's high quality of life and social benefits system.


Q: What is the average salary for an engineer in France?

A: The average salary for an engineer in France is around €45,000 per year.


Q: What factors affect salaries for engineers in France?

A: Factors that can affect salaries for engineers in France include field of expertise, level of experience, and location. Engineers in more specialized fields or with more experience can generally earn higher salaries, as well as those working in larger cities with a higher cost of living.


Q: How can engineers negotiate higher salaries in France?

A: Engineers can negotiate higher salaries in France by building relationships with industry professionals, establishing themselves as experts in their field, and researching the average salaries and cost of living in their desired location.

Conclusion of Salary in France for Engineers

While salaries for engineers in France may seem low compared to other countries, it's important to consider the country's cost of living and social benefits system when evaluating a salary offer. With an average salary of €45,000 per year and a high quality of life, France can be an excellent place to live and work as an engineer. By networking, building relationships with industry professionals, and negotiating from a position of strength, engineers can secure more lucrative job opportunities and earn higher salaries in France.
