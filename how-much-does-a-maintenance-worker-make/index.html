How Much Does A Maintenance Worker Make

Written by Christine Oct 20, 2023 Â· 4 min read

When it comes to planning a career path, one of the major considerations is the salary. After all, everyone wants to be compensated fairly for their work. If you're considering becoming a maintenance worker, you may be curious about how much you can expect to make. In this post, we'll take a closer look at how much does a maintenance worker make, and what factors can impact their salary.

Factors That Affect a Maintenance Worker's Salary

There are several factors that can impact how much does a maintenance worker make. These include:

  • Location
  • Experience
  • Education
  • Type of employer

Let's take a closer look at each of these factors.

Location

One of the biggest factors that can impact how much does a maintenance worker make is their location. Salaries can vary significantly depending on where you work. For example, a maintenance worker in a major city may make more than one in a rural area.

Experience

As with most jobs, experience can have a big impact on how much does a maintenance worker make. Those with more experience in the field may command a higher salary than those who are just starting out.

Education

While a degree isn't always required to become a maintenance worker, having a relevant degree or certification can help increase your earning potential. This is especially true for more specialized maintenance jobs.

Type of Employer

The type of employer can also have an impact on how much does a maintenance worker make. For example, a maintenance worker for a school district may make less than one who works for a private company.

Personal Experience

When I first started out as a maintenance worker, I wasn't sure what to expect in terms of salary. I had some experience in the field, but not a lot. I ended up accepting a job with a private company that paid a fair wage for my level of experience.

Over time, I gained more experience and took on additional responsibilities. This allowed me to negotiate a higher salary with my employer. I also took some courses related to maintenance, which helped me increase my earning potential even further.

Salary Range for Maintenance Workers

So, how much does a maintenance worker make? According to data from the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual salary for maintenance workers was $39,180 as of May 2020. The lowest 10 percent earned less than $25,980, while the highest 10 percent earned more than $64,890.

What Are the Job Prospects Like for Maintenance Workers?

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, employment of maintenance workers is projected to grow 6 percent from 2019 to 2029, which is faster than the average for all occupations. This growth is due to the need for maintenance workers in a variety of industries.

How Can You Increase Your Earning Potential as a Maintenance Worker?

If you're looking to increase your earning potential as a maintenance worker, there are several steps you can take. These include:

  • Gaining more experience in the field
  • Obtaining relevant certifications or degrees
  • Taking on additional responsibilities
  • Working for a company that offers higher salaries

Conclusion of How Much Does a Maintenance Worker Make

While the salary for maintenance workers can vary depending on several factors, it's generally a fair wage for the work performed. With some experience and additional education, it's possible to increase your earning potential and advance in the field.
