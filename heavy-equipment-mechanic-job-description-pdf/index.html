Question and Answer

Q: What kind of equipment will I be working on as a heavy equipment mechanic?

A: As a heavy equipment mechanic, you may work on a variety of different types of equipment, including bulldozers, cranes, excavators, and more.

Q: Do I need to have a degree to become a heavy equipment mechanic?

A: While a degree is not always required, it can be helpful in securing a job as a heavy equipment mechanic. Additionally, you will need to have the right technical training and certifications in order to work on certain types of equipment.

Q: What kind of salary can I expect as a heavy equipment mechanic?

A: Salaries for heavy equipment mechanics can vary depending on a number of factors, including your location, level of experience, and the type of equipment that you are working on. However, according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for heavy equipment mechanics was $53,370 as of May 2020.

Q: What are some of the challenges of working as a heavy equipment mechanic?

A: Some of the challenges of working as a heavy equipment mechanic include the physical demands of the job, the noise and dirt associated with working on heavy machinery, and the need to constantly stay up-to-date with new equipment and technology.
