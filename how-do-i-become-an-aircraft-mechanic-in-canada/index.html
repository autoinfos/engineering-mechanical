How To Become An Aircraft Mechanic In Canada

Written by Eveline May 28, 2023 · 4 min read

Do you have a passion for aviation and want to work on planes? Becoming an aircraft mechanic in Canada can be a fulfilling career choice, but it can also be overwhelming to navigate the requirements and certifications needed to get started.

One of the biggest pain points for those interested in becoming an aircraft mechanic in Canada is understanding the necessary steps to take and the different certifications required.

First, you will need to obtain a Transport Canada Aircraft Maintenance Engineer (AME) license. To do so, you must complete an approved training program and gain a certain amount of work experience. You will also need to pass a series of exams and fulfill additional requirements depending on the specific area of work you want to specialize in.

In summary, becoming an aircraft mechanic in Canada requires completing an approved training program, gaining work experience, passing exams, and obtaining a Transport Canada AME license.

Understanding the Training and Certification Process

When I decided to pursue a career as an aircraft mechanic in Canada, I was unsure of where to start. I eventually found an approved training program at a local college that offered a mix of classroom instruction and hands-on experience. The program covered everything from basic mechanical principles to advanced avionics systems.

After completing the training program, I started working as an apprentice under a licensed AME. This allowed me to gain the work experience necessary to apply for my own AME license. I also had to pass a series of exams, including a written exam and a practical exam, which tested my ability to perform maintenance tasks on aircraft.

Specializing in a Specific Area

One of the benefits of becoming an aircraft mechanic in Canada is the ability to specialize in a specific area of work. For example, you can specialize in airframe maintenance, avionics systems, or engines. Each specialization requires additional training and certification beyond the basic AME license.

Obtaining Additional Certifications

If you want to specialize in a specific area of work, you will need to obtain additional certifications beyond the basic AME license. For example, if you want to specialize in avionics systems, you will need to obtain a Transport Canada Avionics Maintenance Engineer (AME) license. This requires completing an approved training program and passing a series of exams specific to avionics systems.

Continuing Education and Staying Up-to-Date

Once you become an aircraft mechanic in Canada, it is important to continue your education and stay up-to-date with the latest technologies and techniques. This can include attending conferences and workshops, taking additional training courses, and staying informed about changes to regulations and industry standards.

Personal Experience

As someone who has gone through the process of becoming an aircraft mechanic in Canada, I can attest to the challenging but rewarding nature of the career. The training and certification process can be time-consuming and expensive, but the ability to work on planes and ensure their safety is a fulfilling experience.

Question and Answer

Q: How long does it take to become an aircraft mechanic in Canada?
A: The length of time it takes to become an aircraft mechanic in Canada varies depending on the training program and work experience required. On average, it can take around 2-3 years.

Q: Is it necessary to specialize in a specific area of work?
A: No, it is not necessary to specialize in a specific area of work, but it can provide additional job opportunities and higher pay.

Q: Are there any physical requirements to become an aircraft mechanic in Canada?
A: Yes, aircraft mechanics must be able to perform physically demanding tasks, such as lifting heavy objects and working in tight spaces.

Q: What are some common job duties for aircraft mechanics in Canada?
A: Common job duties for aircraft mechanics in Canada include performing routine maintenance tasks, troubleshooting mechanical problems, and repairing and replacing aircraft components.

Conclusion of How to Become an Aircraft Mechanic in Canada

Becoming an aircraft mechanic in Canada requires completing an approved training program, gaining work experience, passing exams, and obtaining a Transport Canada AME license. Specializing in a specific area of work can provide additional job opportunities and higher pay, but requires additional training and certification. Continuing education and staying up-to-date with the latest technologies and techniques is important for a successful career as an aircraft mechanic in Canada.
