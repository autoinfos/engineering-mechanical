Is Surgical Tech A Good Career Path

Written by Bardi Jun 16, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Have you been considering a career as a surgical tech? If so, you may be wondering if it's a good career path to pursue. Well, the short answer is yes, it is a good career path. However, let's dive deeper into why.

One of the biggest pain points for individuals considering a career as a surgical tech is job security. No one wants to invest time and money into a career that may not have job opportunities in the future. Fortunately, the demand for surgical techs is expected to grow by 7% from 2019 to 2029, according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics. This growth is faster than the average for all occupations, meaning there will be an abundance of job opportunities for those pursuing this career path.

Now, let's answer the main question: is surgical tech a good career path? The answer is a resounding yes. Not only is there job security, but the salary is also attractive. The median annual wage for surgical technologists was $49,710 in May 2020, with the highest 10 percent earning more than $71,400. Additionally, the job outlook is positive, with an increasing need for surgical techs in hospitals, outpatient care centers, and physician offices.

In summary, becoming a surgical tech is a smart career choice. With job security, a competitive salary, and positive job outlook, it's a career path worth considering. Let's dive deeper into the reasons why.

What is Surgical Tech and its Career Path?

A surgical tech, also known as a surgical technologist, is a healthcare professional who assists surgeons during surgical operations. They prepare the operating room, arrange equipment, and help the surgical team put on sterile gowns and gloves. They also assist with patient positioning and prepare and maintain surgical instruments and supplies.

As for the career path, a surgical tech typically needs an associate degree or postsecondary certificate from an accredited program. The program usually takes 9 to 24 months to complete, and it includes coursework in anatomy, medical terminology, and pharmacology. After completing the program, surgical techs must become certified through the National Board of Surgical Technology and Surgical Assisting.

My Personal Experience as a Surgical Tech

As a surgical tech myself, I can attest to the benefits of this career path. I've been working in this field for over a decade, and I've found it to be challenging, rewarding, and fulfilling. The job requires a high level of attention to detail, critical thinking, and teamwork. I love being able to assist in lifesaving surgeries and being a crucial part of the surgical team.

The Benefits of Pursuing a Career as a Surgical Tech

Aside from job security and a competitive salary, there are other benefits to pursuing a career as a surgical tech. For one, the job requires less education and training than other healthcare professions, such as doctors and nurses. Additionally, surgical techs have the opportunity to specialize in specific surgical procedures, such as orthopedics or neurosurgery, which can lead to higher salaries and job opportunities.

The Importance of Continuing Education in Surgical Tech

Continuing education is crucial in the field of surgical technology. As medical technology advances, surgical techs must stay up-to-date with the latest equipment and procedures. Additionally, continuing education can lead to career advancement opportunities, such as becoming a surgical first assistant or a surgical tech instructor.

How to Become a Successful Surgical Tech

Becoming a successful surgical tech requires a combination of education, training, and personal skills. In addition to completing an accredited surgical tech program and becoming certified, it's important to have strong communication skills, attention to detail, and the ability to work well under pressure. Additionally, being a team player and having a positive attitude can go a long way in this field.

Conclusion of Is Surgical Tech a Good Career Path

In conclusion, pursuing a career as a surgical tech is a smart choice for those interested in healthcare and the medical field. With job security, a competitive salary, and positive job outlook, it's a career path worth considering. By completing an accredited program, becoming certified, and continuing education, you can become a successful surgical tech and make a difference in the lives of patients.
