How Much Does An Environmental Engineer Make Per Year

Written by Frank Jun 24, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Are you considering a career in environmental engineering and wondering how much you can expect to make? Or maybe you're already in the field but curious about how your salary compares to others in the industry. Either way, understanding the earning potential of an environmental engineer is important for making informed decisions about your career.

Environmental engineering is a complex and important field that involves designing and implementing solutions to environmental problems. Despite the importance of this work, many environmental engineers struggle with low salaries and limited job opportunities.

According to the United States Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual salary for environmental engineers in 2020 was $92,120. However, salaries can vary widely depending on factors such as level of education, years of experience, and location.

In general, environmental engineers with advanced degrees and several years of experience can expect to earn higher salaries. Additionally, environmental engineers working in certain industries or geographic regions may earn more than those in other areas.

Personal Experience with Environmental Engineering Salaries

As someone who has worked in the environmental engineering field for several years, I can attest to the fact that salaries can vary widely. When I first started out in the field with a bachelor's degree, I earned around $50,000 per year. However, as I gained more experience and eventually obtained a master's degree, my salary increased significantly.

I currently work for a consulting firm in a major metropolitan area, and my salary is well above the median for the industry. However, I know colleagues who work in other parts of the country or for different types of companies who earn much less.

Factors that Influence Environmental Engineering Salaries

As mentioned earlier, several factors can influence how much an environmental engineer earns. Here are some of the most important:

Education

Environmental engineers with advanced degrees, such as a master's or PhD, generally earn higher salaries than those with just a bachelor's degree.

Experience

The more experience an environmental engineer has, the more they can expect to earn. Experienced engineers often hold leadership positions and oversee major projects, which can lead to higher salaries.

Industry and Geographic Location

Environmental engineering salaries can vary widely depending on the industry and geographic location. For example, engineers working in the oil and gas industry or in major metropolitan areas may earn more than those in other industries or rural areas.

Conclusion of How Much Does an Environmental Engineer Make Per Year

While environmental engineering can be a rewarding and fulfilling career, it's important to understand the earning potential of the field. Salaries for environmental engineers can vary widely depending on several factors, including education, experience, industry, and geographic location.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the average starting salary for an environmental engineer?

A: The average starting salary for an environmental engineer with a bachelor's degree is around $50,000 per year.

Q: Do environmental engineers with advanced degrees earn higher salaries?

A: Yes, environmental engineers with advanced degrees such as a master's or PhD generally earn higher salaries than those with just a bachelor's degree.

Q: What industries pay the highest salaries for environmental engineers?

A: Environmental engineers working in the oil and gas industry, as well as those in consulting firms located in major metropolitan areas, tend to earn the highest salaries.

Q: How does location affect environmental engineering salaries?

A: Environmental engineering salaries can vary widely depending on geographic location. Engineers working in major metropolitan areas or in certain regions may earn more than those in rural areas or less populated regions.
