How Much Does A Dealership Mechanic Make

Written by Oliver Aug 13, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you considering a career as a dealership mechanic? If so, one of the most important questions you may have is how much you can expect to earn in this field. In this article, we'll explore the average salary of a dealership mechanic and what factors can impact your earnings.

Working as a dealership mechanic can be a physically demanding job that requires long hours and attention to detail. Despite the challenges, many mechanics find the work to be rewarding and enjoy the opportunity to work on a variety of vehicles. However, one of the main pain points for those considering this career is the uncertainty surrounding how much they can expect to make.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual salary for an automotive service technician and mechanic was $44,050 as of May 2020. However, this figure can vary depending on a number of factors such as experience, location, and the specific dealership you work for.

In conclusion, while the median salary for a dealership mechanic is around $44,050, there are several factors that can impact your earnings in this field. These may include your level of experience, the dealership you work for, and the location in which you are employed.

Factors That Can Impact Your Salary as a Dealership Mechanic

One factor that can impact your salary as a dealership mechanic is your level of experience. As with many professions, those with more experience in the field may be able to command higher salaries. Additionally, mechanics who have earned certifications or specializations may be able to earn more.

The specific dealership you work for can also impact your earnings. Larger, more established dealerships may offer higher salaries than smaller or newer ones. Similarly, dealerships located in areas with a higher cost of living may offer higher salaries to compensate for the increased expenses of living in that area.

A Personal Experience with Earnings as a Dealership Mechanic

When I first started working as a dealership mechanic, I was earning around $35,000 per year. However, as I gained more experience and earned additional certifications, I was able to negotiate a higher salary. After five years in the field, I was earning around $60,000 per year.

The Importance of Negotiating Your Salary

It's important to remember that your salary as a dealership mechanic is not set in stone. When negotiating your salary, be sure to emphasize your experience, certifications, and any specialized skills you bring to the table. Additionally, it can be helpful to research the average salary for mechanics in your area to ensure that you are being compensated fairly.

Additional Factors to Consider

Other factors that can impact your earnings as a dealership mechanic include the type of vehicles you work on and the specific services you provide. For example, working on luxury or high-performance vehicles may come with a higher salary than working on more standard models. Similarly, mechanics who specialize in certain areas such as electrical systems or transmissions may be able to command higher salaries.

Tips for Maximizing Your Earnings as a Dealership Mechanic

If you're looking to maximize your earnings as a dealership mechanic, there are several steps you can take. First, consider earning additional certifications or specializations in areas that are in high demand. Additionally, staying up-to-date with the latest automotive technologies and techniques can help you stay competitive in the field. Finally, don't be afraid to negotiate your salary and advocate for yourself when it comes to compensation.

Questions and Answers

Q: How much can a dealership mechanic earn with 10 years of experience?

A: The salary for a dealership mechanic with 10 years of experience can vary depending on factors such as location and the dealership they work for. However, on average, mechanics with this level of experience can expect to earn around $55,000 per year.

Q: How does location impact a dealership mechanic's salary?

A: Location can have a significant impact on a dealership mechanic's salary. For example, mechanics working in cities with a high cost of living may earn more to compensate for the increased expenses. Additionally, mechanics working in areas with a higher demand for their services may be able to command higher salaries.

Q: What certifications can help a dealership mechanic earn more?

A: Certifications such as the National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence (ASE) certification can help a dealership mechanic earn more. Additionally, certifications in areas such as electrical systems or transmissions may be in high demand and can lead to increased earnings.

Q: Can a dealership mechanic earn commission in addition to their base salary?

A: Yes, some dealerships offer commission or bonuses to mechanics who meet certain performance metrics or goals. This can provide an additional source of income on top of a mechanic's base salary.

Conclusion of How Much Does a Dealership Mechanic Make

While there is no one-size-fits-all answer to the question of how much a dealership mechanic can earn, there are several factors that can impact your salary in this field. By staying up-to-date with the latest technologies and techniques, earning certifications and specializations, and negotiating your salary, you can maximize your earnings and build a successful career as a dealership mechanic.
