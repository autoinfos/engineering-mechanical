How Long Does It Take To Become A Mechanic In Ontario

Written by Petter Jun 06, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you considering a career as an automotive mechanic in Ontario, but not sure where to start? Are you wondering how long it will take to become a mechanic and start working in the field?

Many people who are interested in becoming a mechanic have questions and concerns about the process, such as the time commitment required, the educational requirements, and the job outlook in the field. In this post, we will explore the various aspects of becoming a mechanic in Ontario and answer the question of how long it takes to achieve this goal.

To become a licensed mechanic in Ontario, you must complete a combination of education and work experience. The process involves several steps, including obtaining the necessary education, completing an apprenticeship, and passing a certification exam.

Overall, the process of becoming a mechanic in Ontario can take anywhere from 4-5 years. This includes 2-3 years of education and 2 years of on-the-job training as an apprentice. However, the exact timeline may vary depending on the individual's circumstances and the specific requirements of their training program.

Education Requirements

The first step in becoming a mechanic in Ontario is to obtain the necessary education. This typically involves completing a college-level automotive technology program, which can take 2-3 years to complete. During this time, students will learn the foundational knowledge and skills necessary to work as a mechanic, such as engine repair, electrical systems, and brake systems.

After completing their education, aspiring mechanics must then complete an apprenticeship, which involves working under the supervision of a licensed mechanic for a period of 2 years. During this time, apprentices will gain hands-on experience in the field and develop their practical skills and knowledge.

Work Experience Requirements

After completing their apprenticeship, mechanics must then pass a certification exam in order to become licensed to work in Ontario. This exam tests the individual's knowledge of various automotive systems and their ability to diagnose and repair common problems.

Once licensed, mechanics may choose to specialize in a particular area of the field, such as engine repair, transmission repair, or electrical systems. They may also choose to work in a variety of settings, such as automotive repair shops, dealerships, or as independent contractors.

Job Outlook

The job outlook for mechanics in Ontario is generally positive, with a high demand for skilled workers in the field. According to the Government of Canada Job Bank, employment opportunities for automotive service technicians and mechanics are expected to remain steady over the next several years, with an average salary of $25-$35 per hour.

Training and Certification

To ensure the best job prospects and earning potential, it is important for aspiring mechanics to obtain the necessary training and certification. This involves completing a college-level automotive technology program, completing an apprenticeship, and passing a certification exam.

Personal Experience

As someone who has worked in the automotive industry for several years, I can attest to the importance of obtaining the necessary education and work experience to become a successful mechanic. While the process can be challenging and time-consuming, it is well worth the effort in terms of the job opportunities and earning potential that are available.

FAQs

Q: How long does it take to become a licensed mechanic in Ontario?

A: The process typically takes 4-5 years, including 2-3 years of education and 2 years of on-the-job training as an apprentice.

Q: What are the education requirements for becoming a mechanic in Ontario?

A: Aspiring mechanics must typically complete a college-level automotive technology program, which takes 2-3 years to complete.

Q: What is the job outlook for mechanics in Ontario?

A: The job outlook is generally positive, with a high demand for skilled workers in the field and an average salary of $25-$35 per hour.

Q: What is the certification process for becoming a licensed mechanic in Ontario?

A: After completing their education and apprenticeship, mechanics must pass a certification exam in order to become licensed to work in Ontario.

Conclusion

Becoming a mechanic in Ontario requires a significant amount of time and effort, but the rewards are well worth it in terms of the job opportunities and earning potential that are available. By obtaining the necessary education and work experience, aspiring mechanics can achieve their career goals and enjoy a fulfilling and rewarding career in the automotive industry.
