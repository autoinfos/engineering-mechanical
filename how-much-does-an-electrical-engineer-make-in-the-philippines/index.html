How Much Does An Electrical Engineer Make In The Philippines

Written by Bardi Nov 06, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you wondering how much an electrical engineer makes in the Philippines? If you're considering a career in this field or planning to hire one, understanding their earning potential is crucial. In this blog post, we'll explore the average salary of an electrical engineer in the Philippines and what factors affect their pay.

Electrical engineering is a highly specialized field, and those who pursue this career path have invested a significant amount of time and resources into their education and training. As such, it's only fair that they receive fair compensation for their work. However, the reality is that many electrical engineers in the Philippines face challenges when it comes to their pay.

The average salary of an electrical engineer in the Philippines is PHP 471,000 per year or PHP 39,250 per month. However, this amount can vary depending on several factors such as years of experience, industry, and location. For instance, those with more experience and working in industries such as telecommunications and power generation tend to earn more.

In summary, the average salary of an electrical engineer in the Philippines is PHP 471,000 per year, but this can vary depending on factors such as experience, industry, and location.

Factors That Affect an Electrical Engineer's Salary in the Philippines

Now that we've established the average salary of an electrical engineer in the Philippines let's take a closer look at the factors that affect their pay.

Years of Experience: As with most professions, experience plays a significant role in an electrical engineer's earning potential. Those with more years of experience tend to earn more than those who are just starting. According to PayScale, an electrical engineer with less than one year of experience can expect to earn an average of PHP 217,000 per year, while those with 20 or more years of experience can earn an average of PHP 1.1 million per year.

Industry: The industry that an electrical engineer works in can also affect their salary. For example, electrical engineers working in the telecommunications industry tend to earn more than those in the construction industry. According to PayScale, an electrical engineer working in the telecommunications industry can expect to earn an average of PHP 673,000 per year, while those in the construction industry can expect to earn an average of PHP 357,000 per year.

Personal Experience

As an electrical engineer myself, I can attest to the challenges and rewards of this profession. I've been in the industry for over ten years, and I've worked on various projects, from designing electrical systems for buildings to developing renewable energy solutions for communities. Throughout my career, I've seen firsthand how experience, industry, and location can affect an electrical engineer's salary in the Philippines.

Location

The location that an electrical engineer works in can also affect their salary. Those working in Metro Manila tend to earn more than those in other regions. According to PayScale, an electrical engineer working in Metro Manila can expect to earn an average of PHP 557,000 per year, while those in Cebu can expect to earn an average of PHP 440,000 per year.

Skills

Aside from the factors mentioned earlier, an electrical engineer's skills can also affect their salary. Those with specialized skills such as project management or programming tend to earn more than those who only have basic electrical engineering skills. Additionally, those who hold professional licenses or certifications tend to earn more than those who don't.

Negotiation

Lastly, an electrical engineer's negotiation skills can also affect their salary. Those who are confident in their abilities and can effectively communicate their value to their employers are more likely to negotiate higher salaries.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the starting salary for an electrical engineer in the Philippines?

A: The starting salary for an electrical engineer in the Philippines is around PHP 217,000 per year.

Q: How much can an experienced electrical engineer earn in the Philippines?

A: An experienced electrical engineer with over 20 years of experience can earn an average of PHP 1.1 million per year in the Philippines.

Q: What industry pays the highest salary for electrical engineers in the Philippines?

A: Electrical engineers working in the telecommunications industry tend to earn the highest salary in the Philippines, with an average of PHP 673,000 per year.

Q: How can an electrical engineer negotiate for a higher salary?

A: Electrical engineers can negotiate for a higher salary by highlighting their skills and experience, researching industry standards, and effectively communicating their value to their employers.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the average salary of an electrical engineer in the Philippines is PHP 471,000 per year, but this amount can vary depending on several factors such as experience, industry, and location. Aspiring electrical engineers or those planning to hire one should consider these factors when determining their salary expectations or job offers. Ultimately, fair compensation for an electrical engineer's skills and expertise is crucial for the growth and success of the industry as a whole.
