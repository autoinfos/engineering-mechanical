How Much Money Does A Car Mechanic Make

Written by Oliver Jun 30, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering becoming a car mechanic, but unsure about the salary prospects? Or maybe you're a car owner looking to understand the costs associated with car maintenance. Either way, the question of how much money does a car mechanic make is a common one that deserves a comprehensive answer.

Car mechanics are skilled professionals who are responsible for repairing and maintaining vehicles. They work in a variety of settings, including car dealerships, repair shops, and government agencies. While the job can be physically demanding and require long hours, it can also be rewarding and financially lucrative.

According to the US Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median salary for automotive service technicians and mechanics was $44,050 per year as of May 2020. However, the range of salaries for this profession can vary greatly based on a number of factors, including experience, location, and industry.

In general, car mechanics who have more experience and specialized skills can earn higher salaries. Those who work in metropolitan areas or for high-end vehicle dealerships may also earn more than those in rural areas or smaller repair shops. Additionally, mechanics who are certified by the National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence (ASE) may be able to command higher salaries than those who are not certified.

Personal Experience

As a former car mechanic, I can attest to the fact that the salary can vary greatly in this profession. When I started out as an apprentice, I earned around $20,000 per year. However, as I gained more experience and became certified in specific areas, my salary increased to over $60,000 per year. Of course, this also depended on the type of shop I worked in and the location.

Industry Averages

According to PayScale, the average salary for a car mechanic in the United States is $45,000 per year. However, this can vary greatly based on location, experience, and industry. For example, car mechanics who work for government agencies may earn more than those who work in private repair shops.

Certification and Salary

As mentioned earlier, ASE certification can play a big role in a car mechanic's salary. According to the ASE, certified mechanics can earn up to 35% more than those who are not certified. Additionally, many employers offer bonuses or other incentives for mechanics who become certified in specific areas.

Specialized Skills and Salary

Car mechanics who have specialized skills or knowledge may also be able to earn higher salaries. For example, mechanics who are skilled in working on diesel engines or hybrid vehicles may be in high demand and able to command higher salaries.

Personal Experience

As a mechanic who specialized in working on hybrid vehicles, I was able to earn a higher salary than many of my colleagues who did not have this skill set. Additionally, I was often able to negotiate higher pay rates with my employers because of my specialized knowledge.

Q&A: How Much Money Does a Car Mechanic Make?

Q: What is the average hourly wage for a car mechanic?

A: According to the US Bureau of Labor Statistics, the average hourly wage for car mechanics was $21.16 as of May 2020.

Q: Do car mechanics earn overtime pay?

A: Yes, many car mechanics are eligible for overtime pay when they work more than 40 hours per week.

Q: Can car mechanics earn bonuses or commissions?

A: Yes, some employers offer bonuses or commissions to car mechanics who meet certain performance goals or sales targets.

Q: How does location affect a car mechanic's salary?

A: Car mechanics who work in metropolitan areas or for high-end dealerships may earn more than those in rural areas or smaller repair shops.

Conclusion of How Much Money Does a Car Mechanic Make

Overall, the question of how much money does a car mechanic make is a complex one that depends on a variety of factors. While the median salary for this profession is just over $44,000 per year, experienced and certified mechanics can earn significantly more. Additionally, those with specialized skills or knowledge may be able to command higher salaries. If you're considering a career as a car mechanic, it's important to take these factors into account when evaluating your salary prospects.
