How Much Does It Cost To Clear A Car In Kenya

Written by Oliver Oct 04, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you planning to import a car to Kenya and wondering how much it will cost you to clear it? Well, you are not alone. Many people face this challenge, and it's essential to understand the costs involved before making any purchase decisions.

Importing a car to Kenya can be a daunting task, and the cost of clearing it can add up quickly. The process involves paying various fees, including import duty, excise tax, value-added tax, and other miscellaneous costs. Failure to understand these costs could lead to unexpected expenses, which can be frustrating.

The cost of clearing a car in Kenya depends on various factors such as the car's value, the year of manufacture, the engine capacity, and the mode of transportation. Generally, the cost can range from Ksh. 150,000 to Ksh. 500,000, and it can take up to 21 days to clear the vehicle.

In summary, the cost of clearing a car in Kenya can be significant, but it's crucial to understand the expenses involved to make informed decisions. Factors such as the car's value, engine capacity, and mode of transportation can affect the cost. It's essential to work with a reputable clearing agent to avoid any unexpected costs.

Understanding the Cost of Clearing a Car in Kenya

When I decided to import a car to Kenya, I was excited about the process, but I didn't understand the costs involved. I quickly realized that it wasn't as easy as I thought. The cost of clearing a car in Kenya depends on the car's value, engine capacity, and mode of transportation. For instance, if the car's value is high, the import duty will be high, and if the engine capacity is high, the excise tax will be high. Additionally, if you choose to transport the car via the sea, it will take longer to clear than if you transported it by air.

Import Duty and Excise Tax

Import duty is a tax levied on imported goods, including cars. The tax is calculated based on the car's value, year of manufacture, and engine capacity. Excise tax, on the other hand, is a tax levied on specific goods, including cars. The tax is calculated based on the car's engine capacity and year of manufacture.

Mode of Transportation

The mode of transportation can affect the cost of clearing a car in Kenya. If you choose to transport the car via the sea, it will take longer to clear than if you transported it by air. Additionally, sea transportation is cheaper than air transportation, but it takes longer to clear.
