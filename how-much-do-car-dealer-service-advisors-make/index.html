How Much Do Car Dealer Service Advisors Make

Written by Luffy Oct 15, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you thinking about becoming a car dealer service advisor but unsure about the salary? Or are you currently working in this position and wondering if you are being paid fairly? Either way, understanding how much car dealer service advisors make is important.

Car dealer service advisors are responsible for greeting customers, scheduling appointments, and communicating with mechanics to ensure customers' vehicles are repaired in a timely and efficient manner. However, it can be a high-pressure job dealing with upset customers and trying to upsell services. This can make the compensation package a critical factor in the decision to pursue this career.

According to Glassdoor, the average salary for a car dealer service advisor in the United States is $48,000 per year. However, this can vary based on location, experience, and the dealership's size. For example, service advisors working in larger cities may earn more due to the higher cost of living, while those working in smaller towns may earn less.

In addition to base pay, car dealer service advisors may also receive bonuses or commissions based on their sales performance. This can significantly increase their total compensation. However, it is important to note that these bonuses are not guaranteed and can vary based on the dealership's sales goals and the employee's performance.

Personal Experience

As a former car dealer service advisor, I can attest that the job can be challenging but rewarding. I worked at a medium-sized dealership in a suburban area and started at a base salary of $45,000 per year. However, I was able to earn additional bonuses throughout the year based on my sales performance. In total, I was able to earn around $55,000 per year.

Factors That Affect Salary

As mentioned earlier, location, experience, and dealership size can all affect a car dealer service advisor's salary. However, other factors can also come into play. For example, a service advisor who is bilingual may earn more due to their ability to communicate with a wider range of customers. Additionally, those who have received certification or training in automotive repair may earn a higher salary due to their specialized knowledge.

Certification and Training

While not required, certification and training in automotive repair can make a car dealer service advisor a more attractive candidate to potential employers. This can lead to a higher salary or additional benefits such as paid training or tuition reimbursement for continuing education courses. Some organizations that offer certification for service advisors include the National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence (ASE) and the Automotive Management Institute (AMI).

Negotiating Salary

If you are currently a car dealer service advisor or looking to become one, it is important to remember that you have the ability to negotiate your salary. Research the average salary for your area and experience level, and be prepared to explain why you believe you are worth more. Additionally, consider negotiating other benefits such as additional vacation time or a flexible work schedule.

Conclusion

While the average salary for a car dealer service advisor is $48,000 per year, this can vary based on location, experience, and dealership size. Additionally, bonuses and commissions based on sales performance can significantly increase total compensation. By understanding the factors that affect salary and negotiating with potential employers, car dealer service advisors can ensure they are being fairly compensated for their work.

Question and Answer

Q: Is experience the most important factor in determining a car dealer service advisor's salary?
A: No, while experience is a factor, other factors such as location, dealership size, and specialized knowledge can also affect salary.

Q: Can car dealer service advisors earn bonuses or commissions?
A: Yes, many dealerships offer bonuses or commissions based on sales performance.

Q: Is certification or training required to become a car dealer service advisor?
A: No, certification or training is not required, but it can make a candidate a more attractive hire and lead to a higher salary.

Q: How can car dealer service advisors negotiate their salary?
A: Research the average salary for your area and experience level, and be prepared to explain why you believe you are worth more. Additionally, consider negotiating other benefits such as additional vacation time or a flexible work schedule.
