Are you interested in becoming a certified pharmacy technician at Walgreens and wondering how long it will take? If so, you're in the right place! In this blog post, we will explore the timeline for becoming a certified pharmacy technician at Walgreens.

One of the pain points related to becoming a pharmacy technician is the amount of time it takes to complete the necessary education and training. Many people worry that it will take too long or that they won't be able to balance work and school. However, with the right plan and dedication, becoming a certified pharmacy technician at Walgreens is within reach.

To become a certified pharmacy technician at Walgreens, you will need to complete a training program and pass an exam. The length of the training program will depend on the specific program you choose, but most programs take between 6 months and 2 years to complete. Once you have completed the training program, you will need to pass the Pharmacy Technician Certification Exam (PTCE) to become certified.

In summary, becoming a certified pharmacy technician at Walgreens can take anywhere from 6 months to 2 years, depending on the training program you choose. Once you complete the training program, you will need to pass the PTCE to become certified.

What is the Pharmacy Technician Certification Exam (PTCE)?

The Pharmacy Technician Certification Exam (PTCE) is a national certification exam that tests the knowledge and skills required to work as a pharmacy technician. The exam is administered by the Pharmacy Technician Certification Board (PTCB) and consists of 90 multiple-choice questions.

Personally, I found the PTCE to be challenging, but manageable with the right preparation. I used study guides and practice exams to prepare, and I also took advantage of any training or support offered by my program.

What are the Requirements to Become a Certified Pharmacy Technician at Walgreens?

The requirements to become a certified pharmacy technician at Walgreens include completing a training program and passing the PTCE. Additionally, you must have a high school diploma or equivalent and be at least 18 years old.

To make the process smoother, I recommend researching different training programs and choosing one that fits your schedule and learning style. Some programs may offer online courses or evening classes, making it easier to balance work and school.

What Topics are Covered in a Pharmacy Technician Training Program?

A pharmacy technician training program will cover a variety of topics, including pharmacology, pharmacy law and ethics, medication safety, and dosage calculations. You will also learn how to use pharmacy software and other tools, as well as the basics of customer service and communication.

Personally, I found the program to be comprehensive and well-organized. I appreciated the hands-on experience and the opportunity to learn from experienced pharmacy technicians.

How Much Does It Cost to Become a Certified Pharmacy Technician at Walgreens?

The cost to become a certified pharmacy technician at Walgreens will vary depending on the training program you choose. Some programs may offer financial aid or scholarships, which can help offset the cost. Additionally, Walgreens may offer tuition reimbursement for employees who complete the certification process.

Personally, I found the cost to be reasonable and well worth the investment. The certification has opened up new career opportunities and allowed me to take on more responsibilities at work.

Conclusion

If you're interested in becoming a certified pharmacy technician at Walgreens, the process can take anywhere from 6 months to 2 years, depending on the training program you choose. You will need to complete a training program and pass the Pharmacy Technician Certification Exam (PTCE) to become certified. With dedication and hard work, becoming a certified pharmacy technician at Walgreens is within reach!
