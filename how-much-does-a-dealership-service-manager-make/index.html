How Much Does A Dealership Service Manager Make

Written by Robby Jul 21, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career as a dealership service manager? The automotive industry can be lucrative, but it's important to know what to expect in terms of salary. In this blog post, we'll explore how much a dealership service manager makes and what factors can impact their earnings.

Understanding the Pain Points

It's no secret that the automotive industry can be a challenging and demanding field. As a dealership service manager, you'll be responsible for overseeing the service department, managing staff, and ensuring customer satisfaction. You may also be responsible for maintaining inventory, ordering parts, and managing budgets. All of these responsibilities can be stressful and time-consuming.

Answering the Target

So, how much does a dealership service manager make? According to salary.com, the average salary for a dealership service manager in the United States is $96,995 per year. However, salaries can vary depending on factors such as location, experience, and education. Some service managers may earn over $100,000 a year, while others may earn closer to $70,000.

Summary of Main Points

In summary, a dealership service manager can expect to make an average salary of $96,995 per year, but this can vary depending on location, experience, and education. The role can be demanding and stressful, requiring the manager to oversee the service department and ensure customer satisfaction.

Exploring the Factors

Location can have a significant impact on a dealership service manager's salary. In areas with a higher cost of living, such as New York City or San Francisco, a service manager may earn a higher salary to compensate for the higher expenses. Experience and education can also play a role in salary negotiations. A service manager with years of experience and advanced education may be able to negotiate a higher salary than someone with less experience or education.

Considering the Benefits

While salary is an important factor to consider when pursuing a career as a dealership service manager, it's also important to take into account the benefits that may come with the job. Many dealerships offer health insurance, retirement plans, and paid time off. Some may also offer bonuses or commissions based on the service department's performance.

Negotiating Your Salary

If you're considering a career as a dealership service manager, it's important to be prepared to negotiate your salary. Research the average salary for the position in your area, and come prepared with evidence of your experience and education. Be open to discussing benefits and bonuses, and be willing to negotiate if necessary.

Personal Experience

As a former dealership service manager, I can attest to the demanding nature of the job. However, the salary and benefits were competitive, and I was able to negotiate a higher salary based on my experience and education. The job required long hours and a lot of multitasking, but it was ultimately rewarding to see the service department succeed and satisfy customers.

Question and Answer

Q: Can a dealership service manager earn bonuses or commissions?

A: Yes, many dealerships offer bonuses or commissions based on the service department's performance.

Q: How does location impact a dealership service manager's salary?

A: In areas with a higher cost of living, such as New York City or San Francisco, a service manager may earn a higher salary to compensate for the higher expenses.

Q: How can experience and education impact a dealership service manager's salary negotiations?

A: A service manager with years of experience and advanced education may be able to negotiate a higher salary than someone with less experience or education.

Q: What benefits may come with a career as a dealership service manager?

A: Many dealerships offer health insurance, retirement plans, and paid time off.

Conclusion

Overall, a career as a dealership service manager can be rewarding both financially and personally. It's important to be prepared to negotiate your salary, consider the benefits, and be willing to work hard to ensure the service department's success. With dedication and hard work, a dealership service manager can earn a competitive salary and enjoy a fulfilling career in the automotive industry.
