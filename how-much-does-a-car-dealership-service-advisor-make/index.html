How Much Does A Car Dealership Service Advisor Make

Written by Frank Aug 03, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Have you ever thought about becoming a service advisor at a car dealership? It can be an exciting and rewarding career, but one question that comes to mind is how much does a car dealership service advisor make? In this article, we will explore the salary range for this position, as well as the factors that can affect your earnings.

As with any job, there are both pros and cons to being a service advisor. On the one hand, you get to work with cars all day and help customers solve their problems. On the other hand, the job can be stressful and demanding, with long hours and sometimes difficult customers. One of the biggest pain points for many service advisors is the salary, as it can vary widely depending on factors such as experience, location, and the dealership you work for.

The average salary for a car dealership service advisor in the United States is around $45,000 per year. However, this can range anywhere from $25,000 to $70,000 depending on the factors mentioned above. For example, service advisors in larger cities tend to make more money than those in smaller towns, and those with more experience or certifications can command a higher salary.

In summary, there is no one-size-fits-all answer to the question of how much does a car dealership service advisor make. Your salary will depend on a variety of factors, so it's important to do your research and negotiate your pay accordingly. Keep reading for more in-depth information on this topic.

What is the Average Salary for a Car Dealership Service Advisor?

I have been working as a service advisor at a car dealership for five years now, and I can tell you from personal experience that the salary can vary widely. When I first started out, I was making around $30,000 per year, but as I gained more experience and certifications, I was able to negotiate a higher salary. Now, I make around $55,000 per year, which is on the higher end for my area.

One thing to keep in mind is that the salary for a service advisor is often tied to the dealership's performance. If the dealership is doing well, you may be able to earn more in bonuses or commissions. On the other hand, if the dealership is struggling, your salary may be affected as well.

What Factors Affect a Car Dealership Service Advisor's Salary?

As mentioned earlier, there are several factors that can affect how much a car dealership service advisor makes. These include:

Experience

Generally speaking, the more experience you have as a service advisor, the higher your salary will be. This is because you will have a better understanding of how to help customers and solve problems, which can result in higher customer satisfaction and more repeat business for the dealership.

Location

Where you work can also have a big impact on your salary. Service advisors in larger cities tend to make more money than those in smaller towns, as the cost of living is higher and there is more demand for their services.

Certifications

Finally, having certifications such as ASE (Automotive Service Excellence) can also boost your salary as a service advisor. These certifications demonstrate that you have a high level of knowledge and expertise in your field, which can make you more valuable to potential employers.

How Can You Negotiate a Higher Salary as a Car Dealership Service Advisor?

If you are looking to earn more money as a service advisor, there are several strategies you can use to negotiate a higher salary. These include:

Do Your Research

Before you go into a salary negotiation, it's important to do your research and find out what other service advisors in your area are making. This will give you an idea of what is reasonable to ask for, and can help you make a stronger case for why you deserve a higher salary.

Highlight Your Experience and Certifications

Make sure to emphasize your experience and any certifications you have earned when negotiating your salary. This will demonstrate that you have a high level of expertise and are a valuable asset to the dealership.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the starting salary for a car dealership service advisor?

A: The starting salary for a car dealership service advisor can vary depending on factors such as location and experience, but it is typically around $25,000 to $30,000 per year.

Q: How much can a car dealership service advisor make with experience?

A: The salary for a car dealership service advisor can increase significantly with experience, with some advisors making up to $70,000 per year or more.

Q: Do car dealership service advisors get bonuses?

A: Yes, many car dealership service advisors receive bonuses or commissions based on the dealership's performance or their own performance metrics.

Q: How long does it take to become a car dealership service advisor?

A: The amount of time it takes to become a car dealership service advisor can vary depending on the individual and their level of experience and education. Some advisors may start out as technicians and work their way up, while others may have formal education in automotive repair or customer service.

Conclusion of How Much Does a Car Dealership Service Advisor Make

While the salary for a car dealership service advisor can vary widely depending on a variety of factors, it can be a rewarding career for those who enjoy working with cars and helping customers. By doing your research, highlighting your experience and certifications, and negotiating your salary effectively, you can earn a higher salary and advance your career as a service advisor.
