What Degree Does A Pharmacy Technician Need

Written by Aprilia May 24, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Have you ever considered becoming a pharmacy technician? It's a rewarding career that requires a strong attention to detail and a passion for helping others. However, one question that many people have is: what degree does a pharmacy technician need?

For many, the idea of pursuing a degree can be daunting. It can be time-consuming and expensive, and some people may not have the means to attend a traditional four-year college. However, when it comes to becoming a pharmacy technician, the degree requirements are actually quite manageable.

To become a pharmacy technician, you will need to earn either a certificate or an associate's degree in pharmacy technology. These programs typically take one to two years to complete and can be found at community colleges, vocational schools, and some universities.

During your coursework, you will learn about pharmaceutical calculations, pharmacy laws and regulations, medication safety, and more. You will also gain hands-on experience through internships or externships at local pharmacies.

The Benefits of Earning a Degree in Pharmacy Technology

While it is possible to become a pharmacy technician without a degree, there are several benefits to earning one. For one, having a degree can make you a more competitive job candidate. Additionally, some states require pharmacy technicians to hold a degree in order to become licensed.

Furthermore, earning a degree in pharmacy technology can open up opportunities for career advancement. With a degree, you may be able to become a pharmacy supervisor, a pharmacy manager, or even a pharmacist with additional education and training.

My Experience Earning a Degree in Pharmacy Technology

When I decided to pursue a career as a pharmacy technician, I knew that earning a degree would be the best path for me. I enrolled in a local community college and completed a two-year program in pharmacy technology.

During my coursework, I learned about the intricacies of medications, how to properly handle and store them, and how to communicate effectively with both patients and pharmacists. I also gained valuable hands-on experience through an internship at a local pharmacy, where I was able to put my skills to the test in a real-world setting.

The Importance of Continuing Education

While earning a degree in pharmacy technology is an important first step in your career, it's also important to continue your education and stay up-to-date on the latest industry trends and advancements. This can include attending workshops and conferences, reading industry publications, and pursuing additional certifications.

Common Certifications for Pharmacy Technicians

Some common certifications for pharmacy technicians include:

  • Pharmacy Technician Certification Board (PTCB)
  • ExCPT Exam for the Certification of Pharmacy Technicians (ExCPT)
  • National Pharmacy Technician Association (NPTA)

Tips for Passing the PTCB Exam

If you're planning to pursue certification through the PTCB, there are several tips that can help you pass the exam:

  • Take a PTCB exam prep course
  • Study regularly and consistently
  • Use flashcards to memorize drug names and classifications
  • Take practice exams to identify areas where you need improvement

FAQ: What Degree Does a Pharmacy Technician Need?

Q: Can I become a pharmacy technician without a degree?

A: Yes, it is possible to become a pharmacy technician without a degree. However, many employers prefer to hire candidates who have completed a pharmacy technology program.

Q: How long does it take to earn a degree in pharmacy technology?

A: A degree in pharmacy technology typically takes one to two years to complete.

Q: What courses will I take in a pharmacy technology program?

A: Courses in a pharmacy technology program may include pharmaceutical calculations, pharmacology, pharmacy laws and regulations, medication safety, and more.

Q: Do I need to be licensed to work as a pharmacy technician?

A: Licensing requirements vary by state. Some states require pharmacy technicians to hold a license, while others do not.

Conclusion of What Degree Does a Pharmacy Technician Need

Overall, earning a degree in pharmacy technology can be a valuable investment in your future career. Whether you're just starting out or looking to advance in the field, a degree can help you stand out from the competition and open up new opportunities for growth and advancement.
