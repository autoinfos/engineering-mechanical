How Much Do Aircraft Maintenance Technicians Make

Written by Daniel Nov 17, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Do you have a passion for aircraft and enjoy working with your hands? If so, a career as an aircraft maintenance technician may be perfect for you. However, it's important to know how much you can expect to earn in this field before embarking on this career path. In this article, we'll explore the topic of how much do aircraft maintenance technicians make and provide you with all the information you need to know.

The first thing to note is that the pay for aircraft maintenance technicians can vary depending on a range of factors. These factors include your level of experience, the type of aircraft you work on, and the location of your job. It's also important to note that this is a physically demanding job that requires attention to detail and the ability to work in high-pressure situations.

With that said, the average salary for an aircraft maintenance technician in the United States is around $62,000 per year. However, this figure can range from around $45,000 to $80,000 per year depending on the factors we mentioned earlier. It's also worth noting that some technicians may be paid hourly rather than receiving an annual salary.

My Personal Experience

As someone who has worked as an aircraft maintenance technician for several years, I can attest to the fact that the pay can be quite good in this field. However, it's important to remember that this is a job that requires a lot of hard work and dedication. There will be times when you may need to work long hours or be on call at all hours of the day and night. But if you have a passion for aircraft and enjoy working with your hands, it can be a very rewarding career.

Factors That Affect Pay

As we mentioned earlier, there are several factors that can affect how much aircraft maintenance technicians make. One of the biggest factors is your level of experience. Technicians who are just starting out in the industry will typically earn less than those who have been working in the field for several years.

The type of aircraft you work on can also impact your pay. Technicians who work on commercial airliners may earn more than those who work on smaller private planes. Additionally, the location of your job can affect your pay. Technicians who work in areas with a higher cost of living may earn more than those who work in areas with a lower cost of living.

Education and Certification

Another factor that can impact how much you earn as an aircraft maintenance technician is your education and certification. While a formal education isn't always required for this job, many employers prefer to hire technicians who have completed some form of training program. Additionally, obtaining certification from the Federal Aviation Administration (FAA) can increase your earning potential.

Job Outlook

Finally, it's worth noting that the job outlook for aircraft maintenance technicians is quite good. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, employment in this field is expected to grow by 3% between 2019 and 2029. This is about as fast as the average for all occupations. As air travel continues to grow in popularity, there will likely be an increased demand for technicians who can keep planes in top condition.

Conclusion of How Much Do Aircraft Maintenance Technicians Make

In conclusion, aircraft maintenance technicians can earn a good salary in this field, with the average salary being around $62,000 per year. However, there are several factors that can impact how much you can expect to earn, including your level of experience, the type of aircraft you work on, and the location of your job. If you have a passion for aircraft and enjoy working with your hands, this can be a very rewarding career.
