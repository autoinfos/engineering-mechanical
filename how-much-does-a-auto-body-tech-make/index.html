How Much Does An Auto Body Tech Make

Written by Eveline Aug 22, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career as an auto body tech? If so, you may be wondering how much money you can expect to make in this line of work. The answer is not simple, as there are many factors that can impact your earnings as an auto body tech.

One of the biggest pain points for those considering a career in this field is the fear that they will not be able to support themselves or their families on their earnings. It's true that auto body techs do not typically make as much as doctors or lawyers, but they can still earn a decent living if they have the right skills and work for the right employer.

So, how much can you expect to make as an auto body tech? The answer varies depending on several factors, including your level of experience, the location where you work, and the type of employer you work for.

In general, the average salary for an auto body tech in the United States is around $44,000 per year. However, this number can vary widely depending on the factors mentioned above. For example, auto body techs who work in major metropolitan areas tend to earn more than those who work in rural areas. Similarly, those who work for large dealerships or specialty shops may earn more than those who work for small independent shops.

My Experience with Auto Body Tech Salaries

When I first started working as an auto body tech, I was a bit concerned about my earning potential. However, I quickly learned that my salary would be largely determined by my skill level and the type of employer I worked for. After a few years of working at a small independent shop, I was able to build up my skills and earn a higher salary by moving to a larger dealership. Now, I earn a comfortable living doing work that I love.

Factors That Impact Auto Body Tech Salaries

As mentioned earlier, there are several factors that can impact how much an auto body tech will make. Some of the most important factors include:

Experience

Auto body techs who have more experience in the field tend to earn higher salaries. This is because they have developed a higher level of skill and are able to complete jobs more quickly and efficiently.

Location

Auto body techs who work in major cities or affluent areas tend to earn more than those who work in rural or less populated areas. This is because there is more demand for their services in these areas, and employers are willing to pay more to attract skilled workers.

Conclusion of How Much Does an Auto Body Tech Make

In conclusion, the amount of money you can make as an auto body tech is not set in stone. However, by developing your skills, choosing the right employer, and working in the right location, you can earn a comfortable living doing work that you love. If you are passionate about cars and enjoy working with your hands, this may be the perfect career for you.

Question and Answer

Q: How much does an entry-level auto body tech make?

A: Entry-level auto body techs can expect to earn between $25,000 and $35,000 per year, depending on their location and employer.

Q: Can auto body techs earn more than the average salary?

A: Yes, auto body techs who have a high level of skill and work for the right employer can earn more than the average salary. Some experienced auto body techs earn upwards of $60,000 per year.

Q: Is it possible to make a living as a freelance auto body tech?

A: While it is possible to work as a freelance auto body tech, it can be difficult to earn a steady income this way. Most auto body techs work for employers such as dealerships or specialty shops.

Q: Are there any educational requirements for becoming an auto body tech?

A: While there are no formal education requirements for becoming an auto body tech, most employers prefer to hire candidates who have completed a training program in auto body repair. These programs can be found at vocational schools and community colleges.
