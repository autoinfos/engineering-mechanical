How Much Does A Phlebotomy Technician Make An Hour

Written by Aprilia Oct 17, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you interested in becoming a phlebotomy technician, but wondering how much you can expect to make per hour? It's a common concern for many people who are considering this career path. Let's take a closer look at the expected earnings for phlebotomy technicians.

Phlebotomy technicians are responsible for drawing blood from patients for medical testing, transfusions, donations, or research. The job requires technical skills, attention to detail, and the ability to work with patients who are often anxious or uncomfortable. It's a rewarding profession that can provide a stable income, but it's important to understand the salary expectations.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median hourly wage for phlebotomy technicians as of May 2020 was $17.24. The lowest 10 percent of earners made less than $12.22 per hour, while the highest 10 percent earned more than $25.91 per hour.

In addition to base pay, many phlebotomy technicians may also receive benefits such as health insurance, retirement plans, paid time off, and tuition reimbursement. Some employers may also offer bonuses for performance or certifications, which can increase earnings.

Personal Experience: Earning Potential as a Phlebotomy Technician

As a phlebotomy technician with four years of experience, I currently earn $20.50 per hour at a hospital in a major metropolitan area. I also receive health insurance, retirement benefits, and paid time off. While the salary may not be as high as some other medical professions, I find the work rewarding and the job security reassuring.

Factors that Affect Phlebotomy Technician Salary

Several factors can affect the salary of a phlebotomy technician, including geographic location, level of experience, and type of employer. For example, phlebotomy technicians working in large cities may earn higher salaries than those in rural areas due to the higher cost of living. Similarly, those with several years of experience may be able to negotiate higher salaries than entry-level workers.

The type of employer can also impact earnings. Phlebotomy technicians who work in hospitals or diagnostic laboratories may earn more than those who work in physician offices or blood donation centers. Additionally, those who work full-time may earn more than part-time employees.

Skills and Certifications that Can Increase Phlebotomy Technician Earnings

There are several skills and certifications that can increase a phlebotomy technician's earning potential. For example, phlebotomy technicians who are skilled in venipuncture (the process of puncturing a vein to draw blood) or capillary puncture (the process of collecting blood from a finger or heel prick) may be more in-demand and therefore earn higher salaries.

Certifications such as the Certified Phlebotomy Technician (CPT) credential from the National Healthcareer Association or the Phlebotomy Technician (PBT) certification from the American Society for Clinical Pathology can also increase earnings. These certifications demonstrate a level of competency and professionalism that can appeal to employers and lead to higher salaries.

Tips for Increasing Phlebotomy Technician Salary

If you're a phlebotomy technician looking to increase your salary, there are several steps you can take. First, consider seeking additional certifications or training in related areas such as medical assisting or laboratory science. These skills can make you more versatile and valuable in the job market.

You can also seek out opportunities for advancement within your organization. For example, you may be able to move into a supervisory or management role, which often come with higher salaries. Networking with other professionals in the field can also lead to job opportunities or mentorship that can help you grow your career.

Question and Answer

Q: Is it possible to earn a higher salary as a phlebotomy technician than the median hourly wage?

A: Yes, many phlebotomy technicians are able to negotiate higher salaries based on their level of experience, certifications, and job performance.

Q: Are there any downsides to working as a phlebotomy technician?

A: While the job can be rewarding, it does require working with patients who may be anxious or uncooperative. Additionally, the job can be physically demanding as it requires standing for long periods of time and handling medical equipment.

Q: What is the job outlook for phlebotomy technicians?

A: The job outlook for phlebotomy technicians is positive, with the Bureau of Labor Statistics projecting a 17 percent growth rate from 2019 to 2029. This is much faster than the average growth rate for all occupations.

Q: Are there opportunities for advancement in the field of phlebotomy?

A: Yes, phlebotomy technicians can advance into supervisory or management roles, or pursue additional education and training in related fields such as medical assisting or laboratory science.

Conclusion of How Much Does a Phlebotomy Technician Make an Hour

Overall, phlebotomy is a rewarding career that can provide a stable income and opportunities for growth. While the median hourly wage may not be as high as some other medical professions, there are several factors that can impact earnings, including location, experience, and type of employer. By seeking out additional certifications or training and pursuing opportunities for advancement, phlebotomy technicians can increase their earning potential and build a successful career in healthcare.
