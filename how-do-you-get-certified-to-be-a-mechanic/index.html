How Do You Get Certified To Be A Mechanic

Written by Frank Aug 28, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Do you have a passion for cars and engines? Do you enjoy tinkering with engines and figuring out how things work? If so, becoming a mechanic may be the perfect career path for you. But how do you get certified to be a mechanic? In this blog post, we will explore the steps you need to take to become a certified mechanic.

Many people may have questions and concerns when it comes to obtaining certification to become a mechanic. For starters, how do you begin the process? What qualifications do you need? How much does it cost? How long does it take? These are just some of the questions that individuals may have when they are considering a career in mechanics.

The first step to obtaining certification is to complete a formal education program in automotive technology. Many community colleges and trade schools offer programs in this field. These programs typically take anywhere from six months to two years to complete and prepare students with the necessary skills and knowledge to pass certification exams.

After completing an education program, the next step is to obtain certification from a recognized organization. The National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence (ASE) is one such organization that offers certification exams to mechanics. To become ASE certified, mechanics must pass an exam and have at least two years of relevant work experience.

My Experience with Becoming a Certified Mechanic

When I decided to become a mechanic, I enrolled in a two-year program at my local community college. The program covered everything from basic engine repair to advanced electrical systems. After completing the program, I obtained my ASE certification by passing the exam and working in the field for two years.

The Cost of Certification

The cost of certification varies depending on the organization you choose. For example, the ASE certification exam costs $39 per test, while the Automotive Service Association (ASA) charges $60 per exam. Some employers may cover the cost of certification for their employees.

Continuing Education

Once you've obtained certification, it's important to stay up to date with changes in technology and techniques. Many organizations offer continuing education programs and courses to help mechanics stay current with the latest advancements.

Networking and Career Advancement

Networking with other mechanics and industry professionals can help you advance your career. Attending industry events and conferences can also help you stay current with trends and advancements in the field.

FAQs

What qualifications do I need to become a certified mechanic?

You must complete a formal education program in automotive technology and pass a certification exam offered by a recognized organization.

How much does it cost to become a certified mechanic?

The cost of certification varies depending on the organization you choose. The ASE certification exam costs $39 per test, while the Automotive Service Association (ASA) charges $60 per exam.

How long does it take to become a certified mechanic?

It typically takes anywhere from six months to two years to complete a formal education program in automotive technology. After completing the program, you must pass a certification exam and have at least two years of relevant work experience to obtain certification.

What organizations offer certification exams for mechanics?

The National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence (ASE) and the Automotive Service Association (ASA) are two organizations that offer certification exams for mechanics.

Conclusion of How Do You Get Certified to Be a Mechanic

Becoming a certified mechanic requires dedication, hard work, and a willingness to learn. By completing a formal education program and passing a certification exam, you can take the first steps towards a rewarding career in the automotive industry. Remember, continuing education and networking can help you stay current with advancements in the field and advance your career.
