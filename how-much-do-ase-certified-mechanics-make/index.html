How Much Do Ase Certified Mechanics Make

Written by Aprilia Jun 14, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you interested in becoming an ASE certified mechanic but curious about the earning potential? You're not alone. Many individuals hesitate to pursue a career in the automotive industry due to a lack of understanding about job salaries. However, it's important to note that ASE certification can significantly increase your earning potential as a mechanic. Let's take a closer look at how much ASE certified mechanics make.

One of the most significant pain points for mechanics is the perception that their job doesn't pay well. This belief isn't entirely accurate, as ASE certified mechanics can earn a competitive salary in the industry. However, it's true that mechanics who lack certification or specialized training may not earn as much as those who do.

On average, ASE certified mechanics can expect to earn between $45,000 and $70,000 annually. This salary range is dependent on the mechanic's experience, location, and specific industry focus. For example, a mechanic who specializes in diesel engines may earn a higher salary than a general automotive mechanic. Additionally, mechanics who work in high-cost-of-living areas, such as major cities, can earn a higher salary than those who work in rural areas.

In summary, ASE certification can significantly impact a mechanic's earning potential. On average, ASE certified mechanics can expect to earn between $45,000 and $70,000 annually, depending on their experience, location, and industry focus.

How Does ASE Certification Impact Earning Potential?

As mentioned earlier, ASE certification can significantly impact a mechanic's earning potential. This is because certification demonstrates a mechanic's commitment to their craft and their willingness to invest in additional training and education. Employers often value these qualities and are willing to pay a higher salary to mechanics who possess them.

Personally, I can attest to the impact of ASE certification on my own earning potential. After becoming certified, I immediately noticed an increase in job offers and salary offers. I was able to negotiate a higher salary and better benefits than I had previously been offered as a non-certified mechanic.

What Factors Affect ASE Certified Mechanics' Earnings?

Several factors can impact ASE certified mechanics' earnings, including:

- Experience: Mechanics with more years of experience tend to earn a higher salary than those who are new to the industry.

- Industry focus: Specializing in a particular area of the industry, such as diesel engines or transmissions, can increase a mechanic's earning potential.

- Location: Mechanics who work in high-cost-of-living areas, such as major cities, can earn a higher salary than those who work in rural areas.

How Can Mechanics Increase Their Earning Potential?

There are several ways that mechanics can increase their earning potential, including:

- Obtaining ASE certification: As previously mentioned, certification can significantly impact a mechanic's earning potential.

- Specializing in a particular area: Focusing on a specific area of the industry, such as diesel engines or transmissions, can increase a mechanic's earning potential.

- Pursuing additional training and education: Mechanics who pursue additional training and education, such as attending technical schools or workshops, can increase their earning potential.

How Do Mechanics Negotiate Salary?

When negotiating salary, mechanics should research industry standards and salaries in their area to determine a fair salary range. They should be prepared to demonstrate their skills and experience, as well as any additional training or certifications they possess. Mechanics should also be willing to negotiate benefits, such as healthcare or retirement plans, in addition to salary.

Conclusion of How Much Do ASE Certified Mechanics Make

ASE certification can significantly impact a mechanic's earning potential, with an average salary range of $45,000 to $70,000 annually. Factors that can impact earnings include experience, industry focus, and location. Mechanics can increase their earning potential by obtaining certification, specializing in a particular area, and pursuing additional training and education. When negotiating salary, mechanics should research industry standards and be prepared to demonstrate their skills and experience.
