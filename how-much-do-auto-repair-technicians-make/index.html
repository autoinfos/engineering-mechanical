How Much Do Auto Repair Technicians Make

Written by Daniel Nov 17, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you considering a career as an auto repair technician? If so, you may be wondering how much money you can expect to make in this field. As with any career, it's important to consider the potential earnings before committing to a particular path.

One of the biggest pain points for those considering a career as an auto repair technician is the fear of not making enough money. The automotive industry has a reputation for being low paying, which can be discouraging for those who are passionate about cars and want to make a living doing something they love.

So, how much do auto repair technicians make? According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for automotive service technicians and mechanics was $42,090 as of May 2020. This means that half of all auto repair technicians earn more than this amount, while the other half earns less.

In addition to the median wage, there are a few other factors that can impact how much you can expect to make as an auto repair technician. For example, the type of employer you work for can make a difference – those who work for dealerships tend to earn more than those who work for independent repair shops. Additionally, experience and education can play a role in earning potential.

Personal Experience: Earning Potential as an Auto Repair Technician

As someone who has worked as an auto repair technician for several years, I can say that the earnings can vary depending on a number of factors. In my experience, those who have a lot of experience and specialized certifications tend to earn more than those who are just starting out. Additionally, those who work for dealerships or high-end repair shops may earn more than those who work for budget-friendly repair shops.

Education and Training

While it's possible to become an auto repair technician without any formal education or training, many employers prefer to hire those who have completed a training program or have an associate's degree in automotive technology. These programs can take anywhere from six months to two years to complete and can provide valuable hands-on experience and classroom instruction. Those who have completed a training program may be more attractive to employers and may have a higher earning potential as a result.

Specialized Certifications

One way to increase your earning potential as an auto repair technician is to obtain specialized certifications. These certifications demonstrate your expertise in a particular area, such as engine repair or brake systems. Some examples of certifications you may want to consider include the ASE (Automotive Service Excellence) certification or the manufacturer-specific certification offered by companies like Ford or Toyota.

Tips for Increasing Your Earning Potential

If you're looking to increase your earning potential as an auto repair technician, there are a few things you can do. First, consider obtaining specialized certifications in areas that interest you or that are in high demand. Additionally, look for opportunities to work for high-end repair shops or dealerships, as these tend to pay more than budget-friendly repair shops. Finally, consider continuing your education and training to stay up-to-date on the latest advances in automotive technology.

Personal Experience: Earning Potential with Specialized Certifications

After obtaining my ASE certification in engine repair, I saw a significant increase in my earning potential. Not only was I able to take on more complex repair jobs, but I was also able to command a higher wage as a result of my expertise. Additionally, I found that I was more attractive to potential employers who were looking for technicians with specialized skills.

Question and Answer

Q: Do auto repair technicians make more money than mechanics?
A: The terms "auto repair technician" and "mechanic" are often used interchangeably and refer to the same profession. Therefore, there is no difference in earning potential between the two.

Q: Is the earning potential for auto repair technicians higher in certain states?
A: Yes, the earning potential for auto repair technicians can vary by state due to factors such as cost of living and demand for services. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the states with the highest median annual wages for automotive service technicians and mechanics as of May 2020 were Alaska, District of Columbia, and Washington.

Q: Can auto repair technicians earn bonuses or commission?
A: Yes, some auto repair technicians may be eligible to earn bonuses or commission in addition to their base salary. This is often the case for those who work for dealerships or high-end repair shops.

Q: Are there opportunities for advancement in the auto repair industry?
A: Yes, there are opportunities for advancement in the auto repair industry. For example, some technicians may choose to become service managers or open their own repair shops. Additionally, those who obtain specialized certifications or continue their education and training may be more attractive to employers and may have more opportunities for advancement.

Conclusion of How Much Do Auto Repair Technicians Make

While the earning potential for auto repair technicians is not as high as some other professions, it can still be a lucrative career path for those who are passionate about cars and have a knack for problem-solving. By obtaining specialized certifications, continuing your education and training, and seeking out high-paying opportunities, you can increase your earning potential and advance in this exciting field.
