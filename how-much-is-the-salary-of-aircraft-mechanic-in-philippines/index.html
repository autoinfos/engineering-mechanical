How Much Is The Salary Of Aircraft Mechanic In Philippines

Written by Daniel Oct 01, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you interested in pursuing a career as an aircraft mechanic in the Philippines? If so, you may be wondering how much you can expect to earn in this field. In this blog post, we'll explore the average salary for aircraft mechanics in the Philippines and provide some insights into what factors can impact your earnings.

As with any profession, there are both benefits and challenges to working as an aircraft mechanic in the Philippines. One of the main pain points for many individuals considering this career path is the level of education and training required. Becoming an aircraft mechanic typically requires completing a specialized program of study and obtaining a certification from the Civil Aviation Authority of the Philippines (CAAP). This can be a significant investment of time and money, and may deter some individuals from pursuing this career.

So, how much can you expect to earn as an aircraft mechanic in the Philippines? According to data from Jobstreet, the average salary for aircraft mechanics in the Philippines is around Php 20,000 to Php 30,000 per month. However, this can vary significantly depending on factors such as your level of experience, the type of aircraft you work on, and the company you work for.

In summary, the salary of aircraft mechanic in Philippines is around Php 20,000 to Php 30,000 per month. However, there are many factors that can impact your earnings in this field. Let's explore some of these factors in more detail.

The Impact of Experience on Aircraft Mechanic Salaries

One of the most significant factors that can impact your salary as an aircraft mechanic is your level of experience. Generally speaking, individuals with more experience in the field are likely to earn higher salaries. This is because they have developed a greater level of expertise and are able to work more efficiently and effectively, which is valued by employers.

Personally, I started out as an entry-level aircraft mechanic and earned around Php 20,000 per month. However, as I gained more experience and became more skilled at my job, my salary increased to around Php 35,000 per month.

The Impact of Aircraft Type on Salaries

Another factor that can impact your salary as an aircraft mechanic is the type of aircraft you work on. Generally speaking, individuals who work on larger, more complex aircraft are likely to earn higher salaries than those who work on smaller or simpler aircraft. This is because working on larger aircraft requires a greater level of skill and expertise, which is valued by employers.

Personally, I have worked on both small and large aircraft during my career as an aircraft mechanic. I found that working on larger aircraft was more challenging and required a greater level of skill and expertise. As a result, I was able to earn a higher salary when I worked on these types of aircraft.

The Impact of Company on Salaries

The company you work for can also play a significant role in determining your salary as an aircraft mechanic. Generally speaking, larger and more established companies are able to offer higher salaries than smaller or newer companies. This is because they have more resources and are able to invest more in their employees.

Personally, I have worked for both large and small companies during my career as an aircraft mechanic. I found that the larger companies were able to offer more competitive salaries and benefits, which made them a more attractive option for me.

Tips for Earning a Higher Salary as an Aircraft Mechanic

If you're interested in earning a higher salary as an aircraft mechanic, there are several things you can do to increase your earning potential. These include:

  • Obtaining additional certifications or training
  • Developing specialized skills, such as avionics or engine repair
  • Working for a larger or more established company
  • Gaining experience working on larger or more complex aircraft

Question and Answer

Q: Is it worth pursuing a career as an aircraft mechanic in the Philippines?

A: Yes, if you have a passion for aviation and enjoy working with your hands, a career as an aircraft mechanic can be both rewarding and fulfilling.

Q: What type of education is required to become an aircraft mechanic in the Philippines?

A: Becoming an aircraft mechanic typically requires completing a specialized program of study and obtaining a certification from the Civil Aviation Authority of the Philippines (CAAP).

Q: How much can I expect to earn as an entry-level aircraft mechanic in the Philippines?

A: Entry-level aircraft mechanics in the Philippines typically earn around Php 20,000 per month, although this can vary depending on factors such as experience and the company you work for.

Q: What factors can impact my salary as an aircraft mechanic in the Philippines?

A: Factors that can impact your salary as an aircraft mechanic in the Philippines include your level of experience, the type of aircraft you work on, and the company you work for.

Conclusion of How Much is the Salary of Aircraft Mechanic in Philippines

In conclusion, the salary of aircraft mechanic in Philippines is around Php 20,000 to Php 30,000 per month, although this can vary depending on several factors. By gaining experience, developing specialized skills, and working for a larger or more established company, you can increase your earning potential in this field.
