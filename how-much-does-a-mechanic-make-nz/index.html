How Much Does A Mechanic Make In Nz

Written by Aprilia Jun 19, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering becoming a mechanic in New Zealand but wondering about the earning potential? Look no further! In this article, we will explore the average salary of a mechanic in NZ and what factors can affect their income.

Being a mechanic can be a challenging but rewarding profession. However, one of the biggest concerns for those considering this career path is how much they can expect to earn. It's understandable to want to know this information before investing time and money into training.

The average salary for a mechanic in New Zealand is around $54,000 per year. However, this can vary depending on factors such as experience, location, and the type of employer you work for. Some mechanics can earn up to $80,000 per year with the right qualifications and experience.

In summary, the earning potential for a mechanic in New Zealand is decent, with the average salary being around $54,000 per year. However, factors such as experience and location can affect this number.

How Location Affects a Mechanic's Salary

The location of a mechanic can have a significant impact on their salary. In general, cities tend to pay higher salaries than rural areas. This is because there are more vehicles in cities, which means more opportunities for work. Additionally, larger cities tend to have higher living costs, which means employers need to offer higher salaries to attract and retain employees.

Personally, when I worked as a mechanic in Auckland, I earned around $60,000 per year. However, I have friends who work in smaller towns and earn closer to the average salary of $54,000 per year.

How Experience Affects a Mechanic's Salary

Experience is another factor that can affect a mechanic's salary. Generally, the more experience a mechanic has, the higher their earning potential. This is because experienced mechanics are often more efficient and can complete jobs faster, which means they can work on more vehicles in a day.

When I first started as a mechanic, I earned around $40,000 per year. However, after gaining several years of experience and completing additional training courses, my salary increased to around $60,000 per year.

The Importance of Qualifications

Qualifications are another important factor when it comes to a mechanic's salary. In New Zealand, there are several qualifications that mechanics can obtain, such as the National Certificate in Motor Industry (Automotive Electrical and Mechanical Engineering) Level 2 and 3. These qualifications demonstrate a mechanic's skills and knowledge, which can result in higher salaries.

Personally, after completing my Level 3 qualification, I saw an increase in my salary. Employers were more willing to pay me a higher wage because they knew I had the skills necessary to complete jobs efficiently and accurately.

Negotiating Your Salary

When it comes to negotiating your salary as a mechanic, it's important to do your research. Look at job advertisements for similar positions in your area to get an idea of what the average salary is. Additionally, consider your qualifications and experience and how they may affect your earning potential.

During my last job interview, I researched the average salary for a mechanic in Auckland and negotiated my salary based on that information. As a result, I was able to secure a higher wage than I would have otherwise.

Conclusion of How Much Does a Mechanic Make in NZ

Overall, the earning potential for a mechanic in New Zealand is decent, with the average salary being around $54,000 per year. However, factors such as experience, location, and qualifications can all affect a mechanic's salary. If you're considering a career as a mechanic, make sure to do your research and consider these factors when negotiating your salary.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the lowest salary a mechanic can earn in NZ?

A: The lowest salary a mechanic can earn in NZ is around $35,000 per year.

Q: How much can a mechanic earn with a Level 4 qualification?

A: A mechanic with a Level 4 qualification can earn up to $80,000 per year.

Q: Do mechanics in rural areas earn less than those in cities?

A: Yes, mechanics in rural areas tend to earn less than those in cities due to the lower demand for their services.

Q: Can a mechanic earn more by working for a dealership versus an independent garage?

A: Yes, mechanics who work for dealerships tend to earn more than those who work for independent garages. This is because dealerships often have higher profit margins and can afford to pay their employees more.
