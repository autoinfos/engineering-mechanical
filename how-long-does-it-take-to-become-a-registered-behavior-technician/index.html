How Long Does It Take To Become A Registered Behavior Technician

Written by Daniel Nov 16, 2023 · 5 min read

Do you have a passion for helping people with developmental disabilities, mental health issues or behavioral challenges? If so, you may be interested in becoming a registered behavior technician (RBT). However, the process of becoming an RBT can be confusing and overwhelming, especially when it comes to knowing how long it will take. In this article, we'll explore the timeline for becoming an RBT and provide helpful tips along the way.

One of the biggest pain points for those interested in becoming an RBT is the uncertainty around the timeline. Many people are unsure of how long it takes to complete the required coursework, gain supervised experience and pass the certification exam. Without this information, it can be difficult to plan and prepare for a career as an RBT.

The process of becoming an RBT typically takes between 6 months to 1 year. This timeline includes completing the 40-hour RBT training course, gaining supervised experience (1.5 years of supervised experience by a BCBA is required), and passing the RBT certification exam. However, the timeline may vary depending on a number of factors including the availability of supervision and the individual's ability to complete the coursework in a timely manner.

In summary, becoming an RBT requires completing the 40-hour RBT training course, gaining supervised experience (1.5 years of supervised experience by a BCBA is required), and passing the RBT certification exam. The process typically takes between 6 months to 1 year. However, the timeline may vary depending on a number of factors including the availability of supervision and the individual's ability to complete the coursework in a timely manner.

Understanding the RBT Training Course

When you begin the process of becoming an RBT, you will need to complete the 40-hour RBT training course. This course covers the principles of applied behavior analysis (ABA) and provides an overview of the role of an RBT. The course can be completed online or in-person and typically takes 5-10 days to complete.

Personally, I completed my RBT training course online and it took me about 7 days to complete. The course was self-paced, which allowed me to work at my own speed and fit it into my schedule. I found the course to be informative and engaging, and it provided me with a solid foundation for my career as an RBT.

Gaining Supervised Experience

After completing the RBT training course, you will need to gain supervised experience. This involves working under the supervision of a board-certified behavior analyst (BCBA) who will observe your work and provide feedback. You will need to complete 1.5 years of supervised experience before you can apply for the RBT certification exam.

Personally, I was fortunate to have a BCBA supervisor who provided me with consistent and supportive supervision. However, some individuals may struggle to find a BCBA supervisor, which can delay the timeline for becoming an RBT.

Passing the RBT Certification Exam

Once you have completed the RBT training course and gained supervised experience, you will be eligible to take the RBT certification exam. The exam is administered by the Behavior Analyst Certification Board (BACB) and consists of 75 multiple-choice questions. You will have 90 minutes to complete the exam and must score at least 80% to pass.

Personally, I found the RBT certification exam to be challenging but manageable. I spent several weeks studying for the exam using study materials provided by my training program. I also took several practice exams to prepare myself for the format and content of the exam.

Tips for Becoming an RBT

1. Research RBT Training Programs: There are many RBT training programs available, so it's important to do your research and find one that meets your needs. Look for a program that is accredited by the BACB and provides comprehensive training in ABA principles.

2. Find a BCBA Supervisor: As mentioned earlier, gaining supervised experience is an essential part of becoming an RBT. Make sure to network and reach out to BCBA's in your area to find a supervisor who can provide you with the required supervision.

3. Study for the Certification Exam: The RBT certification exam covers a wide range of topics, so it's important to study thoroughly. Use practice exams and study materials to prepare yourself for the exam.

4. Stay Up-to-Date with the Latest Research: As an RBT, it's important to stay up-to-date with the latest research and best practices in the field of ABA. Attend conferences, read journals and connect with other professionals in the field to stay informed.

Question and Answer

A: The RBT training course typically takes between 5-10 days to complete.

Q: How long does it take to gain supervised experience?

A: You will need to complete 1.5 years of supervised experience before you can apply for the RBT certification exam.

Q: How many questions are on the RBT certification exam?

A: The RBT certification exam consists of 75 multiple-choice questions.

Q: How long do I have to complete the RBT certification exam?

A: You will have 90 minutes to complete the RBT certification exam.

Conclusion of How Long Does it Take to Become a Registered Behavior Technician

Becoming an RBT is a rewarding career path that requires dedication and hard work. By completing the 40-hour RBT training course, gaining supervised experience and passing the RBT certification exam, you can become a qualified and skilled RBT. While the timeline for becoming an RBT may vary, with the right resources and support, you can achieve your goal of becoming an RBT and making a positive impact on the lives of individuals with developmental disabilities, mental health issues, and behavioral challenges.
