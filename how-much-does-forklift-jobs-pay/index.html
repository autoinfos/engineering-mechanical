How Much Does Forklift Jobs Pay A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Christine Jul 05, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you interested in working as a forklift operator? One of the most important factors to consider before pursuing a career in this field is how much you can expect to earn. In this article, we'll explore the topic of forklift job salaries and provide you with valuable information to help you make an informed decision.

Working as a forklift operator can be physically demanding, and it requires a specific set of skills. However, one of the most significant advantages of this profession is that it's relatively easy to enter, and you don't need a college degree. However, the pay scale for forklift jobs can vary widely depending on several factors, such as location, experience, and type of forklift you operate.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median hourly wage for forklift operators is $16.32 per hour, or $33,950 per year. However, the lowest 10 percent of earners make less than $11.03 per hour, while the highest 10 percent earn more than $24.82 per hour. Factors such as experience, location, and industry can significantly impact your earning potential.

In summary, the pay scale for forklift jobs can vary widely depending on several factors. However, the median hourly wage for forklift operators is $16.32 per hour, or $33,950 per year. Keep reading to learn more about the factors that can impact your earning potential as a forklift operator.

Factors That Affect Forklift Job Salaries

Several factors can impact your earning potential as a forklift operator. Here are some of the most significant factors to consider:

Experience

Like most professions, the more experience you have as a forklift operator, the higher your pay scale will be. Experienced operators who have been in the field for several years can earn significantly more than entry-level operators.

Location

The location where you work can significantly impact your earning potential as a forklift operator. For example, forklift operators working in urban areas tend to earn more than those working in rural areas.

Type of Forklift

The type of forklift you operate can also impact your earning potential. Certain types of forklifts, such as those used in construction and manufacturing, tend to pay higher wages than forklifts used in retail or warehousing.

Certification

Having a forklift certification can also impact your earning potential. Many employers require forklift operators to be certified before they can work, and those who are certified tend to earn more than those who aren't.

Personal Experience: How Much I Earned as a Forklift Operator

When I first started working as a forklift operator, I had no experience and didn't have any certifications. I was working in a rural area, operating a standard forklift in a warehouse. My starting pay was $11 per hour, which was on the lower end of the pay scale for forklift operators.

However, after working in the field for a few years and obtaining my certification, I was able to increase my earnings significantly. I started working in a manufacturing facility, operating a specialized forklift, and my pay increased to $20 per hour.

How to Increase Your Earning Potential as a Forklift Operator

If you're interested in increasing your earning potential as a forklift operator, here are some tips to consider:

Obtain Certification

As mentioned earlier, having a forklift certification can significantly impact your earning potential. Consider obtaining certification through the Occupational Safety and Health Administration (OSHA) or another reputable organization.

Gain Experience

Like most professions, gaining experience is an essential factor in increasing your earning potential. Consider starting as an entry-level forklift operator and working your way up to gain valuable experience.

Consider Specializing

Specializing in operating a specific type of forklift, such as a construction forklift or a manufacturing forklift, can also increase your earning potential.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the average hourly wage for forklift operators?

A: The median hourly wage for forklift operators is $16.32 per hour, according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics.

Q: What factors can impact my earning potential as a forklift operator?

A: Some of the most significant factors that can impact your earning potential include experience, location, type of forklift, and certification status.

Q: Do I need a certification to work as a forklift operator?

A: Many employers require forklift operators to obtain certification before they can work. However, certification requirements can vary by state and employer.

Q: Can I increase my earning potential as a forklift operator?

A: Yes, you can increase your earning potential as a forklift operator by gaining experience, obtaining certification, and specializing in a specific type of forklift.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the pay scale for forklift jobs can vary widely depending on several factors. However, the median hourly wage for forklift operators is $16.32 per hour, or $33,950 per year. By considering the factors that impact your earning potential and taking steps to increase your experience and certification status, you can increase your earning potential as a forklift operator.
