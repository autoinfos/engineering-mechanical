How Long Does It Take To Become A Maintenance Technician

Written by Robby Oct 29, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career as a maintenance technician? Perhaps you're wondering how long it will take you to become one. Becoming a maintenance technician can be a rewarding career choice, but it's important to understand the time commitment required to achieve your goals.

One of the biggest pain points when it comes to becoming a maintenance technician is the amount of time it takes to complete the necessary education and training. Many people find themselves wondering if they have the time to commit to this career path.

The amount of time it takes to become a maintenance technician can vary depending on the specific job and employer requirements. However, most maintenance technicians complete some type of formal education, such as an associate degree or vocational training program. These programs can typically be completed in two years or less.

In addition to formal education, many maintenance technicians also complete on-the-job training and apprenticeships. This can take an additional one to three years, depending on the employer and job requirements.

In summary, becoming a maintenance technician typically takes between two and five years of education and training. However, the time commitment can vary depending on the specific job and employer requirements.

Personal Experience with Becoming a Maintenance Technician

When I decided to become a maintenance technician, I knew it would require a significant time commitment. I enrolled in a two-year vocational training program and completed my education in just under two years. After graduation, I was able to secure an entry-level maintenance technician position, which provided on-the-job training and allowed me to continue to develop my skills. After three years of on-the-job training, I was offered a promotion to a higher-level maintenance technician position.

Benefits of Becoming a Maintenance Technician

While the time commitment required to become a maintenance technician can be significant, there are many benefits to this career path. Maintenance technicians are in high demand, which means job security and competitive salaries. Additionally, maintenance technicians often have the opportunity to work in a variety of settings, including manufacturing facilities, hospitals, and schools.

Formal Education Requirements

Most maintenance technicians have a combination of formal education and on-the-job training. Formal education requirements can vary depending on the specific job and employer requirements, but most maintenance technicians have at least an associate degree or vocational training certificate in a related field.

On-The-Job Training and Apprenticeships

In addition to formal education, many maintenance technicians complete on-the-job training and apprenticeships. This type of training is typically provided by the employer and can take one to three years to complete. During this time, maintenance technicians work alongside experienced professionals to learn the skills necessary to excel in their roles.

Challenges of Becoming a Maintenance Technician

While becoming a maintenance technician can be a rewarding career choice, there are some challenges to consider. Maintenance technicians often work in physically demanding environments and may be required to work long or irregular hours. Additionally, the job can be stressful, as maintenance technicians are responsible for ensuring that equipment and facilities are functioning properly.
