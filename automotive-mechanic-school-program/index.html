Question and Answer

Q: How long does an automotive mechanic school program take to complete?
A: The length of an automotive mechanic school program can vary depending on the school and the specific program. Some programs can be completed in as little as six months, while others may take two years or more to complete.

Q: What kind of jobs can I get with an automotive mechanic school program?
A: Graduates of an automotive mechanic school program can work in a variety of different roles in the automotive industry. Some common job titles include mechanic, technician, service writer, and service manager.

Q: Do I need any prior experience to enroll in an automotive mechanic school program?
A: No prior experience is necessary to enroll in an automotive mechanic school program. However, some programs may require students to have a high school diploma or equivalent.

Q: How much can I expect to earn after completing an automotive mechanic school program?
A: Salaries for graduates of an automotive mechanic school program can vary depending on factors like location, experience, and job title. However, according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for automotive service technicians and mechanics was $44,050 as of May 2020.
