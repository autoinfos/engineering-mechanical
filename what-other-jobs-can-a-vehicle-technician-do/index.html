What Other Jobs Can A Vehicle Technician Do

Written by Frank Oct 15, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're a vehicle technician, you're probably aware of the many opportunities for you in the automotive industry. However, in addition to working in dealerships or repair shops, there are other jobs you can also explore. In this blog post, we'll dive into what other jobs can a vehicle technician do and the various career paths you can take.

As a vehicle technician, you may be looking for a change in your career. Perhaps you're tired of working in a repair shop or dealership, or maybe you're looking for more challenging work. Whatever your reasons may be, it's important to explore your options and find a career that is both fulfilling and financially rewarding.

One of the most popular career paths for vehicle technicians is becoming a mobile mechanic. This job allows you to work independently, travel to different locations, and work on various types of vehicles. You can also offer your services to businesses or individuals who need repairs on their fleets or personal vehicles.

Another option is becoming a service advisor or manager. As a service advisor, you'll work with customers to diagnose their vehicle problems and provide solutions. As a service manager, you'll oversee the operations of a repair shop or dealership and ensure everything runs smoothly.

Personal Experience: Becoming a Fleet Maintenance Manager

After years of working in a dealership, I decided to explore other options. I stumbled upon a job opening for a fleet maintenance manager, and it caught my attention. The job required me to oversee the maintenance and repair of a fleet of vehicles for a company.

Although I had no experience in fleet management, I applied for the job and was hired. It was challenging at first, but I quickly learned the ropes and found the job to be rewarding. I was able to work independently and manage my own team, and the pay was excellent.

Becoming a Technical Writer or Instructor

If you enjoy teaching others and have a knack for writing, you may want to consider becoming a technical writer or instructor. As a technical writer, you'll create manuals and other instructional materials for vehicle technicians. As an instructor, you'll teach others how to diagnose and repair vehicles.

Working for a Manufacturer

Another option is working for a manufacturer. You can work in research and development or quality control, testing new vehicles and parts to ensure they meet safety and performance standards. You can also work in customer service, assisting customers with technical problems or questions.

Starting Your Own Business

If you're an entrepreneur at heart, you may want to consider starting your own business. You can start a repair shop or mobile mechanic service, or even a parts or accessories store. With your knowledge and expertise, you can build a successful business that caters to the needs of vehicle owners.

Personal Experience: Starting My Own Mobile Mechanic Service

After years of working in a repair shop, I decided to start my own mobile mechanic service. I invested in a van and tools, and started advertising my services. It was challenging at first, but with hard work and dedication, I was able to build a loyal customer base. Today, my business is thriving, and I'm able to work on a variety of vehicles in different locations.

Question and Answer

Q: Can a vehicle technician work in the aviation industry?
A: Yes, a vehicle technician can work in the aviation industry as an aircraft mechanic. However, additional training and certification may be required.

Q: Can a vehicle technician work in the manufacturing industry?
A: Yes, a vehicle technician can work in the manufacturing industry as a production technician or quality control inspector.

Q: Can a vehicle technician work in the military?
A: Yes, a vehicle technician can work in the military as a mechanic or technician.

Q: Can a vehicle technician work in the racing industry?
A: Yes, a vehicle technician can work in the racing industry as a race mechanic or technician.

Conclusion of What Other Jobs Can a Vehicle Technician Do

As you can see, there are many career paths you can take as a vehicle technician. Whether you want to work independently, teach others, or work for a manufacturer, there are plenty of opportunities available. By exploring your options and finding a career that aligns with your interests and skills, you can build a successful and fulfilling career in the automotive industry.
