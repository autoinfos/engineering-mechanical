How Much Money Does The Average Diesel Mechanic Make An Hour

Written by Eveline Sep 12, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career as a diesel mechanic? If so, one of the most important factors to consider is how much money you can expect to make. In this article, we'll discuss the average hourly wage for diesel mechanics and provide some tips on how to increase your earning potential.

Let's face it: money is important. And when it comes to choosing a career, one of the most important factors to consider is your earning potential. This is especially true for diesel mechanics, who often work long hours in challenging environments.

So, how much money does the average diesel mechanic make per hour? According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median hourly wage for diesel service technicians and mechanics was $23.18 as of May 2020. This means that half of all diesel mechanics earned more than this amount, while half earned less.

In addition to your experience and qualifications, there are several factors that can impact your earning potential as a diesel mechanic. For example, working for a large company or in a high-demand area can often lead to higher wages. Additionally, obtaining certifications and continuing your education can increase your skills and make you a more valuable employee.

My Personal Experience

As a diesel mechanic with over 10 years of experience, I can tell you that there is a wide range of salaries in this field. When I started out, I was making around $18 per hour. However, as I gained more experience and certifications, my hourly wage increased to over $30 per hour.

One thing that I've learned is that it's important to negotiate your salary when starting a new job. Don't be afraid to ask for a higher wage, especially if you have valuable skills or experience. Additionally, always be willing to learn and improve your skills, as this can lead to higher-paying job opportunities down the line.

How to Increase Your Earning Potential

If you want to increase your earning potential as a diesel mechanic, there are several steps you can take. First, consider obtaining certifications such as ASE or EVT. These can demonstrate your knowledge and expertise to employers, making you a more valuable employee.

Additionally, consider specializing in a particular area of diesel mechanics such as heavy equipment or marine engines. This can make you a more sought-after employee and increase your earning potential.

Other Factors that Affect Your Salary

Aside from your experience and qualifications, there are several other factors that can impact your salary as a diesel mechanic. For example, your location can play a big role in how much you can expect to earn. Large cities and high-demand areas often have higher wages for diesel mechanics.

Your employer can also impact your salary. Working for a large company or a government agency can often lead to higher wages and better benefits. Additionally, working in a unionized environment can provide job security and higher pay.

Tips for Negotiating Your Salary

If you're starting a new job as a diesel mechanic, it's important to negotiate your salary. Here are some tips to help you get the best possible wage:

  • Research the average salary for diesel mechanics in your area
  • Highlight your skills and experience during the interview process
  • Be willing to negotiate and don't be afraid to ask for a higher wage
  • Consider other benefits such as health insurance or paid time off

Conclusion

So, how much money does the average diesel mechanic make per hour? While the median hourly wage is around $23 per hour, there are many factors that can impact your earning potential as a diesel mechanic. By obtaining certifications, specializing in a particular area, and negotiating your salary, you can increase your earnings and build a successful career in this field.
