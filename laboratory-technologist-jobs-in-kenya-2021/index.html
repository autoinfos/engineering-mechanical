Laboratory Technologist Jobs In Kenya 2021 An Overview

Written by Aprilia Oct 07, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you thinking about pursuing a career in laboratory technology in Kenya? If so, you're not alone. Laboratory technologist jobs are in high demand and can offer a rewarding career path for those with the right skills and qualifications. But before you jump in, it's important to understand what the job entails, the requirements for getting started, and what you can expect in terms of salary and job prospects.

One of the biggest pain points for those interested in laboratory technologist jobs is the level of education required. Most positions require at least a bachelor's degree in medical laboratory science, and some may require additional certifications or specialized training. Additionally, competition for jobs can be fierce, especially in certain areas of the country or in specific industries.

The target of laboratory technologist jobs in Kenya 2021 is to provide an overview of the field, including the education and training requirements, job outlook, and potential salary range. Whether you're just starting out in your career or are looking to make a change, this guide will help you understand what it takes to succeed as a laboratory technologist in Kenya.

What Do Laboratory Technologists Do?

As a laboratory technologist, you'll be responsible for performing a variety of tests and analyses on biological samples, such as blood, urine, and tissue, to help diagnose and treat diseases. You'll work with sophisticated equipment and technology, and will be expected to maintain accurate records and communicate effectively with other members of the healthcare team.

One of the challenges of working as a laboratory technologist is the need to stay current with advances in technology and medical research. You'll need to be able to adapt to new techniques and procedures as they arise, and to keep up with changes in regulations and best practices.

Key Skills and Qualifications

To become a laboratory technologist in Kenya, you'll typically need to have a bachelor's degree in medical laboratory science from a recognized institution. Some employers may also require additional certifications, such as those offered by the Kenya Medical Laboratory Technicians and Technologists Board (KMLTTB).

In addition to formal education and training, there are several key skills and traits that can help you succeed in this field. These include attention to detail, strong critical thinking and problem-solving skills, good communication and teamwork abilities, and a commitment to ongoing learning and professional development.

Job Outlook and Salary Range

According to recent data from the KMLTTB, there is a high demand for qualified laboratory technologists in Kenya, particularly in rural areas and in specialized areas such as molecular biology and genetics. Salaries can vary widely depending on experience, location, and industry, but entry-level positions typically start at around Kshs 30,000 per month, with more experienced professionals earning upwards of Kshs 70,000 per month.

Personal Experience in the Field

As someone who has worked as a laboratory technologist for over a decade, I can attest to the challenges and rewards of this field. One of the things I love most about my job is the opportunity to work with cutting-edge technology and to be at the forefront of medical research and discovery. However, it's also important to be prepared for the daily demands of the job, which can include long hours, repetitive tasks, and the need to maintain high levels of accuracy and attention to detail.

Training and Professional Development

To succeed as a laboratory technologist in Kenya, it's essential to stay up to date with advances in technology and research. This can involve attending conferences, workshops, and other training opportunities, as well as pursuing additional certifications or specialized training in areas such as genetics or microbiology.

How to Get Started

If you're interested in pursuing a career as a laboratory technologist in Kenya, the first step is to research educational programs and job opportunities in your area. Look for accredited institutions that offer bachelor's degrees in medical laboratory science, and consider reaching out to local employers to learn more about their requirements and job openings.

The Importance of Networking

One of the most important things you can do to advance your career as a laboratory technologist is to build a strong professional network. Attend industry events, join professional organizations such as the Kenya Association of Clinical Laboratory Sciences (KACLS), and connect with other professionals in your field through social media and other online platforms.

Conclusion of Laboratory Technologist Jobs in Kenya 2021

If you're considering a career in laboratory technology, there's never been a better time to get started. With high demand for qualified professionals and a wide range of opportunities available, this field offers a rewarding and challenging career path for those with the right skills and qualifications. By pursuing the right education and training, staying up to date with advances in technology and research, and building a strong professional network, you can position yourself for success in this exciting and dynamic field.
