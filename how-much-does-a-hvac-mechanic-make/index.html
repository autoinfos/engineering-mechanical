So, how much does a HVAC mechanic make? According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for heating, air conditioning, and refrigeration mechanics and installers was $50,590 as of May 2020. This means that half of HVAC mechanics earned more than this amount and half earned less. The lowest 10 percent earned less than $31,910, while the highest 10 percent earned more than $79,820.

In summary, HVAC mechanics can earn a decent salary, with the median annual wage being $50,590. However, there is a wide range of earnings within the field, with some earning less than $31,910 and others earning more than $79,820. Factors that can impact earnings include location, experience, and industry.

What Factors Affect How Much a HVAC Mechanic Makes?

Location, experience, and industry are all factors that can impact how much a HVAC mechanic makes. For example, HVAC mechanics who work in cities or metropolitan areas may earn more than those who work in rural areas. Additionally, those with more experience in the field may earn higher salaries than those who are just starting out.

The industry in which a HVAC mechanic works can also impact their earnings. For example, those who work in commercial HVAC may earn more than those who work in residential HVAC. This is because commercial HVAC systems are often more complex and require more specialized knowledge and skills.
