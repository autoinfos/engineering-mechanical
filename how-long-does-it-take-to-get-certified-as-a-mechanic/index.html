How Long Does It Take To Get Certified As A Mechanic

Written by Frank Jun 13, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering becoming a mechanic and wondering how long it takes to get certified? Well, you've come to the right place. In this blog post, we will answer all your questions about the timeline for becoming a certified mechanic.

Learning a new skill can be exciting, but the timeline to achieve that skill can be daunting. It's normal to feel overwhelmed by the process of becoming a certified mechanic, but with the right information and preparation, you can achieve your goals.

The timeline for getting certified as a mechanic varies depending on your preferred certification program, your educational background, and your work experience. However, on average, it takes about two to five years to become a certified mechanic.

In summary, to become a certified mechanic, you need to complete a formal education program, gain practical experience, and pass an exam. You can earn certification through various organizations, including the National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence (ASE) and the Automotive Service Association (ASA).

What Does ASE Certification Entail?

ASE certification is one of the most recognized certifications in the automotive industry. It requires passing several exams, including a written test and a practical exam. The exams cover various topics, including engine repair, brakes, steering and suspension, and electrical systems.

My personal experience with ASE certification was challenging but rewarding. I completed a two-year automotive technology program at a technical college, gained practical experience through an apprenticeship, and then passed the ASE exams. The process took me four years, but it was worth it because I gained valuable knowledge and skills.

What Is ASA Certification?

ASA certification is another option for becoming a certified mechanic. It requires completing an education program, gaining practical work experience, and passing an exam. ASA certification covers various topics, including collision repair, mechanical repair, and business operations.

My friend became ASA certified after completing a one-year automotive collision repair program at a vocational school. He then worked at a collision repair shop for two years and passed the ASA exams. The entire process took him three years, but he now has a successful career as a collision repair technician.

How Can You Prepare for Certification?

To prepare for certification, you need to complete a formal education program, gain practical experience through an apprenticeship or work experience, and study for the exams. You can also take advantage of online resources, study guides, and practice exams.

It's essential to have a strong foundation in automotive technology and to stay up-to-date with new technologies and advancements. Some certification programs require continuing education to maintain certification, so it's crucial to stay current with industry changes.

How Long Does It Take to Complete an Education Program?

The length of an education program varies depending on the program's level and type. Certificate programs typically take one year to complete, while associate degree programs take two years. Bachelor's degree programs take four years to complete but may offer more career opportunities.

It's essential to research different education programs and choose one that aligns with your career goals and timeline. Some programs may offer accelerated options or online courses, which can help you complete the program faster.

Conclusion of How Long Does It Take to Get Certified as a Mechanic

In conclusion, becoming a certified mechanic takes time and effort, but it's a rewarding career path. The timeline for becoming certified varies depending on your preferred certification program, your educational background, and your work experience. To prepare for certification, you need to complete a formal education program, gain practical experience, and pass an exam. It's essential to research different certification programs and education options to choose one that aligns with your goals.

Question and Answer

Q: How much does it cost to become a certified mechanic?

A: The cost of becoming a certified mechanic depends on the education program and certification program you choose. Expect to pay several thousand dollars for an education program and exam fees.

Q: Can you become a certified mechanic without formal education?

A: Yes, you can become a certified mechanic without formal education, but it may be more challenging. Most certification programs require completing an education program and gaining practical experience.

Q: Is certification necessary to work as a mechanic?

A: Certification is not required to work as a mechanic, but it can improve your job prospects and earning potential. Employers often prefer to hire certified mechanics because they have proven knowledge and skills.

Q: Can you get certified in multiple areas of automotive repair?

A: Yes, you can get certified in multiple areas of automotive repair through different certification programs. For example, you can become ASE certified in engine repair and brakes, or ASA certified in mechanical repair and collision repair.
