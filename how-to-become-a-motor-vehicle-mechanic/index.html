How To Become A Motor Vehicle Mechanic A Beginner s Guide

Written by Aprilia Aug 03, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you fascinated by cars and the mechanics behind them? Do you dream of becoming a motor vehicle mechanic but don't know where to start? If yes, then you have come to the right place. Becoming a motor vehicle mechanic is not only a fulfilling career but also a lucrative one. However, the journey to becoming a mechanic can be challenging, and many people are unsure of where to start. In this article, we will guide you through the steps you need to take to become a motor vehicle mechanic.

Learning how to become a motor vehicle mechanic can be overwhelming, especially if you have no prior experience in the field. Many people are unsure of the qualifications they need or the courses they should take to become a mechanic. Additionally, the automotive industry is vast, and choosing a specialization can be daunting.

The first step to becoming a motor vehicle mechanic is to obtain a high school diploma or GED. From there, you can enroll in a trade school or community college to obtain a certificate or associate's degree in automotive technology. These programs will provide you with the basic knowledge and skills required to work as a mechanic.

Once you have completed your education, you can gain practical experience by working as an apprentice under a licensed mechanic. This will give you the opportunity to learn on the job and understand the complexities of automotive repair. It is also advisable to seek certification from the National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence (ASE) to demonstrate your competence in the field.

Specialization and Career Opportunities

After obtaining your certification, you can choose to specialize in a particular area of automotive repair. Specializing in a particular area will make you more marketable and increase your earning potential. Some of the areas you can specialize in include:

1. Engine Repair: This involves diagnosing and repairing problems with an engine, such as oil leaks, overheating, or engine misfires.

2. Brake Repair: This involves diagnosing and repairing problems with a vehicle's braking system, such as worn brake pads or faulty brake lines.

3. Transmission Repair: This involves diagnosing and repairing problems with a vehicle's transmission, such as slipping gears or faulty clutches.

4. Suspension and Steering Repair: This involves diagnosing and repairing problems with a vehicle's suspension and steering system, such as worn shocks or faulty power steering.

After specializing in a particular area, you can work as a mechanic in various settings, including dealerships, repair shops, or even start your own business.

Personal Experience

My journey to becoming a motor vehicle mechanic began when I was in high school. I took an automotive technology class and fell in love with the mechanics of cars. After graduating, I enrolled in a trade school where I obtained a certificate in automotive technology. I then worked as an apprentice under a licensed mechanic and obtained my ASE certification. I now work as a brake repair specialist at a local repair shop.

Job Outlook and Salary

The job outlook for motor vehicle mechanics is positive, with the Bureau of Labor Statistics projecting a job growth rate of 6% from 2019-2029. The median annual wage for motor vehicle mechanics is around $42,000, with experienced mechanics earning up to $66,000 per year.

Conclusion

Becoming a motor vehicle mechanic requires hard work, dedication, and a passion for the mechanics of cars. By obtaining a high school diploma, enrolling in a trade school or community college, working as an apprentice, and obtaining certification from ASE, you can become a successful motor vehicle mechanic. Specializing in a particular area and obtaining practical experience can also increase your earning potential and open up various career opportunities.

Question and Answer

Q: What qualifications do I need to become a motor vehicle mechanic?

A: You need a high school diploma or GED, a certificate or associate's degree in automotive technology, and certification from the National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence (ASE).

Q: What are some of the areas I can specialize in as a motor vehicle mechanic?

A: You can specialize in engine repair, brake repair, transmission repair, or suspension and steering repair.

Q: What is the job outlook for motor vehicle mechanics?

A: The job outlook is positive, with a projected job growth rate of 6% from 2019-2029.

Q: How much do motor vehicle mechanics earn?

A: The median annual wage for motor vehicle mechanics is around $42,000, with experienced mechanics earning up to $66,000 per year.
