How To Become An X Ray Technician In California A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Jun 21, 2023 · 3 min read

Are you interested in a career as an X-Ray Technician in California? Do you want to know what it takes to become one? In this article, we’ll cover everything you need to know about how to become an X-Ray Technician in California.

X-Ray Technicians, also known as Radiologic Technologists, are healthcare professionals who specialize in performing diagnostic imaging examinations. They work closely with physicians and other healthcare professionals to provide accurate and timely images for patient diagnosis and treatment.

The first step to becoming an X-Ray Technician in California is to obtain a high school diploma or equivalent. You will then need to complete an accredited Radiologic Technology program. These programs can be found at community colleges, vocational schools, and universities.

After completing your education, you will need to become certified by the American Registry of Radiologic Technologists (ARRT) or the California Department of Public Health (CDPH). Certification requirements vary by state, so it’s important to check with your state’s licensing board to ensure you meet all the necessary requirements.

In summary, to become an X-Ray Technician in California, you will need to:

Complete High School or Equivalent

Before you can enroll in a Radiologic Technology program, you will need to have a high school diploma or equivalent. If you don’t have one, you can obtain a GED or High School Equivalency Certificate.

Complete an Accredited Radiologic Technology Program

Once you have your high school diploma or equivalent, you can enroll in an accredited Radiologic Technology program. These programs typically take 1-2 years to complete and include both classroom and clinical instruction.

During your program, you will learn how to use X-Ray equipment, position patients for imaging exams, and protect patients and yourself from unnecessary radiation exposure. You will also learn how to interact with patients and healthcare professionals in a clinical setting.

Become Certified

After completing your Radiologic Technology program, you will need to become certified by the ARRT or CDPH. Certification requirements vary by state, but typically include passing an exam and meeting continuing education requirements. Certification is required to practice as an X-Ray Technician in California.

Continuing Education Requirements

Once you become certified, you will need to complete continuing education courses to maintain your certification. These courses can be taken online or in-person and cover a range of topics related to Radiologic Technology.

Professional Organizations

Joining a professional organization such as the California Society of Radiologic Technologists (CSRT) can help you stay up-to-date on the latest developments in the field and connect with other professionals in your area.

Job Outlook and Salary

The job outlook for X-Ray Technicians in California is strong, with an expected growth rate of 9% between 2018-2028. The average salary for X-Ray Technicians in California is $72,000 per year.

Conclusion

Becoming an X-Ray Technician in California requires completing a Radiologic Technology program and becoming certified by the ARRT or CDPH. Continuing education is also required to maintain certification. Joining a professional organization can help you stay up-to-date on the latest developments in the field and connect with other professionals.

Question and Answer

Q: How long does it take to become an X-Ray Technician in California?

A: It typically takes 1-2 years to complete an accredited Radiologic Technology program and become certified.

Q: What are the certification requirements for X-Ray Technicians in California?

A: X-Ray Technicians in California must be certified by the ARRT or CDPH. Certification requirements vary by state, but typically include passing an exam and meeting continuing education requirements.

Q: What is the job outlook for X-Ray Technicians in California?

A: The job outlook for X-Ray Technicians in California is strong, with an expected growth rate of 9% between 2018-2028.

Q: What is the average salary for X-Ray Technicians in California?

A: The average salary for X-Ray Technicians in California is $72,000 per year.
