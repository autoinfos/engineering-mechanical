What Jobs Can You Get With A Automotive Degree

Written by Daniel Aug 13, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering pursuing a degree in automotive technology but unsure of what job opportunities await you after graduation? Look no further as we explore the various career paths available with a degree in automotive technology.

As a prospective student, it can be daunting to invest time and money into a degree without a clear understanding of the job prospects available in the field. However, the automotive industry is vast, and there are numerous job opportunities available for graduates with a degree in automotive technology.

With a degree in automotive technology, you can pursue careers in various industries, including manufacturing, engineering, sales, and marketing. Depending on your interests and skill set, you can specialize in a particular area of the automotive industry.

In summary, graduates with a degree in automotive technology can explore a wide range of job opportunities, including positions in manufacturing, engineering, sales, and marketing.

What Jobs Can You Get with a Automotive Degree - Career Options

After completing your degree in automotive technology, you can explore various career paths. Here are some of the most popular options:

Automotive Engineer

As an automotive engineer, you will be responsible for designing and developing new cars, trucks, and other vehicles. You will work with a team of designers and engineers to ensure that the vehicle design meets safety standards and is efficient and cost-effective.

Automotive Technician

As an automotive technician, you will be responsible for diagnosing and repairing vehicles. You will work with a team of mechanics to inspect vehicles and repair any issues that may arise. You will also be responsible for maintaining and servicing vehicles to ensure that they are safe and efficient on the road.

Automotive Sales Representative

As an automotive sales representative, you will be responsible for selling vehicles to customers. You will work with a team of other sales representatives to promote and market vehicles to potential buyers. You will also be responsible for maintaining relationships with customers and ensuring that they are satisfied with their purchase.

Personal Experience

As a recent graduate with a degree in automotive technology, I decided to pursue a career as an automotive technician. I enjoy working with my hands and diagnosing and repairing vehicles. I work with a team of mechanics to inspect vehicles and ensure that they are safe and efficient on the road.

However, I am also considering exploring other career paths within the automotive industry, such as automotive engineering or sales. With my degree in automotive technology, I have many options available to me, and I am excited to see where my career takes me.

Job Outlook

The job outlook for graduates with a degree in automotive technology is positive. The Bureau of Labor Statistics predicts that employment in the automotive industry will grow at a rate of six percent from 2016 to 2026, which is about as fast as the average for all occupations.

Conclusion

Graduates with a degree in automotive technology can explore a wide range of job opportunities in various industries, including manufacturing, engineering, sales, and marketing. Whether you choose to become an automotive engineer, technician, or sales representative, there are plenty of career paths available to you in the automotive industry.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the average salary of an automotive engineer?

A: According to Glassdoor, the average salary for an automotive engineer in the United States is $83,000 per year.

Q: What skills are necessary for a career in automotive sales?

A: Successful automotive sales representatives must have excellent communication and interpersonal skills. They should be knowledgeable about the vehicles they are selling and be able to explain their features and benefits to potential buyers.

Q: Is it necessary to have a degree to become an automotive technician?

A: While a degree in automotive technology is not required to become an automotive technician, it can be beneficial. Employers may prefer to hire technicians with a degree as it demonstrates a level of expertise and knowledge in the field.

Q: What is the job growth rate for automotive sales representatives?

A: The Bureau of Labor Statistics predicts that employment in sales and related occupations, including automotive sales representatives, will grow at a rate of five percent from 2016 to 2026.
