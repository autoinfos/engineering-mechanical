Truck Mechanic Salary Canada How Much Can You Expect To Earn

Written by Bardi Jun 30, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you considering a career as a truck mechanic in Canada? If so, one of the top questions on your mind is likely how much you can expect to earn. After all, you want to make sure that you’re being compensated fairly for your hard work and expertise. In this blog post, we’ll explore the ins and outs of truck mechanic salary in Canada, so you can make an informed decision about your future career.

Let’s face it, money is an important factor in any career decision. As a truck mechanic, you’ll likely be working long hours in a physically demanding environment. You may also be required to work weekends and holidays, so it’s important to know that your efforts are being rewarded.

The average truck mechanic salary in Canada is around $60,000 per year, but this can vary depending on a number of factors. For example, your level of experience, location, and the type of company you work for can all impact your salary.

In general, the more experience you have, the more you can expect to earn. According to the Canadian Trucking Alliance, truck mechanics with five years of experience or more can earn up to $80,000 per year.

My Personal Experience with Truck Mechanic Salary in Canada

As a truck mechanic with over a decade of experience, I can attest to the fact that salary can vary greatly depending on where you work. When I first started out, I was earning around $40,000 per year. However, as I gained more experience and moved to larger companies, my salary increased significantly.

One thing to keep in mind is that location can play a big role in your salary. For example, truck mechanics in larger cities like Toronto or Vancouver may earn more than those in smaller towns.

Factors That Can Impact Your Truck Mechanic Salary in Canada

As mentioned earlier, there are several factors that can impact your salary as a truck mechanic in Canada. Here are a few to keep in mind:

Experience

The more experience you have, the more you can expect to earn. This is because experienced truck mechanics are in higher demand and can command higher salaries.

Location

As mentioned earlier, location can play a big role in your salary. Truck mechanics in larger cities may earn more than those in smaller towns.

How to Increase Your Truck Mechanic Salary in Canada

If you’re looking to increase your truck mechanic salary in Canada, there are a few things you can do:

Specialize

Specializing in a certain area, such as diesel engines or electrical systems, can make you more valuable to employers and increase your earning potential.

Continue Your Education

Continuing your education through courses or certifications can also make you more valuable to employers and increase your earning potential.

Question and Answer

Q: How much does a truck mechanic apprentice make in Canada?

A: According to the Government of Canada, truck mechanic apprentices make an average of $18 per hour.

Q: What is the highest-paying province for truck mechanics in Canada?

A: According to Indeed, Alberta is the highest-paying province for truck mechanics in Canada.

Q: Do truck mechanics in Canada receive benefits?

A: Many truck mechanics in Canada receive benefits such as health insurance, vacation time, and retirement plans.

Q: Is there room for advancement in a truck mechanic career?

A: Yes, there is definitely room for advancement in a truck mechanic career. With experience and additional certifications, you can become a senior mechanic, a shop manager, or even start your own business.

Conclusion of Truck Mechanic Salary Canada

As you can see, there are many factors that can impact your truck mechanic salary in Canada. While the average salary is around $60,000 per year, your experience, location, and employer can all impact your earnings. By specializing, continuing your education, and gaining experience, you can increase your earning potential and build a successful career in the trucking industry.
