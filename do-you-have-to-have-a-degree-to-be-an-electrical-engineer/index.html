Do You Have To Have A Degree To Be An Electrical Engineer

Written by Robby Jul 13, 2023 · 4 min read

Have you ever wondered if you need a degree to be an electrical engineer? Maybe you're considering a career change, or perhaps you're just curious about the field. Whatever your reason for asking, the answer might surprise you.

Many people assume that you need a degree to become an electrical engineer. After all, it's a highly technical field that involves complex systems and cutting-edge technology. However, the truth is that you don't necessarily need a degree to work in this field.

That being said, a degree can certainly give you an advantage when it comes to finding a job and advancing in your career. In this article, we'll explore the pros and cons of getting an electrical engineering degree, as well as some alternative paths you can take if you don't have a degree.

Pros and Cons of Getting an Electrical Engineering Degree

As with any degree program, there are both advantages and disadvantages to pursuing an electrical engineering degree.

One of the biggest advantages is that a degree can give you a strong foundation in the principles of electrical engineering. You'll learn about circuits, systems, and other fundamental concepts that will be invaluable throughout your career. Additionally, a degree can help you stand out in a competitive job market, and it can open up opportunities for advancement and higher salaries.

On the other hand, there are also some downsides to getting a degree. For one thing, it can be expensive and time-consuming. You'll also need to be prepared to commit several years of your life to studying and completing coursework. Additionally, there's no guarantee that having a degree will automatically lead to a job or a successful career.

Alternative Paths to Becoming an Electrical Engineer

If you decide that getting a degree isn't the right choice for you, there are still plenty of ways to break into the field of electrical engineering.

One option is to pursue an apprenticeship or on-the-job training. Many companies offer training programs for aspiring electricians or electrical engineers. These programs can provide you with hands-on experience and give you the chance to learn from experienced professionals in the field. Additionally, you may be able to earn certifications or other credentials that can help you advance in your career.

Another option is to start out in a related field and work your way up. For example, you could start out as a technician or an electrician and then gradually transition into an engineering role. This can be a great way to gain valuable experience and build your skills while working your way towards your ultimate goal.

Answering Your Questions About Becoming an Electrical Engineer

1. Can you become an electrical engineer without a degree?

Yes, it is possible to become an electrical engineer without a degree. However, having a degree can give you an advantage when it comes to finding a job and advancing in your career.

2. What qualifications do you need to be an electrical engineer?

In addition to a degree, many electrical engineering jobs require certifications or other credentials. These can include things like the Fundamentals of Engineering exam, which is often a prerequisite for becoming a licensed engineer.

3. How long does it take to become an electrical engineer?

The amount of time it takes to become an electrical engineer can vary depending on your educational path and your career goals. Generally, you can expect to spend at least four years earning a bachelor's degree in electrical engineering, and you may need to complete additional coursework or earn certifications in order to advance in your career.

4. What skills do you need to be an electrical engineer?

Some key skills for electrical engineers include problem-solving, critical thinking, and strong communication abilities. Additionally, electrical engineers should have a solid understanding of mathematics, physics, and computer systems.

Conclusion

So, do you have to have a degree to be an electrical engineer? The answer is no, but having a degree can certainly give you an advantage in this competitive field. Whether you choose to pursue a degree or take an alternative path, there are plenty of opportunities available for those who are passionate about electrical engineering.
