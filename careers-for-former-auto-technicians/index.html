Careers For Former Auto Technicians Exploring Your Options

Written by Daniel Jun 14, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you a former auto technician looking for a new career path? Maybe you've been in the industry for years, but are ready for a change. Whatever your reason, the good news is that there are plenty of career options out there for someone with your skills and experience. In this post, we'll explore some of the best careers for former auto technicians.

As a former auto technician, you may have experienced some of the pain points that come with the job. These can include physical strain from lifting heavy parts, exposure to hazardous chemicals, and the need to work in uncomfortable positions for extended periods of time. Additionally, the pay for auto technicians can sometimes be lower than what they deserve for their hard work and expertise.

If you're ready to move on from the auto industry, there are plenty of options available to you. The skills you've gained as an auto technician, such as problem-solving, attention to detail, and mechanical aptitude, are highly transferable and can be applied to a variety of careers.

So, what are some of the best careers for former auto technicians? Let's take a closer look.

Career Option 1: Manufacturing

One option for former auto technicians is to move into the manufacturing industry. Many manufacturing jobs require mechanical skills, and your experience working with cars could make you a valuable asset to a company that produces anything from electronics to appliances to machinery. In this role, you might work on assembly lines or help to troubleshoot and repair equipment.

Personally, I know of a former auto technician who now works for a company that makes industrial equipment. He's responsible for installing and maintaining the machines, and his experience working with cars has been a huge asset in this role.

Career Option 2: Sales

If you're looking for a change of pace, a career in sales might be a good fit. Your experience working with cars could make you a natural fit for a sales job at a dealership or auto parts store. Additionally, your knowledge of cars could be a valuable asset when it comes to helping customers make informed decisions about their purchases.

One former auto technician I know of now works as a sales associate at a dealership. He loves being able to use his knowledge of cars to help customers find the perfect vehicle for their needs.

Career Option 3: Fleet Management

If you're interested in a career that involves managing vehicles, fleet management could be a good fit for you. In this role, you would be responsible for overseeing a company's fleet of vehicles, making sure they're properly maintained and running smoothly. Your experience as an auto technician would be a huge asset in this role, as you would be able to identify potential issues before they become major problems.

One former auto technician I know of now works as a fleet manager for a delivery company. He's responsible for making sure all of the trucks are in good working order, and his experience working with cars has been invaluable in this role.

Career Option 4: Education

If you're passionate about cars and want to share your knowledge with others, a career in education could be a good fit. You could work as a teacher at a vocational school or community college, helping to train the next generation of auto technicians. Alternatively, you could work as a trainer for a dealership or auto parts store, helping to educate employees and customers alike.

I know of a former auto technician who now works as a teacher at a vocational school. He loves being able to share his knowledge and experience with his students, and he finds it incredibly rewarding to see them succeed in the auto industry.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I use my experience as an auto technician in other industries?

A: Absolutely! The skills you've gained as an auto technician are highly transferable and can be applied to a variety of industries, including manufacturing, sales, and fleet management.

Q: Will I need additional training or education to switch to a new career?

A: It depends on the career you choose, but in many cases, you'll need some additional training or education. For example, if you want to work in manufacturing, you may need to learn new skills related to electronics or machinery. However, your experience as an auto technician will still be a valuable asset in any new career you pursue.

Q: How do I find job opportunities in a new industry?

A: There are a variety of job search websites that can help you find job openings in new industries. Additionally, you may want to consider networking with people in your desired industry to learn about job opportunities and gain insight into the field.

Q: Will I be able to make a similar salary in a new career?

A: It depends on the career you choose, but in many cases, you can make a similar or even higher salary in a new career. For example, a fleet manager or sales associate at a dealership may make more than they did as an auto technician.

Conclusion of Careers for Former Auto Technicians

If you're a former auto technician looking for a new career path, there are plenty of options available to you. Your skills and experience are highly transferable and can be applied to a variety of industries. By exploring these options and finding a career that's a good fit for you, you can enjoy a fulfilling and rewarding career outside of the auto industry.
