What Are Radiology Tech Schools in Georgia?

Radiology tech schools in Georgia are educational institutions that offer programs in radiologic technology. These programs are designed to provide students with the knowledge and skills needed to perform diagnostic imaging procedures, such as X-rays, CT scans, and MRI scans. They typically include classroom instruction, hands-on training, and clinical experience.

Students who complete a radiology tech program in Georgia are eligible to take the American Registry of Radiologic Technologists (ARRT) certification exam, which is required for licensure in Georgia.

FAQ About Radiology Tech Schools in Georgia

1. What Are the Requirements for Admission to a Radiology Tech School in Georgia?

Requirements for admission to a radiology tech school in Georgia vary by program. However, most programs require a high school diploma or equivalent, as well as completion of prerequisite courses in biology, anatomy, and math.

2. How Long Does It Take to Complete a Radiology Tech Program in Georgia?

The length of a radiology tech program in Georgia varies by program type. Certificate programs typically take 12-18 months to complete, while associate's and bachelor's degree programs take 2-4 years.

3. What Is the Job Outlook for Radiologic Technologists in Georgia?

The job outlook for radiologic technologists in Georgia is strong. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, employment of radiologic technologists in Georgia is projected to grow 16 percent from 2018 to 2028.
