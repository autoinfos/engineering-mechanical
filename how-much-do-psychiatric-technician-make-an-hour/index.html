How Much Do Psychiatric Technicians Make An Hour

Written by Frank Jul 01, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you interested in pursuing a career as a psychiatric technician but are unsure about the salary prospects? If so, you're not alone. Many people are curious about how much psychiatric technicians earn hourly, and for good reason. Salary is an important factor when deciding on a career path, and knowing the earning potential can help you make an informed decision. In this post, we'll explore the average hourly wage for psychiatric technicians and provide some insights into the factors that can affect their earnings.

Working as a psychiatric technician can be both rewarding and challenging. These professionals play a crucial role in the mental health care system by assisting in the treatment of patients with mental illnesses. However, the job can also come with its share of stress and emotional demands. Despite the challenges, many people find the work fulfilling and satisfying. But one of the most common questions people have when considering this career is, "How much do psychiatric technicians make an hour?"

The hourly wage for psychiatric technicians can vary depending on a variety of factors. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median hourly wage for psychiatric technicians in the United States is $16.99. The lowest 10 percent of earners make less than $11.59 per hour, while the highest 10 percent earn more than $26.05 per hour.

In general, factors such as education, experience, location, and employer can all affect how much psychiatric technicians make an hour. For example, those with more advanced degrees or certifications may be able to command higher salaries. Similarly, those with more experience in the field may be able to negotiate higher wages. Location can also be a significant factor, as salaries can vary widely between different states and cities. And, of course, working for a larger, more established employer may offer better compensation packages than working for a smaller organization.

My Personal Experience with Psychiatric Technician Salary

When I first started working as a psychiatric technician, I was earning around $14 per hour. However, as I gained more experience and expertise in the field, my hourly wage increased to almost $20 per hour. I found that my employer was willing to negotiate my salary based on my performance and the value I brought to the organization. Additionally, I found that working in a larger city with a higher cost of living meant that the salaries were generally higher than in smaller towns or rural areas.

The Importance of Education and Certification in Psychiatric Technician Salary

If you're interested in maximizing your earning potential as a psychiatric technician, it's important to consider furthering your education and obtaining relevant certifications. While not required in all states, certifications such as the Certified Psychiatric Technician (CPT) credential can demonstrate your expertise and commitment to the field, making you a more attractive candidate to potential employers. Additionally, pursuing higher education such as an associate's or bachelor's degree in a related field can also open up opportunities for higher-paying positions.

How to Negotiate Your Salary as a Psychiatric Technician

If you're already working as a psychiatric technician and feel that you're not being adequately compensated for your work, there are steps you can take to negotiate a higher salary. First, do your research to determine what the average salary is for someone in your position with your level of experience and education. Use this information to make a case for why you deserve a higher wage. Next, schedule a meeting with your supervisor or HR representative to discuss your concerns and present your case. Be prepared to provide specific examples of your contributions to the organization and how you've added value. Finally, be open to compromise and negotiation, as your employer may not be able to meet all of your demands but may be willing to offer other benefits or perks.

The Future Outlook for Psychiatric Technician Wages

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, employment of psychiatric technicians is projected to grow 7 percent from 2019 to 2029, faster than the average for all occupations. This growth is expected to be driven by an increasing demand for mental health services and the need for more workers to provide these services. As the demand for psychiatric technicians increases, it's possible that wages may also increase due to market forces. However, it's important to keep in mind that many factors can influence wage growth, and it's impossible to predict the future with certainty.

Conclusion of How Much Do Psychiatric Technicians Make an Hour

Overall, the hourly wage for psychiatric technicians can vary widely depending on factors such as education, experience, location, and employer. However, the median hourly wage in the United States is $16.99. Pursuing further education, obtaining relevant certifications, and negotiating with your employer can all be effective strategies for increasing your earning potential. With the demand for mental health services expected to increase in the coming years, it's possible that psychiatric technician wages may also rise. If you're considering a career as a psychiatric technician, it's important to weigh the salary prospects alongside the other factors that are important to you.
