What to Look for in an X Ray Tech School in Utah

When looking for an X Ray Tech School in Utah, there are several factors to consider. First and foremost, you want to make sure that the school is accredited by a reputable accrediting agency such as the Joint Review Committee on Education in Radiologic Technology (JRCERT). Accreditation

Accreditation is crucial when it comes to choosing an X Ray Tech School in Utah. Cost

FAQs About X Ray Tech Schools in Utah

Q: What is the average salary of an X Ray Technician in Utah?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the average salary for an X Ray Technician in Utah is $62,420 per year.

Q: How long does it take to become an X Ray Technician in Utah?

A: Typically, it takes about two years to complete an X Ray Tech program in Utah. However, some programs may take longer or shorter to complete depending on the school and the student's schedule.

Q: What are the job prospects for X Ray Technicians in Utah?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, job prospects for X Ray Technicians in Utah are excellent. The field is expected to grow by 9% between 2018 and 2028, which is faster than the average for all occupations.

Q: What kind of certification or licensure is required to become an X Ray Technician in Utah?

A: In Utah, X Ray Technicians are required to be certified by the Utah Department of Health's Bureau of Radiation Control before they can practice in the field. Additionally, they may need to pass a national certification exam offered by the American Registry of Radiologic Technologists (ARRT).
