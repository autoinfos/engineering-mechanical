How Much Do Pharmacy Tech Make In Florida

Written by Frank Oct 14, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career as a pharmacy technician in Florida but wondering how much you can expect to earn? Perhaps you're already working as a pharmacy tech but want to know if you're being paid fairly. Whatever your situation, understanding the average salary for pharmacy techs in Florida can help you make informed decisions about your career.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the average hourly wage for pharmacy technicians in Florida is $16.33, with an annual salary of $33,960. However, it's important to note that salaries can vary depending on factors such as location, experience, and type of employer.

In addition to the hourly wage, pharmacy technicians may also receive benefits such as health insurance, retirement plans, and paid time off. Some employers may also offer bonuses or incentives for performance.

In summary, pharmacy technicians in Florida can expect to earn an average of $16.33 per hour or $33,960 per year. However, actual salaries may vary based on individual circumstances and employer.

What Affects Pharmacy Tech Salaries in Florida?

Experience is one of the main factors that can affect pharmacy technician salaries in Florida. Those with more experience may be able to negotiate higher wages or qualify for higher-paying positions. Location can also play a role, as pharmacy techs working in cities or areas with a higher cost of living may earn more.

The type of employer is another factor that can impact pharmacy tech salaries. Pharmacy techs working in hospitals or other healthcare facilities may earn more than those working in retail pharmacies. Additionally, those working for larger employers may receive better pay and benefits than those working for smaller employers.

Education and Certification

While education and certification are not necessarily required to work as a pharmacy technician in Florida, they can help increase earning potential. Employers may be more likely to hire pharmacy techs with a formal education or certification, and these credentials may also lead to higher salaries.

Pharmacy techs can earn certification through organizations such as the Pharmacy Technician Certification Board or the National Healthcareer Association. These certifications typically require passing an exam and completing continuing education requirements.

Advancement Opportunities

Finally, pharmacy technicians in Florida may be able to increase their salaries by pursuing advancement opportunities. This may include taking on additional responsibilities or pursuing further education or certification. Some pharmacy techs may also be able to transition into management or administrative roles, which typically come with higher salaries.

Personal Experience

As a pharmacy technician in Florida, I can attest to the variability in pay among different employers. While working for a retail pharmacy, I earned an hourly wage of $15. However, after transitioning to a hospital pharmacy, my hourly wage increased to $18. Additionally, I was able to negotiate for better benefits such as health insurance and paid time off.

FAQs

Q: What is the job outlook for pharmacy technicians in Florida?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, employment of pharmacy technicians in Florida is projected to grow 6% from 2019 to 2029, which is about as fast as the average for all occupations.

Q: How much do pharmacy technicians make in Miami, Florida?

A: According to Salary.com, the average hourly wage for pharmacy technicians in Miami is $17, with an annual salary of $35,202.

Q: Can pharmacy technicians work remotely in Florida?

A: While some aspects of the pharmacy technician job can be performed remotely, such as data entry or customer service, many tasks require in-person interaction with patients or healthcare providers. Remote work opportunities for pharmacy techs may be limited.

Q: What are the requirements to become a pharmacy technician in Florida?

A: In Florida, pharmacy technicians must be at least 18 years old, have a high school diploma or equivalent, and pass a criminal background check. They must also register with the Florida Board of Pharmacy and complete a Board-approved training program or have equivalent work experience.

Conclusion

Understanding how much pharmacy technicians make in Florida can help you make informed decisions about your career. While the average hourly wage is $16.33, salaries can vary based on factors such as location, experience, and employer. Pursuing education or certification and seeking advancement opportunities can also increase earning potential. If you're considering a career as a pharmacy technician in Florida, be sure to research potential employers and negotiate for fair pay and benefits.
