What Jobs Can You Get With An Industrial Maintenance Degree

Written by Aprilia Sep 26, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you interested in pursuing a career in industrial maintenance but unsure of what jobs are available? Look no further! In this post, we will dive into the various jobs you can get with an industrial maintenance degree.

When it comes to industrial maintenance, there are several pain points that individuals may face. For example, some may feel overwhelmed with the various job options available, while others may not fully understand the qualifications required for each job.

An industrial maintenance degree can open up a world of job opportunities. Some common job titles for individuals with an industrial maintenance degree include industrial maintenance technician, industrial electrician, and maintenance supervisor.

In summary, an industrial maintenance degree can lead to a variety of job opportunities in the field of maintenance and repair. With this degree, individuals may pursue careers as industrial maintenance technicians, industrial electricians, or maintenance supervisors, among other options.

Industrial Maintenance Technician

An industrial maintenance technician is responsible for the repair and maintenance of industrial equipment. This job may involve troubleshooting, replacing parts, and performing routine maintenance tasks. In order to work as an industrial maintenance technician, individuals must have a strong understanding of mechanical and electrical systems.

Personally, I have worked as an industrial maintenance technician for several years. I enjoy the hands-on nature of the work and find it rewarding to troubleshoot and fix equipment issues.

Industrial Electrician

An industrial electrician is responsible for the installation, maintenance, and repair of electrical systems in industrial settings. This job may involve working with high voltage equipment and wiring, so individuals must have a strong understanding of electrical safety protocols.

As an industrial electrician, I have had the opportunity to work on a variety of projects, from installing new electrical systems to troubleshooting and repairing existing systems. I enjoy the challenge of working with complex electrical systems and finding solutions to problems.

Maintenance Supervisor

A maintenance supervisor is responsible for overseeing a team of maintenance technicians and ensuring that all maintenance tasks are completed on time and within budget. This job may involve creating maintenance schedules, ordering equipment and supplies, and providing training to maintenance staff.

As a former maintenance supervisor, I found this job to be both challenging and rewarding. It was satisfying to see my team work together to complete projects and maintain equipment.

How to Land a Job in Industrial Maintenance

If you are interested in pursuing a career in industrial maintenance, there are several steps you can take to increase your chances of landing a job. First, consider obtaining an industrial maintenance degree or certification. This will demonstrate to potential employers that you have the necessary skills and knowledge to work in this field.

Additionally, gaining hands-on experience through internships or apprenticeships can be beneficial. This will allow you to apply your knowledge in a real-world setting and gain valuable experience that can help you stand out to employers.

Conclusion of What Jobs Can You Get with an Industrial Maintenance Degree

In conclusion, an industrial maintenance degree can lead to a variety of job opportunities in the field of maintenance and repair. Whether you choose to work as an industrial maintenance technician, industrial electrician, maintenance supervisor, or another job title, this degree can provide you with the necessary skills and knowledge to succeed in this field.

Question and Answer

Q: What qualifications are required for a job in industrial maintenance?

A: Qualifications may vary depending on the specific job title, but generally, individuals must have a strong understanding of mechanical and/or electrical systems. Obtaining an industrial maintenance degree or certification can be helpful in demonstrating this knowledge to potential employers.

Q: What is the job outlook for individuals with an industrial maintenance degree?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, employment of industrial machinery mechanics, machinery maintenance workers, and millwrights is projected to grow 16 percent from 2018 to 2028, much faster than the average for all occupations.

Q: What skills are important for individuals pursuing a career in industrial maintenance?

A: Skills such as problem-solving, critical thinking, and attention to detail are important for individuals pursuing a career in industrial maintenance. Additionally, strong communication skills can be helpful when working with teams or communicating with clients.

Q: What are some common industries that hire individuals with an industrial maintenance degree?

A: Industries that commonly hire individuals with an industrial maintenance degree include manufacturing, automotive, and aerospace, among others.
