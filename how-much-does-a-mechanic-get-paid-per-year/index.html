How Much Does A Mechanic Get Paid Per Year

Written by Daniel Jun 12, 2023 · 4 min read

Do you know how much a mechanic earns per year? If you’re someone who’s interested in pursuing a career in the automotive industry, it’s important to understand how much you can expect to earn as a mechanic.

Working as a mechanic has its pros and cons. On one hand, it can be a rewarding and fulfilling career that allows you to work with your hands and help people. On the other hand, it can be a physically demanding job that requires long hours and a high level of skill and knowledge.

So, how much does a mechanic get paid per year? According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for automotive service technicians and mechanics was $44,050 as of May 2020. However, the range of salaries can vary widely depending on factors such as location, experience, and type of employer.

In order to earn a higher salary as a mechanic, it’s important to gain experience and specialize in a particular area such as diesel engines, foreign cars, or transmissions. Additionally, mechanics who work for dealerships or specialty repair shops may earn more than those who work for independent garages.

In conclusion, becoming a mechanic can be a lucrative career choice for those who enjoy working with their hands and have a passion for automobiles. While the median annual wage may not be as high as some other professions, there is room for growth and specialization in the field.

How to Increase Your Salary as a Mechanic

One way to increase your salary as a mechanic is to gain experience and specialize in a particular area. For example, if you become an expert in diesel engines or hybrid vehicles, you may be able to command a higher salary than someone who has a more general knowledge of automotive repair.

Another way to increase your salary is to work for a dealership or specialty repair shop. These types of employers often pay more than independent garages and may offer additional benefits such as health insurance and retirement plans.

The Importance of Continuing Education for Mechanics

Continuing education is important for mechanics who want to stay up-to-date with the latest technology and techniques in the automotive industry. By attending training courses and earning certifications, mechanics can increase their knowledge and skills, which can lead to higher salaries and more job opportunities.

How Much Does a Mechanic Apprentice Get Paid?

As an apprentice, a mechanic can expect to earn between $15 and $20 per hour. This can vary depending on the employer and location. However, as an apprentice gains experience and becomes a journeyman mechanic, their salary will increase.

How to Negotiate Salary as a Mechanic

When negotiating salary as a mechanic, it’s important to have a clear understanding of your skills and experience. Research the average salary for mechanics in your area and be prepared to explain why you believe you are worth a higher salary.

Additionally, consider negotiating for additional benefits such as health insurance, retirement plans, and paid vacation time. These benefits can be just as valuable as a higher salary.

Real Life Experience of a Mechanic

As a mechanic for over 10 years, I’ve experienced firsthand the ups and downs of the profession. While the work can be physically demanding and stressful at times, there’s nothing more satisfying than fixing a car and seeing the smile on a customer’s face.

Question and Answer

Q: How much does a mechanic make hourly?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median hourly wage for automotive service technicians and mechanics was $21.16 as of May 2020.

Q: What is the highest paying mechanic job?

A: The highest paying mechanic jobs are typically those that require specialization in areas such as diesel engines, foreign cars, or transmissions. Mechanics who work for dealerships or specialty repair shops may also earn higher salaries.

Q: How long does it take to become a certified mechanic?

A: It can take anywhere from six months to two years to complete a training program to become a certified mechanic. Additionally, mechanics may need to pass a certification exam in order to become certified.

Q: What is the job outlook for mechanics?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, employment of automotive service technicians and mechanics is projected to grow 4 percent from 2019 to 2029, about as fast as the average for all occupations.

Conclusion of How Much Does a Mechanic Get Paid Per Year

While the salary for a mechanic may not be as high as some other professions, it can be a rewarding and fulfilling career for those who have a passion for automobiles and enjoy working with their hands. By gaining experience, specializing in a particular area, and continuing education, mechanics can increase their knowledge and skills, which can lead to higher salaries and more job opportunities.
