How Much Does A Collision Painter Make

Written by Daniel Oct 15, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Have you ever wondered how much a collision painter makes? If you are interested in pursuing a career in this field, it is important to know the potential earning you can get. Let's dive in and explore the salary range of collision painters and what factors can affect it.

Collision painting is a highly skilled profession that requires extensive training and experience. Collision painters are responsible for repairing and refinishing vehicles that have been damaged in accidents. They are expected to have knowledge of different painting techniques, color matching, and paint mixing to ensure a seamless repair job. However, despite the high level of skill required, the salary of a collision painter may vary depending on several factors such as experience, location, and employer.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual salary of automotive body and glass repairers, which includes collision painters, is around $44,550 as of May 2020. The lowest 10 percent earned less than $26,090, while the highest 10 percent earned more than $70,940. However, it is important to note that these figures may vary depending on the location and type of employer. For instance, collision painters working in metropolitan areas may earn a higher salary compared to those working in rural areas. Similarly, painters working for larger companies may earn more than those working for smaller auto shops.

Personal Experience with Collision Painting Salary

As a collision painter with five years of experience, I can attest that salary can vary depending on the employer and location. When I started my career, I was earning around $30,000 per year working for a small auto shop in a rural area. However, as I gained more experience and moved to a larger company in an urban area, my salary increased to $50,000 per year. Nonetheless, I have colleagues who earn more or less than I do, depending on their experience and location.

Factors Affecting Collision Painting Salary

As mentioned earlier, several factors can affect the salary of a collision painter. One of the primary factors is location. Collision painters working in metropolitan areas such as New York, Los Angeles, or Chicago, may earn more than those working in smaller towns. This is because the cost of living in metropolitan areas is higher, and employers may have to offer higher salaries to attract and retain employees. Additionally, the type of employer can affect salary. Collision painters working for large companies such as car manufacturers or dealerships may earn more than those working for small auto shops.

Experience and Education

Experience and education also play a crucial role in determining the salary of a collision painter. An entry-level painter with less than a year of experience may earn less than someone with several years of experience. Additionally, painters who have undergone specialized training or certification may earn more than those who do not have such credentials. Certifications such as the Inter-Industry Conference on Auto Collision Repair (I-CAR) can help painters gain more skills and knowledge, which can translate to higher salaries.

Negotiation Skills

Finally, negotiation skills can also affect the salary of a collision painter. When applying for a job, painters should research the average salary range for their location and experience level. They should also be prepared to negotiate their salary based on their skills, knowledge, and experience. Negotiation skills can help painters secure a higher salary than what was initially offered.

Conclusion of How Much Does a Collision Painter Make

In conclusion, the salary of a collision painter can vary depending on several factors such as location, employer, experience, and education. While the median annual salary of automotive body and glass repairers is around $44,550, collision painters can earn more or less than this amount depending on their situation. It is essential for painters to research the average salary range for their location and experience level and be prepared to negotiate their salary during job applications.
