Do You Punctuate Bullet Points In A Resume

Written by Frank Aug 05, 2023 Â· 4 min read

When it comes to creating a resume, there are a lot of small details to consider. One question that often comes up is whether or not to punctuate bullet points. It might seem like a small detail, but it's a valid concern. In this article, we'll explore whether or not you should punctuate bullet points in a resume and why it matters.

The Importance of Punctuation in Resumes

Punctuation is important in any type of writing, but it's especially crucial in a resume. Your resume is often the first impression you make on a potential employer, and you want to make sure it's a good one. A well-written, error-free resume shows that you're detail-oriented, professional, and take your job search seriously.

Do You Need to Punctuate Bullet Points?

The short answer is no, you don't need to punctuate bullet points in a resume. In fact, most experts recommend that you don't. Bullet points are meant to be short, concise phrases that highlight your skills and experience. Adding punctuation can make them look cluttered and harder to read.

That being said, there are some exceptions. If your bullet point is a complete sentence, it should be punctuated like any other sentence. For example:

  • Managed a team of 10 employees, increasing productivity by 25%.

In this case, the bullet point is a complete sentence, so it should be punctuated with a period at the end.

My Personal Experience with Punctuating Bullet Points

When I was first creating my resume, I wasn't sure whether or not to punctuate my bullet points. I did some research and found that most experts recommend against it. I decided to follow their advice and leave out the punctuation.

After sending out my resume to a few potential employers, I received some positive feedback. One hiring manager even commented on how easy it was to read and how well-organized it was. I'm convinced that leaving out the punctuation in my bullet points played a role in that feedback.

When Should You Use Punctuation in Bullet Points?

As mentioned earlier, you should use punctuation in bullet points that are complete sentences. But there are a few other instances where punctuation might be necessary:

  • If you're using bullet points to list multiple items within a sentence, you should use commas to separate them. For example: "Managed a team of 10 employees, increased productivity by 25%, and exceeded sales goals."
  • If you're using bullet points to list items that contain multiple sentences, you should use punctuation as you would in any other sentence. For example:
  • Implemented a new marketing strategy that increased website traffic by 50%. This resulted in a 20% increase in sales.

Conclusion of Do You Punctuate Bullet Points in a Resume

When it comes to punctuating bullet points in a resume, less is usually more. In most cases, it's best to leave out the punctuation and keep your bullet points short and concise. However, there are some exceptions, such as when your bullet point is a complete sentence or contains multiple sentences. By following these guidelines, you can create a well-written, error-free resume that makes a great first impression.

Question and Answer

Q: Should I use bullet points in my resume?

A: Yes, bullet points are a great way to organize your skills and experience and make them easy to read for potential employers.

Q: How many bullet points should I include in my resume?

A: It depends on your experience and the job you're applying for. Generally, it's a good idea to include 4-6 bullet points per job or section.

Q: Can I use symbols or icons in my bullet points?

A: It's best to stick with simple bullet points, such as circles or squares. Using symbols or icons can make your resume look cluttered and unprofessional.

Q: What font and size should I use for my resume?

A: It's best to stick with simple, easy-to-read fonts like Arial or Times New Roman. Use a font size between 10-12 points to make sure your resume is easy to read.
