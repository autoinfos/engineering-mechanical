Entry Level Painter Salary What You Need To Know

Written by Aprilia Oct 04, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you considering a career as an entry level painter but unsure about the salary? Painting is a rewarding profession that requires skill and attention to detail, but it can be hard to know what to expect in terms of pay. In this post, we'll explore the ins and outs of entry level painter salary and what you can expect as you begin your career.

One of the main concerns for those starting out in any profession is how much they will earn. As an entry level painter, it's natural to feel uncertain about what you can expect in terms of pay. This uncertainty can create anxiety and make it difficult to plan for the future. However, with a little research and some guidance, you can gain a better understanding of what you can expect as an entry level painter.

Entry level painter salary can vary depending on a number of factors, including experience, location, and the specific job you are applying for. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median pay for painters in 2020 was $20.45 per hour, or $42,530 per year. This includes both entry level and experienced painters, so it's important to keep in mind that as an entry level painter, your salary may be lower than this figure.

My Personal Experience with Entry Level Painter Salary

When I first started out as an entry level painter, I was nervous about what I could expect in terms of salary. I had heard that painters didn't make a lot of money, and I was worried that I wouldn't be able to support myself. However, after doing some research and talking to other painters, I found that entry level painter salary can vary quite a bit depending on where you live and what type of painting you are doing.

In my experience, the starting salary for an entry level painter can range from $12 to $18 per hour. This may not seem like a lot, but it's important to remember that as you gain experience and hone your skills, your salary will likely increase.

Factors that Affect Entry Level Painter Salary

As mentioned earlier, there are several factors that can affect entry level painter salary. One of the biggest factors is location. Painters who live in areas with a higher cost of living may earn more than those who live in areas with a lower cost of living. Additionally, painters who work for larger companies or who have specialized skills may earn more than those who work for smaller companies or who are just starting out.

Another factor that can affect entry level painter salary is the type of painting you are doing. For example, painters who specialize in commercial or industrial painting may earn more than those who do residential painting. Additionally, painters who work in hazardous environments or who need to use specialized equipment may earn more than those who do not.

Training and Experience

One of the most important factors that can affect entry level painter salary is training and experience. Painters who have completed apprenticeships or who have received formal training may earn more than those who are just starting out. Additionally, painters who have worked in the industry for several years and who have developed a reputation for quality work may be able to command higher salaries.

Tips for Increasing Your Entry Level Painter Salary

If you're an entry level painter and you want to increase your salary, there are several things you can do. First, focus on developing your skills and becoming an expert in your field. The more knowledge and experience you have, the more valuable you will be to potential employers.

Additionally, consider getting certified in specialized areas of painting, such as lead abatement or hazardous materials handling. These certifications can help you stand out from other painters and may lead to higher paying jobs.

Conclusion of Entry Level Painter Salary

While entry level painter salary may not be as high as some other professions, it's important to remember that painting is a skilled profession that requires attention to detail and a commitment to quality work. As you gain experience and build your reputation, your salary will likely increase. By focusing on developing your skills and becoming an expert in your field, you can increase your earning potential and build a successful career as an entry level painter.

Question and Answer

Q: How much does an entry level painter make per hour?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median pay for painters in 2020 was $20.45 per hour. However, entry level painters may earn less than this figure depending on their experience and location.

Q: What factors can affect entry level painter salary?

A: Entry level painter salary can be affected by a number of factors, including location, type of painting, and training and experience.

Q: How can I increase my entry level painter salary?

A: To increase your entry level painter salary, focus on developing your skills and becoming an expert in your field. Consider getting certified in specialized areas of painting, and look for opportunities to work with larger companies or on more specialized projects.

Q: Is painting a good career choice?

A: Yes, painting can be a rewarding career choice for those who enjoy working with their hands and have an eye for detail. While entry level painter salary may not be as high as some other professions, painting can offer a great deal of job satisfaction and the opportunity to work on interesting projects.
