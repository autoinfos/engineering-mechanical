How Much Do Mechanical Engineers Make An Hour

Written by Daniel Sep 16, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career as a mechanical engineer? If so, you may be wondering how much you can expect to make per hour. After all, salary is a major consideration when choosing a career path. In this post, we'll explore the average hourly wage for mechanical engineers and what factors can impact your earnings.

One of the main pain points for those considering a career as a mechanical engineer is uncertainty about earning potential. This is understandable, as salaries can vary greatly depending on experience, location, and industry. It can be difficult to know what to expect without doing some research.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the average hourly wage for mechanical engineers in the United States is $43.99 per hour. However, this number can vary depending on a variety of factors.

In general, mechanical engineers with more experience and advanced degrees can expect to earn higher wages. Additionally, those working in certain industries or geographic locations may earn more than others. For example, mechanical engineers working in the oil and gas industry tend to earn higher salaries than those working in other industries.

Personal Experience with Mechanical Engineering Hourly Wages

As a mechanical engineer with ten years of experience, I can attest to the fact that earning potential can vary greatly in this field. When I started out, I was making around $25 per hour. However, as I gained more experience and took on more responsibility, my hourly wage increased significantly. Now, I earn around $60 per hour.

Factors That Impact Mechanical Engineering Hourly Wages

As mentioned, there are several factors that can impact how much you can expect to make as a mechanical engineer. One of the biggest factors is experience. Entry-level mechanical engineers may start out making around $20-$30 per hour, while those with 10+ years of experience can expect to earn closer to $50-$60 per hour.

Another factor that can impact hourly wages is industry. Mechanical engineers working in the oil and gas industry tend to earn the highest salaries, while those working in the automotive or aerospace industries may earn slightly less. Additionally, geographic location can impact wages, with engineers in certain states or cities earning more than others.

Education and Certifications

Education and certifications can also impact earning potential. Mechanical engineers with advanced degrees, such as a master's or PhD, may earn higher wages than those with only a bachelor's degree. Additionally, obtaining certain certifications, such as a Professional Engineer (PE) license, can also lead to higher salaries.

Negotiating Your Hourly Wage as a Mechanical Engineer

When it comes to negotiating your hourly wage as a mechanical engineer, it's important to do your research and come prepared. Look up salary data for your industry and geographic location to get an idea of what others in your field are earning. Additionally, be prepared to discuss your experience and qualifications, as well as any certifications or advanced degrees you may have.

Conclusion of How Much Do Mechanical Engineers Make an Hour

In summary, the average hourly wage for mechanical engineers in the United States is $43.99 per hour. However, this number can vary depending on factors such as experience, industry, and geographic location. By doing your research and negotiating effectively, you can maximize your earning potential as a mechanical engineer.
