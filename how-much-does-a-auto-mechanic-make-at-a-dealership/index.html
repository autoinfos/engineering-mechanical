How Much Does An Auto Mechanic Make At A Dealership

Written by Frank Oct 02, 2023 · 4 min read

As someone interested in becoming an auto mechanic, you may be wondering how much does an auto mechanic make at a dealership. This can be a pain point for many people as salary is an important factor to consider when choosing a career path. In this article, we will explore the average salary of an auto mechanic at a dealership and what factors can affect their earnings.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for automotive service technicians and mechanics, including those who work in dealerships, was $42,090 as of May 2020. However, salaries can vary based on experience, location, and type of dealership. For instance, a luxury car dealership may pay more than a regular dealership, and a mechanic with several years of experience may earn more than a new hire.

In summary, an auto mechanic at a dealership can expect to make a median annual wage of $42,090, but this can vary based on factors such as experience, location, and type of dealership.

Factors that Affect an Auto Mechanic's Salary at a Dealership

Experience is one of the primary factors that can affect an auto mechanic's salary at a dealership. A mechanic who has been working in the industry for several years is likely to earn more than a new hire. Additionally, location can play a role in how much a mechanic makes. For example, a mechanic working in a high-cost-of-living area may earn more than one working in a less expensive city. The type of dealership can also impact a mechanic's salary. Luxury car dealerships may pay more than regular dealerships.

My Experience as an Auto Mechanic at a Dealership

As someone who has worked as an auto mechanic at a dealership, I can attest to the fact that experience plays a significant role in how much you can earn. When I first started out, I was making around $30,000 a year. However, as I gained more experience and became more skilled, my salary increased to around $50,000 a year. Additionally, working at a luxury car dealership allowed me to earn more than I would have at a regular dealership.

How to Increase Your Salary as an Auto Mechanic at a Dealership

If you want to increase your salary as an auto mechanic at a dealership, there are several things you can do. First and foremost, gaining experience and skills is key. The more experienced and skilled you are, the more valuable you become to your employer. Additionally, getting certified by the National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence (ASE) can also increase your earning potential. ASE-certified mechanics are in high demand and can command higher salaries.

Other Factors to Consider

Aside from salary, there are other factors to consider when choosing a career as an auto mechanic at a dealership. For instance, the work can be physically demanding and may require working long hours or on weekends. Additionally, the job can be stressful as you are responsible for diagnosing and fixing complex mechanical issues. However, if you have a passion for cars and enjoy problem-solving, becoming an auto mechanic can be a rewarding career choice.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I negotiate my salary as an auto mechanic at a dealership?

A: Yes, you can negotiate your salary as an auto mechanic at a dealership. However, it is important to do your research beforehand and have a clear understanding of what your skills and experience are worth.

Q: What benefits can I expect as an auto mechanic at a dealership?

A: Benefits can vary based on the dealership, but common benefits include health insurance, retirement plans, and paid time off.

Q: Do I need a college degree to become an auto mechanic at a dealership?

A: No, a college degree is not required to become an auto mechanic at a dealership. However, completing a vocational or technical program in automotive technology can be beneficial and may increase your chances of getting hired.

Q: Is the demand for auto mechanics at dealerships increasing?

A: Yes, the demand for auto mechanics at dealerships is expected to grow by 4% from 2019 to 2029, according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the median annual wage for an auto mechanic at a dealership is $42,090. However, this can vary based on factors such as experience, location, and type of dealership. Gaining experience and skills, getting certified by ASE, and working at a luxury car dealership can all increase your earning potential. While the job can be physically demanding and stressful, it can also be a rewarding career for those with a passion for cars and problem-solving.
