How To Become A Pharmacy Technician In Texas

Written by Christine Oct 19, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you are interested in pursuing a career as a pharmacy technician in Texas, you may be wondering where to start. Becoming a pharmacy technician requires training and certification, and the process can be confusing if you don't know where to begin. In this blog post, we will discuss the steps you need to take to become a pharmacy technician in Texas.

To become a pharmacy technician in Texas, you must meet certain requirements. First, you must be at least 18 years old and have a high school diploma or GED. You must also have no felony convictions and no drug or pharmacy-related convictions. Additionally, you must complete a training program or have equivalent work experience.

The first step in becoming a pharmacy technician in Texas is to enroll in a training program. There are many programs available, both online and in-person, that will teach you the skills you need to become a pharmacy technician. These programs typically last between 6 and 12 months and cover topics such as pharmacology, pharmacy law, and medication safety.

After completing your training program, you must pass the Pharmacy Technician Certification Exam (PTCE) or the Exam for the Certification of Pharmacy Technicians (ExCPT). These exams test your knowledge of pharmacy law, pharmacology, medication safety, and other important topics. Once you pass the exam, you will be a certified pharmacy technician (CPhT) and can begin working in a pharmacy.

In summary, to become a pharmacy technician in Texas, you must meet certain requirements, complete a training program, and pass a certification exam. Once you have done these things, you can begin working in a pharmacy as a certified pharmacy technician.

What is the Job of a Pharmacy Technician?

As a pharmacy technician, your job is to assist the pharmacist with tasks such as filling prescriptions, managing inventory, and providing customer service. You will also be responsible for ensuring that medications are dispensed safely and accurately, and that the pharmacy is in compliance with all state and federal regulations.

My Experience Becoming a Pharmacy Technician in Texas

When I decided to become a pharmacy technician in Texas, I enrolled in an online training program. The program was self-paced, which allowed me to work around my schedule. I spent about 6 months completing the program, and then I took the PTCE exam. After passing the exam, I was able to find a job in a local pharmacy. I have been working as a pharmacy technician for several years now, and I love my job.

How Long Does it Take to Become a Pharmacy Technician in Texas?

The amount of time it takes to become a pharmacy technician in Texas depends on the training program you choose. Most programs last between 6 and 12 months. After completing your training, you will need to pass the certification exam, which can take several weeks to prepare for.

What Skills Do You Need to Become a Pharmacy Technician in Texas?

To become a pharmacy technician in Texas, you will need to have good communication skills, attention to detail, and the ability to work well in a team. You should also be comfortable working with technology, as most pharmacies use computerized systems to manage their inventory and fill prescriptions.

How Much Does it Cost to Become a Pharmacy Technician in Texas?

The cost of becoming a pharmacy technician in Texas varies depending on the training program you choose. Some programs are more expensive than others, and some may offer financial aid or scholarships. You will also need to pay a fee to take the certification exam.

Conclusion of How to Become a Pharmacy Technician in Texas

If you are interested in pursuing a career as a pharmacy technician in Texas, there are several steps you will need to take. You must meet certain requirements, complete a training program, and pass a certification exam. However, with dedication and hard work, you can become a certified pharmacy technician and begin a rewarding career in the healthcare industry.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the job outlook for pharmacy technicians in Texas?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, employment of pharmacy technicians is expected to grow by 7% from 2019 to 2029, which is faster than the average for all occupations.

Q: Can I become a pharmacy technician without completing a training program?

A: In Texas, you can become a pharmacy technician through work experience if you have at least 12 months of experience working in a pharmacy. However, completing a training program is recommended, as it will give you the knowledge and skills you need to pass the certification exam.

Q: How much do pharmacy technicians in Texas make?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual salary for pharmacy technicians in Texas is $35,150.

Q: How often do pharmacy technicians need to renew their certification in Texas?

A: In Texas, pharmacy technicians must renew their certification every two years. To renew their certification, they must complete 20 hours of continuing education and pass an exam.
