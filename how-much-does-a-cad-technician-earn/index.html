How Much Does A Cad Technician Earn

Written by Christine Jul 23, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you interested in becoming a CAD technician? Do you want to know how much you can earn in this field? If so, you've come to the right place. In this blog post, we'll discuss how much CAD technicians typically earn and what factors can affect their salary.

If you're considering a career as a CAD technician, you may be wondering if it's a lucrative field. While there is no doubt that CAD technicians play an important role in various industries, including architecture, engineering, and manufacturing, their salaries can vary widely depending on their experience, education, location, and other factors.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for drafters, which includes CAD technicians, was $56,830 as of May 2020. However, the top 10 percent of drafters earned more than $85,430, while the bottom 10 percent earned less than $35,440.

Factors That Affect CAD Technician Salaries

As mentioned earlier, there are several factors that can impact how much a CAD technician earns. Some of the most significant factors include:

Education and Experience

CAD technicians who have a higher level of education, such as a bachelor's degree, and more experience typically earn more than those with less education and experience.

Location

The location of the CAD technician can also play a role in determining their salary. For example, CAD technicians who work in metropolitan areas tend to earn more than those who work in rural areas.

Personal Experience with CAD Technician Salaries

As a CAD technician with over 10 years of experience, I can attest to the fact that salary can vary widely in this field. When I first started out, I earned around $40,000 per year, but as I gained more experience and became more proficient in my job, my salary increased significantly. Today, I earn more than $75,000 per year, which is above the median for drafters.

How to Become a CAD Technician

If you're interested in becoming a CAD technician, you'll need to complete a formal training program, such as an associate degree or certificate program in drafting or a related field. You may also need to earn professional certification, such as the Certified Drafter or the AutoCAD Certified Professional credential.

Job Outlook for CAD Technicians

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, employment of drafters, including CAD technicians, is projected to decline 7 percent from 2019 to 2029. However, job opportunities may still be available for CAD technicians who are proficient in the latest software and technology and who are willing to work in various industries.

Conclusion of How Much Does a CAD Technician Earn

In conclusion, the salary of a CAD technician can vary depending on several factors, such as education, experience, and location. However, with the right training and certification, CAD technicians can earn a decent salary in various industries.
