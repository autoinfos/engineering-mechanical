What Is A Good Answer To Why Should We Hire You

Written by Aprilia Aug 02, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you nervous for an upcoming job interview? One common question that often throws people off is "why should we hire you?" It can be difficult to know how to answer this question without sounding too boastful or not confident enough. However, with a little preparation, you can confidently answer this question and impress your potential employer.

Identifying the Pain Points

Before we dive into how to answer this question, let's first identify why it's important. Employers ask this question because they want to know what sets you apart from other candidates and how you can contribute to their company. They want to know that you have the skills and experience needed to excel in the position and that you are a good fit for their company culture.

The Answer

The best way to answer this question is to tie your skills and experience directly to the job description and company values. Use specific examples of your past accomplishments that relate to the job at hand. Emphasize your unique skills and how they will benefit the company. Additionally, show enthusiasm for the position and company.

Summary

In summary, a good answer to "why should we hire you?" should include specific examples of past accomplishments that relate to the job at hand, emphasize unique skills, show enthusiasm for the position and company, and tie skills and experience directly to the job description and company values.

Personal Experience: Tailoring Your Answer

When I was interviewing for a marketing position, I made sure to tailor my answer to the company's focus on social media. I highlighted my experience with creating successful social media campaigns and showed how I could apply those skills to the company's goals. By tailoring my answer to the company's values, I was able to stand out among other candidates.

Personal Experience: Confidence is Key

Another time, I was interviewing for a customer service position. Instead of feeling nervous about the question, I used it as an opportunity to show my confidence in my abilities. I highlighted my past experience in customer service and my ability to handle difficult situations with ease. By showing confidence in my skills, I was able to convince the employer that I was the best fit for the position.

Going Deeper: How to Prepare for the Question

To prepare for this question, start by researching the company and job description. Identify the company values and what they're looking for in a candidate. Then, make a list of your skills and experiences that directly relate to the job. Practice answering the question out loud and refine your answer until it feels natural.

The Follow-Up: Thank You Notes

After the interview, make sure to send a thank you note to the interviewer. In the note, reiterate your strengths and how they align with the company's values. This is a great opportunity to remind the employer of why you are the best fit for the position.

Personal Experience: Highlighting Soft Skills

During an interview for a project management position, I made sure to highlight my soft skills in addition to my technical skills. I emphasized my ability to communicate effectively with team members and stakeholders, as well as my strong organizational skills. By highlighting my soft skills, I was able to show the employer that I would be a valuable asset to their team.

Question and Answer

Q: Can you give an example of a good answer to "why should we hire you?"

A: "Based on my experience in project management and my ability to effectively communicate with team members and stakeholders, I believe I would be a valuable asset to your team. Additionally, my strong organizational skills and attention to detail would allow me to successfully manage projects and meet deadlines."

Q: How can I avoid sounding too boastful in my answer?

A: Use specific examples of past accomplishments and tie them directly to the job description and company values. Emphasize your unique skills and how they will benefit the company. Avoid using general statements without backing them up with evidence or examples.

Q: Should I mention my weaknesses in my answer?

A: It's generally best to focus on your strengths in your answer. However, if the employer specifically asks about weaknesses, be honest but also show how you are actively working to improve in those areas.

Q: How can I show enthusiasm for the position and company?

A: Research the company and job description beforehand and identify what excites you about the position and company. Show that you've done your research and express genuine enthusiasm for the opportunity to work with the company.

Conclusion of What is a Good Answer to "Why Should We Hire You?"

Answering the question "why should we hire you?" can be nerve-wracking, but with preparation and confidence, you can impress your potential employer. Tailor your answer to the company's values and job description, emphasize your unique skills, and show enthusiasm for the position and company. With these tips in mind, you'll be well on your way to acing your next job interview.
