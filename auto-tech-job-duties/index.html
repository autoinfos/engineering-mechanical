Auto Tech Job Duties What You Need To Know

Written by Bardi Aug 04, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career in the automotive industry? Perhaps you're already an auto tech but aren't sure about your job duties. Whatever your situation, this post will give you the information you need to know about auto tech job duties.

Auto techs have a lot of responsibilities on their plate. Not only do they need to diagnose and repair vehicles, but they also need to follow safety procedures, keep up with the latest technology, and communicate effectively with customers.

The primary goal of an auto tech is to identify and fix problems with vehicles. This could involve anything from replacing a part to overhauling an entire system. In addition to diagnosing and repairing issues, auto techs also perform routine maintenance tasks, such as changing oil and checking fluid levels.

To be a successful auto tech, you need to have a solid understanding of automotive systems and their components. You also need to be able to use diagnostic tools effectively and keep up with the latest industry trends and technology.

Auto tech job duties can be broken down into several categories, including diagnosis and repair, maintenance, safety, and customer service. Let's take a closer look at each of these categories.

Diagnosis and Repair

One of the primary job duties of an auto tech is to diagnose and repair vehicle issues. This involves using diagnostic tools to identify the problem and then implementing a solution. As an auto tech, you may need to replace parts, repair systems, or overhaul entire engines. It's important to have a strong understanding of automotive systems and their components to be successful in this role.

Personally, I've had to diagnose and repair a wide range of issues throughout my career. From simple problems like a dead battery to more complex issues like internal engine damage, my job is to find the root cause and implement a solution that will get the vehicle back on the road as quickly as possible.

Maintenance

Another important aspect of auto tech job duties is performing routine maintenance tasks. This includes things like changing oil, checking fluid levels, and inspecting brake systems. By performing regular maintenance, you can help prevent more serious issues from occurring down the road.

Personally, I enjoy performing maintenance tasks because it gives me a chance to get up close and personal with the vehicle. I can inspect various systems and components to ensure that everything is working properly and catch any issues before they become bigger problems.

Safety

As an auto tech, safety is always a top priority. This means following proper safety procedures when working on vehicles, using personal protective equipment (PPE), and ensuring that the vehicle is safe to drive before returning it to the customer.

Personally, I take safety very seriously in my job. I always wear the proper PPE, follow safety protocols, and double-check my work before the vehicle leaves the shop.

Customer Service

Finally, good customer service is an essential part of auto tech job duties. This involves communicating effectively with customers, explaining issues in layman's terms, and providing high-quality service that meets or exceeds customer expectations.

Personally, I find that good customer service is what sets great auto techs apart from mediocre ones. By taking the time to explain issues and answer questions, you can build trust with customers and establish a strong reputation in the industry.

Question and Answer

Q: What kind of training do I need to become an auto tech?

A: Most auto techs have a degree or certificate in automotive technology from a vocational school or community college. You may also need to complete an apprenticeship or on-the-job training program.

Q: What kind of tools do I need as an auto tech?

A: Auto techs need a wide range of tools, including diagnostic equipment, hand tools, and power tools. Some employers provide tools, while others require you to purchase your own.

Q: What kind of salary can I expect as an auto tech?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual salary for auto techs is $42,090. However, salaries can vary widely depending on experience, location, and employer.

Q: What are some of the biggest challenges facing auto techs today?

A: One of the biggest challenges is keeping up with the latest technology and industry trends. As vehicles become more advanced, auto techs need to stay ahead of the curve to remain competitive in the industry.

Conclusion of Auto Tech Job Duties

Auto tech job duties cover a wide range of responsibilities, from diagnosing and repairing vehicle issues to performing routine maintenance tasks and providing top-notch customer service. To be successful in this field, you need to have a solid understanding of automotive systems and components, keep up with the latest technology and industry trends, and prioritize safety and customer service. If you're up for the challenge, a career as an auto tech can be both rewarding and fulfilling.
