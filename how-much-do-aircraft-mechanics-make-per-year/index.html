How Much Do Aircraft Mechanics Make Per Year

Written by Aprilia Nov 14, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you interested in becoming an aircraft mechanic? Are you wondering how much you can expect to make in this field? If so, you're in the right place. In this post, we'll explore the topic of how much do aircraft mechanics make per year, and provide you with some valuable insights into this in-demand career field.

When it comes to the topic of how much do aircraft mechanics make per year, there are several factors to consider. For example, the location where you work, the type of aircraft you are working on, and your level of experience can all impact your earning potential. Additionally, the industry as a whole is subject to fluctuations in demand, which can also affect your salary.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual salary for aircraft mechanics and service technicians is $64,090 as of May 2020. The lowest 10 percent earned less than $38,350, and the highest 10 percent earned more than $98,040. In general, those who work for commercial airlines tend to earn more than those who work in general aviation or for the government.

Personal Experience: What I Earn as an Aircraft Mechanic

As an experienced aircraft mechanic, I can tell you that the salary range for this field can vary widely. When I first started out in this industry, I was earning around $40,000 per year. However, as I gained experience and took on more challenging roles, my salary increased significantly. Today, I earn around $80,000 per year, which is a comfortable living for me and my family.

The Impact of Experience on Aircraft Mechanic Salaries

When it comes to how much do aircraft mechanics make per year, experience is a major factor. Those who are just starting out in this field can expect to earn less than those who have been working as mechanics for several years. Additionally, those who have specialized skills or certifications may be able to command higher salaries.

Factors That Can Affect Aircraft Mechanic Salaries

In addition to experience, there are several other factors that can impact how much do aircraft mechanics make per year. For example, those who work in high-demand areas or for in-demand airlines may be able to earn more than those who work in less-populated areas or for smaller companies. Additionally, those who are willing to work overtime or take on extra responsibilities may be able to increase their earning potential.

How to Increase Your Earning Potential as an Aircraft Mechanic

If you're interested in maximizing your earning potential as an aircraft mechanic, there are several steps you can take. First, consider pursuing additional certifications or specialized training in areas such as avionics or composite repair. Additionally, consider seeking out opportunities to work for high-paying airlines or in-demand areas. Finally, be willing to take on extra responsibilities or work overtime when it is available.

Personal Experience: The Benefits of Working as an Aircraft Mechanic

One of the things I love about working as an aircraft mechanic is that it is a stable, in-demand career. There will always be a need for skilled mechanics to keep planes in top condition, which means that job security is high. Additionally, the work is challenging and interesting, and there is always something new to learn.

Question and Answer:

Q: What education or training is required to become an aircraft mechanic?

A: To become an aircraft mechanic, you typically need to complete a program at a Federal Aviation Administration (FAA)-approved school. These programs can range from several months to several years, depending on the level of certification you are seeking.

Q: Are there any other requirements to become an aircraft mechanic?

A: In addition to completing an FAA-approved program, you must also pass a series of exams and gain a certain amount of work experience before you can become certified as an aircraft mechanic.

Q: How much does it cost to become an aircraft mechanic?

A: The cost of becoming an aircraft mechanic can vary widely depending on the program you choose and your location. However, you can expect to pay several thousand dollars for a quality program.

Q: Are there opportunities for advancement in the aircraft mechanic field?

A: Yes, there are many opportunities for advancement in the aircraft mechanic field. Those who are willing to pursue additional certifications or take on leadership roles may be able to move into management positions or higher-paying roles within their company.

Conclusion of How Much Do Aircraft Mechanics Make Per Year

In conclusion, if you are interested in becoming an aircraft mechanic, you can expect to earn a median annual salary of $64,090 as of May 2020. However, your earning potential can vary widely depending on factors such as your level of experience, the type of aircraft you work on, and your location. By pursuing additional certifications, seeking out high-paying opportunities, and being willing to take on extra responsibilities, you can maximize your earning potential in this exciting and rewarding career field.
