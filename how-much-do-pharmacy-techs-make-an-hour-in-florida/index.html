How Much Do Pharmacy Techs Make An Hour In Florida

Written by Luffy Sep 09, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you considering becoming a pharmacy technician in Florida? One of the biggest concerns for anyone entering a new profession is how much they can expect to earn. In this blog post, we'll explore the average hourly wage for pharmacy techs in Florida and what factors can impact your earning potential.

Working as a pharmacy tech can be a rewarding career choice, but it's important to understand the potential challenges and limitations of the job. One of the most significant pain points for pharmacy techs is the relatively low pay compared to other healthcare professions. However, with the right training and experience, you can increase your earning potential and build a successful career in this field.

The average hourly wage for pharmacy techs in Florida is $15.00 per hour. However, this can vary widely depending on several factors, such as your level of experience, the type of pharmacy you work in, and your location within the state. Some pharmacy techs earn as little as $12.00 per hour, while others can earn up to $20.00 per hour or more with additional certifications and training.

In summary, pharmacy techs in Florida can expect to earn an average of $15.00 per hour. However, your earning potential can be impacted by several factors, including your experience level, type of pharmacy, and location within the state.

Factors That Can Impact Your Earning Potential

As mentioned earlier, several factors can impact your earning potential as a pharmacy tech in Florida. One of the most significant factors is your level of experience. Entry-level pharmacy techs typically earn less than those with several years of experience under their belts. Additionally, the type of pharmacy you work in can impact your salary. Retail pharmacies typically pay less than hospital or specialty pharmacies. Finally, your location within the state can also impact your earning potential. Pharmacy techs in larger cities may earn a higher wage than those in rural areas.

Additional Certifications and Training Can Boost Your Salary

If you're looking to increase your earning potential as a pharmacy tech, pursuing additional certifications and training can be a wise investment. Some of the most common certifications include the Certified Pharmacy Technician (CPhT) and the Pharmacy Technician Certification Board (PTCB) certification. These certifications demonstrate your knowledge and expertise in the field and can help you qualify for higher-paying positions.

How to Negotiate a Higher Salary as a Pharmacy Tech

If you're already working as a pharmacy tech and are looking to increase your salary, there are several strategies you can use to negotiate a higher wage. First, do your research and determine what the average salary is for pharmacy techs in your area. Then, come prepared with a list of your accomplishments and contributions to the pharmacy. Finally, be willing to negotiate and consider alternative forms of compensation, such as bonuses or flexible scheduling.
