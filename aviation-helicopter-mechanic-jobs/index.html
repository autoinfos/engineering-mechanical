Aviation Helicopter Mechanic Jobs A Guide To Your Dream Job

Written by Daniel Jun 27, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Have you ever dreamed of working on helicopters and being part of the aviation industry? If so, becoming an aviation helicopter mechanic might be the perfect career path for you. This exciting and fulfilling job allows you to work with advanced technology, travel to different locations, and help keep helicopters safe and reliable for their pilots and passengers.

However, like any job, aviation helicopter mechanic positions come with their own set of challenges. Long hours, demanding deadlines, and the need for precision and attention to detail are all part of the job description. But for those who are passionate about helicopters and aviation, these challenges can be rewarding and worth the effort.

So, what exactly is an aviation helicopter mechanic? In short, an aviation helicopter mechanic is responsible for maintaining and repairing helicopters to ensure they are safe and fully operational. This includes performing routine maintenance checks, identifying and fixing any mechanical issues, and installing new equipment or parts as needed.

In this article, we will discuss the ins and outs of aviation helicopter mechanic jobs, including the skills and education needed, the job outlook and salary expectations, and what a typical day on the job looks like.

Skills and Education Required for Aviation Helicopter Mechanic Jobs

While formal education is not always required to become an aviation helicopter mechanic, it is highly recommended. Many employers prefer applicants who have completed a certification program or degree in aviation maintenance technology or a related field. These programs typically include both classroom instruction and hands-on training in areas such as aircraft electrical systems, avionics, and maintenance practices.

In addition to formal education, aviation helicopter mechanics must possess a variety of skills and qualities to be successful in their job. These include:

  • Attention to detail and precision
  • Strong problem-solving and critical thinking skills
  • Ability to work well under pressure and meet tight deadlines
  • Excellent communication and teamwork skills
  • Physical dexterity and hand-eye coordination

Job Outlook and Salary Expectations

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the employment of aircraft and avionics equipment mechanics and technicians, including helicopter mechanics, is projected to grow 5 percent from 2019 to 2029, about as fast as the average for all occupations. This growth is due in part to the increasing demand for air travel and the need for more helicopters for various purposes, including medical transport, law enforcement, and military operations.

The median annual wage for aircraft and avionics equipment mechanics and technicians was $64,090 in May 2020, with the highest 10 percent earning more than $98,960. However, salaries can vary depending on the employer, location, and level of experience.

Typical Day on the Job

A typical day for an aviation helicopter mechanic can be varied and unpredictable. Depending on the employer and the type of helicopter being serviced, a mechanic may be responsible for performing routine inspections, troubleshooting and diagnosing mechanical issues, or conducting major repairs or overhauls.

Other tasks may include ordering and stocking parts, maintaining maintenance records, and communicating with pilots and other aviation professionals. Working conditions can also vary, with some mechanics working outside in all weather conditions and others working in climate-controlled hangars or repair facilities.

How to Become an Aviation Helicopter Mechanic

If you are interested in pursuing a career as an aviation helicopter mechanic, there are several steps you can take to get started. These include:

  • Research aviation maintenance technology programs and schools in your area
  • Complete a certification program or degree in aviation maintenance technology or a related field
  • Gain hands-on experience through internships or entry-level positions in the aviation industry
  • Obtain certification from the Federal Aviation Administration (FAA) by passing a series of exams and meeting other requirements

Conclusion of Aviation Helicopter Mechanic Jobs

Aviation helicopter mechanic jobs offer an exciting and challenging career path for those who are passionate about helicopters and aviation. While the job comes with its own set of challenges, the rewards can be great, including a fulfilling and rewarding career, the opportunity to work with advanced technology, and the chance to travel to different locations. By pursuing formal education, gaining hands-on experience, and obtaining certification from the FAA, you can pave the way to a successful career in aviation maintenance.
