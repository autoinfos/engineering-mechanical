How Much Do Aircraft Technicians Make

Written by Robby Nov 11, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Have you ever wondered how much an aircraft technician makes? If you're considering a career in aviation, it's essential to know what to expect in terms of compensation. In this blog post, we'll discuss how much aircraft technicians make, the factors that affect their salaries, and the benefits of pursuing a career in aviation maintenance.

For many people, salary is the most crucial factor when choosing a career. Aircraft technicians are no exception. However, determining how much they make can be challenging, as it varies depending on several factors, such as experience, education, geographical location, and the type of aircraft they work on.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for aircraft and avionics equipment mechanics and technicians was $66,680 in May 2020. The lowest 10 percent earned less than $41,440, and the highest 10 percent earned more than $100,930. The highest-paying industries for aircraft technicians were aerospace product and parts manufacturing, scheduled air transportation, and the federal government.

In conclusion, aircraft technicians can earn a good salary, but it depends on several factors. If you're considering a career in aviation maintenance, it's essential to research the industry's average salaries in your area and factor in the cost of living.

Factors That Affect Aircraft Technicians' Salaries

As mentioned earlier, several factors can affect how much an aircraft technician makes. Here are some of the most significant ones:

Experience

The more experience an aircraft technician has, the more they can expect to earn. Experienced technicians are more efficient and can diagnose and repair complex issues faster, making them more valuable to employers.

Education

Education is also a crucial factor in determining an aircraft technician's salary. Those with a degree or certification from an FAA-approved school may receive higher pay and have more job opportunities than those without one.

Benefits of Pursuing a Career in Aviation Maintenance

Aside from the potential for a good salary, pursuing a career in aviation maintenance has several other benefits. First, the demand for skilled aircraft technicians is expected to grow in the coming years, which means job security for those in the industry. Additionally, aircraft technicians work in a challenging and exciting field, where they can learn new skills and work with cutting-edge technology.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the average hourly rate for aircraft technicians?
A: The average hourly rate for aircraft technicians is $32.06, according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics.

Q: What type of aircraft do aircraft technicians work on?
A: Aircraft technicians work on all types of aircraft, from small private planes to large commercial airliners.

Q: What are the working conditions like for aircraft technicians?
A: Aircraft technicians work in a variety of settings, including hangars, repair stations, and airports. The work can be physically demanding and may require working in tight spaces or at heights.

Q: Is there room for advancement in the aviation maintenance field?
A: Yes, experienced aircraft technicians can advance to supervisory or management positions. Additionally, some may choose to specialize in a particular area of aviation maintenance, such as avionics or structural repair.

Conclusion of How Much Do Aircraft Technicians Make

In conclusion, aircraft technicians can expect to earn a good salary, but it depends on several factors, such as experience, education, and geographical location. Pursuing a career in aviation maintenance has several benefits, including job security, opportunities for advancement, and exposure to cutting-edge technology. If you're considering a career in aviation maintenance, do your research, and find an FAA-approved school that can help you achieve your goals.
