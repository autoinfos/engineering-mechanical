How Much Do Elevator Mechanics Make In Florida

Written by William May 27, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Have you ever wondered how much elevator mechanics make in Florida? Are you considering a career in this field and want to know if it's worth pursuing? Elevator mechanics play an important role in maintaining and repairing elevators, escalators, and other lifting devices. They ensure that these machines are safe and reliable for people to use on a daily basis.

However, being an elevator mechanic can be a challenging and physically demanding job. It requires specialized training and knowledge, as well as the ability to work in confined spaces and at heights. In addition, the job can be unpredictable as emergency repairs and maintenance needs may arise at any time.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual salary for elevator mechanics in Florida is $75,110 as of May 2020. This is slightly higher than the national median salary for this occupation, which is $79,780 per year. Entry-level positions typically pay around $43,000 per year, while experienced professionals can earn up to $107,000 per year or more.

Personal Experience with Elevator Mechanics Salary in Florida

My cousin has been working as an elevator mechanic in Florida for the past five years. He started out as an apprentice and worked his way up to a senior mechanic. According to him, the salary for this job is quite good, especially considering that it doesn't require a college degree. He also mentioned that the job offers good benefits and opportunities for advancement.

Factors that Affect Elevator Mechanics Salary in Florida

Several factors can affect how much elevator mechanics make in Florida. These include:

Experience

The more experience an elevator mechanic has, the higher their salary is likely to be. Experienced mechanics are often assigned more complex and challenging repair and maintenance tasks, which can lead to higher pay.

Location

The location of the job can also impact an elevator mechanic's salary. Large urban areas tend to offer higher salaries due to the higher cost of living and demand for skilled workers.

Training and Education

In order to become an elevator mechanic, you typically need to complete an apprenticeship program or vocational training. These programs can last anywhere from three to five years and involve a combination of classroom instruction and on-the-job training. The more training and education you have, the more you can earn in this field.

Union Membership

Many elevator mechanics are members of a union, which can offer better pay and benefits than non-union positions. Union members may also have access to additional training and job opportunities.

Conclusion of How Much Do Elevator Mechanics Make in Florida

Overall, being an elevator mechanic in Florida can be a lucrative and rewarding career choice. With the right training and experience, you can earn a good salary and enjoy job security in a growing field. However, it's important to consider the physical demands and potential risks of this job before pursuing it as a career.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the average hourly rate for elevator mechanics in Florida?

A: The average hourly rate for elevator mechanics in Florida is $36.14 per hour.

Q: What industries employ the most elevator mechanics in Florida?

A: Most elevator mechanics in Florida work in the building equipment contractors industry, followed by government agencies and the manufacturing sector.

Q: Are there any certifications or licenses required to become an elevator mechanic in Florida?

A: Yes, elevator mechanics in Florida are required to have a license from the state. In addition, some employers may require certification from organizations such as the National Elevator Industry Educational Program (NEIEP) or the International Union of Elevator Constructors (IUEC).

Q: How does the job outlook for elevator mechanics in Florida compare to other states?

A: The job outlook for elevator mechanics in Florida is positive, with an expected growth rate of 7.6% between 2019 and 2029. This is slightly lower than the national growth rate of 7.9% for this occupation.
