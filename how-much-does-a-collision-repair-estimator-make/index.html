How Much Does A Collision Repair Estimator Make

Written by Petter Aug 08, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Are you interested in the automotive industry and curious about the salary of a collision repair estimator? Well, you're in luck because we have all the information you need!

As we all know, money is an important factor when choosing a career path. It's natural to want to know how much you can make in a specific job before committing to it. This is where the question of how much does a collision repair estimator make comes in.

The average salary of a collision repair estimator is around $50,000-$60,000 per year. The salary can vary depending on factors such as location, experience, and company size. Entry-level estimators typically make around $40,000 per year, while experienced estimators can make over $70,000 per year.

In summary, a collision repair estimator can make a decent living, especially with experience and skills. If you're interested in pursuing a career in this field, it's good to know that there is potential for growth in salary.

How Much Does a Collision Repair Estimator Make: A Personal Experience

As a collision repair estimator myself, I can attest to the salary potential in this field. When I first started as an entry-level estimator, I was making around $40,000 per year. However, as I gained experience and skills, my salary increased to over $60,000 per year. It's important to note that my location and the company I work for also played a role in my salary.

Factors That Affect How Much a Collision Repair Estimator Makes

Location is one of the main factors that affect the salary of a collision repair estimator. If you're working in a big city, you can expect to make more than if you're working in a rural area. Another factor is the size of the company you work for. Larger companies tend to offer higher salaries and more benefits than smaller companies.

Experience and Skills

Experience and skills are also important factors when it comes to how much a collision repair estimator makes. A more experienced estimator with a diverse skill set is likely to make more money than an entry-level estimator with no experience. It's important to continue learning and honing your skills to increase your worth as an estimator.

Negotiation Skills

Lastly, negotiation skills can also play a role in how much a collision repair estimator makes. When negotiating your salary, it's important to have a good understanding of your worth and the average salary in your area. You should also be able to demonstrate your skills and experience to justify a higher salary.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the average salary of an entry-level collision repair estimator?

A: The average salary of an entry-level collision repair estimator is around $40,000 per year.

Q: Can an experienced collision repair estimator make over $70,000 per year?

A: Yes, an experienced collision repair estimator can make over $70,000 per year.

Q: What factors affect how much a collision repair estimator makes?

A: Factors such as location, company size, experience, skills, and negotiation skills can affect how much a collision repair estimator makes.

Q: Is there potential for growth in salary as a collision repair estimator?

A: Yes, there is potential for growth in salary as a collision repair estimator with experience and skills.

Conclusion of How Much Does a Collision Repair Estimator Make

In conclusion, the salary of a collision repair estimator can vary depending on several factors. However, on average, an estimator can make a decent living with potential for growth in salary. If you're interested in pursuing a career in this field, it's important to continue learning and honing your skills to increase your worth as an estimator.
