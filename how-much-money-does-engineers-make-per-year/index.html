How Much Money Does Engineers Make Per Year

Written by Oliver Nov 16, 2023 · 3 min read

Are you wondering how much money engineers make per year? It's a common question, especially for those considering a career in engineering or those already in the field who want to know if they're being paid fairly. In this blog post, we'll explore the average salaries of engineers, their pain points, and what factors affect their income.

Engineering can be a lucrative career, but it also involves a lot of hard work and dedication. Engineers often have to work long hours, meet tight deadlines, and constantly update their skills to stay competitive. Despite these challenges, many engineers find the work rewarding and the pay worth the effort.

The salary of an engineer can vary widely depending on their industry, experience, and location. According to the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for all engineers in May 2020 was $81,440. The lowest 10 percent earned less than $51,250, while the highest 10 percent earned more than $138,880.

In general, engineers who work in industries such as oil and gas extraction, computer and electronic product manufacturing, and aerospace product and parts manufacturing tend to have higher salaries. Engineers who have advanced degrees or certifications also often earn more than those who only have a bachelor's degree.

My Experience as an Engineer

As an electrical engineer with 10 years of experience, I can say that the industry has been rewarding both financially and intellectually. My starting salary was around $60,000 per year, which has now increased to $100,000 per year. However, the journey to reach this point was not easy.

My first job involved working long hours and dealing with tight deadlines. I had to constantly update my skills and knowledge to stay competitive. Although it was challenging, I found the work interesting and fulfilling, which made the effort worth it. Over time, my experience and expertise grew, and so did my salary.

Factors that Affect Engineer Salaries

Several factors can affect an engineer's salary. Here are some of the most important:

Industry

The industry an engineer works in can have a big impact on their salary. Some industries, like oil and gas extraction or aerospace product and parts manufacturing, tend to pay more than others.

Education and Certification

Engineers who have advanced degrees or certifications often earn more than those who only have a bachelor's degree. Continuing education is also important to stay competitive in the field.

Conclusion of How Much Money Does Engineers Make Per Year

Overall, engineering can be a rewarding and lucrative career. While the pay can vary depending on several factors, the median annual wage for all engineers is around $81,440. With hard work and dedication, engineers can continue to grow their expertise and increase their salary over time.

Question and Answer

Q: How much does a mechanical engineer make per year?

A: The median annual wage for mechanical engineers was $88,430 in May 2020.

Q: Do engineers in the tech industry make more money?

A: Yes, engineers who work in the computer and electronic product manufacturing industry tend to have higher salaries.

Q: How important is continuing education for engineers?

A: Continuing education is important for engineers to stay competitive in the field and to increase their earning potential.

Q: Do engineers with advanced degrees earn more money?

A: Yes, engineers with advanced degrees or certifications often earn more than those who only have a bachelor's degree.
