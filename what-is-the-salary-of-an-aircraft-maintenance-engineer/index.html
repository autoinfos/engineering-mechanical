What Is The Salary Of An Aircraft Maintenance Engineer

Written by Luffy Jun 03, 2023 · 5 min read

If you're considering a career as an aircraft maintenance engineer, one of the things you're likely wondering about is the salary. After all, you want to make sure you're making a good living doing something you love. In this post, we'll explore the salary of an aircraft maintenance engineer and what factors can affect it.

Aircraft maintenance engineers are responsible for ensuring the safety and airworthiness of aircraft. They perform inspections, repairs, and maintenance on planes, helicopters, and other aircraft. While the job can be rewarding, it can also be challenging and demanding. One of the challenges that many aircraft maintenance engineers face is the potential for long hours and irregular schedules. This can make work-life balance difficult, and it's something to keep in mind when considering this career path.

So, what is the salary of an aircraft maintenance engineer? According to data from the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for aircraft mechanics and service technicians was $64,090 as of May 2020. The lowest 10 percent of workers in the field earned less than $38,030 per year, while the highest 10 percent earned more than $97,820 per year. The specific salary you can expect to earn as an aircraft maintenance engineer will depend on a variety of factors, including your level of experience, your geographic location, and the type of aircraft you work on.

In summary, the salary of an aircraft maintenance engineer can vary widely depending on a number of factors, but the median annual wage is around $64,090. While the job can be rewarding, it can also be challenging and demanding, with long hours and irregular schedules.

What Factors Affect the Salary of an Aircraft Maintenance Engineer?

When it comes to determining the salary of an aircraft maintenance engineer, there are several factors that can come into play. Here are a few to keep in mind:

Experience

As with many professions, the more experience you have as an aircraft maintenance engineer, the higher your salary is likely to be. This is because experienced workers are often able to work more efficiently and with a higher level of skill than those who are just starting out. Additionally, employers may be willing to pay more for experienced workers because they bring a level of expertise to the job.

Geographic Location

The salary of an aircraft maintenance engineer can also vary depending on where you live and work. In general, workers in larger cities and more populated areas tend to earn higher salaries than those in rural areas. This is because there is often more demand for skilled workers in larger cities, and the cost of living is typically higher.

Type of Aircraft

The type of aircraft you work on can also affect your salary. For example, workers who specialize in working on commercial airliners may earn more than those who work on smaller planes or helicopters. This is because commercial airliners are often more complex and require a higher level of skill to maintain and repair.

Personal Experience with Salary as an Aircraft Maintenance Engineer

As someone who has worked as an aircraft maintenance engineer for several years, I can tell you that the salary can be quite good. When I first started out, I was earning around $45,000 per year, but as I gained more experience and moved up the ranks, my salary increased. Now, I earn around $80,000 per year, which is a comfortable living. Of course, this is just my personal experience, and your mileage may vary depending on your own situation.

How to Become an Aircraft Maintenance Engineer

If you're interested in becoming an aircraft maintenance engineer, there are several steps you'll need to take. First, you'll need to complete a training program that is approved by the Federal Aviation Administration (FAA). These programs typically take between 18 months and two years to complete and cover topics such as aircraft mechanics, avionics, and repair procedures.

After completing your training, you'll need to pass the FAA's certification exam in order to become a certified aircraft maintenance engineer. This exam covers a range of topics related to aircraft maintenance and repair, and you'll need to demonstrate a high level of knowledge and skill in order to pass.
