How Much Do Electrical Mechanics Make

Written by Robby Jul 14, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you interested in becoming an electrical mechanic? Do you want to know how much money you can make in this field? If so, you're in the right place! In this article, we'll explore the average salary for electrical mechanics and explain what factors can impact your earning potential.

As with any profession, there are some downsides to being an electrical mechanic. For example, the work can be physically demanding and may require you to work long hours or on weekends. Additionally, you may need to complete an apprenticeship or certification program before you can start earning a full-time salary.

The average salary for electrical mechanics varies depending on several factors, including your level of experience, the industry you work in, and the location of your job. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for electrical mechanics was $62,190 as of May 2020. This means that half of all electrical mechanics earned more than this amount, while the other half earned less.

In general, electrical mechanics who work in industries like construction, manufacturing, and utilities tend to earn more than those who work in residential or commercial settings. Additionally, those who have several years of experience or who hold advanced certifications may be able to command higher salaries.

Personal Experience with Electrical Mechanics Salaries

When I was first starting out as an electrical mechanic, I was unsure about what kind of salary I could expect. However, as I gained more experience and moved into more advanced positions, I found that my earning potential increased significantly.

One thing that I learned is that it's important to be proactive in seeking out higher-paying jobs and negotiating for better salaries. For example, when I was offered a job at a new company, I researched the average salary for my position and asked for a higher rate based on my experience and qualifications. By doing this, I was able to secure a higher starting salary and set myself up for future raises and promotions.

Factors that Impact Electrical Mechanics Salaries

As mentioned earlier, there are several factors that can impact how much money you can make as an electrical mechanic. One of the biggest factors is your level of experience. In general, those who are just starting out in the field will earn less than those who have been working for several years.

Another factor that can impact your salary is the industry you work in. For example, those who work in the utilities sector tend to earn more than those who work in residential settings. Additionally, those who hold advanced certifications or who specialize in certain areas may be able to earn higher salaries.

Education and Certifications

Education and certifications are also important factors to consider when it comes to electrical mechanics salaries. Those who have completed an apprenticeship or vocational training program may be able to command higher salaries than those who have only completed on-the-job training.

Additionally, those who hold certifications like the National Electrical Code (NEC) certification or the International Society of Automation (ISA) certification may be able to earn more than those who do not have these credentials.

Negotiating Your Salary as an Electrical Mechanic

Finally, it's important to note that you have some control over your salary as an electrical mechanic. By negotiating for better pay, seeking out higher-paying jobs, and investing in your education and certifications, you can increase your earning potential and set yourself up for long-term success in this field.

Conclusion of How Much Do Electrical Mechanics Make

So, how much do electrical mechanics make? The answer is that it varies depending on several factors, including your level of experience, the industry you work in, and the location of your job. However, with dedication and hard work, you can increase your earning potential and build a successful career in this field.
