How To Become A Certified Mechanical Engineer A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Sep 03, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you interested in becoming a certified mechanical engineer, but don't know where to start? The road to certification can seem daunting, but with the right guidance, you can achieve your goals and launch a fulfilling career in this exciting field.

One of the biggest pain points for aspiring mechanical engineers is understanding the qualifications and requirements needed to become certified. Additionally, many individuals may not know where to find the resources and support necessary to succeed in their pursuit of certification.

To become a certified mechanical engineer, you must first obtain a bachelor's degree in mechanical engineering or a related field. You will also need to pass the Fundamentals of Engineering (FE) exam, complete four years of work experience, and pass the Principles and Practice of Engineering (PE) exam. Once you have completed these requirements, you will be eligible to receive your certification.

In summary, the steps to becoming a certified mechanical engineer include obtaining a bachelor's degree, passing the FE exam, completing four years of work experience, and passing the PE exam. It is also important to seek out resources and support throughout your journey, such as mentorship and professional organizations.

Getting a Bachelor's Degree in Mechanical Engineering

When pursuing a career in mechanical engineering, the first step is obtaining a bachelor's degree. This typically involves completing a four-year program in mechanical engineering or a related field, such as aerospace or civil engineering. During your studies, you will learn about mechanics, thermodynamics, materials science, and other key concepts related to mechanical engineering.

Personally, my journey towards becoming a certified mechanical engineer began when I enrolled in a mechanical engineering program at my local university. I was fascinated by the field and eager to learn more about how machines and systems work.

Passing the FE Exam

The next step in becoming a certified mechanical engineer is passing the Fundamentals of Engineering (FE) exam. This exam tests your knowledge of fundamental engineering concepts and is typically taken during your senior year of college or shortly after graduation.

When I took the FE exam, I found it to be challenging but manageable with the right preparation. I recommend utilizing study materials and practice exams to ensure that you are fully prepared for the exam.

Completing Work Experience

After passing the FE exam, you must complete four years of work experience under the supervision of a licensed professional engineer. This experience should be in a field related to mechanical engineering, and you must document your work experience and obtain references from your supervisor.

During my four years of work experience, I had the opportunity to work on a wide range of projects and gain valuable hands-on experience in the field. I also found it helpful to seek out mentorship from experienced engineers who could offer guidance and advice throughout my journey.

Passing the PE Exam

The final step in becoming a certified mechanical engineer is passing the Principles and Practice of Engineering (PE) exam. This exam tests your knowledge of mechanical engineering principles and your ability to apply these principles in real-world scenarios.

When I took the PE exam, I found it to be challenging but rewarding. Passing the exam was a major accomplishment and a testament to the hard work and dedication that I had put into my career.

Question and Answer

Q: What resources are available to help me study for the FE and PE exams?

A: There are a variety of study materials and resources available, such as review courses, practice exams, and study guides. Additionally, many professional organizations offer resources and support to aspiring engineers.

Q: How important is work experience in becoming a certified mechanical engineer?

A: Work experience is a crucial component of becoming certified, as it allows you to apply your knowledge in a real-world setting and gain valuable hands-on experience in the field.

Q: Are there any professional organizations that can support me in my pursuit of certification?

A: Yes, there are many professional organizations that offer resources and support to aspiring engineers, such as the American Society of Mechanical Engineers (ASME) and the National Society of Professional Engineers (NSPE).

Q: What are the benefits of becoming a certified mechanical engineer?

A: Becoming certified can open up new job opportunities, increase your earning potential, and demonstrate your expertise and commitment to the field of mechanical engineering.

Conclusion

While the journey to becoming a certified mechanical engineer can seem daunting, with the right preparation and support, you can achieve your goals and launch a successful career in this exciting field. By obtaining a bachelor's degree, passing the FE and PE exams, completing work experience, and seeking out mentorship and professional organizations, you can become a certified mechanical engineer and make a meaningful impact in the world of engineering.
