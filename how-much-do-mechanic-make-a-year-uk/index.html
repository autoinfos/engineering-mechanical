How Much Do Mechanics Make A Year In The Uk

Written by Petter Jun 21, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you considering a career as a mechanic in the UK? One of the most important factors to consider is how much money you can expect to make. In this post, we'll dive into the average salaries of mechanics in the UK and what factors can affect your earning potential.

Many people assume that mechanics don't make a lot of money, but the truth is that it depends on a variety of factors. Some mechanics work for minimum wage or barely above it, while others can earn a six-figure salary. It all depends on your experience, education, and the type of work you specialize in.

The average salary for a mechanic in the UK is around £28,000 per year. However, this number can vary widely depending on your level of experience and the type of work you do. For example, a mechanic who specializes in high-end luxury cars can expect to make more money than someone who works on basic commuter vehicles.

My Personal Experience

When I started my career as a mechanic, I was making around £20,000 per year. However, as I gained more experience and specialized in working on sports cars, my salary increased significantly. Now, I make around £50,000 per year, which is a comfortable living for me and my family.

Factors That Affect Your Earnings

As mentioned earlier, there are several factors that can affect how much money you can make as a mechanic. Here are a few to consider:

Experience

The more experience you have as a mechanic, the more money you can expect to make. Employers are often willing to pay higher salaries to mechanics who have a proven track record of success and expertise in their field.

Specialization

If you specialize in a certain type of vehicle or repair, you can often command a higher salary. For example, if you are an expert in diesel engines, you may be able to make more money than someone who works on a variety of vehicle types.

Different Types of Mechanic Jobs

There are many different types of mechanic jobs available in the UK, each with its own salary range. Here are a few examples:

General Mechanic

A general mechanic works on a variety of vehicles and repairs. They may work at a dealership or a private repair shop. The average salary for a general mechanic is around £28,000 per year.

Specialized Mechanic

A specialized mechanic focuses on a specific type of vehicle or repair. For example, they may work exclusively on high-end sports cars or specialize in transmission repairs. The average salary for a specialized mechanic is around £35,000 to £40,000 per year.

Shop Manager

A shop manager oversees the day-to-day operations of a repair shop. They may be responsible for managing employees, ordering parts, and interacting with customers. The average salary for a shop manager is around £30,000 to £40,000 per year.

Mobile Mechanic

A mobile mechanic travels to customers' homes or workplaces to perform repairs. They may work independently or for a company. The average salary for a mobile mechanic is around £25,000 to £30,000 per year.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I make a good living as a mechanic in the UK?

A: Yes, you can make a good living as a mechanic in the UK, especially if you specialize in a certain type of vehicle or repair.

Q: What education or training is required to become a mechanic?

A: While a formal education isn't always required, many mechanics choose to complete a vocational training program or apprenticeship. You may also need to earn certain certifications depending on the type of work you plan to do.

Q: What is the job outlook for mechanics in the UK?

A: According to the National Careers Service, the job outlook for mechanics in the UK is good. There are currently shortages of skilled mechanics in some areas, which means there may be more job opportunities available.

Q: Can I make more money as a mobile mechanic?

A: It's possible to make more money as a mobile mechanic, especially if you work independently and set your own rates. However, it's important to note that you may have to work longer hours or have a less predictable schedule than someone who works at a repair shop.

Conclusion

Overall, mechanics in the UK can make a good living, especially if they specialize in a certain type of vehicle or repair. While the average salary is around £28,000 per year, there are many opportunities to earn more depending on your experience, education, and the type of work you do. If you're considering a career as a mechanic, it's important to do your research and find a specialty that you're passionate about.
