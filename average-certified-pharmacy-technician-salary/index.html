The Average Certified Pharmacy Technician Salary What You Need To Know

Written by Luffy Jul 21, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you considering becoming a certified pharmacy technician but wondering about the average salary? Or are you a current pharmacy technician looking to see if you're being paid fairly? Whatever your situation, understanding the average certified pharmacy technician salary is important for anyone in this field.

As a pharmacy technician, you may experience some pain points related to salary. For example, you may feel like you're not being paid enough for the work you do, or you may see others in the field making more money than you. Additionally, the cost of living in your area may be high, making it difficult to make ends meet on your current salary.

So, what is the average certified pharmacy technician salary? According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual salary for pharmacy technicians in 2020 was $34,890, which breaks down to about $16.79 per hour. However, it's important to note that this can vary based on factors such as location, experience, and education level.

In summary, the average certified pharmacy technician salary is around $34,890 per year, or $16.79 per hour. Keep in mind that this can vary based on several factors, but it's a good benchmark to use when considering a career in this field or negotiating your salary.

What to Expect from an Average Certified Pharmacy Technician Salary

When I first started working as a certified pharmacy technician, I was excited about the job but concerned about the salary. However, I was pleasantly surprised to find that the average certified pharmacy technician salary was higher than I expected.

Of course, this can vary based on where you live and work. In some areas, pharmacy technicians may make more or less than the national average. Additionally, certain factors such as experience and education level can impact your earning potential.

To make the most of your salary as a certified pharmacy technician, it's important to stay up-to-date on industry trends and regulations. This can help you identify opportunities for career advancement or negotiate a higher salary based on your skills and knowledge.

How to Negotiate Your Salary as a Certified Pharmacy Technician

When it comes to negotiating your salary as a certified pharmacy technician, there are a few things to keep in mind. First, do your research to understand what the average certified pharmacy technician salary is in your area. This can help you determine what a fair salary would be based on your experience and education level.

Next, be prepared to make a case for why you deserve a higher salary. This could involve highlighting your skills and knowledge or discussing any additional responsibilities you've taken on since starting your job. Additionally, be open to negotiation and consider factors such as benefits and vacation time in addition to salary.

Factors That Affect the Average Certified Pharmacy Technician Salary

As mentioned earlier, several factors can impact the average certified pharmacy technician salary. Here are a few to keep in mind:

  • Location: Pharmacy technicians in certain areas may make more or less than the national average based on the cost of living in their area.
  • Experience: As with any job, more experience can lead to a higher salary.
  • Education: Pharmacy technicians with additional education or certifications may be able to command a higher salary.
  • Industry: Pharmacy technicians working in certain industries, such as hospitals or research facilities, may make more than those in retail settings.

How to Increase Your Average Certified Pharmacy Technician Salary

If you're looking to increase your average certified pharmacy technician salary, there are several steps you can take. Consider pursuing additional education or certifications, which can help you stand out in the field and command a higher salary.

You may also want to consider switching to a different industry or location, as this can impact your earning potential. Additionally, look for opportunities to take on additional responsibilities or leadership roles within your current job, which can also lead to a higher salary.

Conclusion of Average Certified Pharmacy Technician Salary

Understanding the average certified pharmacy technician salary is important for anyone considering or currently working in this field.
