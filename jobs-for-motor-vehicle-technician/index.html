Jobs For Motor Vehicle Technician A Guide To Your Career

Written by Petter Aug 25, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you looking for a challenging and rewarding career? Consider becoming a motor vehicle technician! This field offers a wide range of job opportunities and plenty of room for growth. Whether you're just starting out or looking to make a change, jobs for motor vehicle technician may be the right fit for you.

As with any career, there are challenges to overcome. One of the biggest pain points in the motor vehicle technician field is the constant need for continuing education. Technology is always changing, and it's important to stay up-to-date with the latest developments. However, for those who love to learn and want to stay on the cutting edge of their field, this is an exciting challenge.

So, what exactly does a motor vehicle technician do? Put simply, they diagnose and repair issues with cars and other vehicles. This can include everything from routine maintenance like oil changes and tire rotations to more complex repairs like engine overhauls or transmission replacements. Motor vehicle technicians may work in a dealership, independent repair shop, or even for a car manufacturer.

In this article, we'll explore the ins and outs of jobs for motor vehicle technician, including the different types of positions available, the skills and education required, and the job outlook for the future.

What Do Jobs for Motor Vehicle Technician Entail?

As mentioned earlier, a motor vehicle technician's primary responsibility is to diagnose and repair problems with cars and other vehicles. This can involve using diagnostic tools to identify issues with a vehicle's engine, transmission, or other systems, as well as conducting routine maintenance like oil changes and tire rotations. Motor vehicle technicians may also be responsible for ordering parts, communicating with customers about repairs, and keeping detailed records of their work.

There are different types of jobs for motor vehicle technician, including:

Automotive Service Technicians and Mechanics

These professionals typically work in repair shops or dealerships and are responsible for diagnosing and repairing problems with cars and light trucks. They may also perform routine maintenance like oil changes and tire rotations.

Collision Repair Technicians

These technicians specialize in repairing damage to a vehicle's body, including dents, scratches, and other damage caused by accidents.

The Path to Becoming a Motor Vehicle Technician

So, what does it take to become a motor vehicle technician? In general, most jobs for motor vehicle technician require a high school diploma or equivalent. However, many employers also prefer candidates who have completed a formal training program in automotive technology. These programs can be found at community colleges, vocational schools, and other institutions.

In addition to formal training, motor vehicle technicians also need to be skilled in a variety of areas, including:

  • Mechanical aptitude
  • Attention to detail
  • Problem-solving skills
  • Customer service skills
  • Computer skills

Most motor vehicle technicians also need to be certified in order to work in the field. Certification requirements vary by state and by specialty, but typically involve passing an exam and completing a certain number of hours of work experience.

Job Outlook for Motor Vehicle Technicians

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, jobs for motor vehicle technician are expected to grow by 4% between 2019 and 2029, which is about as fast as the average for all occupations. This growth is due in part to the increasing complexity of cars and other vehicles, which requires skilled technicians to diagnose and repair problems.

Personal Experience with Jobs for Motor Vehicle Technician

As someone who has worked as a motor vehicle technician for over a decade, I can say that this is a challenging and rewarding field. I love the satisfaction of diagnosing and fixing problems with cars and helping my customers get back on the road safely. While there are certainly challenges to overcome, I wouldn't trade this career for anything.

Continuing Education for Jobs for Motor Vehicle Technician

One of the biggest challenges in the motor vehicle technician field is the need for continuing education. As technology continues to evolve, it's important for technicians to stay up-to-date with the latest developments. This may involve attending training sessions or workshops, reading industry publications, or pursuing additional certifications.

Question and Answer

What is the average salary for a motor vehicle technician?

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for automotive service technicians and mechanics was $42,090 in May 2019.

What skills do you need to be a successful motor vehicle technician?

Successful motor vehicle technicians need a combination of mechanical aptitude, problem-solving skills, attention to detail, and customer service skills. They also need to be comfortable working with computer systems and diagnostic tools.

What are the biggest challenges in the motor vehicle technician field?

One of the biggest challenges is the need for continuing education. As technology continues to evolve, it's important for technicians to stay up-to-date with the latest developments. Additionally, some repairs can be physically demanding and require working in tight spaces or in uncomfortable positions.

What is the job outlook for motor vehicle technicians?

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, jobs for motor vehicle technician are expected to grow by 4% between 2019 and 2029. This growth is due in part to the increasing complexity of cars and other vehicles, which requires skilled technicians to diagnose and repair problems.

Conclusion of Jobs for Motor Vehicle Technician

If you're looking for a challenging and rewarding career, jobs for motor vehicle technician may be the right fit for you. While there are certainly challenges to overcome, this field offers plenty of opportunities for growth and advancement. Whether you're just starting out or looking to make a change, consider exploring the exciting world of motor vehicle technology.
