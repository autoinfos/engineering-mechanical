Living In Dubai With A 7000 Aed Salary Is It Possible

Written by Daniel Aug 22, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Living in Dubai can be an exciting prospect, with its modern architecture, beautiful beaches, and vibrant culture. However, with a salary of 7000 AED, many people wonder whether it is possible to enjoy all that Dubai has to offer without breaking the bank.

With the cost of living in Dubai being relatively high, it can be challenging to make ends meet on a 7000 AED salary. Rent, food, transportation, and entertainment can all add up, leaving little room for savings or luxury expenses.

Despite the challenges, it is possible to live comfortably on a 7000 AED salary in Dubai. With careful budgeting, smart spending, and a bit of creativity, you can still enjoy the city's many pleasures without overspending.

To make the most of your 7000 AED salary in Dubai, it is important to prioritize your expenses. Rent is likely to be your biggest expense, so consider sharing an apartment or living in a less expensive area. Food can also be costly, so try cooking at home instead of dining out, and take advantage of local markets and discount stores for groceries.

Personal Experience: Making 7000 AED Work in Dubai

When I first moved to Dubai with a 7000 AED salary, I was worried about whether I would be able to afford everything I wanted to do. However, by being mindful of my expenses and prioritizing my spending, I was able to make it work.

I decided to share an apartment with a friend, which helped to keep my living expenses low. I also made a habit of cooking at home and bringing my lunch to work, which saved me a lot of money on food. By using public transportation instead of taxis or renting a car, I was able to save even more.

Despite these measures, I still had enough money left over to enjoy some of the city's many attractions, such as visiting the Burj Khalifa and taking a desert safari. By being smart with my spending, I was able to make the most of my 7000 AED salary in Dubai.

Tips for Making 7000 AED Work in Dubai

Here are some tips for making the most of your 7000 AED salary in Dubai:

1. Share accommodation

Sharing an apartment or room with someone can help to lower your living expenses significantly. Consider finding a roommate or joining a flat-sharing community to save money on rent.

2. Cook at home

Eating out in Dubai can be expensive, so try cooking at home instead. Not only is it cheaper, but it can also be healthier and more enjoyable. Take advantage of local markets and discount stores for groceries.

Personal Experience: Entertainment on a Budget

One of the biggest challenges of living in Dubai on a tight budget is finding affordable entertainment options. However, there are plenty of things to do in Dubai that won't break the bank.

For example, some of the city's best beaches and parks are free to visit. You can also explore local markets and museums, which often have little or no admission fee. Many of the city's malls also offer free events and entertainment, such as live music and cultural performances.

FAQs

Q: Is it possible to save money on a 7000 AED salary in Dubai?

A: Yes, it is possible to save money on a 7000 AED salary in Dubai. By being mindful of your expenses, prioritizing your spending, and taking advantage of free and low-cost entertainment options, you can still enjoy the city without overspending.

Q: How much should I budget for rent on a 7000 AED salary in Dubai?

A: Rent can vary widely in Dubai, depending on the area and the type of accommodation. However, as a general rule, you should aim to spend no more than 30% of your salary on rent. This means that on a 7000 AED salary, you should aim to spend no more than 2100 AED per month on rent.

Q: What are some affordable food options in Dubai?

A: There are plenty of affordable food options in Dubai, ranging from traditional Arabic street food to international cuisine. Look for local markets and food trucks for budget-friendly meals, and consider cooking at home to save money.

Q: How can I save money on transportation in Dubai?

A: Public transportation in Dubai is relatively affordable, with buses and metros connecting most areas of the city. Consider purchasing a rechargeable Nol card for discounted fares, and avoid taking taxis or renting a car, which can be expensive.

Conclusion of 7000 AED Salary in Dubai

While living in Dubai on a 7000 AED salary can be challenging, it is possible to make it work with careful budgeting, smart spending, and a bit of creativity. By prioritizing your expenses, taking advantage of free and low-cost entertainment options, and being mindful of your spending, you can still enjoy all that Dubai has to offer without breaking the bank.
