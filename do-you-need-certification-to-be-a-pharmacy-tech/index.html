Do You Need Certification To Be A Pharmacy Tech

Written by Robby Aug 23, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Working as a pharmacy technician can be a fulfilling career. However, if you're considering this path, you may be wondering if you need certification to be a pharmacy tech. The answer is not a simple yes or no, and there are a few factors to consider.

One pain point that many aspiring pharmacy technicians face is the cost and time commitment of obtaining certification. Additionally, some may feel discouraged by the idea of studying for and passing an exam. Others may be concerned about the job market and whether certification is necessary to secure a position.

The short answer to whether you need certification to be a pharmacy tech is no, you do not. However, this does not mean that certification is not beneficial. In fact, many employers prefer or require their pharmacy technicians to be certified.

In summary, while certification is not mandatory, it can be advantageous for your career as a pharmacy tech. It can also provide you with a more comprehensive understanding of the field and help you stand out to potential employers.

Benefits of Certification

Obtaining certification as a pharmacy technician can offer a variety of benefits. For starters, it can increase your job prospects and earning potential. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, certified pharmacy technicians typically earn more than their non-certified counterparts.

Additionally, certification can give you a deeper understanding of pharmacy law and ethics, as well as the technical aspects of the job. This can help you provide better patient care and ensure that you are following all necessary protocols and procedures.

My Experience

As someone who has worked as a pharmacy technician for several years, I highly recommend obtaining certification. While I initially started working without certification, I found that getting certified not only helped me advance in my career but also gave me a greater sense of confidence and knowledge in my work.

Studying for the certification exam can be daunting, but there are many resources available to help you prepare. I found that taking practice exams and attending review courses were especially helpful in my own preparation.

Necessary Requirements for Certification

If you do decide to pursue certification, there are a few requirements that you must meet. These can vary slightly depending on the certification program, but generally, you will need to have a high school diploma or equivalent, complete a pharmacy technician training program, and pass a certification exam.

Exam Details

The certification exam typically covers topics such as pharmacy law, medication safety, and drug classification. It may also include a skills test, which requires you to demonstrate your ability to perform various pharmacy tasks, such as filling prescriptions and compounding medications.

How to Prepare for the Exam

There are many resources available to help you prepare for the certification exam. Some options include taking a review course, purchasing study materials or practice exams, and utilizing online resources such as forums and study groups.

Conclusion

In conclusion, while certification is not necessarily required to work as a pharmacy technician, it can be highly beneficial for both your career and patient care. If you're considering pursuing certification, take advantage of the resources available to you and don't be afraid to ask for help or guidance along the way.
