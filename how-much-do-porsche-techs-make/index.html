How Much Do Porsche Techs Make

Written by Robby Nov 12, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Have you ever wondered how much Porsche technicians make? If you're considering a career as a Porsche technician, or you're just curious about the earning potential in this field, you're in the right place. In this article, we'll explore the salary range for Porsche technicians and discuss what factors can influence their earnings.

Working as a Porsche technician can be a rewarding career, but it's not without its challenges. One of the biggest pain points for technicians is their salary. Many technicians feel that they are not compensated fairly for their skills and expertise, especially considering the high cost of living in many areas.

So, how much do Porsche technicians actually make? According to data from Glassdoor, the average salary for a Porsche technician is around $60,000 per year. However, this can vary depending on a number of factors, such as location, experience, and certification.

In general, Porsche technicians who work in larger cities or more affluent areas tend to earn more than those who work in smaller cities or rural areas. Additionally, technicians who have more experience or who hold advanced certifications may be able to command higher salaries.

Personal Experience with Porsche Technician Salary

As a former Porsche technician myself, I can attest to the fact that salary can be a touchy subject in this field. When I first started out, I was making around $40,000 per year, which was barely enough to make ends meet in the expensive city where I lived.

However, as I gained more experience and became certified in more areas, my salary increased significantly. By the time I left the field, I was making over $80,000 per year, which was a comfortable salary for me and my family.

Factors That Influence Porsche Technician Salary

As previously mentioned, there are a number of factors that can influence how much a Porsche technician makes. Some of the most important factors include:

Location

Technicians who work in larger cities or more affluent areas tend to earn more than those who work in smaller cities or rural areas. This is largely due to the higher cost of living in these areas, which can make it difficult for technicians to make ends meet on lower salaries.

Experience

Technicians who have more experience in the field are generally able to command higher salaries. This is because they have developed more skills and expertise over time, which makes them more valuable to employers.

Training and Certification

Technicians who hold advanced certifications, such as ASE or Porsche-specific certifications, may be able to earn higher salaries than those who do not. This is because these certifications demonstrate a higher level of skill and expertise, which can be valuable to employers.

Conclusion of How Much Do Porsche Techs Make

In conclusion, the salary range for Porsche technicians can vary widely depending on factors such as location, experience, and certification. While some technicians may feel that they are not compensated fairly for their skills and expertise, there is potential for significant earning potential in this field with the right training and experience.
