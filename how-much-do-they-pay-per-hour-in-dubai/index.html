How Much Do They Pay Per Hour In Dubai

Written by Eveline Sep 04, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you planning to move to Dubai and wondering how much you can expect to earn per hour? Or perhaps you are already living in Dubai and want to know if your salary is in line with the average hourly wage. Whatever your situation may be, understanding the pay scale in Dubai can be overwhelming. With various industries, job roles, and levels of experience, it can be challenging to pinpoint exactly how much you can expect to earn per hour.

One of the biggest pain points for job seekers in Dubai is the lack of transparency when it comes to salaries. Employers often do not disclose the salary range for a job, leaving candidates in the dark about what they can expect to earn. This can make it difficult to negotiate a fair salary and can result in employees being underpaid.

The average hourly wage in Dubai varies greatly depending on the industry and job role. According to data from PayScale, the average hourly wage in Dubai is AED 66.05 for all jobs. However, this number can range from AED 18.96 to AED 396.90 depending on the job.

In general, jobs in the hospitality and retail industries tend to have lower hourly wages, while jobs in finance and engineering tend to have higher hourly wages. Additionally, more experienced professionals tend to earn higher hourly wages than entry-level professionals.

My Experience with Hourly Wages in Dubai

When I first moved to Dubai, I was working in the hospitality industry and earning AED 25 per hour. However, as I gained more experience in my role, my hourly wage increased to AED 50 per hour. I also found that by negotiating my salary during the hiring process, I was able to earn a higher hourly wage than what was initially offered.

Factors that Affect Hourly Wages in Dubai

Several factors can influence the hourly wage you can expect to earn in Dubai. These include:

Your Industry and Job Role:

As mentioned earlier, different industries and job roles have different hourly wage ranges. Researching the average hourly wage for your particular job can give you an idea of what to expect.

Your Level of Experience:

The more experience you have in your role, the higher your hourly wage is likely to be. Employers value experienced professionals and are willing to pay more for their expertise.

How to Negotiate a Fair Hourly Wage in Dubai

If you are not satisfied with the hourly wage being offered to you, it is important to negotiate with your employer. Here are some tips for negotiating a fair hourly wage:

Do Your Research:

Research the average hourly wage for your job role and industry in Dubai. This will give you a realistic idea of what you can expect to earn and can help you negotiate a fair wage.

Highlight Your Experience:

If you have extensive experience in your job role, be sure to highlight this during the negotiation process. Employers are willing to pay more for experienced professionals.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the average hourly wage in Dubai?

A: The average hourly wage in Dubai is AED 66.05 for all jobs.

Q: What industries tend to have higher hourly wages in Dubai?

A: Industries such as finance and engineering tend to have higher hourly wages in Dubai.

Q: Can I negotiate my hourly wage in Dubai?

A: Yes, it is possible to negotiate your hourly wage in Dubai. Doing research on the average hourly wage for your job role and highlighting your experience can help you negotiate a fair wage.

Q: How can I ensure that I am being paid a fair hourly wage in Dubai?

A: Researching the average hourly wage for your job role and industry in Dubai can help you ensure that you are being paid a fair wage. Additionally, negotiating your salary during the hiring process can help you earn a higher hourly wage.

Conclusion of How Much Do They Pay Per Hour in Dubai

Understanding how much you can expect to earn per hour in Dubai can be challenging, but doing your research and negotiating your salary can help you earn a fair wage. Remember to consider your industry, job role, and experience level when estimating your hourly wage. With the right approach, you can earn a competitive hourly wage in Dubai.
