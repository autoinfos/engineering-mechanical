How Much Do Technicians Make A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Bardi Aug 07, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career as a technician but don't know how much you can expect to make? This guide will provide you with comprehensive information about technician salaries, including the factors that affect pay and tips for negotiating your salary.

Many technicians find themselves wondering how much they should expect to make. After all, salary is a crucial factor in choosing a career. Technicians may also be concerned about whether their salary will be enough to support themselves and their families, or if they will be able to save for retirement.

The answer to the question, "how much do technicians make?" depends on several factors, including the industry in which they work, their level of experience, and their education and training. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for all technicians was $52,270 as of May 2020. The lowest 10 percent earned less than $31,610, and the highest 10 percent earned more than $87,810.

In summary, the factors that affect how much technicians make include their industry, experience, and education and training. Technicians in higher-paying industries, such as aerospace and medical equipment manufacturing, can earn significantly more than those in lower-paying industries. Additionally, technicians with more experience and education or training typically earn higher salaries.

How Much Do Entry-Level Technicians Make?

When starting a career as a technician, it's important to know what you can expect to make at the entry level. On average, entry-level technicians can expect to make around $35,000 to $40,000 per year. However, this can vary depending on the industry and location.

When I started my career as a technician, I was making around $38,000 per year. I had just graduated from a technical school and was working for an automotive repair shop. While the pay wasn't great, I was happy to be gaining valuable experience in my field.

How Much Do Experienced Technicians Make?

As technicians gain experience, their earning potential typically increases. Experienced technicians can make anywhere from $50,000 to $100,000 or more per year, depending on their industry and level of expertise.

After several years of working as a technician, I was able to negotiate a higher salary. I was making around $60,000 per year, which was a significant increase from my entry-level salary. I had gained a lot of experience and had become proficient in my field, which allowed me to command a higher salary.

Factors That Affect Technician Salaries

Several factors can affect how much technicians make, including:

  • The industry in which they work
  • Their level of experience
  • Their education and training
  • Their geographic location
  • Their employer

For example, technicians who work in industries such as aerospace, medical equipment manufacturing, and telecommunications tend to earn higher salaries than those in lower-paying industries such as automotive repair and maintenance. Additionally, technicians with more experience and education or training can command higher salaries.

Tips for Negotiating Your Technician Salary

If you're a technician who wants to negotiate a higher salary, here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Do your research: Find out what other technicians in your industry and location are making.
  • Highlight your experience and expertise: Emphasize your skills, experience, and education or training to show that you're a valuable employee.
  • Be confident: Don't be afraid to ask for what you're worth. Be confident in your abilities and the value you bring to your employer.
  • Be flexible: Consider negotiating other aspects of your compensation package, such as benefits, vacation time, or a flexible work schedule.

Question and Answer

Q: How much do technicians make per hour?

A: Technicians can make anywhere from $15 to $40 per hour, depending on their industry and level of expertise.

Q: Is it possible to make six figures as a technician?

A: Yes, experienced technicians with specialized skills and expertise can make six figures or more per year.

Q: Do technicians get paid overtime?

A: Technicians who work more than 40 hours per week are typically eligible for overtime pay, which is usually 1.5 times their regular hourly rate.

Q: Are there any certifications or licenses that can increase a technician's earning potential?

A: Yes, certifications and licenses can demonstrate a technician's expertise and increase their earning potential. Some examples include ASE certification for automotive technicians and CompTIA A+ certification for computer technicians.

Conclusion of How Much Do Technicians Make

In conclusion, technicians can make a good living depending on their industry, level of experience, and education or training. Entry-level technicians can expect to make around $35,000 to $40,000 per year, while experienced technicians can make anywhere from $50,000 to $100,000 or more per year. By negotiating their salaries and highlighting their skills and expertise, technicians can increase their earning potential and build successful careers in their fields.
