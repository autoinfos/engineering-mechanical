How Do You Get Certified To Be A Pharmacy Technician

Written by Eveline Oct 06, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Have you ever considered becoming a pharmacy technician? It's a rewarding job that allows you to help people in need and work in a fast-paced environment. However, before you can start your career, you need to become certified. Don't worry, though - the process is straightforward and worth it in the end.

The Pain Points of Becoming a Certified Pharmacy Technician

The process of becoming a certified pharmacy technician may seem overwhelming at first. You may be unsure of where to start or what the requirements are. It can also be challenging to balance studying for certification while working or going to school.

Answering the Target: How Do You Get Certified to Be a Pharmacy Technician?

The first step in becoming a certified pharmacy technician is to complete a training program accredited by the Accreditation Council for Pharmacy Education (ACPE) or the American Society of Health-System Pharmacists (ASHP). These programs can be found at community colleges, vocational schools, or online.

After completing a training program, you must pass the Pharmacy Technician Certification Exam (PTCE) or the Exam for the Certification of Pharmacy Technicians (ExCPT). The PTCE is offered by the Pharmacy Technician Certification Board (PTCB), while the ExCPT is offered by the National Healthcareer Association (NHA).

Once you pass the exam, you must renew your certification every two years and complete 20 hours of continuing education during each renewal period.

Summarizing the Main Points of Becoming a Certified Pharmacy Technician

In summary, to become a certified pharmacy technician, you must:

  • Complete an ACPE- or ASHP-accredited training program
  • Pass either the PTCE or ExCPT exam
  • Renew your certification every two years
  • Complete 20 hours of continuing education during each renewal period

My Personal Experience Becoming a Certified Pharmacy Technician

I became a certified pharmacy technician five years ago, and it was one of the best decisions I've ever made. I completed an online training program while working full-time, which allowed me to study at my own pace. I also found a study group at my local library, which helped me stay motivated and on track.

Passing the PTCE exam was challenging, but I felt confident in my knowledge thanks to my training program and study group. Since becoming certified, I've worked in various pharmacy settings and have continued my education through online courses and attending conferences.

The Benefits of Becoming a Certified Pharmacy Technician

Becoming a certified pharmacy technician opens up many opportunities for career advancement and increased earning potential. It also allows you to work in a variety of settings, including hospitals, retail pharmacies, and long-term care facilities. Additionally, you'll have the satisfaction of knowing that you're helping people every day by ensuring they receive the correct medications and dosages.

How to Stand Out as a Certified Pharmacy Technician

If you want to stand out as a certified pharmacy technician, consider specializing in a particular area of pharmacy, such as compounding or oncology. You can also become certified in areas like immunization or medication therapy management. Continuing education is essential to stay up-to-date on the latest medications and technologies.

Networking as a Certified Pharmacy Technician

Networking is also crucial in the pharmacy industry. Attend conferences and seminars to meet other pharmacy professionals and learn about new advancements in the field. Join professional organizations like the National Pharmacy Technician Association (NPTA) to stay connected and informed.

Question and Answer Section

Q: How much does it cost to become a certified pharmacy technician?

A: The cost of a training program and exam fees vary, but it typically ranges from $1,000 to $5,000.

Q: Can I become a pharmacy technician without certification?

A: While it's possible to work as a pharmacy technician without certification, most employers require it.

Q: How long does it take to become a certified pharmacy technician?

A: It typically takes six months to two years to complete a training program and pass the certification exam.

Q: Is continuing education required to maintain certification?

A: Yes, you must complete 20 hours of continuing education during each renewal period.

Conclusion of How Do You Get Certified to Be a Pharmacy Technician

Becoming a certified pharmacy technician is a rewarding career that allows you to help people and work in a fast-paced environment. While the process may seem daunting at first, completing an accredited training program and passing the certification exam is worth it in the end. With dedication and hard work, you can become a certified pharmacy technician and advance your career in the pharmacy industry.
