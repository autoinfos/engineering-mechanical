How Much Does A Pharmacy Tech Get Paid Hourly

Written by Eveline Jun 15, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career as a pharmacy tech but wondering how much you can expect to make hourly? Look no further as we explore the salary range, factors affecting pay, and what you can do to increase your earning potential.

As a pharmacy tech, you may experience pain points related to your hourly wage. These can include feeling undervalued for the work you do, struggling to make ends meet, or feeling limited in your career growth due to your salary. It's important to understand the factors affecting pay and what you can do to improve your situation.

What Is the Average Hourly Wage for a Pharmacy Tech?

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the average hourly wage for a pharmacy tech in the United States is $16.58. However, this can vary depending on your location, experience, and employer. For example, pharmacy techs in California tend to make a higher hourly wage than those in other states.

Factors that can affect your pay as a pharmacy tech include your level of experience, certifications, and the type of pharmacy you work in. For example, working in a hospital or specialty pharmacy may result in a higher hourly wage than working in a retail setting.

How Can You Increase Your Hourly Wage as a Pharmacy Tech?

One way to increase your earning potential as a pharmacy tech is to obtain certifications, such as the Pharmacy Technician Certification Board (PTCB) or the National Healthcareer Association (NHA) certification. These certifications demonstrate your knowledge and skills, making you a more valuable asset to your employer.

Another way to increase your hourly wage is to gain experience in a specialized area, such as compounding or sterile products. This can make you a more in-demand employee and potentially lead to higher pay.

What Are Some Other Benefits of Being a Pharmacy Tech?

Aside from the hourly wage, there are other benefits to being a pharmacy tech. For example, you may have the opportunity to work in a fast-paced and rewarding environment, helping patients with their medications and improving their health outcomes. Additionally, many pharmacy techs enjoy a flexible work schedule and opportunities for career growth.

How Can You Get Started as a Pharmacy Tech?

To become a pharmacy tech, you typically need a high school diploma or equivalent and on-the-job training. However, many employers prefer applicants with formal training or certification. You can pursue formal training through vocational schools, community colleges, or online programs.

Personal Experience: How Much Can You Make as a Pharmacy Tech?

As a pharmacy tech with several years of experience and certification, I currently make $20.50 per hour. However, when I first started out with no experience, I was making only $12 per hour. By obtaining my certification and gaining experience in a hospital setting, I was able to increase my hourly wage and overall earning potential.

FAQs About How Much Does a Pharmacy Tech Get Paid Hourly

Q: Is the hourly wage for a pharmacy tech the same across all states?

A: No, the hourly wage for a pharmacy tech can vary depending on your location. For example, pharmacy techs in California tend to make a higher hourly wage than those in other states.

Q: What factors can affect my hourly wage as a pharmacy tech?

A: Factors that can affect your pay as a pharmacy tech include your level of experience, certifications, and the type of pharmacy you work in.

Q: How can I increase my earning potential as a pharmacy tech?

A: You can increase your earning potential by obtaining certifications, gaining experience in a specialized area, and seeking out job opportunities in higher-paying settings.

Q: What are some other benefits of being a pharmacy tech besides the hourly wage?

A: Other benefits of being a pharmacy tech can include a fast-paced and rewarding work environment, flexible scheduling, and opportunities for career growth.

Conclusion of How Much Does a Pharmacy Tech Get Paid Hourly

As a pharmacy tech, your hourly wage can vary depending on a variety of factors. However, by obtaining certifications, gaining experience in a specialized area, and seeking out higher-paying job opportunities, you can increase your earning potential and improve your overall job satisfaction.
