FAQ About Auto Body Repair Technician Schools

Q: What qualifications do I need to enroll in an auto body repair technician school?

A: Most auto body repair technician schools require a high school diploma or equivalent. Some schools may also require applicants to pass a placement test or demonstrate basic math and reading skills.

Q: How long does it take to complete an auto body repair technician program?

A: The length of auto body repair technician programs can vary depending on the school and program. Some programs can be completed in as little as six months, while others may take two years or more.

Q: Will I need to purchase my own tools and equipment for an auto body repair technician program?

A: Many auto body repair technician schools provide students with the tools and equipment needed for the program. However, some schools may require students to purchase their own tools and equipment.

Q: What career opportunities are available for auto body repair technicians?

A: Auto body repair technicians can work in a variety of settings, including auto body repair shops, car dealerships, and custom car shops. Some technicians may also choose to start their own businesses.
