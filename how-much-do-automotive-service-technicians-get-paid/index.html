How Much Do Automotive Service Technicians Get Paid

Written by Robby Sep 27, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you considering a career as an automotive service technician? If so, you're probably wondering how much you can expect to earn in this field. The good news is that automotive service technicians can earn a competitive salary, but there are a few factors that can impact your earning potential.

One of the main pain points related to how much do automotive service technicians get paid is the misconception that this career isn't financially stable. However, this couldn't be further from the truth. With the rise of technology and the increasing complexity of vehicles, skilled technicians are in high demand.

So, how much do automotive service technicians get paid? According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for automotive service technicians and mechanics was $44,050 as of May 2020. However, the top 10% of technicians earned more than $68,880 per year.

In summary, automotive service technicians can earn a competitive salary, with the potential to earn more as you gain experience and specialize in certain areas. It's important to note that factors such as location, industry, and level of experience can impact your earning potential.

How Location Impacts Pay for Automotive Service Technicians

When it comes to how much do automotive service technicians get paid, location is a significant factor. Technicians who work in areas with a higher cost of living tend to earn more. For example, technicians who work in urban areas or regions with a higher demand for skilled workers may earn more than those who work in rural areas or regions with a lower demand for technicians.

However, it's important to note that location isn't the only factor that impacts pay. The industry you work in, and the level of experience you have, can also play a role in determining your salary.

How Industry Impacts Pay for Automotive Service Technicians

The industry you work in can also impact how much you earn as an automotive service technician. For example, technicians who work for car dealerships tend to earn more than those who work for independent repair shops. This is because dealerships generally have higher profit margins and can afford to pay their technicians more.

Additionally, technicians who specialize in certain areas such as hybrid and electric vehicles or diesel engines may earn more than those who work on traditional gasoline-powered vehicles.

How Level of Experience Impacts Pay for Automotive Service Technicians

As with most careers, your level of experience can impact your earning potential as an automotive service technician. Technicians who are just starting out in the field may earn less than those who have several years of experience under their belt. However, as you gain experience and specialize in certain areas, you can expect to earn more.

How to Increase Your Earning Potential as an Automotive Service Technician

If you're interested in increasing your earning potential as an automotive service technician, there are a few things you can do. First, consider specializing in a certain area such as hybrid and electric vehicles or diesel engines. This can make you a more valuable employee and increase your earning potential.

Additionally, consider pursuing additional certifications or training. The more certifications you have, the more valuable you are as an employee, which can lead to higher pay.

Personal Experience of Earning Potential as an Automotive Service Technician

As a former automotive service technician, I can attest to the fact that this career can be financially rewarding. I started out earning around $30,000 per year, but as I gained experience and specialized in certain areas, my earning potential increased. By the time I left the field, I was earning around $60,000 per year.

A: Yes, technicians who work for car dealerships tend to earn more than those who work for independent repair shops.

Conclusion of How Much Do Automotive Service Technicians Get Paid

In conclusion, automotive service technicians can earn a competitive salary, with the potential to earn more as they gain experience and specialize in certain areas. Location, industry, and level of experience can impact your earning potential, but there are steps you can take to increase your salary such as specializing in a certain area, pursuing additional certifications or training, and gaining experience in the field.
