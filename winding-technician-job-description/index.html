How to Become a Winding Technician?

To become a winding technician, you'll need at least a high school diploma or equivalent, and some technical training in mechanical or electrical engineering. You can also take specialized courses in winding equipment repair and maintenance to improve your skills and knowledge. Some employers may also require certification from a recognized organization, such as the International Association of Machinists and Aerospace Workers (IAMAW).

Once you have the necessary education and training, you can start looking for job opportunities in various industries, such as electrical, automotive, and manufacturing. You can also work as a freelancer or start your own repair and maintenance business.
