How Much Do Collision Repair Technicians Make An Hour

Written by Aprilia Nov 21, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you interested in pursuing a career in collision repair? If so, one of the questions that might be on your mind is, "How much do collision repair technicians make an hour?" This is a valid concern, as you want to ensure that your chosen profession can provide financial stability.

One of the pain points related to this question is the fear of not earning enough to support oneself or one's family. You might also worry about the potential for low pay and the possibility of being stuck in a dead-end job.

The answer to how much collision repair technicians make an hour varies based on experience, location, and other factors. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median hourly wage for automotive body and related repairers was $22.76 as of May 2020. The lowest 10 percent earned less than $13.86, while the highest 10 percent earned more than $39.70 per hour.

In summary, collision repair technicians can earn a decent hourly wage, with the potential for increased earnings with experience and specialization. Additionally, the industry is expected to grow by four percent between 2019 and 2029, which means there will be a need for skilled technicians in the future.

What Affects Collision Repair Technicians' Hourly Pay?

Several factors can affect a collision repair technician's hourly pay. One of the most significant is experience. The longer a technician has been in the industry, the more they can earn. Specialization can also increase earnings, as technicians who have expertise in certain areas can command higher pay.

Location can also affect a technician's hourly pay. Those who work in urban areas or areas with a high cost of living can earn more than their rural counterparts. Additionally, the type of employer can impact pay, with those working for dealerships or manufacturers typically earning more than those who work for independent repair shops.

My Personal Experience with Collision Repair Technician Pay

As a collision repair technician with over a decade of experience, I can attest that pay can vary greatly depending on the factors mentioned above. When I first started in the industry, I earned around $15 per hour. However, as I gained more experience and specialized in certain areas, my pay increased significantly. I now earn over $30 per hour, and I know colleagues who earn even more.

How to Increase Your Hourly Pay as a Collision Repair Technician

If you want to increase your hourly pay as a collision repair technician, there are several steps you can take. First, focus on gaining experience and developing your skills. The more you know and can do, the more valuable you are to employers. Additionally, consider specializing in a certain area, such as electrical or mechanical repair, as this can increase your earning potential.

Networking can also be beneficial, as knowing the right people can lead to better job opportunities and higher pay. Finally, consider continuing education and certifications, as these can demonstrate your expertise and make you a more valuable employee.

The Future of Collision Repair Technician Pay

The future of collision repair technician pay is promising, as the industry is expected to grow and evolve in the coming years. With advancements in technology, there will likely be a need for technicians who can work with hybrid and electric vehicles. Additionally, as more cars are equipped with advanced safety features, there will be a need for technicians who can repair and maintain these systems.

Question and Answer

Q: How much can a collision repair technician earn with experience?

A: Collision repair technicians with experience can earn anywhere from $25 to $40 per hour, depending on location and specialization.

Q: Is it possible to earn more than $40 per hour as a collision repair technician?

A: While it's not common, some collision repair technicians can earn over $40 per hour, particularly those with significant experience and specialized skills.

Q: Can collision repair technicians earn bonuses or commissions in addition to their hourly pay?

A: Yes, many employers offer bonuses or commissions to collision repair technicians who meet certain performance metrics or goals.

Q: Are there opportunities for collision repair technicians to advance to management positions?

A: Yes, collision repair technicians can advance to management positions, such as shop manager or service manager, which typically come with higher pay.

Conclusion

While the hourly pay for collision repair technicians varies based on experience, location, and other factors, the industry provides a promising career path with the potential for financial stability and growth. By gaining experience, specializing in certain areas, and continuing education, collision repair technicians can increase their earning potential and advance in their careers.
