Vet Tech Colleges In Houston Texas Everything You Need To Know

Written by Oliver Nov 11, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you considering becoming a veterinary technician? If you're located in Houston, Texas, you're in luck! There are several colleges in the area that offer vet tech programs. But with so many options, it can be overwhelming to decide which one to choose. In this blog post, we'll cover everything you need to know about vet tech colleges in Houston Texas and help you make an informed decision on which program is right for you.

As a prospective vet tech student, you may have some concerns or pain points about the process of finding the right college. Some common issues include figuring out the cost of tuition, finding a program that fits your schedule, and making sure the program is accredited. These concerns are all valid and can make the process of choosing a college stressful.

The main target of vet tech colleges in Houston Texas is to provide students with the education and training they need to become veterinary technicians. These programs typically take 2-3 years to complete and offer a combination of classroom instruction and hands-on training. Upon completion of the program, graduates are eligible to take the Veterinary Technician National Exam (VTNE) and become certified veterinary technicians.

In summary, when looking for vet tech colleges in Houston Texas, it's important to consider factors such as cost, accreditation, and program length. It's also a good idea to research the curriculum and make sure it aligns with your career goals. Now, let's take a closer look at some of the top vet tech colleges in Houston Texas.

Sanford-Brown College

Sanford-Brown College in Houston offers a Veterinary Technology Associate of Applied Science degree program that can be completed in as little as 18 months. The program is accredited by the American Veterinary Medical Association (AVMA) and includes courses such as animal nursing, veterinary anatomy and physiology, and clinical pathology. One of the unique features of this program is the opportunity for students to gain hands-on experience working with animals in the college's veterinary clinic.

Lone Star College

Lone Star College has multiple locations throughout the Houston area and offers a Veterinary Technology Associate of Applied Science degree program. This program is also accredited by the AVMA and includes courses such as veterinary pharmacology, clinical pathology, and veterinary surgical nursing. In addition to classroom instruction, students have the opportunity to complete clinical rotations at local veterinary clinics and animal hospitals.

University of Houston Downtown

The University of Houston Downtown offers a Bachelor of Applied Science in Veterinary Technology program. This program is also accredited by the AVMA and includes courses such as animal behavior and welfare, veterinary radiology, and veterinary anesthesia. Students in this program have the opportunity to gain hands-on experience working with animals through clinical rotations at local veterinary clinics and animal hospitals.

How to Choose the Right Vet Tech College for You

When choosing a vet tech college in Houston Texas, it's important to consider factors such as cost, accreditation, program length, and curriculum. It's also a good idea to visit the campus and speak with current students and faculty members to get a better sense of the program. By doing your research and asking the right questions, you can make an informed decision on which college is right for you.

My Personal Experience

When I was looking for a vet tech college in Houston Texas, I visited multiple campuses and spoke with students and faculty members. Ultimately, I chose Sanford-Brown College because of its hands-on clinical experience and shorter program length. The program was challenging but rewarding, and I felt well-prepared for the Veterinary Technician National Exam (VTNE) after graduation. I now work as a certified veterinary technician at a local animal hospital and couldn't be happier with my career choice.

Question and Answer

Q: How much does it cost to attend a vet tech college in Houston Texas?

A: The cost of attending a vet tech college in Houston Texas varies depending on the school and program. It's important to research the cost of tuition, fees, and supplies before making a decision.

Q: How long does it take to become a certified veterinary technician?

A: Becoming a certified veterinary technician typically takes 2-3 years, depending on the program. After graduation, graduates are eligible to take the Veterinary Technician National Exam (VTNE) and become certified.

Q: Can I work as a veterinary technician without certification?

A: No, in order to work as a veterinary technician, you must be certified. Certification ensures that you have the necessary knowledge and skills to provide high-quality care to animals.

Q: What kind of courses will I take in a vet tech program?

A: Vet tech programs typically include courses such as animal anatomy and physiology, pharmacology, clinical pathology, and surgical nursing.

Conclusion of Vet Tech Colleges in Houston Texas

Choosing the right vet tech college in Houston Texas is an important decision that can impact your career as a veterinary technician. By considering factors such as cost, accreditation, program length, and curriculum, you can make an informed decision on which program is right for you. Whether you choose Sanford-Brown College, Lone Star College, or the University of Houston Downtown, you'll receive the education and training you need to succeed in your career as a veterinary technician.
