The Top 5 Highest Paid Helicopter Jobs That Will Take Your Career To New Heights

Written by Christine Jul 13, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you looking for a high-paying career that offers excitement, adventure, and a unique work environment? If so, then a career in the helicopter industry may be just the ticket. Helicopter jobs are some of the most lucrative in the aviation industry, with a wide range of positions available to suit every skill level and interest.

However, finding the right helicopter job can be a challenge, especially if you are not sure where to start. With so many different types of helicopter jobs available, it can be difficult to determine which ones offer the highest salaries, the best benefits, and the most opportunities for advancement.

If you are interested in pursuing a career in the helicopter industry, then you have come to the right place. In this article, we will explore the top 5 highest paid helicopter jobs, and provide you with all the information you need to get started on the path to success.

Helicopter Pilot

As a helicopter pilot, you will be responsible for flying helicopters for a variety of purposes, including transport, search and rescue, and aerial surveying. This is one of the most popular and highest paid helicopter jobs, with salaries ranging from $60,000 to $150,000 per year, depending on experience, skill level, and the type of helicopter you fly.

As a helicopter pilot, you will need to have excellent hand-eye coordination, spatial awareness, and the ability to remain calm under pressure. You will also need to have a commercial pilot's license, which requires extensive training and experience.

Helicopter Engineer

As a helicopter engineer, you will be responsible for maintaining and repairing helicopters, ensuring that they are safe and in good working order. This is one of the highest paid helicopter jobs, with salaries ranging from $70,000 to $120,000 per year, depending on experience, skill level, and the type of helicopter you work on.

As a helicopter engineer, you will need to have a strong background in mechanical engineering, as well as experience working with helicopters and other aircraft. You will also need to have excellent problem-solving skills, attention to detail, and the ability to work well under pressure.

Helicopter Sales Representative

As a helicopter sales representative, you will be responsible for selling helicopters to customers, including private individuals, corporations, and government agencies. This is one of the highest paid helicopter jobs, with salaries ranging from $80,000 to $150,000 per year, depending on experience, skill level, and the type of helicopter you sell.

As a helicopter sales representative, you will need to have excellent communication skills, a strong understanding of the helicopter industry, and the ability to build and maintain relationships with customers. You will also need to have a background in sales, marketing, or business development.

Helicopter Tour Guide

As a helicopter tour guide, you will be responsible for providing tours of scenic areas, cities, and other attractions from the air. This is one of the most exciting and highest paid helicopter jobs, with salaries ranging from $50,000 to $100,000 per year, depending on experience, skill level, and the type of helicopter you fly.

As a helicopter tour guide, you will need to have excellent communication skills, a strong understanding of the areas you are touring, and the ability to provide a safe and enjoyable experience for your customers. You will also need to have a commercial pilot's license and experience flying helicopters.

Helicopter Emergency Medical Services (EMS) Pilot

As a helicopter EMS pilot, you will be responsible for transporting patients to and from hospitals, emergency scenes, and other medical facilities. This is one of the most important and highest paid helicopter jobs, with salaries ranging from $80,000 to $150,000 per year, depending on experience, skill level, and the type of helicopter you fly.

As a helicopter EMS pilot, you will need to have excellent flying skills, a strong understanding of emergency medical procedures, and the ability to work well under pressure. You will also need to have a commercial pilot's license, as well as specialized training in emergency medical services.

Conclusion

The helicopter industry offers a wide range of exciting and high-paying career opportunities, including helicopter pilot, engineer, sales representative, tour guide, and EMS pilot. Whether you are looking for adventure, challenge, or financial rewards, there is a helicopter job that will suit your needs and interests.

So, what are you waiting for? Take your career to new heights with one of these top 5 highest paid helicopter jobs.
