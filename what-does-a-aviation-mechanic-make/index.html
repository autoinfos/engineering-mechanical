What Does An Aviation Mechanic Make Everything You Need To Know

Written by Petter Oct 02, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Have you ever wondered what an aviation mechanic makes? If you're considering a career in aviation, or just curious about the wages of an aviation mechanic, then you're in the right place. In this blog post, we'll break down everything you need to know about what an aviation mechanic makes, including the average salary, factors that affect pay, and more.

Being an aviation mechanic can be a challenging and rewarding career, but it's essential to understand the pay before committing to this profession. Many factors can affect an aviation mechanic's salary, including experience, location, and industry. Generally, aviation mechanics are well-compensated for their skills and knowledge.

What Does an Aviation Mechanic Make: Answered

The average salary for an aviation mechanic in the United States is $64,090 per year, according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics. However, this number can vary depending on the type of aviation mechanic, location, and experience level. For example, an aviation mechanic that works on commercial aircraft may earn more than one that works on smaller private planes. Additionally, an aviation mechanic in a high-cost-of-living city like San Francisco may earn more than one in a rural area.

Other factors that can affect an aviation mechanic's salary include certifications, education, and job responsibilities. A mechanic with advanced certifications or education may earn more than one without. Additionally, a mechanic that takes on more job responsibilities, such as managing a team of mechanics, may earn more than one that only performs maintenance tasks.

Factors that Affect an Aviation Mechanic's Salary

As mentioned above, several factors can affect an aviation mechanic's salary. Here are some of the most important:

Experience Level

Generally, the more experience an aviation mechanic has, the higher their salary will be. Experienced mechanics have a wealth of knowledge and expertise that is invaluable to employers. They can troubleshoot problems quickly and efficiently, which can save airlines or private owners significant amounts of money.

Location

Location is another significant factor that can affect an aviation mechanic's salary. Mechanics in high-cost-of-living cities, such as New York or San Francisco, may earn more than those in rural areas or small towns. The cost of living in a particular area can affect salaries, as employers need to offer competitive salaries to attract qualified candidates.

Certifications and Education

Aviation mechanics with advanced certifications or education may earn more than those without. Certifications and education demonstrate a mechanic's knowledge and expertise, which can make them more valuable to employers. Additionally, some certifications, such as the Airframe and Powerplant (A&P) certificate, are required to work on aircraft.

Job Responsibilities

Aviation mechanics that take on more job responsibilities, such as managing a team of mechanics, may earn more than those that only perform maintenance tasks. As mechanics gain experience and knowledge, they may take on more significant roles within their organizations.

Personal Experience: What I Make as an Aviation Mechanic

As an aviation mechanic with over ten years of experience, I can tell you that the pay can be quite good. I work for a major airline, and my salary is in the six-figure range. However, this is not typical for all aviation mechanics, and it's essential to consider the factors mentioned above when evaluating potential salaries.

How to Become an Aviation Mechanic

There are several steps to becoming an aviation mechanic. First, you'll need to obtain a high school diploma or equivalent. Then, you'll need to complete a Federal Aviation Administration (FAA)-approved training program, which can take anywhere from 18 months to two years. Once you've completed your training, you'll need to pass the FAA's Airframe and Powerplant (A&P) certification exam.

FAQs About What Does an Aviation Mechanic Make

Q: What is the average salary for an aviation mechanic?

A: The average salary for an aviation mechanic in the United States is $64,090 per year.

Q: What factors affect an aviation mechanic's salary?

A: Factors that can affect an aviation mechanic's salary include experience, location, certifications, education, and job responsibilities.

Q: How much can a highly experienced aviation mechanic make?

A: Highly experienced aviation mechanics can make in the six-figure range.

Q: Is it necessary to have certifications to work as an aviation mechanic?

A: Yes, some certifications, such as the Airframe and Powerplant (A&P) certificate, are required to work on aircraft.

Conclusion of What Does an Aviation Mechanic Make

In summary, an aviation mechanic's pay can vary depending on several factors, including experience, location, certifications, education, and job responsibilities. However, the average salary for an aviation mechanic in the United States is $64,090 per year. Being an aviation mechanic can be a challenging but rewarding career, and it's essential to consider the pay and other factors carefully before committing to this field.
