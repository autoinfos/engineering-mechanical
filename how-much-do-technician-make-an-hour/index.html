How Much Do Technicians Make Per Hour

Written by William Sep 05, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career as a technician? One of the most important questions to ask is how much you'll be paid per hour. Let's dive into the world of technician salaries and find out what you can expect to make.

For many people, salary is a major pain point when it comes to choosing a career. You want to be sure that you're choosing a job that will pay you enough to support yourself and your family. Technicians are no exception - you want to be sure that you're being compensated fairly for your skills and work.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median hourly wage for technicians is $22.76 per hour. However, this can vary widely depending on the industry you work in and your level of experience. For example, technicians working in the aerospace industry tend to make more than those working in the automotive industry.

In general, technicians with more experience and education tend to make more than those just starting out. Additionally, technicians who specialize in a particular area - such as HVAC or medical equipment - may be able to command higher salaries.

My Experience

When I started out as a technician, I was making around $18 per hour. However, as I gained more experience and certifications, my salary increased. Now, after several years in the industry, I make around $30 per hour.

Factors Affecting Technician Pay

There are several factors that can affect how much technicians make per hour. Some of these include:

Industry

As mentioned earlier, the industry you work in can have a major impact on your hourly wage. For example, technicians working in the aerospace industry tend to make more than those working in the automotive industry.

Experience and Education

Technicians with more experience and education tend to make more than those just starting out. Additionally, technicians who specialize in a particular area - such as HVAC or medical equipment - may be able to command higher salaries.

Certifications and Licenses

Having certain certifications or licenses can also impact your hourly wage. For example, a technician with a NATE certification is likely to make more than one without it.

Geographic Location

Finally, where you live can also impact how much you make as a technician. Technicians working in larger cities or metropolitan areas often make more than those working in more rural locations.

Conclusion

As you can see, there are many factors that can impact how much technicians make per hour. However, with the right experience, education, and certifications, you can make a comfortable living as a technician. Just be sure to do your research and choose an industry and specialty that aligns with your skills and interests.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the average hourly wage for technicians?

A: The median hourly wage for technicians is $22.76 per hour.

Q: Do technicians working in certain industries make more than others?

A: Yes, technicians working in industries such as aerospace and medical equipment tend to make more than those working in industries such as automotive or HVAC.

Q: How can I increase my hourly wage as a technician?

A: You can increase your hourly wage as a technician by gaining more experience and education, earning certifications or licenses, and specializing in a particular area.

Q: Does geographic location impact how much technicians make per hour?

A: Yes, technicians working in larger cities or metropolitan areas often make more than those working in more rural locations.

Conclusion of How Much Do Technicians Make Per Hour

The world of technician salaries can be complex, but with the right education, experience, and certifications, you can make a comfortable living as a technician. Keep in mind the factors that can impact your hourly wage, and choose an industry and specialty that aligns with your skills and interests.
