How Much Do Electrical Technicians Really Make

Written by Oliver Jun 11, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career as an electrical technician? If so, you're probably wondering about the earning potential in this field. While it's true that salary for electrical technician can vary based on a number of factors, there are some general figures that can give you an idea of what to expect.

One of the biggest pain points related to salary for electrical technician is the fact that many people assume this job doesn't pay well. However, this is simply not true. In fact, electrical technicians can earn a decent salary right out of the gate, and the earning potential only increases with experience and education.

According to the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual salary for electrical technicians was $62,190 as of May 2020. This means that half of all electrical technicians earned more than this amount, while the other half earned less. Keep in mind, however, that this figure can vary based on factors such as location, education, and experience.

In conclusion, salary for electrical technician is not as low as some people may think. In fact, it can be a lucrative field for those who are willing to put in the work and gain experience. By getting the right education and working in the right location, you can increase your earning potential significantly.

The Target of Salary for Electrical Technician

The target of salary for electrical technician is to ensure that those who work in this field are fairly compensated for their skills and expertise. Electrical technicians are responsible for installing, repairing, and maintaining electrical systems in a variety of settings, including residential, commercial, and industrial environments. Because this work requires a high level of technical knowledge and skill, it's important that electrical technicians are paid accordingly.

Personally, I've been working as an electrical technician for over a decade, and I can attest to the fact that the earning potential in this field is quite good. When I started out, I was earning around $40,000 per year, but as I gained more experience and took on more advanced roles, my salary increased significantly. Now, I earn well over the median annual salary for electrical technicians, and I'm able to provide for my family comfortably.

Factors That Impact Salary for Electrical Technician

There are a number of factors that can impact salary for electrical technician. Some of the most important include:

Location

Where you work can have a big impact on how much you earn as an electrical technician. In general, areas with a high cost of living tend to pay more, as do areas where there is a high demand for skilled workers.

Education and Experience

As with many professions, education and experience can play a big role in determining your salary as an electrical technician. Those with more advanced degrees or certifications, as well as those with more years of experience, tend to earn more.

My Personal Experience with Salary for Electrical Technician

As I mentioned earlier, I've been working as an electrical technician for over a decade. During that time, I've been able to earn a good salary and provide for my family comfortably. However, I've also had to work hard and continually improve my skills in order to stay competitive in the field.

One thing that has helped me increase my earning potential as an electrical technician is continuing education. By taking courses and earning certifications in advanced electrical work, I've been able to take on more challenging projects and earn a higher salary as a result.

Question and Answer

Q: Is it possible to earn a good salary as an electrical technician right out of school?

A: Yes, it is possible to earn a decent salary as an electrical technician right out of school, especially if you have the right certifications and skills.

Q: What is the average hourly rate for electrical technicians?

A: According to the BLS, the median hourly wage for electrical technicians was $29.89 as of May 2020.

Q: What are some of the highest-paying industries for electrical technicians?

A: Some of the highest-paying industries for electrical technicians include aerospace product and parts manufacturing, natural gas distribution, and electric power generation.

Q: What is the job outlook for electrical technicians?

A: According to the BLS, employment of electrical technicians is projected to grow 8 percent from 2019 to 2029, faster than the average for all occupations.

Conclusion of Salary for Electrical Technician

Overall, salary for electrical technician can be quite good, especially for those who are willing to put in the work and gain experience. By getting the right education, working in the right location, and continually improving your skills, you can earn a comfortable living as an electrical technician.
