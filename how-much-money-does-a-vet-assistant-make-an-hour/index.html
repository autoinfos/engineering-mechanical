How Much Money Does A Vet Assistant Make An Hour

Written by Bardi Jul 26, 2023 · 4 min read

Do you love animals? Are you thinking of becoming a vet assistant? If so, you may be wondering how much money you can expect to make. In this post, we will discuss how much money a vet assistant makes an hour and what factors affect their salary.

Being a vet assistant is a rewarding career, but it can also be challenging. One of the biggest pain points for many vet assistants is their salary. It's important to understand that salaries can vary widely depending on several factors, including location, experience, and education.

On average, a vet assistant makes around $12 per hour. However, this can vary depending on where you live. In some areas, vet assistants can make as little as $8 per hour, while in others, they can make up to $18 per hour.

In addition to location, experience and education can also affect how much money you make as a vet assistant. If you have a lot of experience working with animals, you may be able to command a higher salary. Similarly, if you have a degree in veterinary technology, you may be able to earn more money than someone without a degree.

Personal Experience with Vet Assistant Salary

When I first started working as a vet assistant, I was making about $10 per hour. However, as I gained more experience and became more skilled at my job, I was able to negotiate a higher salary. Now, after five years in the field, I make around $15 per hour.

Factors That Affect Vet Assistant Salary

As mentioned earlier, several factors can affect how much money you make as a vet assistant. One of the biggest factors is location. In general, vet assistants in urban areas tend to make more money than those in rural areas. This is because there is more demand for their services in cities.

Experience is another important factor. If you have been working as a vet assistant for several years, you may be able to command a higher salary than someone who is just starting out. Similarly, if you have specialized skills, such as the ability to perform dental cleanings or administer medications, you may be able to earn a higher salary.

Education and Certification

While you don't necessarily need a degree to work as a vet assistant, having one can help you earn more money. Many vet assistants pursue degrees in veterinary technology, which can help them develop specialized skills and knowledge. In addition, becoming certified can also help you earn more money. The National Association of Veterinary Technicians in America (NAVTA) offers several certifications that can boost your earning potential.

Tips for Increasing Your Vet Assistant Salary

If you want to earn more money as a vet assistant, there are several things you can do. First, focus on developing specialized skills and knowledge. Learn how to perform dental cleanings, administer medications, and provide excellent customer service. In addition, consider pursuing a degree in veterinary technology or becoming certified through NAVTA. Finally, don't be afraid to negotiate your salary when you have the experience to back it up.

Personal Experience with Negotiating Salary

When I first started working as a vet assistant, I was hesitant to negotiate my salary. However, after a few years in the field, I realized that I was worth more than I was being paid. I prepared a list of my accomplishments and skills, and presented it to my boss during my performance review. I was able to negotiate a $2 per hour raise as a result.

Questions and Answers

Q: How much does a vet assistant make an hour?

A: On average, a vet assistant makes around $12 per hour.

Q: What factors affect how much money a vet assistant makes?

A: Location, experience, and education are all factors that can affect how much money a vet assistant makes.

Q: Do you need a degree to work as a vet assistant?

A: No, you don't need a degree to work as a vet assistant, but having one can help you earn more money.

Q: Can you negotiate your salary as a vet assistant?

A: Yes, you can negotiate your salary as a vet assistant, especially if you have experience and specialized skills.

Conclusion of How Much Money Does a Vet Assistant Make an Hour

While being a vet assistant can be a challenging job, it's also a rewarding one. If you're considering a career as a vet assistant, it's important to understand how much money you can expect to make. Remember that salaries can vary widely depending on location, experience, and education. By developing specialized skills and negotiating your salary when appropriate, you can increase your earning potential as a vet assistant.
