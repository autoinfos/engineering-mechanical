How Much Money Does A Lineman Make An Hour

Written by Petter Nov 21, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Have you ever wondered how much money does a lineman make an hour? If you are considering a career in the electrical industry, it is essential to know how much you can expect to earn. In this blog post, we will explore the salary of a lineman and provide you with valuable insights into what you can expect from this profession.

Working as a lineman can be physically and mentally challenging. It involves working outdoors in all weather conditions, climbing poles, and handling electrical equipment. However, the job can also be rewarding, as you play a crucial role in maintaining the electrical infrastructure that powers our communities.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median hourly wage for a lineman in the United States is $32.14. This means that half of all linemen earn more than this amount, while the other half earns less. The lowest 10 percent of linemen earn less than $20.05 per hour, while the highest 10 percent earn more than $47.85 per hour.

In addition to their hourly wage, linemen may also receive benefits such as health insurance, retirement plans, and paid time off. These benefits vary by employer and may impact their overall compensation package.

What Influences a Lineman's Salary?

Several factors can influence a lineman's salary, including their level of experience, the type of employer, and their geographic location. Linemen who have been in the industry for several years and have advanced skills may earn a higher wage than those who are just starting.

The type of employer can also impact a lineman's salary. For example, linemen who work for a utility company may earn a higher wage than those who work for a contractor. Similarly, linemen who work in urban areas may earn more than those who work in rural locations.

Personal Experience

As a former lineman, I can attest that the job can be both challenging and rewarding. I started my career as an apprentice and worked my way up to a journeyman lineman. During my time in the industry, I earned an hourly wage of $35.50, along with excellent benefits such as health insurance and a retirement plan.

However, the job was not without its challenges. I often worked long hours, including weekends and holidays, and had to be on-call in case of emergencies. The work was physically demanding, and I had to be comfortable working at heights and in all weather conditions.

Career Advancement

Linemen who want to advance their careers may consider becoming a foreman, supervisor, or manager. These positions come with additional responsibilities and may require additional education or training. However, they also come with higher salaries and more significant opportunities for career growth.

Education and Training

To become a lineman, you will need to complete an apprenticeship program. These programs typically last four to five years and combine classroom instruction with on-the-job training. Apprentices receive a wage while they learn and can expect to earn a journeyman's salary upon completion of the program.

Future Job Outlook

The job outlook for linemen is positive, with a projected growth rate of 4 percent between 2019 and 2029. This growth is due to an increased demand for electricity and the need to upgrade and maintain existing electrical infrastructure.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the median hourly wage for a lineman in the United States is $32.14. However, this can vary depending on factors such as experience, type of employer, and geographic location. While the job can be challenging, it can also be rewarding and provide opportunities for career growth.
