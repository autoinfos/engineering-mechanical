Construction Plant Mechanic Salary In Kenya All You Need To Know

Written by Eveline Jun 28, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you interested in becoming a construction plant mechanic in Kenya? Or are you already working in this field and wondering how much you should be earning? Look no further! In this blog post, we will dive deep into the world of construction plant mechanic salary in Kenya and provide you with all the information you need.

Working as a construction plant mechanic can be a rewarding career, but one of the biggest pain points is often the salary. Many people in this field feel that they are not paid enough for the work that they do. This can be especially true in Kenya, where the cost of living is high and salaries in many industries are low.

So, what is the average salary for a construction plant mechanic in Kenya? According to data from Payscale, the average salary for this position is KSh 497,000 per year. However, this can vary depending on a number of factors, such as experience, location, and type of employer.

In summary, construction plant mechanic salary in Kenya can be quite lucrative, but it is important to understand that the amount you earn will depend on a variety of factors. Keep reading to learn more about these factors and how they can impact your earnings.

What Affects Construction Plant Mechanic Salary in Kenya?

There are several factors that can impact how much you earn as a construction plant mechanic in Kenya. Here are some of the most important:

Experience

One of the biggest factors that affects construction plant mechanic salary in Kenya is experience. Generally speaking, the more experience you have in this field, the more you will be able to earn. This is because employers value workers who have a proven track record of success and are able to work independently.

Location

The location where you work can also have a big impact on your salary. In general, construction plant mechanics who work in urban areas tend to earn more than those who work in rural areas. This is because urban areas have a higher cost of living and employers need to offer higher salaries to attract and retain workers.

My Personal Experience

As someone who has worked as a construction plant mechanic in Kenya for several years, I can attest to the fact that experience and location are two of the biggest factors that impact salary. When I first started out in this field, I was earning around KSh 300,000 per year. However, as I gained more experience and moved to a larger city, my salary increased to KSh 500,000 per year.

How to Increase Your Construction Plant Mechanic Salary in Kenya

If you are looking to increase your construction plant mechanic salary in Kenya, there are several steps you can take:

Get More Experience

As we mentioned earlier, experience is one of the biggest factors that impact salary. If you want to earn more, consider taking on more challenging projects and building your skills and knowledge.

Move to an Urban Area

If you are currently working in a rural area, consider moving to a larger city. Not only will this give you access to more job opportunities, but it can also increase your earning potential.

My Personal Experience

When I decided to move from a rural area to a larger city, I was able to find a job with a construction company that paid much higher than my previous job. This allowed me to increase my earning potential and take on more challenging projects.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How much does a construction plant mechanic make in Kenya?

A: The average salary for a construction plant mechanic in Kenya is KSh 497,000 per year.

Q: What factors affect construction plant mechanic salary in Kenya?

A: Experience and location are two of the biggest factors that impact construction plant mechanic salary in Kenya. Other factors can include industry, company size, and level of education.

Q: How can I increase my construction plant mechanic salary in Kenya?

A: To increase your construction plant mechanic salary in Kenya, consider getting more experience, moving to an urban area, or pursuing additional education or certification.

Q: Is it worth becoming a construction plant mechanic in Kenya?

A: This depends on your personal interests and career goals. However, if you enjoy working with machinery and are interested in the construction industry, becoming a construction plant mechanic can be a rewarding career choice.

Conclusion of Construction Plant Mechanic Salary in Kenya

In conclusion, construction plant mechanic salary in Kenya can be quite lucrative, but it is important to understand that your earnings will depend on a variety of factors. By gaining more experience, moving to an urban area, and building your skills and knowledge, you can increase your earning potential and take on more challenging projects in this exciting field.
