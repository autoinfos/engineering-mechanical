Mortgage Servicing Explained

Mortgage servicing refers to the process of managing a mortgage loan after it has been originated. This includes tasks such as collecting payments, managing escrow accounts, and providing customer support. Mortgage servicing companies can be either the same as the original lender, or they can be a third-party company that specializes in servicing mortgages.

How to Choose a Mortgage Servicing Company

Choosing a mortgage servicing company is an important decision that should not be taken lightly. Some factors to consider when making this choice include the company's reputation, customer service, and fees. It is also important to read the fine print of any contracts or agreements before signing on with a mortgage servicing company.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the difference between a mortgage lender and a mortgage servicer?
A: A mortgage lender is the company or institution that provides the mortgage loan, while a mortgage servicer is responsible for managing the loan after it has been originated.

Q: Can I choose my mortgage servicer?
A: In some cases, you may be able to choose your mortgage servicer. However, this will depend on the terms of your mortgage agreement.

Q: What should I do if I am unhappy with my mortgage servicer?
A: If you are unhappy with your mortgage servicer, you should first try to address the issue with their customer service team. If this does not resolve the issue, you may need to escalate the matter to a higher authority or consider switching to a different servicer.

Q: How can I find a reputable mortgage servicing company?
A: One way to find a reputable mortgage servicing company is to read reviews from other customers. You can also research the company's reputation and history before making a decision.
