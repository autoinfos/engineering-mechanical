What Does An Automotive Engineering Technology Major Do

Written by Bardi Jul 20, 2023 · 5 min read

Have you ever wondered what an automotive engineering technology major does? If you're interested in cars, engineering, and technology, this might be the perfect major for you. In this blog post, we'll explore the ins and outs of what an automotive engineering technology major does, and what you can expect from this career path.

As an automotive engineering technology major, you'll be responsible for designing, developing, and testing various components of vehicles. You'll work with a team of engineers and technicians to ensure that vehicles are safe, reliable, and efficient. This can involve everything from designing engines and transmissions to developing new safety features and testing prototypes.

If you're interested in pursuing an automotive engineering technology major, you'll need to have a strong foundation in math, physics, and computer science. You'll also need to be detail-oriented, analytical, and able to work well in a team environment. Most automotive engineering technology programs will also require you to complete internships or co-op experiences to gain hands-on experience in the field.

In summary, an automotive engineering technology major is responsible for designing, developing, and testing various components of vehicles. This career path requires a strong foundation in math, physics, and computer science, as well as excellent analytical and teamwork skills.

What Does an Automotive Engineering Technology Major Do: Personal Experience

As an automotive engineering technology major myself, I've had the opportunity to work on some really exciting projects. One of my favorites was designing a new transmission for a high-performance sports car. We had to balance performance, efficiency, and cost to come up with the best possible solution.

Another project I worked on was developing a new safety feature for a family car. We used computer simulations and crash tests to ensure that the feature would protect passengers in the event of an accident. It was incredibly rewarding to see our work come to life and have a real impact on people's lives.

What Does an Automotive Engineering Technology Major Do: Career Opportunities

There are a variety of career paths you can pursue with an automotive engineering technology major. Some of the most popular include working for major car manufacturers, consulting firms, and government agencies. You can also specialize in areas like electric vehicles, autonomous vehicles, or motorsports.

One of the great things about this career path is that there's always something new to work on. As technology continues to evolve, there will be new challenges and opportunities to design and develop innovative vehicles and components.

What Does an Automotive Engineering Technology Major Do: Future Outlook

The future of the automotive industry is exciting and full of possibilities. With the rise of electric and autonomous vehicles, there will be a growing need for engineers and technicians with expertise in these areas. This means that the demand for automotive engineering technology majors is likely to continue to grow in the coming years.

Additionally, there will be opportunities to work on new technologies like hydrogen fuel cells, advanced materials, and new manufacturing techniques. This means that there will be plenty of room for innovation and creativity in this field.

What Does an Automotive Engineering Technology Major Do: How to Succeed in this Career Path

If you're interested in pursuing an automotive engineering technology major, there are a few things you can do to set yourself up for success. First, make sure you have a strong foundation in math, physics, and computer science. You'll also want to gain hands-on experience through internships or co-op experiences.

Networking is also important in this field. Attend industry events, join professional organizations, and connect with professionals on LinkedIn. This can help you stay up-to-date on the latest trends and technologies, and can even lead to job opportunities.

Question and Answer

Q: What kind of classes will I take as an automotive engineering technology major?

A: You'll take classes in math, physics, computer science, and engineering. You'll also take specialized courses in areas like vehicle dynamics, powertrain systems, and materials science.

Q: What kind of salary can I expect as an automotive engineering technology major?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual salary for mechanical engineers (which includes automotive engineers) is $88,430. However, salaries can vary depending on your experience, location, and employer.

Q: What kind of job growth can I expect in this field?

A: The Bureau of Labor Statistics projects that employment of mechanical engineers (which includes automotive engineers) will grow 4 percent from 2019 to 2029, about as fast as the average for all occupations.

Q: What kind of skills do I need to succeed as an automotive engineering technology major?

A: You'll need to be detail-oriented, analytical, and able to work well in a team environment. You'll also need excellent math, physics, and computer science skills.

Conclusion of What Does an Automotive Engineering Technology Major Do

If you're interested in cars, engineering, and technology, an automotive engineering technology major might be the perfect career path for you. With a strong foundation in math, physics, and computer science, and excellent analytical and teamwork skills, you'll be able to design, develop, and test innovative vehicles and components that have a real impact on people's lives.
