How Much Does A Product Manager Make A Year Find Out Here

Written by Eveline Jul 25, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career as a product manager but unsure about the salary? Or, are you already a product manager and wondering if you're being paid fairly? In this blog post, we'll answer the burning question: how much does a product manager make a year?

Product management can be a highly rewarding career, but it can also be challenging, with long hours and a lot of responsibility. One of the biggest pain points for product managers is figuring out if their salary is competitive with others in their industry and region.

The answer to how much does a product manager make a year is not straightforward. It depends on many factors, such as location, industry, company size, years of experience, and education. However, we can provide some general information to give you an idea of what to expect.

In general, product managers in the United States make an average of $107,000 per year. However, salaries can range from $63,000 to $162,000 per year, depending on the factors mentioned above. Product managers in the technology industry tend to make more than those in other industries, with an average salary of $123,000 per year.

My Experience as a Product Manager

As a product manager with five years of experience in the technology industry, I make around $130,000 per year. However, I live in a high-cost-of-living city, so my salary is adjusted accordingly. When I first started as a product manager, I made around $80,000 per year, but my salary increased as I gained more experience and took on more responsibility.

Factors That Affect Product Manager Salaries

Location is a significant factor that can affect a product manager's salary. Product managers in cities with a high cost of living, such as San Francisco or New York, will typically earn more than those in smaller or less expensive cities. Industry is another factor, with technology and finance typically paying higher salaries than other industries.

Company size can also impact a product manager's salary. Larger companies generally pay more than smaller companies, but smaller companies may offer other perks or benefits to make up for the lower salary. Years of experience and education are also important factors. Product managers with more experience or advanced degrees may be able to command higher salaries.

How to Negotiate a Higher Salary as a Product Manager

If you're a product manager looking to negotiate a higher salary, there are a few things you can do. First, do your research to find out what other product managers in your industry and location are making. Use this information to make a case for why you deserve a higher salary.

Second, highlight your accomplishments and successes in your current role. Show your employer how you've added value to the company and why you deserve to be compensated accordingly.

Additional Resources for Product Manager Salary Information

If you're still curious about how much does a product manager make a year, there are several resources you can use to gather more information. Glassdoor, Salary.com, and Payscale are all websites that provide salary information for various job titles and industries. You can also talk to recruiters or other product managers in your network to get a better understanding of salary ranges in your area.

Conclusion of How Much Does a Product Manager Make a Year

While the answer to how much does a product manager make a year is not straightforward, we hope this post has provided some general information to give you an idea of what to expect. Remember, many factors can impact your salary, so it's important to do your research and negotiate for what you're worth.
