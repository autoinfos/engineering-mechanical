Automotive Sales Job Salary How Much Can You Expect To Make

Written by Bardi Sep 30, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career in automotive sales but wondering how much you can expect to earn? Look no further! In this post, we'll dive into the world of automotive sales job salaries and answer all your burning questions.

The Pain Points of Automotive Sales Job Salary

One of the biggest pain points of working in automotive sales is the uncertainty of your income. Many salespeople work on commission, meaning their paychecks can vary greatly from month to month. Additionally, the long hours and high pressure of the job can make it difficult to maintain a healthy work-life balance.

What Can You Expect to Earn in Automotive Sales?

The answer to this question largely depends on your level of experience and the dealership you work for. According to Glassdoor, the average base salary for an automotive salesperson in the United States is around $40,000 per year. However, this number can increase significantly with commission and bonuses.

In addition, top-performing salespeople at luxury dealerships can earn well into six figures. It's important to keep in mind that these high earners are often experienced salespeople who have established a strong customer base.

The Benefits of Working in Automotive Sales

While the uncertainty of your income can be a downside of working in automotive sales, there are also many benefits. For one, the earning potential is high for top performers. Additionally, many dealerships offer benefits such as health insurance, retirement plans, and paid time off.

Working in automotive sales can also be a great opportunity for individuals who are passionate about cars and enjoy helping others find their dream vehicle. If you're a people person with a knack for sales, this could be the perfect career for you.

Commission vs. Salary: Which is Better?

One of the biggest decisions you'll make as an automotive salesperson is whether to work on commission or a set salary. While commission-based pay can be more unpredictable, it also has the potential to earn you more money if you're a top performer.

On the other hand, a set salary can provide more stability and a guaranteed paycheck each month. Many dealerships offer a combination of both, allowing salespeople to earn a base salary with the potential for commission and bonuses.

Tips for Increasing Your Automotive Sales Job Salary

If you're looking to increase your earning potential in automotive sales, there are a few tips to keep in mind. First and foremost, focus on building strong relationships with your customers. Repeat business and referrals can be a major source of income for salespeople.

Additionally, stay up to date on industry trends and new technologies. The more knowledgeable you are about the products you're selling, the more confident and successful you'll be in your role.

Personal Experience: My Journey in Automotive Sales

After working in various customer service roles, I decided to pursue a career in automotive sales. While the long hours and high pressure were challenging, I found the work to be incredibly rewarding. The feeling of helping a customer find their dream car was unbeatable.

Over time, I was able to establish a strong customer base and increase my earnings through commission and bonuses. While the income could be unpredictable at times, I found the earning potential to be one of the biggest benefits of working in automotive sales.

Question and Answer

Q: Is experience necessary to earn a high automotive sales job salary?

A: While experience can certainly be an asset in automotive sales, it's not necessarily a requirement for earning a high salary. Top performers who are able to build strong relationships with customers and consistently meet their sales goals can earn a high income regardless of their level of experience.

Q: Are there any downsides to working in automotive sales?

A: As with any job, there are both pros and cons to working in automotive sales. The long hours and high pressure can make it difficult to maintain a healthy work-life balance, and the uncertainty of your income can be a downside for some salespeople.

Q: What skills are necessary for success in automotive sales?

A: To be successful in automotive sales, it's important to have strong communication and interpersonal skills. Salespeople should also be knowledgeable about the products they're selling and able to build strong relationships with customers.

Q: How can I negotiate a higher salary in automotive sales?

A: When negotiating your salary in automotive sales, it's important to come prepared with data about industry benchmarks and your own sales performance. Additionally, be sure to highlight any unique skills or experiences that make you an asset to the dealership.

Conclusion of Automotive Sales Job Salary

Overall, a career in automotive sales can be both financially rewarding and personally fulfilling. While the uncertainty of your income can be a downside, the earning potential is high for top performers. By building strong relationships with customers and staying up to date on industry trends, you can increase your chances of success in this exciting field.
