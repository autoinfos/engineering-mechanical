How Much Does An It Person Earn In Kenya

Written by William Jul 11, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career in the IT field in Kenya but wondering what kind of salary you can expect? You're not alone. Salary is an important factor to consider when choosing a career path, and it can be difficult to navigate the different industries and positions to determine what you could earn.

IT professionals in Kenya face several pain points when it comes to salary. One of the biggest issues is the lack of standardization in salaries across the industry. Salaries can vary greatly depending on the company, location, and specific job requirements. Additionally, many IT professionals struggle to negotiate for higher salaries due to a lack of transparency around salary information and a fear of being replaced by cheaper workers.

So, how much does an IT person earn in Kenya? The answer is that it depends. According to Payscale, the average salary for an IT professional in Kenya is KES 732,000 per year. However, this number can range from KES 150,000 to KES 3,000,000 per year depending on factors such as experience, job title, and location.

In summary, while there is no standard salary for IT professionals in Kenya, the average salary is around KES 732,000 per year. Factors such as experience, job title, and location can greatly impact this number.

What Factors Affect IT Salaries in Kenya?

One factor that can greatly impact an IT professional's salary in Kenya is their level of experience. Generally, more experienced professionals can command higher salaries due to their expertise and track record. Additionally, the specific job title and responsibilities can play a role in salary negotiations. For example, a software engineer may earn a different salary than a network administrator.

Location is another important factor to consider when it comes to IT salaries in Kenya. Salaries can vary greatly depending on the region and city. For example, IT professionals working in Nairobi may earn a higher salary than those working in a smaller city or rural area.

Personal Experience with IT Salaries in Kenya

As an IT professional in Kenya with 5 years of experience, I have found that negotiating for a higher salary can be challenging. Many companies are not transparent about their salary structures, which can make it difficult to know what to ask for during negotiations. Additionally, I have found that some employers are hesitant to invest in their IT departments, which can lead to lower salaries and limited career growth opportunities.

How to Increase Your IT Salary in Kenya

If you're looking to increase your IT salary in Kenya, there are several strategies you can try. One is to seek out training or certifications that can demonstrate your expertise and value to potential employers. Additionally, networking with other professionals in the industry can help you learn about new job opportunities and negotiate for higher salaries. Finally, it's important to research the market and understand what other IT professionals in your area are earning to ensure that you are being fairly compensated.

Salary Comparison: IT vs Other Industries in Kenya

While IT salaries in Kenya can vary greatly, they generally compare favorably to salaries in other industries. For example, the average salary for a teacher in Kenya is around KES 300,000 per year, while the average salary for a doctor is around KES 1,800,000 per year. IT professionals can earn salaries that fall within this range depending on their level of experience and job title.

Conclusion of How Much Does an IT Person Earn in Kenya

In conclusion, IT salaries in Kenya can be difficult to navigate due to the lack of standardization and transparency in the industry. However, with the right strategies and research, IT professionals can earn competitive salaries that compare favorably to other industries. Factors such as experience, job title, and location can greatly impact an IT professional's salary, so it's important to understand the market and negotiate effectively for fair compensation.
