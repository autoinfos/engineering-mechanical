How Much Does A Service Manager Make At A Dealership

Written by Frank Aug 26, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Working in the automotive industry can be an exciting career choice for many people. However, working as a service manager at a dealership can come with its own set of challenges, including concerns about compensation. In this article, we will explore how much a service manager can expect to earn at a dealership.

When it comes to working as a service manager at a dealership, one of the biggest pain points for many people is uncertainty about pay. The automotive industry is known for being competitive, and many people wonder if they will be fairly compensated for their work.

According to Glassdoor, the average salary for a service manager at a dealership is around $65,000 per year. However, this can vary depending on factors such as location, years of experience, and the specific dealership. Some service managers may earn more than this, while others may earn less.

In summary, service managers at a dealership can expect to earn an average salary of around $65,000 per year. However, this can vary depending on a range of factors.

Personal Experience

Throughout my career in the automotive industry, I have had the opportunity to work with many service managers. From my experience, I have seen that pay can vary widely depending on the dealership and other factors. For example, service managers at luxury car dealerships may earn more than those at more budget-friendly dealerships.

Factors Affecting Service Manager Pay

There are many factors that can affect a service manager's pay at a dealership. One of the biggest factors is location - service managers in larger cities may earn more than those in smaller towns. Additionally, years of experience can also play a role in determining pay. Service managers with more experience may be able to negotiate a higher salary.

Dealer Size

The size of the dealership can also impact service manager pay. Larger dealerships may have more customers and a larger service department, which could result in higher pay for service managers. However, larger dealerships may also have more competition for positions, which could impact pay in the opposite direction.

Negotiation

Finally, it's important to remember that service managers can negotiate their pay with their employer. If you feel that you are not being fairly compensated for your work, it may be worth discussing this with your employer to see if a raise or other compensation is possible.

Personal Experience

In my personal experience, I have seen service managers negotiate their pay with their employer with success. However, it's important to approach this conversation professionally and with evidence to support your argument.

Question and Answer

Q: Can service managers earn bonuses or commissions in addition to their base pay?

A: Yes, many service managers at dealerships can earn bonuses or commissions based on the performance of their department.

Q: How much can service managers earn in bonuses or commissions?

A: This can vary widely depending on the dealership and the service department's performance. However, it's not uncommon for service managers to earn several thousand dollars per year in bonuses or commissions.

Q: Is there room for growth in service manager salaries?

A: Yes, there is the potential for service managers to earn higher salaries as they gain more experience and take on more responsibilities within the dealership.

Q: Can service managers earn benefits in addition to their salary?

A: Yes, many service managers at dealerships can earn benefits such as health insurance, retirement plans, and paid time off.

Conclusion of How Much Does a Service Manager Make at a Dealership

In conclusion, service managers at a dealership can expect to earn an average salary of around $65,000 per year, although this can vary depending on location, experience, and other factors. However, it's important to remember that service managers can negotiate their pay and earn additional compensation through bonuses or commissions. If you're considering a career as a service manager at a dealership, it's important to research the specific dealership and location to get a better idea of what you can expect in terms of compensation.
