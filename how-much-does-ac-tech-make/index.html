How Much Does An Ac Tech Make

Written by Frank Oct 04, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Have you ever wondered how much an AC tech makes? If you're considering a career in HVAC, it's important to know what kind of salary you can expect. The good news is that HVAC is a growing industry, and there's never been a better time to become an AC technician.

As with any career, there are some pain points associated with becoming an AC tech. For example, you'll need to go through training and possibly obtain certification, which can be time-consuming and expensive. Additionally, the job can be physically demanding, and you may have to work in tight, uncomfortable spaces. However, for those who are passionate about fixing things and helping people, the rewards can outweigh the challenges.

So, how much does an AC tech make? The answer depends on several factors, including location, experience, and education. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for heating, air conditioning, and refrigeration mechanics and installers was $50,590 in May 2020. However, this figure can vary widely depending on where you live and work.

In general, AC techs who work in large cities or areas with high demand for HVAC services tend to earn more than those in rural areas. Additionally, those with more experience or advanced certifications can command higher salaries. Some AC techs may also have the opportunity to earn overtime pay or bonuses, particularly during peak seasons.

My Personal Experience as an AC Tech

When I first started working as an AC tech, I was making around $40,000 per year. However, as I gained more experience and became certified in certain areas, I was able to negotiate higher pay. Now, after several years in the field, I make over $60,000 per year, which is a comfortable salary for my family and me.

One thing I've learned is that there's always room for growth in the HVAC industry. Whether you want to specialize in a certain area or move up into management, there are plenty of opportunities to advance your career and increase your earning potential.

How to Become an AC Tech

If you're interested in becoming an AC tech, there are several steps you'll need to take. First, you'll need to complete a training program, which can be found at many vocational schools or community colleges. These programs typically take anywhere from six months to two years to complete.

After completing your training, you may want to obtain certification from a professional organization such as HVAC Excellence or NATE (North American Technician Excellence). This can help you stand out to potential employers and demonstrate your expertise in the field.

Once you have the necessary training and certification, you can start applying for jobs as an AC tech. Many HVAC companies offer on-the-job training or apprenticeships, which can be a great way to gain experience and advance your skills.

How Much Does an Entry-Level AC Tech Make?

As an entry-level AC tech, you can expect to make around $30,000 to $40,000 per year. However, this can vary depending on your location and the demand for HVAC services in your area. With experience and certifications, you can expect to earn more over time.

How to Negotiate Your Salary as an AC Tech

If you're already working as an AC tech and want to negotiate a higher salary, there are a few things you can do. First, make sure you've done your research and know what the average salary is for someone in your position with your level of experience and qualifications.

Next, be prepared to demonstrate your value to your employer. Have you taken on additional responsibilities or completed any additional training or certifications? Have you brought in new clients or helped to increase profits? These are all things that can help you make a case for a higher salary.

Conclusion

Overall, becoming an AC tech can be a rewarding career choice with plenty of opportunities for growth and advancement. While the salary may not be as high as some other industries, it's a stable and reliable field with a strong demand for skilled workers. With the right training and certification, you can earn a comfortable salary and enjoy a fulfilling career in HVAC.

Question and Answer

Q: How much does an experienced AC tech make?

A: Experienced AC techs can make upwards of $70,000 or more per year, depending on their location and qualifications.

Q: Do AC techs get benefits?

A: Many HVAC companies offer benefits such as health insurance, retirement plans, and paid time off to their employees.

Q: Is there a high demand for AC techs?

A: Yes, there is currently a high demand for skilled HVAC technicians, and this trend is expected to continue as more people seek out energy-efficient and environmentally-friendly heating and cooling solutions.

Q: Can AC techs work independently?

A: Yes, some AC techs choose to work independently or start their own HVAC businesses. However, this requires additional training and certifications, as well as the ability to manage and grow a business.
