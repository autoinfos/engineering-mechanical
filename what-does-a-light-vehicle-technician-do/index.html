What Does A Light Vehicle Technician Do

Written by Eveline Jul 03, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you curious about what a light vehicle technician does? Do you want to know if this is the right career path for you? In this article, we will explore the ins and outs of being a light vehicle technician and what this job entails.

As a light vehicle technician, you will be responsible for the maintenance and repair of cars, vans, and light trucks. This can include everything from oil changes and tire rotations to diagnosing and fixing complex mechanical issues.

If you enjoy working with your hands, problem-solving, and have a passion for cars, this could be the perfect career for you. However, being a light vehicle technician is not for everyone. It can be physically demanding, and you will need to have a strong attention to detail and the ability to work under pressure.

What Does a Light Vehicle Technician Do?

As mentioned earlier, a light vehicle technician is responsible for the maintenance and repair of cars, vans, and light trucks. This can include:

Diagnosing and Fixing Mechanical Issues

One of the most important responsibilities of a light vehicle technician is diagnosing and fixing mechanical issues. This can range from something as simple as changing a broken belt to something more complex like a faulty transmission.

How to Diagnose and Fix Mechanical Issues

Diagnosing and fixing mechanical issues requires a strong understanding of the inner workings of vehicles. You will need to be able to identify the root cause of the problem and have the knowledge and skills to fix it. This can involve using specialized tools and equipment, as well as conducting extensive research to find the right solution.

Performing Routine Maintenance

Another important responsibility of a light vehicle technician is performing routine maintenance on vehicles. This can include:

Oil Changes

Changing the oil in a vehicle is one of the most common maintenance tasks that a light vehicle technician will perform. This involves draining the old oil, replacing the oil filter, and adding new oil to the engine.

How to Perform an Oil Change

Performing an oil change requires a strong attention to detail and the ability to follow specific procedures. You will need to know the type of oil to use, the correct amount to add, and how to properly dispose of the old oil.

Tire Rotations

Tire rotations involve moving the tires on a vehicle to ensure even wear and tear. This can help to extend the life of the tires and improve overall vehicle performance.

How to Perform a Tire Rotation

Performing a tire rotation requires knowledge of the proper rotation pattern for the specific vehicle. You will need to remove the tires, rotate them according to the pattern, and then re-install them.

Question and Answer

What qualifications do you need to become a light vehicle technician?

To become a light vehicle technician, you will typically need to complete a vocational or technical training program in automotive service technology. You may also need to obtain certification from a professional organization.

What is the average salary for a light vehicle technician?

The average salary for a light vehicle technician varies depending on factors such as experience, location, and employer. However, according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for automotive service technicians and mechanics was $44,050 in May 2020.

What are the working conditions like for a light vehicle technician?

Working conditions for a light vehicle technician can vary depending on the specific job and employer. However, this job can be physically demanding and may require working in uncomfortable positions or tight spaces. You may also need to work outside in all types of weather conditions.

What are some of the challenges of being a light vehicle technician?

Some of the challenges of being a light vehicle technician include the physical demands of the job, the need to constantly stay up-to-date with new technologies and advancements in the field, and the pressure to complete jobs quickly and efficiently.

Conclusion of What Does a Light Vehicle Technician Do

Being a light vehicle technician can be a rewarding career for those who have a passion for cars and enjoy working with their hands. It requires a strong attention to detail, the ability to problem-solve, and the willingness to constantly learn and adapt to new technologies. If you think this is the right career path for you, consider exploring vocational or technical training programs in automotive service technology to get started.
