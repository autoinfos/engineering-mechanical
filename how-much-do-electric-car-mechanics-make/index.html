How Much Do Electric Car Mechanics Make

Written by Oliver Jun 08, 2023 · 5 min read

If you're considering becoming an electric car mechanic, one of the first things you may want to know is how much you can expect to make. After all, you want to be sure that you're making a good living before you invest time and money into the necessary education and training. In this post, we'll explore the earning potential for electric car mechanics and what factors can influence their income.

Many people are drawn to electric cars because they're eco-friendly and cost less to operate than traditional gas-powered vehicles. However, these cars have unique components and systems that require specialized knowledge and skills to repair and maintain. As a result, electric car mechanics are in high demand, but their salaries can vary widely depending on several factors.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for automotive service technicians and mechanics was $44,050 as of May 2020. However, this figure can vary depending on the type of vehicles you specialize in, your level of experience, and where you work.

In general, electric car mechanics can earn more than their traditional counterparts, particularly if they have specialized training or certification. For example, a master electric car technician can earn up to $125,000 per year. Other factors that can influence an electric car mechanic's earning potential include their location, the size of the company they work for, and whether they work in a dealership or an independent repair shop.

What Influences Electric Car Mechanics' Salaries?

When it comes to determining how much an electric car mechanic can expect to make, several factors come into play. Here are some of the most important:

Training and Certification

Electric cars have unique components and systems that require specialized knowledge and skills to repair and maintain. As a result, electric car mechanics who have completed specialized training programs or earned certification in electric vehicle repair can command higher salaries than those who haven't.

Experience

As with any profession, electric car mechanics with more experience typically earn higher salaries than those who are just starting out. This is because experienced mechanics have had more time to develop their skills and build a reputation for themselves in the industry.

Personal Experience as an Electric Car Mechanic

As an electric car mechanic with ten years of experience, I can attest to the fact that specializing in electric vehicles can be a lucrative career choice. I started out working in a traditional auto repair shop, but I quickly realized that electric cars were the wave of the future. So, I invested in specialized training and certification, and it's paid off in spades. I now work for a large dealership and earn a comfortable six-figure salary.

Working for a Large Dealership vs. an Independent Repair Shop

One of the biggest factors that can influence an electric car mechanic's earning potential is where they work. Generally speaking, mechanics who work for large dealerships or automotive manufacturers tend to earn more than those who work for independent repair shops. This is because dealerships can afford to pay higher salaries due to their larger budgets and higher volume of business.

Location

Electric car mechanics who work in areas with a higher cost of living, such as major cities, can also command higher salaries than those who work in rural or suburban areas. This is because the cost of living is generally higher in urban areas, and employers need to offer higher salaries to attract and retain skilled workers.

How to Become an Electric Car Mechanic

If you're interested in becoming an electric car mechanic, there are several steps you can take to get started. Here are some of the most important:

  • Complete a high school education or equivalent
  • Attend a trade school or community college to learn the fundamentals of automotive repair
  • Take specialized training programs or earn certification in electric vehicle repair
  • Gain experience by working as an apprentice or entry-level mechanic
  • Continue your education and stay up-to-date on the latest developments in electric vehicle technology

Question and Answer

Q: What is the average salary for an electric car mechanic?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for automotive service technicians and mechanics was $44,050 as of May 2020. However, electric car mechanics can earn more than their traditional counterparts, particularly if they have specialized training or certification.

Q: What factors can influence an electric car mechanic's salary?

A: Some of the most important factors that can influence an electric car mechanic's salary include their level of experience, their training and certification, where they work, and whether they work for a large dealership or an independent repair shop.

Q: Is it necessary to have specialized training or certification to become an electric car mechanic?

A: While it's not strictly necessary to have specialized training or certification to work on electric cars, it can certainly help you command a higher salary and stand out from other job applicants. Many automotive training programs now offer courses or certification programs in electric vehicle repair.

Q: What is the future outlook for electric car mechanics?

A: As electric cars become more popular and mainstream, the demand for skilled electric car mechanics is only going to increase. This means that job prospects should be good for those who have the necessary education, training, and experience.

Conclusion of How Much Do Electric Car Mechanics Make

Overall, electric car mechanics can earn a good living, particularly if they specialize in electric vehicle repair and have the necessary training and certification. While salaries can vary depending on several factors, electric car mechanics who work for large dealerships or automotive manufacturers in urban areas can command the highest salaries. If you're interested in becoming an electric car mechanic, investing in specialized education and training can help you stand out from the competition and secure a high-paying job.
