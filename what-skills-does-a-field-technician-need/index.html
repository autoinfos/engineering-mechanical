What Skills Does A Field Technician Need

Written by Robby Oct 18, 2023 Â· 4 min read

As a field technician, you are responsible for maintaining and repairing equipment in the field. This can be a challenging job, but it can also be very rewarding. In order to be successful as a field technician, you need to have a variety of skills. In this post, we will discuss what skills a field technician needs and how to develop them.

One of the biggest pain points for field technicians is the need to work independently. Unlike in an office setting, you may not have coworkers or supervisors nearby to ask for help or guidance. This means that you need to be able to think on your feet and problem-solve independently.

A field technician needs to have strong technical skills. This includes a solid understanding of the equipment they are working on, as well as the ability to troubleshoot and diagnose problems. They also need to be able to read technical manuals and schematics to help them solve problems.

In addition to technical skills, a field technician also needs to have strong communication skills. They need to be able to communicate effectively with customers and coworkers. This includes being able to explain technical information in a way that is easy for others to understand.

In summary, a field technician needs to have a variety of skills, including technical skills, problem-solving skills, and communication skills. They also need to be able to work independently and think on their feet.

Technical Skills

When I first started my job as a field technician, I was overwhelmed by the amount of technical information I needed to know. However, over time, I developed strong technical skills. This included a solid understanding of the equipment I was working on, as well as the ability to troubleshoot and diagnose problems.

To develop your technical skills, you should start by reading technical manuals and schematics. You should also try to gain hands-on experience by working with the equipment you will be responsible for maintaining and repairing.

Communication Skills

As a field technician, you will be communicating with customers and coworkers on a regular basis. This means that you need to have strong communication skills. You should be able to explain technical information in a way that is easy for others to understand.

One way to develop your communication skills is to practice explaining technical information to a friend or family member who is not familiar with the equipment you are working on. This will help you develop your ability to explain technical information in a way that is easy for others to understand.

Problem-Solving Skills

As a field technician, you will encounter a variety of problems on a daily basis. This means that you need to have strong problem-solving skills. You should be able to think on your feet and come up with creative solutions to problems.

To develop your problem-solving skills, you should start by practicing with simple problems. As you become more comfortable with problem-solving, you can move on to more complex problems.

Independence

As mentioned earlier, one of the biggest challenges for field technicians is the need to work independently. This means that you need to be able to think on your feet and problem-solve without the help of coworkers or supervisors.

To develop your independence, you should start by taking on small projects and working your way up to larger projects. This will help you build confidence in your ability to work independently.

Q&A

Q: What are some common equipment field technicians work on?

A: Field technicians work on a variety of equipment, including HVAC systems, industrial machinery, and medical equipment.

Q: How can I improve my technical skills?

A: You can improve your technical skills by reading technical manuals and schematics and gaining hands-on experience.

Q: What are some common problems field technicians encounter?

A: Field technicians encounter a variety of problems, including equipment malfunctions, wiring issues, and software failures.

Q: How can I improve my problem-solving skills?

A: You can improve your problem-solving skills by practicing with simple problems and gradually moving on to more complex problems.

Conclusion

In order to be a successful field technician, you need to have a variety of skills. This includes technical skills, problem-solving skills, communication skills, and the ability to work independently. By developing these skills, you can become a valuable asset to your organization and enjoy a rewarding career as a field technician.
