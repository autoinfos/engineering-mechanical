Software Engineer Salary In Kenya 2020 An In Depth Look

Written by William Nov 14, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you a software engineer in Kenya wondering how your salary stacks up against others in the industry? Or perhaps you're considering a career in software engineering and want to know what kind of salary you can expect? Look no further - this post will provide an in-depth look at software engineer salary in Kenya for 2020.

For many software engineers in Kenya, salary is a pain point. While the demand for software engineers is high, salaries often do not reflect this demand. Additionally, there can be a lack of transparency around salaries, making it difficult for software engineers to negotiate fair compensation.

So, what can software engineers in Kenya expect to make in 2020? According to data from Payscale, the average salary for a software engineer in Kenya is KES 1,654,919 per year. However, this number can vary widely depending on factors such as experience, location, and industry.

In summary, while software engineering can be a lucrative career in Kenya, it's important to do your research and negotiate fair compensation. Keep in mind that salary is just one factor to consider when evaluating a job offer - other factors such as benefits, work-life balance, and growth opportunities should also be taken into account.

What Do Different Levels of Experience Mean for Software Engineer Salary in Kenya 2020?

As mentioned earlier, experience is a key factor in determining software engineer salary in Kenya. Here's a breakdown of how salary can vary based on experience:

Entry-level: Software engineers with 0-2 years of experience can expect to make between KES 420,000 and KES 1,200,000 per year.

Mid-level: Software engineers with 2-5 years of experience can expect to make between KES 800,000 and KES 2,200,000 per year.

Senior-level: Software engineers with 5+ years of experience can expect to make between KES 1,500,000 and KES 4,000,000 per year.

Location Matters: How Does Software Engineer Salary in Kenya 2020 Vary by Region?

Location is another important factor to consider when evaluating software engineer salary in Kenya. Here's a breakdown of how salary can vary by region:

Nairobi: Software engineers in Nairobi can expect to make slightly higher salaries than in other regions, with an average salary of KES 1,803,842 per year.

Mombasa: Software engineers in Mombasa can expect to make slightly lower salaries than in Nairobi, with an average salary of KES 1,441,237 per year.

Eldoret: Software engineers in Eldoret can expect to make the lowest salaries of the three regions mentioned, with an average salary of KES 1,200,000 per year.

Industry Matters: How Does Software Engineer Salary in Kenya 2020 Vary by Industry?

The industry you work in can also have a significant impact on your software engineer salary in Kenya. Here are a few industries and their associated salaries:

Information technology: This is the highest-paying industry for software engineers in Kenya, with an average salary of KES 1,771,429 per year.

Financial services: Software engineers in this industry can expect to make an average salary of KES 1,638,889 per year.

Healthcare: Software engineers in healthcare can expect to make an average salary of KES 1,200,000 per year.

How Can You Negotiate Fair Compensation for Software Engineer Salary in Kenya 2020?

If you're a software engineer in Kenya, negotiating fair compensation can be a challenge. Here are a few tips to help you navigate the process:

Do your research: Before negotiating, do your research to find out what other software engineers in your industry and region are making. Use this information to inform your negotiations.

Be confident: Remember that you bring valuable skills to the table, and don't be afraid to advocate for yourself during negotiations.

Consider other factors: While salary is important, don't forget to consider other factors such as benefits, work-life balance, and growth opportunities when evaluating a job offer.
