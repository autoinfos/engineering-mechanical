How Much Do Pharmacy Technicians Make In Ontario

Written by Eveline Aug 25, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Have you ever wondered how much pharmacy technicians make in Ontario? Perhaps you're considering a career in pharmacy or you're already working in the field and want to know if you're being fairly compensated. Whatever the reason, understanding the average salary for pharmacy technicians in Ontario can help you make informed decisions about your career.

For many pharmacy technicians, salary is a top concern. While pharmacy technicians in Ontario generally enjoy good job security and a high degree of job satisfaction, the pay scale can vary widely depending on a number of factors.

So, how much do pharmacy technicians make in Ontario? According to the latest data from Statistics Canada, the average hourly wage for pharmacy technicians in Ontario is $22.32. This works out to an average annual salary of just over $46,000.

While this is a good starting point, it's important to note that many factors can influence your actual salary as a pharmacy technician. Factors such as your level of experience, your location, your education and certification, and the size and type of pharmacy where you work can all have an impact on your pay.

What Affects Pharmacy Technician Salaries in Ontario?

One of the biggest factors affecting pharmacy technician salaries in Ontario is your level of experience. As with many professions, pharmacy technicians typically earn higher salaries as they gain more experience and become more skilled in their work. Additionally, pharmacists who hold certifications or other specialized training may also be able to command higher salaries.

Another important factor is your location. Salaries for pharmacy technicians can vary widely depending on where you work in Ontario. In general, larger and more urban areas tend to offer higher salaries, while smaller or more rural communities may offer lower salaries.

The size and type of pharmacy where you work can also impact your salary. For example, pharmacy technicians who work in hospitals or other large healthcare facilities may be able to earn higher salaries than those who work in smaller community pharmacies.

Education and Certification

Your education and certification can also have an impact on your salary as a pharmacy technician. In Ontario, pharmacy technicians must be registered with the Ontario College of Pharmacists and hold a certificate from a recognized pharmacy technician program. Those who hold additional certifications or specialized training may be able to command higher salaries.

How to Increase Your Salary as a Pharmacy Technician in Ontario

If you're looking to increase your salary as a pharmacy technician in Ontario, there are several steps you can take. First, consider pursuing additional education or certification to expand your skill set and become more valuable to potential employers.

You can also look for job opportunities in larger or more urban areas, where salaries may be higher. Additionally, consider networking with other pharmacy technicians and professionals in the field to learn about new job openings and opportunities for career advancement.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the starting salary for pharmacy technicians in Ontario?

A: The starting salary for pharmacy technicians in Ontario can vary depending on a number of factors, but the average hourly wage is around $22.32.

Q: Do I need to be certified to work as a pharmacy technician in Ontario?

A: Yes, pharmacy technicians in Ontario must be registered with the Ontario College of Pharmacists and hold a certificate from a recognized pharmacy technician program.

Q: Can I increase my salary as a pharmacy technician in Ontario?

A: Yes, there are several steps you can take to increase your salary as a pharmacy technician in Ontario. These include pursuing additional education or certification, networking with other professionals in the field, and looking for job opportunities in larger or more urban areas.

Q: What is the average annual salary for pharmacy technicians in Ontario?

A: According to Statistics Canada, the average annual salary for pharmacy technicians in Ontario is just over $46,000.

Conclusion of How Much Do Pharmacy Technicians Make in Ontario

While salary is an important consideration for pharmacy technicians in Ontario, it's important to remember that many factors can impact your actual pay. By understanding the various factors that influence pharmacy technician salaries, you can make informed decisions about your career and take steps to increase your earning potential. Whether you're just starting out in the field or you're a seasoned professional, knowing how much pharmacy technicians make in Ontario can help you make the most of your career.
