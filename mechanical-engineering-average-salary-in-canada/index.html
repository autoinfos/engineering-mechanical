Mechanical Engineering Average Salary In Canada All You Need To Know

Written by Bardi Aug 03, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you considering a career in mechanical engineering in Canada but unsure of what to expect in terms of salary? The engineering field is vast and can be quite lucrative, but it can also be overwhelming to navigate, especially when it comes to compensation. In this article, we'll explore the average salary for mechanical engineers in Canada and provide insight into what you can expect in this field.

As with any profession, there are certain pain points to consider when it comes to mechanical engineering salaries in Canada. For starters, the sometimes long and challenging education process required to become a mechanical engineer can be costly, and it's important to understand the potential return on investment. Additionally, the job market can be highly competitive, and finding a job that aligns with your salary expectations can be challenging.

The average salary for mechanical engineers in Canada is around $75,000 CAD per year, with the lowest 10% earning around $52,000 CAD per year and the highest 10% earning around $106,000 CAD per year. This can vary depending on factors such as location, level of education, and years of experience. However, it's worth noting that mechanical engineering is generally considered one of the higher-paying engineering fields.

In summary, mechanical engineering in Canada can be a lucrative career choice with an average salary of around $75,000 CAD per year. While there are challenges to navigate such as education costs and job market competitiveness, the potential return on investment and salary range make it a compelling option for those interested in the field.

What is Mechanical Engineering Average Salary in Canada?

Personally, I've been working as a mechanical engineer in Canada for the past 5 years, and I can say that the average salary is a good representation of what you can expect. Of course, this can vary depending on your level of education, years of experience, and location. For example, if you work in a more expensive city like Vancouver or Toronto, your salary may be higher to account for the higher cost of living.

Factors that Affect Mechanical Engineering Average Salary in Canada

One of the biggest factors that affect mechanical engineering salaries in Canada is education. Generally, the more education you have, the higher your salary will be. For example, holding a master's degree in mechanical engineering can increase your salary by around 20% compared to those with only a bachelor's degree. Additionally, years of experience can also play a role in salary, with those with more experience typically earning more.

Location and Mechanical Engineering Average Salary in Canada

As previously mentioned, location can also play a role in mechanical engineering salaries in Canada. For example, working in a more expensive city like Vancouver or Toronto can mean a higher salary to account for the higher cost of living. Additionally, certain industries may pay more depending on where you're located, such as the oil and gas industry in Alberta.

How to Increase Your Mechanical Engineering Average Salary in Canada

If you're looking to increase your mechanical engineering salary in Canada, there are a few things you can do. For starters, investing in higher education can pay off in the long run by opening up higher-paying job opportunities. Additionally, gaining specialized skills or certifications can also make you more marketable and in turn, increase your salary potential.
