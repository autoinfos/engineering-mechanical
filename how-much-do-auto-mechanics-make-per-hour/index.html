How Much Do Auto Mechanics Make Per Hour

Written by Petter Oct 23, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you interested in becoming an auto mechanic? Or are you currently working as an auto mechanic and wondering if you're getting paid enough? One of the questions that may be on your mind is, "How much do auto mechanics make per hour?"

Many auto mechanics often feel undervalued and underpaid for the work they do. They spend countless hours fixing cars, diagnosing problems, and ensuring that vehicles are safe to drive. However, they may not always receive the compensation they deserve.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median hourly wage for auto mechanics in the United States is $20.24 per hour. However, this can vary depending on factors such as location, experience, and certifications.

In addition, auto mechanics who specialize in certain areas such as diesel engines, transmissions, or hybrid vehicles may earn higher wages. Those who work in dealerships or repair shops may also receive benefits such as health insurance and retirement plans.

Personal Experience

As a former auto mechanic, I can attest to the fact that the hourly wage can be frustrating at times. However, I found that by specializing in a certain area and obtaining additional certifications, I was able to increase my pay and advance in my career.

Factors That Affect Auto Mechanic Pay

Aside from location and specialization, there are other factors that can affect how much an auto mechanic makes per hour. Experience is one of the biggest factors - those who have been working in the industry for many years may earn a higher wage than those who are just starting out.

Certifications are also important - obtaining certifications from organizations such as the National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence (ASE) can demonstrate to employers that you have the necessary skills and knowledge to perform your job effectively.

Benefits of Being an Auto Mechanic

Although the hourly wage for auto mechanics may not be as high as some other professions, there are still many benefits to working in this field. One of the biggest benefits is job security - as long as there are cars on the road, there will be a need for auto mechanics to repair and maintain them.

In addition, auto mechanics often have the opportunity to work on a variety of vehicles and learn new skills. They may also have the satisfaction of knowing that they are helping people by keeping their vehicles safe and reliable.

Tips for Increasing Your Auto Mechanic Salary

If you're currently working as an auto mechanic and are looking to increase your salary, there are a few things you can do. One is to specialize in a certain area and obtain additional certifications - this can make you more valuable to employers and increase your earning potential.

You can also consider working at a dealership or repair shop that offers benefits such as health insurance and retirement plans. Finally, networking with other auto mechanics and staying up-to-date on industry trends and technologies can help you stay competitive and increase your value as an employee.

Conclusion

While the hourly wage for auto mechanics may not be as high as some other professions, there are still many opportunities for growth and advancement in this field. By specializing in a certain area, obtaining certifications, and staying up-to-date on industry trends, auto mechanics can increase their earning potential and enjoy a fulfilling career.

Question and Answer

Q: How much do entry-level auto mechanics make per hour?

A: Entry-level auto mechanics may make around $12-$15 per hour, depending on their location and experience.

Q: How much do experienced auto mechanics make per hour?

A: Experienced auto mechanics may make upwards of $30 per hour, depending on their location, experience, and certifications.

Q: What are some of the benefits of working as an auto mechanic?

A: Some benefits of working as an auto mechanic include job security, the opportunity to work on a variety of vehicles and learn new skills, and the satisfaction of knowing that you are helping people by keeping their vehicles safe and reliable.

Q: What can auto mechanics do to increase their earning potential?

A: Auto mechanics can increase their earning potential by specializing in a certain area and obtaining additional certifications, working at a dealership or repair shop that offers benefits, and staying up-to-date on industry trends and technologies.
