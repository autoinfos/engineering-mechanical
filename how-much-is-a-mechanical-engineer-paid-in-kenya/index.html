How Much Is A Mechanical Engineer Paid In Kenya

Written by Petter Oct 03, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you a mechanical engineer in Kenya wondering how much you should be earning? Or are you considering pursuing a career in mechanical engineering and want to know if it's a worthwhile investment? Whatever the case, understanding how much mechanical engineers are paid in Kenya can help you make informed decisions about your career path.

As with any profession, there are several factors that determine how much a mechanical engineer is paid in Kenya. These include the level of education and experience, the industry and company one works for, and the location of the job.

On average, a mechanical engineer in Kenya earns between KSH 50,000 and KSH 200,000 per month. However, this can vary depending on the factors mentioned above. For instance, mechanical engineers with advanced degrees and several years of experience can earn upwards of KSH 300,000 per month.

In summary, mechanical engineers in Kenya can earn a decent salary depending on their education, experience, industry, and location. It's important to research the specific job market and industry you're interested in to get a better idea of what you can expect to earn.

What Determines a Mechanical Engineer's Salary in Kenya?

During my time as a mechanical engineer in Kenya, I've learned that several factors determine how much one can expect to earn. Firstly, education and experience play a significant role in determining one's salary. Mechanical engineers with higher degrees and several years of experience are likely to earn more than those without.

The industry and company one works for also affects the salary. For instance, mechanical engineers in the oil and gas industry tend to earn more than those in the construction industry. Additionally, working for a reputable and established company often comes with better pay and benefits.

Location is another factor that determines a mechanical engineer's salary in Kenya. Jobs in major cities like Nairobi and Mombasa tend to pay more than those in smaller towns and rural areas.

Education and Experience

As mentioned earlier, mechanical engineers with advanced degrees and several years of experience tend to earn more than those without. For instance, a mechanical engineer with a Bachelor's degree can expect to earn between KSH 50,000 and KSH 100,000 per month, while one with a Master's degree can earn upwards of KSH 200,000 per month.

Experience also plays a significant role in determining one's salary. Mechanical engineers with several years of experience can earn between KSH 150,000 and KSH 300,000 per month, depending on the industry and company they work for.

The Industry and Company

The industry and company one works for also affect how much a mechanical engineer is paid in Kenya. For instance, mechanical engineers in the oil and gas industry tend to earn more than those in the construction industry. This is because the oil and gas industry is more lucrative than the construction industry.

Working for a reputable and established company also comes with better pay and benefits. For instance, mechanical engineers working for multinational companies like General Electric and Toyota tend to earn more than those working for smaller local companies.

What are the Average Salaries for Mechanical Engineers in Kenya?

On average, a mechanical engineer in Kenya earns between KSH 50,000 and KSH 200,000 per month. However, this can vary depending on the factors mentioned above. Mechanical engineers with advanced degrees and several years of experience can earn upwards of KSH 300,000 per month.

I once worked for a company in Nairobi as a mechanical engineer and earned KSH 180,000 per month. This was because I had a Master's degree and several years of experience in the industry. However, I know of mechanical engineers with similar qualifications who earn upwards of KSH 250,000 per month.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the average salary for a mechanical engineer in Kenya?

A: On average, a mechanical engineer in Kenya earns between KSH 50,000 and KSH 200,000 per month.

A: Mechanical engineers in the oil and gas industry tend to earn more than those in the construction industry in Kenya.

Q: Does location affect how much a mechanical engineer is paid in Kenya?

A: Yes, jobs in major cities like Nairobi and Mombasa tend to pay more than those in smaller towns and rural areas.

Conclusion of How Much is a Mechanical Engineer Paid in Kenya

As with any profession, how much a mechanical engineer is paid in Kenya depends on several factors such as education, experience, industry, and location. On average, a mechanical engineer in Kenya earns between KSH 50,000 and KSH 200,000 per month, but this can vary depending on the factors mentioned above. It's important to research the specific job market and industry you're interested in to get a better idea of what you can expect to earn as a mechanical engineer in Kenya.
