Key Responsibilities of a Service Manager

Some key responsibilities of a service manager include:

  • Managing technicians
  • Scheduling repairs
  • Ordering parts
  • Ensuring customer satisfaction
  • Managing inventory
  • Managing finances

How to Become a Service Manager

To become a service manager, you typically need several years of experience in the automotive industry. A degree in business or a related field can also be helpful. It's important to have a deep understanding of automotive repair processes and be able to communicate effectively with customers and employees. Many service managers start out as technicians and work their way up through the ranks.
