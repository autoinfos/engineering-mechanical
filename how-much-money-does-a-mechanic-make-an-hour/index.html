How Much Money Does A Mechanic Make An Hour

Written by Christine Aug 31, 2023 · 4 min read

When you think of a mechanic, you may not consider how much they earn hourly. It can be a daunting task to figure out how much money does a mechanic make an hour. In this article, we will explore the salary of a mechanic and what factors affect their hourly wage.

The pay scale of a mechanic can vary based on different factors, such as location, level of experience, and type of employer. It can be challenging to determine how much money does a mechanic make an hour, but we are here to help.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median hourly wage for an automotive service technician and mechanic is $20.24. The lowest 10% of mechanics earn less than $12.44 per hour, and the highest 10% earn more than $32.19 per hour. The salary of a mechanic can be affected by various factors such as experience, location, and industry.

In conclusion, mechanics can earn a decent hourly wage, but it varies depending on the factors mentioned above. The median hourly wage for a mechanic is $20.24, but it can be lower or higher depending on the employer, experience, and location.

Factors that Affect the Salary of a Mechanic

When it comes to determining how much money does a mechanic make an hour, several factors come into play. One of the significant factors that affect the salary of a mechanic is their level of experience. A mechanic with more experience will earn more than someone who is just starting. Another factor that can affect the salary of a mechanic is their location. Mechanics in urban areas generally earn more than those in rural areas. The type of employer can also affect the salary of a mechanic. Mechanics who work for dealerships or large chain stores usually earn more than those who work for independent repair shops.

When I started working as a mechanic, I was earning around $15 an hour because I had no previous experience. However, as I gained more experience, my salary increased to $25 an hour. Now, after working for over ten years, I earn over $30 an hour.

Types of Mechanics and Their Salaries

There are different types of mechanics, and their salaries can vary based on their role. For example, diesel mechanics, who specialize in repairing large vehicles such as trucks and buses, earn more than regular automotive mechanics. The median hourly wage for a diesel mechanic is $24.34. Aviation mechanics, who specialize in repairing planes, earn a median hourly wage of $32.26.

How to Increase Your Salary as a Mechanic

If you are interested in increasing your salary as a mechanic, there are several ways you can do so. One of the best ways to increase your salary is to gain more experience. The more experience you have, the more valuable you become to employers, and the more they are willing to pay you. Another way to increase your salary is to get certified. There are several certifications available for mechanics, such as the ASE certification, which can increase your earning potential.

How to Become a Mechanic

If you are interested in becoming a mechanic, you must have a high school diploma or equivalent. You can also attend a vocational school or community college to receive formal training in automotive repair. Once you have completed your education, you can gain experience by working as an apprentice or entry-level mechanic.

Wrapping Up

Overall, the salary of a mechanic can vary based on different factors, such as level of experience, location, and type of employer. The median hourly wage for a mechanic is $20.24, but it can be lower or higher depending on the factors mentioned above. However, if you are interested in becoming a mechanic, there are several ways you can increase your earning potential, such as gaining more experience or getting certified.

Question and Answer

Q: How much does a mechanic apprentice make an hour?

A: An apprentice mechanic can earn between $9 and $15 per hour, depending on their location and employer.

Q: How much does a diesel mechanic make an hour?

A: The median hourly wage for a diesel mechanic is $24.34, but it can be lower or higher depending on their experience, location, and employer.

Q: Can a mechanic work part-time?

A: Yes, a mechanic can work part-time. However, their hourly wage may be affected by their employment status.

Q: How much does a mechanic make annually?

A: The median annual wage for a mechanic is $42,090, according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics.

Conclusion of How Much Money Does a Mechanic Make an Hour

In conclusion, the hourly wage of a mechanic can vary based on different factors, such as experience, location, and type of employer. The median hourly wage for a mechanic is $20.24, but it can be lower or higher depending on the factors mentioned above. However, there are several ways to increase your earning potential as a mechanic, such as gaining more experience or getting certified.
