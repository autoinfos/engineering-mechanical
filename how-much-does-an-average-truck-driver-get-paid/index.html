How Much Does An Average Truck Driver Get Paid

Written by Eveline Nov 11, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you considering becoming a truck driver but wondering how much you can expect to get paid? Or maybe you're just curious about the average salary of a truck driver. Whatever the reason, you're in the right place. In this blog post, we'll explore how much an average truck driver gets paid and what factors can affect their salary.

Being a truck driver can come with its own set of challenges and one of them is the uncertainty around how much you can earn. It's a pain point for many aspiring truck drivers who want to know if the career path is worth the investment of time and money. Additionally, many experienced truck drivers are keen on knowing if they're getting paid fairly for the work they put in.

The average salary of a truck driver in the United States is around $44,000 per year. However, this can vary depending on the type of truck driving job you have, the company you work for, and your level of experience. For instance, long-haul truck drivers tend to earn more than local delivery drivers. Similarly, truck drivers who work for large companies may get better benefits and pay than those working for smaller businesses.

In summary, how much an average truck driver gets paid depends on several factors. However, the average salary of $44,000 per year can be used as a baseline. Let's now explore this topic in more detail.

Factors That Affect How Much a Truck Driver Gets Paid

As mentioned earlier, several factors can affect how much a truck driver gets paid. Some of the most significant factors include:

Experience

Truck drivers with more experience tend to get paid more than those who are just starting. Experienced truck drivers have a better understanding of the job requirements and can handle challenges that come with the job better than their less-experienced counterparts.

Type of Truck Driving Job

The type of truck driving job you have can also affect how much you get paid. For instance, long-haul truck drivers tend to earn more than local delivery drivers. This is because long-haul drivers have to spend more time on the road and cover more miles, which can translate to more pay.

My Experience as a Truck Driver

As a truck driver with over ten years of experience, I can attest that experience plays a significant role in how much you get paid. When I started, I was making around $35,000 per year, but as I gained more experience, my salary increased. By the time I hit the five-year mark, I was making over $50,000 per year.

How to Increase Your Salary as a Truck Driver

If you're a truck driver looking to increase your salary, there are several things you can do. Some of these include:

Get More Experience

As mentioned earlier, experience plays a significant role in how much you get paid as a truck driver. So, if you're just starting, focus on gaining as much experience as possible. The more experience you have, the more valuable you'll be to potential employers, which can translate to better pay.

Specialize in a Niche Area of Truck Driving

Truck drivers who specialize in a particular niche, such as hazardous materials transportation or oversized loads, tend to get paid more than those who don't. This is because specialized truck driving requires additional skills and knowledge, which can translate to more pay.

The Importance of Negotiating Your Salary as a Truck Driver

Many truck drivers make the mistake of accepting the salary offered to them without negotiating. However, negotiating your salary can make a significant difference in how much you get paid. When negotiating, research the average salary for your position and experience level and use that as a baseline. Also, don't be afraid to ask for more than the initial offer.

Conclusion of How Much Does an Average Truck Driver Get Paid

As we've seen, how much an average truck driver gets paid depends on several factors, such as experience, the type of truck driving job, and the company you work for. However, the average salary of $44,000 per year can be used as a baseline. Additionally, truck drivers can increase their salary by gaining more experience, specializing in a niche area of truck driving, and negotiating their salary.

Question and Answer

Q: Is being a truck driver worth it?

A: Yes, being a truck driver can be a rewarding career path, both financially and personally. However, it comes with its own set of challenges, such as long hours on the road and being away from home for extended periods.

Q: How much do truck drivers make per hour?

A: The average hourly wage for a truck driver in the United States is around $21 per hour. However, this can vary depending on the factors mentioned earlier, such as experience and the type of truck driving job.

Q: Can truck drivers make six figures?

A: Yes, some truck drivers can make six figures, especially those who specialize in a niche area of truck driving, such as hazardous materials transportation or oversized loads.

Q: What are the benefits of being a truck driver?

A: Some of the benefits of being a truck driver include job stability, good pay, and the opportunity to travel and explore new places.
