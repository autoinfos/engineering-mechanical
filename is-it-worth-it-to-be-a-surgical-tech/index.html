Is It Worth It To Be A Surgical Tech

Written by Christine Sep 05, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career as a surgical tech? Before you make a decision, it's important to weigh the pros and cons of this field. While being a surgical tech can be rewarding, it also comes with its own set of challenges.

One of the biggest pain points of being a surgical tech is the high stress environment. Working in a surgical setting can be fast-paced and require quick thinking under pressure. Additionally, long hours on your feet and exposure to potentially infectious materials can take a toll on your physical and mental health.

However, for those who are passionate about healthcare, being a surgical tech can be an incredibly fulfilling career. Not only do you get to work closely with doctors and other healthcare professionals, but you also get to help improve the lives of patients.

In summary, whether or not being a surgical tech is worth it depends on your personal interests and goals. If you are drawn to the healthcare field and enjoy working in high-stress environments, this career could be a great fit for you.

Is It Worth It to Be a Surgical Tech: My Personal Experience

As a surgical tech for the past five years, I can say that this job definitely has its ups and downs. On one hand, I love the feeling of knowing that I am helping patients receive the care they need. On the other hand, the long hours and physical demands of the job can be exhausting.

However, for me, the pros outweigh the cons. I enjoy the challenge of working in a surgical setting, and I feel like I am making a real difference in people's lives. Plus, the pay and job security are both great.

What Does It Take to Be a Successful Surgical Tech?

In order to be a successful surgical tech, you need to have a few key skills. First and foremost, you need to be able to work well under pressure. Surgical settings can be chaotic, so you need to be able to stay calm and think on your feet.

You also need to have good communication skills. As a surgical tech, you will be working closely with doctors, nurses, and other healthcare professionals. Being able to communicate effectively is crucial for ensuring that everyone is on the same page.

Is It Worth It to Be a Surgical Tech: Training and Education

In order to become a surgical tech, you will need to complete a training program. These programs typically take between 9 and 24 months to complete, and they can be found at community colleges and vocational schools.

During your training, you will learn about anatomy, medical terminology, and surgical procedures. You will also complete clinical rotations to gain practical experience.

How to Advance Your Career as a Surgical Tech

If you are interested in advancing your career as a surgical tech, there are a few options available to you. One option is to become certified through the National Board of Surgical Technology and Surgical Assisting.

You can also choose to specialize in a particular area of surgery, such as neurosurgery or cardiovascular surgery. Specializing can help you become a more valuable member of the surgical team and can lead to higher pay.

Is It Worth It to Be a Surgical Tech: Final Thoughts

Ultimately, whether or not being a surgical tech is worth it depends on your personal goals and interests. While this career can be challenging, it can also be incredibly rewarding for those who are passionate about healthcare.

Question and Answer

Q: How much does a surgical tech make?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for surgical technologists was $48,300 as of May 2020.

Q: What kind of hours do surgical techs work?

A: Surgical techs often work long, irregular hours that can include nights, weekends, and holidays.

Q: Can you become a surgical tech without a degree?

A: While some employers may require a degree, it is possible to become a surgical tech through a vocational program or on-the-job training.

Q: What is the job outlook for surgical techs?

A: The job outlook for surgical techs is positive, with employment projected to grow 7 percent from 2019 to 2029.

Conclusion of Is It Worth It to Be a Surgical Tech

Being a surgical tech can be a challenging but rewarding career. Before pursuing this field, it's important to consider the demands of the job and whether they align with your personal goals and interests. With the right training and skills, however, you can thrive as a surgical tech and make a real difference in the lives of patients.
