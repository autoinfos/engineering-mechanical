What Salary Do Project Managers Make

Written by Robby Aug 25, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you interested in a career in project management but unsure about the salary? Or are you already a project manager looking for salary insights? Either way, you've come to the right place. In this post, we'll discuss what salary project managers can expect and factors that affect their earnings.

Project managers face unique challenges in their job, including managing budgets, timelines, and teams. However, one of the most pressing concerns for many project managers is compensation. It can be difficult to determine what a reasonable salary is and what factors affect earning potential.

The salary for project managers varies depending on factors such as experience, industry, and location. According to Glassdoor, the average salary for project managers in the United States is around $85,000 per year. However, this number can range from $50,000 to over $130,000 depending on the factors mentioned above.

In summary, project managers can expect to make a decent salary, but it's important to consider factors that may affect earnings. Experience, industry, and location are all important factors to consider when determining what salary to expect.

Factors Affecting Project Manager Salary

The salary for project managers is affected by several factors. One of the most significant factors is experience. As with most jobs, the more experience a project manager has, the higher their earning potential. Project managers with 10+ years of experience can expect to make significantly more than those just starting in the field.

Industry is another factor that can affect a project manager's salary. Some industries, such as technology or finance, tend to pay higher salaries than others. The location also plays a role in determining a project manager's salary. Salaries tend to be higher in large cities and metropolitan areas than in rural areas.

Personal Experience

As a project manager with five years of experience, I can attest to the importance of experience in determining salary. When I first started in the field, I made around $60,000 per year. However, as I gained more experience and took on larger projects, my salary increased to around $90,000 per year.

Negotiating Salary

When negotiating salary, it's important to research industry standards and have a clear understanding of your experience and qualifications. It's also important to consider the cost of living in the location where you'll be working. If you're negotiating with a potential employer, be sure to highlight your skills and accomplishments and explain why you're worth the salary you're requesting.

Additional Factors to Consider

Other factors to consider when determining a project manager's salary include education level and certifications. Project managers with advanced degrees or certifications, such as a Project Management Professional (PMP) certification, may have higher earning potential.

Negotiating a Raise

If you're already working as a project manager and want to negotiate a raise, it's important to have a clear understanding of your job responsibilities, accomplishments, and contributions to the company. Be prepared to discuss why you deserve a raise and provide evidence to support your request.

Conclusion

Project management can be a rewarding career both professionally and financially. While salaries vary depending on experience, industry, and location, project managers can expect to make a decent salary. By understanding the factors that affect earning potential and negotiating effectively, project managers can maximize their earning potential and achieve their career goals.

Question and Answer

A: According to Glassdoor, the average salary for a project manager in the United States is around $85,000 per year.

A: Project managers can negotiate a higher salary by researching industry standards, highlighting their skills and accomplishments, and providing evidence to support their request.
