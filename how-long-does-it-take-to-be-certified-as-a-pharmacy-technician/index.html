How Long Does It Take To Be Certified As A Pharmacy Technician

Written by Frank Aug 24, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you interested in a career as a pharmacy technician? Wondering how long it takes to become certified? Look no further, as we break down the process and timeline for becoming a certified pharmacy technician.

Aspiring pharmacy technicians often have concerns about the time and effort required to obtain their certification. Some may feel overwhelmed or uncertain about where to begin. Others may worry about the cost or the difficulty of the certification process.

So, how long does it take to be certified as a pharmacy technician? The answer depends on a few factors, such as the program or course you choose, your experience, and your dedication to the process. However, on average, it takes about 1-2 years to complete the certification process and become a certified pharmacy technician.

Overall, the process of becoming a certified pharmacy technician involves completing an accredited program, passing an exam, and meeting any additional state requirements. Accredited programs can range from 6 months to 2 years, and typically include classroom instruction, laboratory work, and hands-on training. After completing the program, you will need to pass the Pharmacy Technician Certification Exam (PTCE) or the Exam for Certification of Pharmacy Technicians (ExCPT). Once you pass the exam, you will need to meet any additional state requirements and register with your state board of pharmacy.

Personal Experience

As someone who has gone through the process of becoming a certified pharmacy technician, I can attest to the time and dedication required. I chose to enroll in a 1-year accredited program that included both classroom instruction and hands-on training. The program was challenging, but the instructors were knowledgeable and supportive. After completing the program, I spent several months studying and preparing for the PTCE. I passed the exam on my first try, and was able to register with my state board of pharmacy soon after.

Cost and Time Factors

When considering the time and cost of becoming a certified pharmacy technician, it's important to do your research and weigh your options. Accredited programs can range in cost from a few thousand dollars to upwards of $20,000. However, many programs offer financial aid or payment plans to help make the cost more manageable. Additionally, some employers may offer tuition reimbursement or assistance with certification fees. As for time, it's important to remember that the timeline can vary based on your schedule and other commitments. Part-time and online programs may take longer to complete, but can be more flexible for those who are working or have other obligations.

Additional Requirements

While completing an accredited program and passing the PTCE are the main requirements for becoming a certified pharmacy technician, there may be additional state-specific requirements to meet. For example, some states require a criminal background check or proof of immunizations. It's important to research your state's requirements and ensure that you have all necessary documentation before registering with your state board of pharmacy.

Tips for Success

When embarking on the journey towards becoming a certified pharmacy technician, it's important to stay focused and committed. Here are a few tips for success:

  • Stay organized and keep track of deadlines
  • Find a study group or study partner to help stay motivated
  • Take advantage of any resources or support offered by your program or employer
  • Stay up-to-date on industry news and trends

Personal Experience

As someone who has been a certified pharmacy technician for several years now, I can say that the hard work and dedication required to obtain the certification is well worth it. Being a pharmacy technician can be a rewarding and fulfilling career, with opportunities for advancement and growth.

Question and Answer Section

Q: Can I become a certified pharmacy technician without completing an accredited program?

A: No, completing an accredited program is a requirement for becoming a certified pharmacy technician.

Q: How much does it cost to become a certified pharmacy technician?

A: The cost can vary, but accredited programs can range from a few thousand dollars to upwards of $20,000.

Q: Do I need to pass an exam to become a certified pharmacy technician?

A: Yes, passing the Pharmacy Technician Certification Exam (PTCE) or the Exam for Certification of Pharmacy Technicians (ExCPT) is a requirement for becoming a certified pharmacy technician.

Q: How long does it take to complete an accredited program?

A: Accredited programs can range from 6 months to 2 years, depending on the program and schedule.

Conclusion

Overall, becoming a certified pharmacy technician requires time, dedication, and hard work. However, the rewards can be well worth it, with opportunities for a fulfilling career in the healthcare industry. By researching your options, staying organized, and staying committed to the process, you can become a certified pharmacy technician in as little as 1-2 years.
