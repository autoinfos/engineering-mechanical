How Much Does A Computer Support Technician Make

Written by Aprilia Sep 10, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career as a computer support technician and wondering how much you could potentially earn? Well, you're not alone. This is a common question among people who are exploring this field. Understanding the average salary of a computer support technician is essential in determining if this career path is right for you.

Computer support technicians are responsible for diagnosing and resolving technical issues related to computer hardware, software, and network systems. They are often the first point of contact when customers or employees encounter technical problems. The job requires strong problem-solving skills, attention to detail, and a passion for technology.

How Much Does a Computer Support Technician Make?

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS), the median annual wage for computer support specialists was $54,760 as of May 2020. The lowest 10 percent earned less than $32,030, and the highest 10 percent earned more than $88,740. The salary range varies depending on factors such as experience, education, industry, and geographic location.

While the salary range may not be as high as some other technology-related fields, computer support technicians are in high demand, and the job outlook is positive. The BLS projects that employment of computer support specialists will grow 8 percent from 2019 to 2029, much faster than the average for all occupations.

Personal Experience: How Much Does a Computer Support Technician Make?

As a former computer support technician, I can attest that the salary range is accurate. I started in this field with no prior experience and earned around $40,000 per year. However, as I gained more experience and certifications, my salary increased to over $60,000 per year. I also had the opportunity to work in different industries, which allowed me to earn a higher salary.

Factors That Affect Salary

As previously mentioned, several factors can affect a computer support technician's salary. Experience and education are probably the most significant factors. Employers typically prefer candidates with some level of formal education, such as an associate's degree or a bachelor's degree in a computer-related field. Certifications, such as CompTIA A+, Network+, and Security+, can also increase a technician's earning potential.

The industry and geographic location are also significant factors. Technicians working in the computer systems design and related services industry tend to earn higher salaries than those working in other industries. In terms of location, technicians working in metropolitan areas or high-tech regions such as Silicon Valley generally earn higher salaries than those working in rural areas or smaller cities.

Experience

As with most jobs, experience plays a significant role in a computer support technician's salary. Technicians with little to no experience can expect to earn around $30,000 to $40,000 per year, while those with five or more years of experience can earn $50,000 to $70,000 per year.

Education and Certifications

While formal education is not always required for a computer support technician, having an associate's or bachelor's degree in a computer-related field can increase your earning potential. Additionally, obtaining industry-recognized certifications can also increase your salary. Some popular certifications for computer support technicians include CompTIA A+, Network+, and Security+.

Personal Experience: How Much Does a Computer Support Technician Make?

After obtaining my associate's degree in computer science, I was able to earn a higher salary than some of my colleagues who did not have a degree. Additionally, I obtained several certifications, which also increased my earning potential. I would highly recommend pursuing formal education and certifications if you want to earn a higher salary as a computer support technician.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I earn a higher salary as a computer support technician with a bachelor's degree?

A: Yes, typically, computer support technicians with a bachelor's degree tend to earn higher salaries than those with only an associate's degree or no degree.

Q: What industries tend to pay computer support technicians the highest salaries?

A: According to the BLS, computer support technicians working in the computer systems design and related services industry tend to earn the highest salaries.

Q: Do computer support technicians receive benefits?

A: Yes, most employers offer benefits such as health insurance, retirement plans, and paid time off to computer support technicians.

Q: Is the job outlook positive for computer support technicians?

A: Yes, the BLS projects that employment of computer support specialists will grow 8 percent from 2019 to 2029, much faster than the average for all occupations.

Conclusion of How Much Does a Computer Support Technician Make

In conclusion, computer support technicians play a critical role in maintaining and troubleshooting computer systems. While the salary range may not be as high as some other technology-related fields, the job outlook is positive, and the demand for computer support technicians is high. Pursuing formal education, obtaining certifications, and gaining experience can increase your earning potential as a computer support technician.
