How Much Do Elevator Mechanics Make A Year

Written by Bardi Jul 14, 2023 · 4 min read

Have you ever wondered about the salary of elevator mechanics? Perhaps you are considering a career in this field, or maybe you are just curious. Regardless of your reason, it is important to understand how much do elevator mechanics make a year and what factors can affect their earnings.

The salary of elevator mechanics can vary depending on several factors such as location, experience, and the type of employer. For instance, those working in urban areas or in high-rise buildings may earn more than those in rural areas or low-rise buildings. Additionally, those with more experience and skills may earn higher than entry-level mechanics.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual salary for elevator mechanics and installers is $84,990 as of May 2020. The lowest 10 percent earned less than $43,980, while the highest 10 percent earned more than $122,020.

In summary, elevator mechanics can earn a good income depending on their location, experience, and employer. The median annual salary for this profession is $84,990, with opportunities for growth and higher income potential.

Factors Affecting Elevator Mechanics' Earnings

Location and experience are the primary factors that affect the earnings of elevator mechanics. For instance, those working in densely populated cities or in high-rise buildings may earn more than those in rural areas or low-rise buildings. Additionally, those with more experience and skills may earn higher than entry-level mechanics.

Personally, I know an elevator mechanic who has been in the industry for over a decade, and he is now earning more than six figures. He started as an apprentice and worked his way up, gaining experience and skills along the way. He also pursued additional training and certifications to expand his knowledge and expertise.

Elevator Mechanics' Union and Benefits

Most elevator mechanics belong to a union, which can provide job security, higher wages, and benefits such as health insurance, retirement plans, and paid time off. Union membership can also provide training and education opportunities that can help advance their careers and increase their earnings potential.

I have a friend who is an elevator mechanic and belongs to a union. He shared that being in a union has helped him secure better wages and benefits than non-union workers in his field. He also mentioned that union membership provides a sense of community and support among fellow mechanics.

Training and Education for Elevator Mechanics

Elevator mechanics typically need a high school diploma or equivalent and undergo an apprenticeship program that may last up to four years. The program includes both classroom instruction and on-the-job training with experienced mechanics.

Additionally, some elevator mechanics pursue further education and training to expand their knowledge and skills. This can include certifications and licenses that can improve their job prospects and earnings potential.

Career Outlook for Elevator Mechanics

The career outlook for elevator mechanics is positive, with a projected job growth of 7 percent from 2019 to 2029. This growth is attributed to the increasing demand for elevators in residential and commercial buildings.

However, competition for entry-level positions can be tough, and those with certifications and additional training may have an advantage. Additionally, those with experience and skills in new elevator technologies may have better job prospects and earning potential.

Conclusion of How Much Do Elevator Mechanics Make a Year

In conclusion, elevator mechanics can earn a good income depending on their location, experience, and employer. The median annual salary for this profession is $84,990, with opportunities for growth and higher income potential. Additionally, membership in a union and pursuing further education and training can improve job prospects and earnings potential. The career outlook for elevator mechanics is also positive, with a projected job growth of 7 percent in the coming years.

Question and Answer

Q: How much do elevator mechanics make an hour?

A: The average hourly wage for elevator mechanics and installers is $40.84 as of May 2020.


Q: What are the highest-paying industries for elevator mechanics?

A: The highest-paying industries for elevator mechanics include natural gas distribution, electric power generation, and management of companies and enterprises.


Q: Is the job of elevator mechanics dangerous?

A: The job of elevator mechanics can be hazardous, as they work with heavy machinery and are often required to work at great heights. However, following safety protocols and wearing appropriate protective gear can minimize the risks.


Q: What skills are required to become an elevator mechanic?

A: Elevator mechanics should have mechanical skills, problem-solving skills, and the ability to read and interpret blueprints and schematics. They should also have good communication skills and be able to work independently or as part of a team.
