What Does Field Artillery Do In The Army

Written by Eveline Jun 02, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Have you ever wondered what field artillery does in the army? It's a fascinating and important role that requires precision, teamwork, and skill. Whether you're considering a career in the military or just curious about the inner workings of the army, understanding what field artillery does is a great place to start.

Field artillery soldiers are responsible for providing indirect fire support to ground troops. This means they use various types of artillery, such as howitzers and mortars, to launch munitions at enemy targets without directly engaging them. While this may sound like a relatively simple task, it requires a great deal of knowledge and training to execute effectively.

The primary mission of field artillery is to provide support to ground troops by neutralizing enemy targets, disrupting enemy movements, and creating a protective barrier around friendly forces. To achieve this, field artillery soldiers must work closely with infantry, armor, and other units to coordinate their efforts and provide accurate, timely fire support when and where it's needed.

Personal Experience

As a former field artillery soldier, I can attest to the importance of this role in the army. During my time in the military, I worked alongside infantry and armor units to provide fire support in a variety of combat scenarios. This required a great deal of training and coordination, but it was incredibly rewarding to know that our efforts were helping to keep our fellow soldiers safe and achieve our objectives.

The Role of Field Artillery in the Army

Field artillery plays a critical role in the army by providing indirect fire support to ground troops. This support can be used to neutralize enemy targets, disrupt enemy movements, and create a protective barrier around friendly forces. To achieve this, field artillery soldiers must be highly skilled in a variety of areas, including weapons systems, communications, and tactics.

Training for Field Artillery Soldiers

Field artillery soldiers undergo extensive training to prepare them for their role in the army. This includes learning how to operate and maintain various types of artillery, as well as how to communicate effectively with other units on the ground. Additionally, field artillery soldiers receive training in tactics, such as how to position their weapons for maximum effectiveness and how to adjust fire based on changing conditions.

How Field Artillery Supports Ground Troops

Field artillery provides critical support to ground troops by neutralizing enemy targets, disrupting enemy movements, and creating a protective barrier around friendly forces. This support can be used in a variety of scenarios, from small-scale engagements to large-scale conflicts. By working closely with other units on the ground, field artillery soldiers can provide accurate, timely fire support to help achieve the mission and keep their fellow soldiers safe.

Personal Experience

During my time in the military, I had the opportunity to work with some incredibly skilled and dedicated field artillery soldiers. Together, we were able to provide fire support in a variety of scenarios, from small-scale skirmishes to large-scale battles. It was an honor to be part of such an important mission, and I'm proud to have served alongside some of the best soldiers I've ever known.

Question and Answer

Q: What types of artillery do field artillery soldiers use?

A: Field artillery soldiers use a variety of artillery, including howitzers, mortars, and rocket launchers.

Q: How do field artillery soldiers communicate with other units on the ground?

A: Field artillery soldiers use a variety of communication methods, including radios and digital systems, to coordinate their efforts with other units on the ground.

Q: What types of targets do field artillery soldiers engage?

A: Field artillery soldiers engage a variety of targets, including enemy personnel, vehicles, and structures.

Q: How does field artillery support ground troops?

A: Field artillery supports ground troops by providing indirect fire support to neutralize enemy targets, disrupt enemy movements, and create a protective barrier around friendly forces.

Conclusion of What Does Field Artillery Do in the Army

Field artillery is a critical component of the army's mission to protect and defend the nation. By providing indirect fire support to ground troops, field artillery soldiers help neutralize enemy targets, disrupt enemy movements, and create a protective barrier around friendly forces. This requires a great deal of skill, training, and coordination, but it's a task that field artillery soldiers take on with pride and dedication.
