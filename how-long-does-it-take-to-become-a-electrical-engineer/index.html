How Long Does It Take To Become An Electrical Engineer

Written by Daniel Jul 08, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you interested in becoming an electrical engineer, but unsure of the time commitment involved? Pursuing a career in electrical engineering can be a daunting task, but with the right information, you can have a clear understanding of what it takes to achieve your goals.

One of the biggest pain points for those considering a career in electrical engineering is the amount of time it takes to become licensed. Many people assume that a degree in electrical engineering alone will allow them to practice, but this is not the case. In order to become licensed, you must pass the Fundamentals of Engineering exam and complete a certain amount of work experience under a licensed engineer. This process can take several years to complete.

The path to becoming an electrical engineer typically involves obtaining a bachelor's degree in electrical engineering, which takes four years to complete. After graduation, you must pass the Fundamentals of Engineering exam and complete four years of work experience under the supervision of a licensed engineer. Once these requirements are met, you can then take the Professional Engineering exam, which is required to become licensed.

In summary, it typically takes a minimum of eight years of education and work experience to become a licensed electrical engineer. This includes four years to obtain a bachelor's degree in electrical engineering, and four years of work experience under the supervision of a licensed engineer.

Personal Experience with Becoming an Electrical Engineer

As someone who has pursued a career in electrical engineering, I can attest to the time commitment involved. I obtained my bachelor's degree in electrical engineering and then spent four years working under the supervision of a licensed engineer before taking the Professional Engineering exam. This process was challenging, but ultimately rewarding, as I now have the opportunity to work on exciting projects and make a difference in my field.

The Benefits of Becoming an Electrical Engineer

Despite the time commitment involved in becoming an electrical engineer, there are many benefits to pursuing this career path. Electrical engineers are in high demand, and the job outlook is excellent. Additionally, electrical engineers have the opportunity to work on exciting projects, such as developing cutting-edge technology and designing innovative products.

What Does an Electrical Engineer Do?

Electrical engineers design, develop, and test electrical equipment, such as motors, navigation systems, and power generation equipment. They also oversee the installation and operation of this equipment, and may be responsible for troubleshooting any issues that arise.

How to Become an Electrical Engineer

In order to become an electrical engineer, you must obtain a bachelor's degree in electrical engineering from an accredited university. After graduation, you must pass the Fundamentals of Engineering exam and complete four years of work experience under the supervision of a licensed engineer. Once these requirements are met, you can then take the Professional Engineering exam, which is required to become licensed.
