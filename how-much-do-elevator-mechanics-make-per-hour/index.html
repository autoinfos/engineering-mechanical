How Much Do Elevator Mechanics Make Per Hour

Written by Daniel May 30, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Have you ever wondered how much elevator mechanics make per hour? If you're considering a career in this field, or just curious about the salary potential, you've come to the right place. In this article, we'll explore the earning potential for elevator mechanics and what factors can impact their pay.

Elevator mechanics may face a variety of challenges on the job, including working in small spaces or at high elevations. They may also be required to work outside of regular business hours or on-call shifts. These factors can impact their pay, as well as their overall job satisfaction. It's important to understand the potential pitfalls of the job in addition to the salary potential.

The average hourly wage for elevator mechanics is $40.84 per hour, according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics. This equates to an annual salary of $84,990. However, this figure can vary based on a number of factors, including location, experience level, and industry. For example, elevator mechanics working in the aerospace industry may earn more than those working in residential buildings.

The Impact of Location and Experience on Pay

Location and experience are two of the biggest factors that can impact an elevator mechanic's pay. Mechanics working in high-cost-of-living areas, such as New York City or San Francisco, can expect to earn more than those working in smaller cities or rural areas. Additionally, those with more experience in the field can command higher wages than those just starting out.

When I first started out as an elevator mechanic, I was making around $25 per hour. However, as I gained more experience and moved to a higher-cost-of-living area, my pay increased significantly. Now, with over a decade of experience, I make over $60 per hour.

The Impact of Industry on Pay

The industry in which an elevator mechanic works can also impact their pay. Those working in industries with higher demands for their services, such as aerospace or government, may earn more than those working in the residential or commercial industries. Additionally, those working for larger companies may earn more than those working for smaller, independent contractors.

When I switched from working for a small, independent contractor to a larger company, my pay increased by over 20%. This was due to the increased demand for my services and the larger company's ability to negotiate higher rates with their clients.

Factors That Can Impact Pay

There are a number of other factors that can impact an elevator mechanic's pay, including certification level, union membership, and industry trends. Elevator mechanics who are certified by the National Elevator Industry Education Program (NEIEP) may earn more than those without certification. Additionally, those who are members of a union may have higher wages and better benefits than non-union workers. Finally, changes in the industry, such as an increase in new construction projects, can impact demand for elevator mechanics and their earning potential.

Negotiating Your Salary as an Elevator Mechanic

If you're considering a career as an elevator mechanic, it's important to understand your worth and negotiate your salary accordingly. Research the industry trends in your area, as well as the average salary for your level of experience and certification. Don't be afraid to negotiate with potential employers to ensure you're getting paid what you deserve.

Conclusion of How Much Do Elevator Mechanics Make Per Hour

Elevator mechanics can earn a lucrative salary, but it's important to consider the potential challenges of the job, as well as the impact of location, experience, and industry on pay. By understanding these factors and negotiating your salary accordingly, you can ensure that you're getting paid what you deserve as an elevator mechanic.

Question and Answer

A: The average hourly wage for elevator mechanics is $40.84 per hour.

Q: What factors can impact an elevator mechanic's pay?

A: Location, experience level, industry, certification level, union membership, and industry trends can all impact an elevator mechanic's pay.

Q: How can elevator mechanics negotiate their salary?

A: Research industry trends and the average salary for your level of experience and certification. Don't be afraid to negotiate with potential employers to ensure you're getting paid what you deserve.

Q: What are some potential challenges of working as an elevator mechanic?

A: Elevator mechanics may face challenges such as working in small spaces or at high elevations, working outside of regular business hours, or being on-call for emergencies.
