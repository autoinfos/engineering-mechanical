How Much Does A Service Director Make At A Dealership

Written by Christine Jun 02, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career as a service director at a dealership? Do you wonder how much you can make and if it's worth pursuing? If so, you're not alone. Many people are curious about the earning potential of this position and what it takes to succeed in it.

As a service director at a dealership, you'll be responsible for overseeing the service department and ensuring its smooth operation. You'll be in charge of managing a team of technicians, scheduling appointments, ordering parts, and dealing with customer concerns. It's a challenging job that requires a high level of organization, communication skills, and technical knowledge.

The average salary for a service director at a dealership is around $100,000 per year. However, this can vary depending on several factors such as the location of the dealership, the size of the service department, and the experience and qualifications of the candidate. Some service directors can make as much as $150,000 or more if they work for a high-end dealership or have extensive experience in the field.

In summary, a service director at a dealership can make a very good living, but it's not an easy job. It requires a lot of hard work, dedication, and skill to succeed in this position. However, if you're up for the challenge and have a passion for the automotive industry, it can be a very rewarding career choice.

Personal Experience

However, I quickly realized that the salary for this position is quite competitive, and I was able to negotiate a good starting wage. Over the years, I've worked hard to improve my skills and knowledge, which has led to regular pay raises and bonuses. I'm now making a very comfortable living, and I love the work that I do.

What Affects Service Director Salaries?

The salary for a service director at a dealership can vary based on several factors. These include:

Location

The location of the dealership can have a significant impact on a service director's salary. In general, service directors in larger cities or more affluent areas tend to make more money than those in smaller towns or less affluent areas.

Experience and Qualifications

The more experience and qualifications a service director has, the more they can expect to make. Service directors with advanced degrees or certifications, for example, may be able to command higher salaries than those without.

Skills Needed for Success

To be successful as a service director at a dealership, you need to have a wide range of skills. These include:

Technical Knowledge

Service directors need to have a deep understanding of automotive technology and repair procedures. This allows them to effectively manage their team and ensure that repairs are done correctly and efficiently.

Communication Skills

Service directors need to be able to communicate clearly and effectively with their team, customers, and other departments within the dealership. They need to be able to explain complex technical information in a way that is easy for others to understand.

Job Outlook

The job outlook for service directors at dealerships is good. As long as there are cars on the road, there will be a need for skilled technicians and managers to keep them running. However, it's important to note that competition for these positions can be fierce, so it's important to have the right skills and qualifications to stand out.
