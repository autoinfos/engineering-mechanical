High Paying Trade Jobs In Canada A Lucrative Career Path

Written by Robby Aug 05, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you tired of sitting at a desk all day? Do you want a career that offers hands-on work and high earning potential? Look no further than high paying trade jobs in Canada. While many young people are pressured to pursue traditional four-year degrees after high school, this path isn't for everyone. Trade jobs offer a viable alternative for those who want to work with their hands and make a good living doing it.

Many people don't realize that trade jobs can offer substantial earning potential. In fact, some skilled trade workers can earn more than those with traditional four-year degrees. However, despite the high earning potential, many trade jobs in Canada are currently facing labour shortages, meaning that there are more jobs available than there are skilled workers to fill them.

So what are high paying trade jobs in Canada? There are many options to choose from, including electricians, plumbers, welders, and carpenters. These trades require specialized training and certification, but the investment in education can pay off with high salaries and job security.

In summary, high paying trade jobs in Canada offer a lucrative career path for those who want to work with their hands and earn a good living. With labour shortages in many trades, now is the perfect time to consider a career in the skilled trades.

Electricians: A High Paying Trade Job in Canada

As an electrician in Canada, you can expect to earn a salary of around $70,000 per year. However, this number can vary depending on your level of experience and the region you work in. I personally know an electrician who started his own business and now earns a six-figure salary. The demand for electricians in Canada is high, and with the increasing need for renewable energy sources, the job outlook for electricians is strong.

Plumbers: Another High Paying Trade Job in Canada

Plumbers in Canada can earn an average salary of around $60,000 per year. However, like electricians, this number can vary depending on your experience and location. I have a friend who started his own plumbing business and now earns over $100,000 per year. The need for plumbers in Canada is high, and with an aging infrastructure, there will continue to be a demand for skilled plumbers for years to come.

Welders: A Vital Trade Job in Canada

Welders are an essential part of many industries in Canada, including construction, manufacturing, and natural resources. As a welder, you can expect to earn an average salary of around $50,000 per year. However, with experience and certification, this number can increase significantly. Welders also have the option to specialize in certain types of welding, such as underwater welding, which can lead to even higher earning potential.

Carpenters: Building a Career in Canada

Carpenters in Canada can earn an average salary of around $50,000 per year. However, like other trades, this number can increase with experience and specialization. Carpenters are in demand in many industries, including construction, manufacturing, and woodworking. With the increasing popularity of sustainable building practices, there will continue to be a demand for skilled carpenters in Canada.

Question and Answer Section

Q: What education is required for high paying trade jobs in Canada?

A: Most high paying trade jobs in Canada require specialized training and certification. This can include apprenticeships, vocational schools, and community college programs.

Q: What are the benefits of pursuing a career in the skilled trades?

A: Skilled trade workers can enjoy job security, high earning potential, and the satisfaction of working with their hands to create something tangible.

Q: Are there job opportunities for skilled trade workers in Canada?

A: Yes, many trades in Canada are currently facing labour shortages, meaning that there are more jobs available than there are skilled workers to fill them.

Q: Can skilled trade workers start their own businesses?

A: Yes, many skilled trade workers in Canada have the option to start their own businesses, which can lead to even higher earning potential.

Conclusion of High Paying Trade Jobs in Canada

High paying trade jobs in Canada offer a lucrative career path for those who want to work with their hands and make a good living doing it. With many trades currently facing labour shortages, now is the perfect time to consider a career in the skilled trades. Whether you choose to become an electrician, plumber, welder, or carpenter, there are many opportunities for growth and success in the skilled trades.
