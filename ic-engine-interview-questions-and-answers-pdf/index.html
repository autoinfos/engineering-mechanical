Common IC Engine Interview Questions and Answers PDF

Here are some of the common IC engine interview questions and answers PDF that you may come across:

Q: What is the difference between a two-stroke and a four-stroke engine?

A: A two-stroke engine completes a power cycle in two strokes of the piston, while a four-stroke engine completes it in four strokes. A two-stroke engine has a simpler design and is lighter, but it tends to be less efficient and more polluting than a four-stroke engine.

Q: What is the function of the engine's valves?

A: The engine's valves control the flow of air and fuel into the combustion chamber and the exhaust gases out of it. The opening and closing of the valves are timed to ensure efficient combustion and exhaust.

Q: What is an exhaust gas recirculation (EGR) system?

A: An EGR system is used to reduce the emission of nitrogen oxides (NOx) from the engine. It works by recirculating some of the exhaust gases back into the combustion chamber, which lowers the combustion temperature and reduces the formation of NOx.

Q: What is the meaning of the term "compression ratio"?

A: The compression ratio is the ratio of the volume of the combustion chamber when the piston is at the bottom of its stroke to the volume when it is at the top of its stroke. It is a measure of the engine's efficiency and power output.
