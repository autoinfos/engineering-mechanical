How Long Does It Take To Complete Airplane Mechanic School

Written by Bardi Jun 13, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you interested in becoming an airplane mechanic but worried about how long it will take to complete the required schooling? Don't fret! In this post, we will explore the length of airplane mechanic school and provide some insights into the experience.

One of the biggest concerns for prospective airplane mechanics is the length of time it takes to complete the schooling. Many worry that it will take too long or that they won't have the time to commit to it. However, the reality is that the length of airplane mechanic school can vary depending on the program and the student's pace.

The target of airplane mechanic school is to provide students with the skills and knowledge necessary to inspect, repair, and maintain aircraft. This includes learning about engines, electronics, hydraulics, and other essential systems. The length of the program is determined by the FAA requirements, which mandate a minimum of 1,900 classroom hours and 2,400 practical hours.

In summary, the length of airplane mechanic school can vary, but FAA requirements mandate a minimum of 1,900 classroom hours and 2,400 practical hours. The program's length is influenced by the program and student's pace, but most programs take about 18-24 months to complete.

What to Expect in Airplane Mechanic School?

When attending airplane mechanic school, students can expect to learn about a wide range of topics related to aircraft maintenance. These include engine maintenance and repair, electrical systems, aircraft structures, and avionics. The program is designed to provide students with hands-on training, allowing them to gain practical experience with the equipment and tools used in the field.

During my time in airplane mechanic school, I found the experience to be both challenging and rewarding. I appreciated the hands-on nature of the program, which allowed me to learn by doing. The instructors were knowledgeable and supportive, and I felt confident in my abilities by the time I graduated.

The Benefits of Completing Airplane Mechanic School

Completing airplane mechanic school can lead to many benefits. First and foremost, it can lead to a rewarding career in a growing field. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the employment of aircraft and avionics equipment mechanics and technicians is projected to grow 5 percent from 2019 to 2029, faster than the average for all occupations. Additionally, airplane mechanics can earn a competitive salary, with the median annual wage for aircraft mechanics and service technicians at $64,090 as of May 2020.

Furthermore, completing airplane mechanic school can provide job security, as aircraft maintenance is essential for keeping planes safe and operational. Many aviation companies require mechanics to be licensed by the FAA, which can only be obtained through completing an FAA-approved airplane mechanic school program.

The FAA Certification Process

After completing an FAA-approved airplane mechanic school program, graduates must pass a series of exams to obtain their certification. These exams cover general aircraft knowledge, airframe maintenance, and powerplant maintenance. Once certified, mechanics must complete ongoing training to stay up to date on new technologies and techniques.

How to Choose the Right Airplane Mechanic School

When choosing an airplane mechanic school, it's important to consider factors such as program length, cost, location, and reputation. Look for a school that is FAA-approved and has a strong track record of preparing students for certification. Additionally, consider the school's facilities and resources, as well as the experience and qualifications of the instructors.

Conclusion of Airplane Mechanic School How Long

While the length of airplane mechanic school can vary, completing an FAA-approved program is essential for obtaining certification and a successful career in the field. The program typically takes about 18-24 months to complete, and students can expect to learn about a wide range of topics related to aircraft maintenance. With a growing job market and competitive salaries, becoming an airplane mechanic can be a rewarding career choice for those willing to put in the time and effort to complete the required schooling.

Question and Answer

Q: How much does it cost to attend airplane mechanic school?

A: The cost of airplane mechanic school can vary depending on the program and location. However, according to the FAA, the average cost of completing an airplane mechanic program is around $50,000.

Q: Can I work as an airplane mechanic without completing school?

A: While it's possible to gain experience and knowledge through on-the-job training, most aviation companies require mechanics to be licensed by the FAA, which requires completing an FAA-approved airplane mechanic school program.

Q: What is the job outlook for airplane mechanics?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the employment of aircraft and avionics equipment mechanics and technicians is projected to grow 5 percent from 2019 to 2029, faster than the average for all occupations.

Q: How often do certified airplane mechanics need to complete ongoing training?

A: Certified airplane mechanics must complete ongoing training to stay up to date on new technologies and techniques. The FAA requires mechanics to complete at least 16 hours of training every two years to maintain their certification.
