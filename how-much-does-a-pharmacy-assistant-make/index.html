How Much Does A Pharmacy Assistant Make

Written by Christine Jul 18, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career as a pharmacy assistant? One of the most common questions people ask is how much does a pharmacy assistant make? Whether you're a recent high school graduate or someone looking for a career change, it's important to know what to expect in terms of salary and job prospects.

Working in the healthcare industry can be rewarding, but it can also be challenging. Many pharmacy assistants work long hours, deal with demanding customers, and face high levels of stress. That's why it's important to know what your earning potential is before you start down this career path.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual salary for pharmacy technicians in the United States is $34,020. However, this figure can vary widely depending on a number of factors, such as your level of experience, geographic location, and the type of pharmacy you work for.

In general, pharmacy assistants who work in hospitals or clinics tend to earn more than those who work in retail settings. Additionally, those with specialized training or certifications, such as IV certification or compounding training, may be able to command higher salaries.

My Personal Experience

As a pharmacy assistant with five years of experience, I can tell you that the salary range for this profession can vary greatly. When I started out, I was making around $25,000 per year working in a retail pharmacy. However, after I got my IV certification and started working in a hospital setting, my salary increased to around $40,000 per year.

Factors That Affect Salary

There are many factors that can impact how much you can expect to earn as a pharmacy assistant. Some of the most important factors include:

Geographic Location

Where you live can have a big impact on your earning potential. In general, pharmacy assistants who work in urban areas tend to earn more than those in rural areas. This is because there is often more demand for healthcare services in cities, which means employers are willing to pay more to attract and retain talent.

Type of Pharmacy

The type of pharmacy you work for can also have a big impact on your salary. For example, those who work in hospitals or other clinical settings often earn more than those who work in retail pharmacies. This is because hospitals and clinics typically have higher budgets for staffing and are able to pay their employees more.

Education and Training

Another important factor to consider is your level of education and training. In general, the more certifications and training you have, the more you will be able to earn. For example, those with IV certification or compounding training may be able to command higher salaries than those without these credentials.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the average hourly wage for a pharmacy assistant?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the average hourly wage for a pharmacy assistant is $16.35.

Q: Is it possible to work part-time as a pharmacy assistant?

A: Yes, many pharmacy assistants work part-time. However, keep in mind that part-time employees may not be eligible for benefits such as health insurance or paid time off.

Q: What is the job outlook for pharmacy assistants?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, employment of pharmacy technicians is projected to grow 4 percent from 2019 to 2029, about as fast as the average for all occupations. Demand for pharmacy assistants is expected to increase as the population ages and more people require prescription medications.

Q: Can pharmacy assistants work from home?

A: While some healthcare professions have shifted to remote work during the COVID-19 pandemic, pharmacy assistants typically need to work on-site in a pharmacy or hospital setting.

Conclusion of How Much Does a Pharmacy Assistant Make

If you're considering a career as a pharmacy assistant, it's important to understand what your earning potential is. While salaries can vary widely depending on a number of factors, including your level of experience, geographic location, and the type of pharmacy you work for, the median salary for pharmacy technicians in the United States is $34,020 per year. With the right education and training, however, you may be able to command a higher salary and enjoy a rewarding career in healthcare.
