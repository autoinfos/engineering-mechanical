What Is The Average Salary For An Engineering Technician

Written by Frank Jul 11, 2023 · 4 min read

As an engineering technician, you may be wondering what the average salary is for your profession. It can be a tricky subject to navigate, but understanding the average salary can give you a better idea of what to expect and how to negotiate your own salary.

Engineering technicians are responsible for helping engineers design, develop, and test products, equipment, and systems. They work in a variety of industries, including manufacturing, construction, and telecommunications. However, despite the importance of their work, engineering technicians are often paid less than their engineering counterparts.

The average salary for an engineering technician varies depending on factors such as location, experience, and industry. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for engineering technicians is $56,980, with the lowest 10 percent earning less than $35,220 and the highest 10 percent earning more than $88,210.

In addition to location, experience, and industry, the type of work you do as an engineering technician can also affect your salary. For example, technicians who work on complex projects or in specialized fields may earn more than those who work on simple projects or in less specialized areas.

To ensure that you are paid fairly for your work as an engineering technician, it's important to do your research and negotiate your salary. Consider factors such as your experience, skills, and the type of work you will be doing when negotiating your salary. And remember, the average salary is just a starting point - there is always room for negotiation.

Personal Experience with Engineering Technician Salaries

As an engineering technician with over five years of experience, I have seen firsthand the fluctuations in salary within the industry. When I first started out, I was making around $40,000 a year. However, as I gained experience and took on more complex projects, my salary increased to over $60,000 a year.

One thing I learned early on is that it's important to negotiate your salary and not be afraid to ask for more. When I was first offered my position as an engineering technician, the salary offered was much lower than I expected. I negotiated for a higher salary, and was able to get a more competitive offer.

Factors that Affect Engineering Technician Salaries

As mentioned earlier, location, experience, and industry can all affect an engineering technician's salary. Here are some additional factors to consider:

Educational Background

Having a higher level of education can lead to higher salaries for engineering technicians. For example, having a bachelor's degree in engineering technology can lead to higher pay than having just an associate's degree.

Specialization

Specializing in a specific area of engineering can also lead to higher salaries. For example, engineering technicians who specialize in aerospace engineering tend to earn more than those who specialize in other areas.

Conclusion of What is the Average Salary for an Engineering Technician

Overall, the average salary for an engineering technician is around $56,980 per year. However, this can vary depending on factors such as location, experience, industry, and specialization. By understanding these factors and negotiating your salary, you can ensure that you are paid fairly for your work as an engineering technician.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the starting salary for an engineering technician?

A: The starting salary for an engineering technician can vary depending on location and industry, but it is typically around $35,000 to $45,000 per year.

Q: How much do engineering technicians make with experience?

A: With experience, engineering technicians can make anywhere from $50,000 to $80,000 per year.

Q: What industries pay engineering technicians the most?

A: Industries such as aerospace engineering, telecommunications, and scientific research and development tend to pay engineering technicians the most.

Q: Can engineering technicians make six-figure salaries?

A: While it is possible for engineering technicians to make six-figure salaries, it is not common. Those who specialize in high-demand areas or have many years of experience may be able to earn six-figure salaries.
