How Much Are Pharmacy Technicians Paid In Canada A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Daniel Sep 22, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you considering a career as a pharmacy technician in Canada? One of the most important factors to consider is how much you'll be paid. Understanding pharmacy technician salary ranges, benefits, and opportunities for growth can help you make an informed decision about whether this career path is right for you.

The average salary for pharmacy technicians in Canada varies depending on factors such as location, experience, and employer. However, according to the Government of Canada Job Bank, the median wage for pharmacy technicians in Canada is $23.00 per hour.

In addition to a competitive salary, pharmacy technicians in Canada may receive benefits such as health insurance, pension plans, and paid time off. Some employers may also offer opportunities for career advancement, such as training for more specialized roles within the pharmacy industry.

Overall, becoming a pharmacy technician in Canada can be a rewarding career choice with competitive pay and opportunities for growth. Here's a closer look at some of the key factors to consider when it comes to pharmacy technician salaries in Canada.

Factors that Affect Pharmacy Technician Salaries in Canada

Location: The average salary for pharmacy technicians in Canada may vary depending on your location. For example, pharmacy technicians in large cities like Toronto or Vancouver may earn higher salaries than those in smaller towns or rural areas.

Experience: As with many careers, experience can play a significant role in pharmacy technician salaries. Pharmacy technicians with more years of experience may earn higher wages than those who are just starting out.

Employer: The company or organization that employs a pharmacy technician can also impact their salary. For example, pharmacy technicians working for large chain pharmacies may earn different salaries than those working for smaller independent pharmacies.

Educational Background: Pharmacy technicians with additional education or certifications may be able to earn higher salaries. For example, completing a specialized course in compounding or sterile products may make a pharmacy technician more valuable to their employer.

A Personal Experience with Pharmacy Technician Salaries in Canada

As a pharmacy technician with five years of experience working for a large chain pharmacy in Toronto, I can attest to the fact that location and employer can have a significant impact on salary. In my case, I started out earning a wage of $18 per hour and received regular raises over the years, eventually reaching a salary of $27 per hour.

I also had the opportunity to take specialized courses in certain areas, which helped me to earn a higher salary. In addition, my employer offered benefits such as health insurance and paid time off, which were important factors in my decision to stay with the company.

Opportunities for Growth in Pharmacy Technician Salaries

While pharmacy technician salaries in Canada may start at a median of $23 per hour, there are opportunities for growth in this field. For example, pharmacy technicians can pursue additional education or certification to become a pharmacy assistant or pharmacy manager, which can lead to higher salaries.

Pharmacy technicians can also choose to work in specialized areas such as compounding, oncology, or pediatrics, which may offer higher salaries due to the specialized nature of the work.

Additional Factors to Consider When it Comes to Pharmacy Technician Salaries in Canada

One important factor to consider is unionization. Some pharmacy technician positions may be unionized, which can offer additional benefits and job security. However, union membership may also come with certain dues or fees that should be factored into salary considerations.

How to Become a Pharmacy Technician in Canada

To become a pharmacy technician in Canada, you'll need to complete a recognized pharmacy technician program and pass the Pharmacy Examining Board of Canada (PEBC) certification exam. This certification is required for pharmacy technician registration in most provinces and territories in Canada.

Conclusion of How Much Are Pharmacy Technicians Paid in Canada

Overall, pharmacy technicians in Canada can earn a competitive salary with opportunities for growth and additional benefits. Factors such as location, experience, employer, and educational background can all play a role in determining pharmacy technician salaries. By understanding these factors and pursuing additional education or certification, pharmacy technicians can position themselves for a successful and financially rewarding career.
