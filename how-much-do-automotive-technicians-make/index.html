How Much Do Automotive Technicians Make

Written by Bardi Jun 15, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Have you ever wondered how much automotive technicians make? It's a question that many people have, especially those who are considering a career in the automotive industry. If you're one of these people, then you're in the right place. In this blog post, we'll be discussing the average salary of automotive technicians, as well as some factors that can affect their earning potential.

For many people, the biggest pain point when it comes to pursuing a career in the automotive industry is the uncertainty around how much they can expect to make. This can be especially frustrating for those who are passionate about cars and want to turn that passion into a career. It's important to note that while automotive technicians can earn a good living, their salaries can vary greatly depending on a number of factors, such as their level of experience, location, and the type of employer they work for.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for automotive service technicians and mechanics was $44,050 as of May 2020. This means that half of all automotive technicians earn more than this amount, while the other half earn less. The lowest 10 percent of earners in this field made less than $25,560 per year, while the highest 10 percent earned more than $71,940.

In addition to these factors, there are a number of other things that can affect an automotive technician's earning potential. For example, those who have certifications or specialized skills may be able to command higher salaries. Likewise, those who work for dealerships or other high-end automotive businesses may earn more than those who work for independent repair shops.

My Experience

As someone who has worked in the automotive industry for several years, I can attest to the fact that salaries can vary greatly depending on a number of factors. When I first started out as a technician, I was making around $30,000 per year. However, as I gained more experience and became certified in certain areas, my salary increased to around $50,000 per year. Of course, this is just my personal experience, and others may have different experiences depending on their own circumstances.

Factors That Affect Earnings

One of the biggest factors that can affect an automotive technician's earnings is their level of experience. Those who are just starting out in the field may earn less than those who have been working in it for several years. Additionally, certifications and specialized skills can also command higher salaries. Location can also play a role, as those who work in areas with a higher cost of living may earn more than those who work in more affordable areas.

Certifications and Specialized Skills

As mentioned earlier, certifications and specialized skills can have a big impact on an automotive technician's earning potential. For example, those who are certified by the National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence (ASE) may be able to command higher salaries than those who are not. Specialized skills, such as experience working on hybrid or electric vehicles, can also lead to higher salaries.

Type of Employer

The type of employer an automotive technician works for can also affect their earning potential. Those who work for dealerships or high-end automotive businesses may earn more than those who work for independent repair shops. This is because these types of businesses may be able to charge more for their services, which can translate into higher salaries for their employees.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the average salary for an automotive technician?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for automotive service technicians and mechanics was $44,050 as of May 2020.

Q: What factors can affect an automotive technician's salary?

A: Factors that can affect an automotive technician's salary include their level of experience, certifications and specialized skills, location, and the type of employer they work for.

Q: How much can an automotive technician earn with certifications?

A: Those who are certified by the National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence (ASE) may be able to command higher salaries than those who are not.

Q: Do automotive technicians working for dealerships earn more than those who work for independent repair shops?

A: Yes, those who work for dealerships or high-end automotive businesses may earn more than those who work for independent repair shops.

Conclusion of How Much Do Automotive Technicians Make

While the salary of an automotive technician can vary depending on a number of factors, it's clear that there is potential for a good living in this field. By gaining experience, obtaining certifications, and developing specialized skills, automotive technicians can increase their earning potential and build a rewarding career in the automotive industry.
