Automotive Technical Advisor Job Description What You Need To Know

Written by Robby Jul 20, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you passionate about cars and love sharing your knowledge with others? Do you have a knack for troubleshooting and problem-solving? If so, then a career as an automotive technical advisor might be the perfect fit for you. In this blog post, we'll explore what an automotive technical advisor does, the skills required for the job, and how to become one.

Working as an automotive technical advisor can be a challenging, yet rewarding job. One of the biggest pain points for those in this field is the pressure to provide accurate and timely solutions to customers who may be experiencing car troubles. Additionally, technical advisors must have a deep understanding of car mechanics and be able to communicate complex information in a way that is easy for customers to understand.

The target of an automotive technical advisor job description is someone who is passionate about cars and wants to help others. Technical advisors are responsible for answering customer inquiries regarding car issues, providing technical support, and troubleshooting problems. They must also have a thorough understanding of car mechanics and be able to diagnose issues over the phone or via email.

In summary, an automotive technical advisor is responsible for providing technical support to customers who are experiencing car issues. This job requires strong communication skills, a deep understanding of car mechanics, and the ability to troubleshoot problems in a timely manner.

What Does an Automotive Technical Advisor Do?

As an automotive technical advisor, I've had the opportunity to help countless customers with their car troubles. One experience that stands out to me is when a customer called in with a strange noise coming from their engine. After asking a few questions and listening to the sound over the phone, I was able to diagnose the issue as a faulty alternator. I provided the customer with step-by-step instructions on how to replace the alternator, and they were able to fix the issue themselves.

On a day-to-day basis, an automotive technical advisor is responsible for answering customer inquiries, providing technical support, and troubleshooting problems. This can involve diagnosing issues over the phone or via email, providing step-by-step instructions on how to fix problems, and recommending parts and services to customers.

Skills Required for the Job

As an automotive technical advisor, it's important to have a strong understanding of car mechanics and be able to communicate complex information in a way that is easy for customers to understand. Additionally, technical advisors must have excellent customer service skills and be able to work well under pressure.

Some of the key skills required for the job include:

  • Strong technical knowledge of car mechanics
  • Excellent communication skills
  • Ability to troubleshoot problems in a timely manner
  • Strong customer service skills
  • Ability to work well under pressure

How to Become an Automotive Technical Advisor

To become an automotive technical advisor, you'll typically need a high school diploma or equivalent, as well as technical training in car mechanics. Many technical advisors also have experience working in the automotive industry.

You can increase your chances of landing a job as an automotive technical advisor by obtaining certification from organizations such as the National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence (ASE). Additionally, having strong communication and customer service skills can also help set you apart from other candidates.

Tips for Succeeding as an Automotive Technical Advisor

Here are a few tips for succeeding as an automotive technical advisor:

  • Stay up-to-date on the latest car technologies and trends
  • Develop strong communication and customer service skills
  • Be patient and empathetic with customers
  • Practice active listening to better understand customer issues
  • Develop a troubleshooting process to quickly diagnose problems
