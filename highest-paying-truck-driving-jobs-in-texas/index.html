Top 3 Highest Paying Truck Driving Jobs in Texas

1. Tanker Truck Driver - Tanker truck drivers are responsible for transporting liquid or gas materials. This job requires specialized training and experience, as well as a hazardous materials endorsement on your CDL. Tanker truck drivers can earn up to $80,000 per year in Texas.

As a former tanker truck driver, I can tell you that this job requires a lot of skill and attention to detail. You'll need to be able to handle a large vehicle and navigate through tight spaces. However, the pay is worth it, and you'll have the satisfaction of knowing that you're transporting essential materials that keep our economy running.

2. Heavy Haul Truck Driver - Heavy haul truck drivers are responsible for transporting oversized or overweight loads. This job requires specialized training and experience, as well as a wide load endorsement on your CDL. Heavy haul truck drivers can earn up to $100,000 per year in Texas.

As a former heavy haul truck driver, I can tell you that this job requires a lot of patience and attention to detail. You'll need to be able to navigate through tight spaces and handle a load that's much larger than a standard trailer. However, the pay is excellent, and you'll have the satisfaction of knowing that you're transporting essential equipment that's used in construction, mining, and other industries.

3. Refrigerated Truck Driver - Refrigerated truck drivers are responsible for transporting temperature-sensitive materials, such as food and medicine. This job requires specialized training and experience, as well as a refrigerated endorsement on your CDL. Refrigerated truck drivers can earn up to $70,000 per year in Texas.

As a former refrigerated truck driver, I can tell you that this job requires a lot of attention to detail and the ability to handle a load that requires a specific temperature range. However, the pay is good, and you'll have the satisfaction of knowing that you're transporting essential materials that keep people fed and healthy.

What You Need to Know About CDL License Requirements in Texas

In Texas, you'll need to have a Commercial Driver's License (CDL) to operate a commercial vehicle. To get a CDL, you'll need to pass a written test, a driving test, and a medical exam. You'll also need to meet certain age and residency requirements.

Once you have your CDL, you'll need to keep it up to date by taking periodic refresher courses and passing a physical exam. You'll also need to follow strict regulations regarding hours of service, vehicle maintenance, and safety.

How to Find the Best Truck Driving Jobs in Texas

If you're looking for the best truck driving jobs in Texas, then you'll need to do your research. There are many trucking companies and job boards that list job openings. You can also network with other drivers and attend job fairs to find job opportunities.

When looking for a job, be sure to consider factors such as pay, benefits, and job security. You'll also want to look for companies with a good safety record and a reputation for treating their drivers well.
