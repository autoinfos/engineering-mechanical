How Much Does An Auto Body Technician Make

Written by Frank Jul 07, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you interested in becoming an auto body technician, but wondering how much you can expect to make in this field? Look no further, as we explore the salaries and career prospects of auto body technicians.

Auto body technicians are skilled professionals who repair, restore and refinish damaged vehicles. While this can be a rewarding career, one of the biggest concerns for many people is how much they can expect to earn in this profession.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual salary for auto body technicians was $44,550 in May 2020. However, salaries can range from as low as $25,760 to as high as $76,930, depending on factors such as experience, location, and industry.

In addition to salary, auto body technicians may also be eligible for benefits such as health insurance, retirement plans, and paid time off. Some employers may also offer bonuses or commission based on performance or productivity.

Personal Experience with Auto Body Technician Salaries

As someone who has worked in the auto body industry for several years, I can attest to the fact that salaries can vary greatly depending on a variety of factors. For example, working for a high-end dealership or collision center in a major metropolitan area can often result in a higher salary than working for a smaller, independent shop in a rural area.

Another factor to consider is experience. While entry-level technicians may start out earning a lower salary, those with several years of experience and advanced certifications can often command a higher wage.

Career Outlook for Auto Body Technicians

While the median salary for auto body technicians is certainly respectable, it's important to note that this career also has a positive job outlook. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, employment of auto body technicians is projected to grow 4 percent from 2019 to 2029, which is about as fast as the average for all occupations.

This growth is in part due to the fact that many consumers prefer to repair and maintain their vehicles rather than purchase new ones. Additionally, changes in automotive technology are creating new opportunities for technicians with specialized skills such as working with electric or hybrid vehicles.

Factors Affecting Auto Body Technician Salaries

As mentioned earlier, there are several factors that can impact how much an auto body technician can expect to earn. One of the biggest factors is geographic location. In general, technicians working in urban areas or regions with a high cost of living can expect to earn more than those in rural or less expensive areas.

Experience and education are also important factors. Technicians who have completed advanced training or certification programs may be able to command a higher salary than those who have only completed basic training.

How to Increase Your Auto Body Technician Salary

If you're interested in earning more as an auto body technician, there are several steps you can take to increase your earning potential. One option is to pursue additional training or certification in areas such as welding, painting or computer diagnostics.

You may also consider seeking out employment opportunities with higher-paying employers such as luxury car dealerships or high-end collision centers. Additionally, building a strong reputation for quality work and customer service can help you attract more clients and increase your income through repeat business and referrals.

Conclusion of How Much Does an Auto Body Technician Make

In conclusion, auto body technician salaries can vary widely depending on a variety of factors such as location, experience, and industry. However, with a median salary of over $44,000 and positive job outlook, this can be a rewarding and lucrative career for those with the right skills and qualifications.
