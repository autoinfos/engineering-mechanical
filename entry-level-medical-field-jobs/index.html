Entry Level Medical Field Jobs A Guide To Your Career Path

Written by Oliver Nov 15, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you considering a career in the medical field but not sure where to start? As an entry level job seeker, it can be overwhelming to navigate the various positions and requirements in the healthcare industry. But don't worry, we've got you covered. In this post, we'll explore the different entry level medical field jobs available and help you find the right path for your future career.

The medical field can be a challenging environment, but it's also a rewarding one. You may be dealing with patients who are in distress or dealing with life-threatening illnesses. It's important to have a strong passion for helping others and a desire to make a positive impact in people's lives. The long hours, high stress, and emotional strain can also be a challenge. However, if you are truly dedicated to the profession, the rewards can be immeasurable.

So, what exactly are entry level medical field jobs? These are positions that require little to no prior experience or education in the medical field. They are typically support roles that assist healthcare professionals or provide basic care to patients. These positions can be a great way to get your foot in the door and gain valuable experience in the industry.

Now that you know what entry level medical field jobs are, let's take a closer look at some of the positions available.

Certified Nursing Assistant (CNA)

As a CNA, you will work under the supervision of a registered nurse (RN) or licensed practical nurse (LPN) to provide basic care to patients. This includes assisting with activities of daily living, such as bathing, dressing, and feeding. You may also take vital signs, record patient information, and help with mobility. CNAs typically work in nursing homes, hospitals, or assisted living facilities.

Medical Assistant

Medical assistants work in a variety of healthcare settings, including doctors' offices, clinics, and hospitals. They are responsible for administrative tasks such as scheduling appointments, answering phones, and updating medical records. They may also take patient histories, record vital signs, and assist with medical procedures.

Phlebotomist

Phlebotomists are responsible for drawing blood from patients for medical tests and procedures. They work in hospitals, clinics, and laboratories. They must be skilled at finding veins, using needles, and properly labeling blood samples.

Medical Records Technician

Medical records technicians are responsible for managing medical records and ensuring that they are accurate and up-to-date. They work in hospitals, clinics, and other healthcare settings. They must be familiar with medical terminology, coding, and computer systems.

Personal Experience with Entry Level Medical Field Jobs

As someone who started in the medical field as a CNA, I can attest to the value of these entry level positions. Not only did it give me the opportunity to gain experience and learn about the healthcare industry, but it also helped me to develop important skills such as communication, empathy, and teamwork. It was challenging at times, but ultimately, it was a rewarding experience that set me on the path to a fulfilling career.

Advancement Opportunities

One of the great things about entry level medical field jobs is that they can be a stepping stone to other positions in the industry. For example, CNAs can become licensed practical nurses (LPNs) or registered nurses (RNs) with additional education and training. Medical assistants can become office managers or move into specialized areas such as ophthalmology or podiatry. Phlebotomists can become medical laboratory technicians or technologists.

How to Get Started

If you're interested in pursuing a career in the medical field, the first step is to research the different entry level positions available and determine which one is the best fit for you. You can also talk to people who work in the industry, shadow healthcare professionals, or volunteer at a hospital or clinic. Once you've decided on a position, you can enroll in a training program or certification course to gain the necessary skills and knowledge.

Conclusion of Entry Level Medical Field Jobs

Entry level medical field jobs can be a great way to start a career in healthcare. They offer an opportunity to gain valuable experience, develop important skills, and make a difference in people's lives. Whether you're interested in becoming a CNA, medical assistant, phlebotomist, or medical records technician, there are plenty of options to choose from. By taking the time to research and explore your options, you can find the right path for your future career.

Question and Answer

Q: What qualifications do I need for entry level medical field jobs?

A: Most entry level medical field jobs require a high school diploma or equivalent. Some positions may require additional training or certification, such as a CNA program or phlebotomy course.

Q: What skills do I need for entry level medical field jobs?

A: Entry level medical field jobs require a variety of skills, including communication, empathy, attention to detail, and teamwork. You should also be comfortable working with people who are sick or in distress.

Q: What is the salary range for entry level medical field jobs?

A: The salary for entry level medical field jobs varies depending on the position and location. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for nursing assistants was $30,830 in May 2020, while medical assistants earned a median annual wage of $35,850.

Q: What are the career prospects for entry level medical field jobs?

A: Entry level medical field jobs can be a stepping stone to other positions in the industry. With additional education and training, CNAs can become LPNs or RNs, while medical assistants can become office managers or specialize in a particular area of healthcare.
