Entry Level Oil Field Jobs Your Gateway To A Promising Career

Written by Bardi Aug 29, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you looking for a job that offers good pay and opportunities for advancement? Look no further than entry level oil field jobs! In this post, we'll explore the world of oil field jobs and how you can get started in this exciting industry.

Pain Points of Entry Level Oil Field Jobs

Many people are hesitant to pursue entry level oil field jobs due to the perceived danger and physical demands of the work. Additionally, the long hours and remote locations can be a challenge for some. However, with the right training and preparation, these challenges can be overcome.

What are Entry Level Oil Field Jobs?

Entry level oil field jobs are positions that require little to no prior experience in the oil and gas industry. These jobs typically involve manual labor, such as drilling, rigging, and maintenance. They may also involve driving trucks or operating heavy machinery.

While the work can be demanding, entry level oil field jobs offer competitive pay and opportunities for advancement. With experience and additional training, you can progress through the ranks and move into more specialized roles.

The Benefits of Entry Level Oil Field Jobs

One of the biggest benefits of entry level oil field jobs is the pay. Salaries for these positions can range from $50,000 to $80,000 per year. Additionally, many companies offer benefits such as health insurance, retirement plans, and paid vacation time.

Another benefit is the opportunity for advancement. With hard work and dedication, you can move up the ranks and take on more responsibility. This can lead to higher pay and more specialized roles, such as drilling engineer or operations manager.

Personal Experience with Entry Level Oil Field Jobs

When I first started in the oil and gas industry, I was hesitant to take on an entry level position. However, I quickly realized that these jobs offer a great opportunity to learn and grow within the industry. I started as a roughneck on a drilling rig, and within a few years, I was promoted to a drilling engineer position.

My advice to anyone considering entry level oil field jobs is to be prepared for hard work and long hours. But if you're willing to put in the effort, the rewards can be significant.

Training for Entry Level Oil Field Jobs

Many companies offer training programs for entry level oil field jobs. These programs typically cover safety procedures, equipment operation, and basic drilling techniques. Some programs may also offer certifications in areas such as first aid and CPR.

There are also a number of vocational schools and community colleges that offer courses in oil and gas technology. These programs can provide a solid foundation for a career in the industry.

Types of Entry Level Oil Field Jobs

Some common entry level oil field jobs include:

  • Roughneck: Performs manual labor on drilling rigs, such as loading and unloading equipment and handling drilling pipe.
  • Floorhand: Assists the driller and roughnecks with drilling operations, such as operating the tongs that screw and unscrew the drilling pipe.
  • Welder: Joins metal parts together using welding techniques.
  • Heavy Equipment Operator: Operates heavy machinery, such as bulldozers and backhoes, to move earth and other materials.

How to Get Started in Entry Level Oil Field Jobs

If you're interested in entry level oil field jobs, the first step is to research companies in the industry. Many companies have job listings on their websites or with recruiting agencies.

It's also important to be prepared for the physical demands of the work. This may include strength training and cardiovascular exercise. Additionally, you should be prepared to work long hours and to spend extended periods of time away from home.

Personal Growth in Entry Level Oil Field Jobs

Working in the oil and gas industry can be challenging, but it can also be incredibly rewarding. In addition to the financial benefits, entry level oil field jobs offer opportunities for personal growth and development.

Working in a team environment and overcoming challenges can help build confidence and leadership skills. Additionally, the technical knowledge gained from working in the industry can be valuable in a number of other fields.

Question and Answer

A: Entry level oil field jobs can be physically demanding, involving manual labor and long hours. It's important to be in good physical condition and to prepare for the demands of the work.

A: Salaries for entry level oil field jobs can range from $50,000 to $80,000 per year. Additionally, many companies offer benefits such as health insurance, retirement plans, and paid vacation time.

Q: What kind of opportunities for advancement are available in entry level oil field jobs?

A: With experience and additional training, you can progress through the ranks and move into more specialized roles. This can lead to higher pay and more responsibility, such as drilling engineer or operations manager.

Conclusion of Entry Level Oil Field Jobs

Entry level oil field jobs offer a great opportunity for those looking for a career in the oil and gas industry. With competitive pay and opportunities for advancement, these positions can be a great way to build a rewarding career. If you're willing to work hard and prepared for the challenges, entry level oil field jobs may be the perfect fit for you.
