How Much Money Can You Make As An Electrical Engineer

Written by Daniel Jun 24, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career in electrical engineering but wondering how much you can earn? The field of electrical engineering is vast, and the earning potential can vary depending on several factors. In this post, we will explore the different salaries that electrical engineers can earn and what factors can affect their earnings.

As with any profession, there are pain points related to how much money you can make as an electrical engineer. One of the biggest pain points is the potential for a competitive job market. With more people entering the field, it can become challenging to find a high-paying job. Additionally, electrical engineering can be a demanding and complex field, which can require specialized skills and knowledge.

So, how much money can you make as an electrical engineer? According to the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for electrical engineers was $101,250 as of May 2020. However, the salary range can vary significantly based on factors such as experience, education level, certifications, and the industry you work in.

In summary, several factors can influence how much money you can make as an electrical engineer. These include your experience, education level, certifications, and the industry you work in. However, the median annual wage for electrical engineers is $101,250, according to the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics.

Salary Range for Entry-Level Electrical Engineers

As an entry-level electrical engineer, you can expect to earn a salary in the range of $60,000 to $75,000 per year, according to Glassdoor. However, this can vary depending on your location, the industry you work in, and your qualifications. For example, if you have a master's degree or specialized certifications, you may be able to earn a higher salary.

When I started my career as an electrical engineer, I earned around $65,000 per year. However, I was able to increase my earning potential by obtaining additional certifications and specializing in a particular field within electrical engineering.

Salary Range for Experienced Electrical Engineers

Experienced electrical engineers can earn a higher salary, with the potential to earn six figures or more. According to the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics, the top 10% of electrical engineers earned more than $154,000 as of May 2020. However, this can vary depending on your experience, location, and industry.

Personally, I have been working as an electrical engineer for over ten years and have been able to increase my earning potential significantly. I currently earn a six-figure salary and have been able to work on exciting projects in the renewable energy industry.

Factors That Affect Your Salary as an Electrical Engineer

Several factors can influence how much money you can make as an electrical engineer. These include:

  • Experience
  • Education level
  • Specialized certifications
  • Industry
  • Location

For example, if you work in the aerospace industry, you may be able to earn a higher salary than if you work in the telecommunications industry. Additionally, if you have specialized certifications in areas such as power systems or renewable energy, you may be able to command a higher salary.

Tips for Increasing Your Earning Potential as an Electrical Engineer

If you are interested in increasing your earning potential as an electrical engineer, here are some tips:

  • Obtain specialized certifications
  • Consider obtaining a master's degree
  • Network with other professionals in your field
  • Stay up-to-date with the latest industry trends
  • Consider specializing in a particular field within electrical engineering
