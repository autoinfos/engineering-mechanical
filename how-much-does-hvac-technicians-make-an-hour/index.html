How Much Does Hvac Technicians Make An Hour

Written by William Sep 01, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career as an HVAC technician? One of the most important factors to consider before choosing any career is the potential income. So, how much does HVAC technicians make an hour? In this post, we'll dive into the average hourly wage for HVAC technicians, as well as some factors that can affect their earnings.

When it comes to HVAC technicians, the pay can vary depending on several factors. Some of these factors include location, level of experience, and certifications. Additionally, the type of HVAC work can also impact earnings. For example, an HVAC technician who specializes in commercial systems may earn more than one who works primarily on residential systems.

On average, HVAC technicians in the United States make around $25 per hour. However, this can range from $15 to $40 per hour depending on the factors mentioned above. It's important to note that some HVAC technicians also earn additional income through commissions or bonuses for meeting certain performance goals.

In summary, HVAC technicians can earn a decent hourly wage, but their earnings can vary depending on several factors. Let's take a closer look at some of these factors:

The Impact of Location on HVAC Technician Wages

Location is one of the most significant factors that can affect how much an HVAC technician makes per hour. In general, areas with a higher cost of living tend to pay higher wages to compensate for the increased expenses. Additionally, areas that experience extreme temperatures may require more HVAC technicians, which can lead to higher wages due to increased demand.

For example, HVAC technicians working in New York City can expect to earn an average of $29 per hour. On the other hand, those working in rural areas may earn closer to $20 per hour.

The Impact of Certifications on HVAC Technician Wages

Another factor that can impact HVAC technician wages is certifications. Technicians who hold certifications from organizations such as the North American Technician Excellence (NATE) or the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) may be able to earn more due to their specialized knowledge and skills.

Certifications can also lead to more job opportunities, as some employers may require certain certifications for specific positions. This can result in higher wages for those who hold these certifications.

The Importance of Experience in HVAC Technician Wages

Experience is another crucial factor in determining an HVAC technician's earnings. Technicians with more experience typically earn higher wages due to their knowledge and expertise. Additionally, experienced technicians may be able to take on more complex and higher-paying jobs.

Entry-level HVAC technicians can expect to earn around $15 to $18 per hour. However, those with several years of experience can earn closer to $30 per hour.

Tips for Increasing Your HVAC Technician Wage

If you're looking to increase your earnings as an HVAC technician, there are several steps you can take. These include:

  • Obtaining certifications to demonstrate your expertise
  • Seeking out higher-paying job opportunities, such as those in commercial HVAC
  • Continuing your education to stay up-to-date with the latest HVAC technology
  • Networking with other HVAC professionals to learn about job opportunities and industry trends

Questions and Answers about How Much Does HVAC Technicians Make an Hour

Q: Is it possible to earn more than $40 per hour as an HVAC technician?

A: While $40 per hour is on the high end, some HVAC technicians may be able to earn more than this. This is typically reserved for those with significant experience and specialized skills.

Q: Do HVAC technicians typically receive benefits?

A: Yes, many HVAC technicians receive benefits such as health insurance, retirement plans, and paid time off.

Q: Can HVAC technicians work part-time?

A: Yes, part-time HVAC technician positions are available. However, these may pay less than full-time positions.

Q: Are HVAC technician wages expected to increase in the future?

A: Yes, job outlook for HVAC technicians is positive, with a projected growth rate of 4% from 2019 to 2029. This may lead to increased demand and higher wages.

Conclusion of How Much Does HVAC Technicians Make an Hour

Overall, HVAC technicians can earn a decent hourly wage, with an average of $25 per hour. However, this can vary depending on factors such as location, certifications, and experience. By obtaining certifications and seeking out higher-paying job opportunities, HVAC technicians can increase their earnings and advance their careers.
