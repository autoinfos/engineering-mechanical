How Much Do Car Dealers Make

Written by Christine Oct 27, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Car dealerships are a staple of modern life, but have you ever wondered how much money they actually make? In this blog post, we'll dive into the world of car dealership finances to answer the burning question: how much do car dealers make?

For many people, buying a car is a stressful experience. Between negotiating with salespeople, filling out paperwork, and trying to understand all the different fees and charges, it can be overwhelming. One of the biggest sources of anxiety is the fear that you're getting ripped off and that the dealership is making a huge profit on your purchase.

So, how much do car dealers make? The answer is, it depends. There are a lot of factors that go into how much money a dealership makes, including the size of the dealership, the location, the types of cars they sell, and the current market conditions. However, on average, car dealerships make about 2-3% profit on each car they sell.

Now that we've answered the main question, let's dive deeper into the factors that affect how much car dealers make. Firstly, the size of the dealership can have a big impact. Larger dealerships tend to sell more cars and have more revenue streams, such as service and maintenance, which can increase profits. Additionally, dealerships in prime locations, such as in busy urban areas or near major highways, tend to make more money than those in more rural areas.

The Personal Experience of a Car Dealer

As someone who has worked in the car dealership industry for over a decade, I can attest to the fact that how much money a dealership makes can vary widely. At the dealership where I work, we sell both new and used cars, which helps increase our profits. We also offer a range of financing options, which brings in additional revenue. However, the market can be unpredictable, and sales can fluctuate from month to month.

The Impact of Car Sales on Dealer Profits

Another factor that impacts how much car dealers make is the types of cars they sell. Dealerships that specialize in luxury cars or high-end sports cars tend to make more money per sale, but they also have a smaller customer base. On the other hand, dealerships that sell more affordable cars, such as those aimed at first-time buyers or families, may have lower profit margins per sale but can make up for it with a higher volume of sales.

The Role of Market Conditions in Dealer Profits

Market conditions also play a big role in how much car dealers make. When the economy is strong and people have more disposable income, they're more likely to buy cars. Additionally, when gas prices are low, people tend to buy larger, less fuel-efficient cars, which can be more expensive and bring in more profit for dealerships. However, when the economy is weak or gas prices are high, car sales tend to decrease, which can hurt dealership profits.

How to Negotiate with Car Dealers to Get a Good Deal

If you're in the market for a new or used car, it's important to understand how car dealers make their money so that you can negotiate effectively and get a good deal. One tip is to do your research ahead of time and know the fair market value of the car you're interested in. This will give you a baseline for negotiations and help you avoid getting ripped off. You can also try to negotiate on fees and charges, such as documentation fees or extended warranties, which can add up quickly.

Conclusion of How Much Do Car Dealers Make

In conclusion, the answer to how much do car dealers make is that it varies widely depending on a number of factors. However, on average, dealerships make about 2-3% profit per car sold. If you're in the market for a car, it's important to do your research and negotiate effectively to ensure that you get a good deal and don't overpay for your new ride.

Question and Answer

Q: Do car dealers make more money on new or used cars?

A: It depends on the dealership and the types of cars they sell. Dealerships that specialize in luxury or high-end sports cars may make more money per sale on new cars, while dealerships that sell more affordable cars may make more money on used cars.

Q: How do car dealerships make money on financing?

A: Dealerships make money on financing by marking up the interest rate on the loan. They work with banks and other lenders to offer financing options to customers, and they add a percentage to the interest rate as a fee for their services.

Q: Can you negotiate with car dealers on the price of a car?

A: Yes, you can negotiate with car dealers on the price of a car. It's important to do your research ahead of time and know the fair market value of the car you're interested in, so that you have a baseline for negotiations.

Q: How can I avoid getting ripped off by a car dealership?

A: To avoid getting ripped off by a car dealership, it's important to do your research ahead of time, negotiate effectively, and be aware of any fees or charges that may be added to the price of the car. You can also consider getting pre-approved for financing from a bank or credit union, so that you have a better idea of what you can afford and what interest rates you qualify for.
