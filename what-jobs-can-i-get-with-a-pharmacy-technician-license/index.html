What Jobs Can I Get With A Pharmacy Technician License

Written by Christine Oct 30, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Are you a pharmacy technician looking for a new career path? Or perhaps you're considering pursuing a pharmacy technician license but want to know what job opportunities are available? Look no further, as we explore the various career paths that a pharmacy technician license can lead to.

As a pharmacy technician, you may experience pain points such as limited career growth, lack of job satisfaction or burnout. However, obtaining a pharmacy technician license can open up a variety of job opportunities that may alleviate these pain points.

So, what jobs can you get with a pharmacy technician license? The short answer is - quite a few! Here are some of the most common career paths:

Retail Pharmacy Technician

A retail pharmacy technician works in a pharmacy that serves customers directly, such as a drugstore or grocery store pharmacy. The role involves assisting the pharmacist with filling prescriptions, managing inventory, and providing excellent customer service. I started my career as a retail pharmacy technician and found the job to be rewarding as I was able to interact with customers and help them with their health needs.

Hospital Pharmacy Technician

If you prefer a hospital setting, a hospital pharmacy technician may be the right fit for you. In this role, you will work in a hospital or healthcare facility pharmacy and assist the pharmacist with medication dispensing, inventory management, and patient care. I have a friend who works as a hospital pharmacy technician and loves the fast-paced environment and the opportunity to work with patients who have complex medication needs.

Long-Term Care Pharmacy Technician

A long-term care pharmacy technician works in a facility that provides care to patients who require long-term or specialized care, such as nursing homes, assisted living facilities or hospice care. In this role, you will work closely with the pharmacist to ensure that patients receive the appropriate medications and that their medical records are accurately maintained.

Pharmaceutical Industry Technician

A pharmaceutical industry technician works in a laboratory or manufacturing facility that produces medications. In this role, you will assist with the production process, quality control, and packaging of medications. This job offers a unique opportunity to work behind the scenes in the pharmaceutical industry.

Question and Answer

Q: What skills do I need to have to be a pharmacy technician?

A: Pharmacy technicians need to have strong attention to detail, excellent customer service skills, and be comfortable working in a fast-paced environment. Strong communication skills and the ability to work well in a team are also important.

Q: How long does it take to become a pharmacy technician?

A: The time it takes to become a pharmacy technician varies depending on the program. Some programs can be completed in as little as 6 months, while others may take up to 2 years to complete.

Q: Can I become a pharmacy technician if I have a criminal record?

A: It depends on the severity of the criminal offense. Some states may bar individuals with certain criminal convictions from obtaining a pharmacy technician license.

Q: Can I work as a pharmacy technician without a license?

A: It depends on the state laws. Some states do not require licensure for pharmacy technicians, while others do. It is important to check the requirements in your state before applying for jobs.

Conclusion of What Jobs Can I Get with a Pharmacy Technician License

As you can see, there are several career paths available to pharmacy technicians with a pharmacy technician license. Whether you prefer a retail, hospital, or long-term care setting, or are interested in working in the pharmaceutical industry, there is a role that fits your interests and skillset. Pursuing a pharmacy technician license can be a smart career move and can lead to a fulfilling and rewarding career.
