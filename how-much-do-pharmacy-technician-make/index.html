How Much Do Pharmacy Technicians Make

If you're interested in a career as a pharmacy technician, you're probably wondering how much money you can expect to earn. The truth is, pharmacy technicians can make a decent living, but the exact amount you'll earn can vary depending on a number of factors. In this post, we'll take a closer look at how much do.pharmacy technician make.

One of the biggest pain points for people considering a career as a pharmacy technician is the fear that they won't make enough money. After all, no one wants to work hard and not be able to pay the bills. However, the good news is that pharmacy technicians can earn a competitive salary, especially if they have experience or additional certifications.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual salary for pharmacy technicians in 2020 was $34,610. This means that half of all pharmacy technicians earned more than this amount, while the other half earned less. The lowest 10 percent of pharmacy technicians earned less than $25,280 per year, while the highest 10 percent earned more than $49,130 per year.

In conclusion, pharmacy technicians can earn a decent salary, with the median annual salary being around $34,610. However, the exact amount you can expect to earn will depend on a number of factors, including your experience, certifications, and location.

How Much Do Pharmacy Technicians Make: My Personal Experience

When I first started working as a pharmacy technician, I was making around $15 per hour. However, after a few years of experience and obtaining my certification, my salary increased to around $20 per hour. I also live in an area where the cost of living is relatively low, so my salary goes further than it might in other parts of the country.

If you're just starting out as a pharmacy technician, you can expect to make around $15-$18 per hour, depending on your location and employer. However, as you gain experience and obtain certifications, you can expect your salary to increase.

How Much Do Pharmacy Technicians Make: Factors That Impact Salary

There are several factors that can impact how much a pharmacy technician makes. Some of these include:

Experience:

As with most jobs, experience often translates to a higher salary. If you're just starting out as a pharmacy technician, you can expect to make less than someone who has been working in the field for several years.

Location:

Where you live can also impact your salary as a pharmacy technician. Salaries tend to be higher in areas with a higher cost of living, such as large cities.

Certifications:

Obtaining additional certifications can also lead to a higher salary. For example, becoming a Certified Pharmacy Technician (CPhT) can increase your earning potential.

How Much Do Pharmacy Technicians Make: Negotiating Your Salary

If you're currently working as a pharmacy technician and feel that you're not being paid what you're worth, it may be time to negotiate your salary. Before you do, it's important to do your research and find out what other pharmacy technicians in your area are making.
