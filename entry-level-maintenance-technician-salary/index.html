Entry Level Maintenance Technician Salary What You Need To Know

Written by William Aug 18, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're considering a career as an entry level maintenance technician, you're probably wondering about the salary. After all, you want to ensure that your hard work will pay off in the long run. Fortunately, there are plenty of opportunities for growth in this field, and with the right skills and experience, you can earn a competitive salary.

One of the main pain points of entry level maintenance technician salary is that it can vary widely depending on the industry, experience, and location. Additionally, many entry level positions may start at a lower salary until you gain more experience and skills.

The target salary for entry level maintenance technicians ranges from $30,000 to $40,000 per year, depending on the industry and location. However, with experience and additional certifications, you can earn a higher salary, with some technicians earning up to $60,000 per year.

In summary, entry level maintenance technician salary can vary based on a variety of factors, but with experience and dedication, you can earn a competitive salary in this field. Let's take a closer look at some of the key factors that impact entry level maintenance technician salary.

Factors that Impact Entry Level Maintenance Technician Salary

One of the biggest factors that impact entry level maintenance technician salary is experience. Many entry level positions may start at a lower salary until you gain more experience and skills in the field. Additionally, certifications and specialized training can also increase your earning potential.

Industry and location also play a big role in entry level maintenance technician salary. For example, those working in the manufacturing industry may earn a higher salary compared to those working in the hospitality industry. Similarly, those in high-cost cities like San Francisco or New York may earn a higher salary compared to those in smaller, less expensive cities.

My Experience with Entry Level Maintenance Technician Salary

When I first started as an entry level maintenance technician, I was earning around $35,000 per year. However, as I gained more experience and certifications, my salary increased to around $50,000 per year. Additionally, I found that working in the healthcare industry offered higher salaries compared to other industries.

Opportunities for Growth in Entry Level Maintenance Technician Salary

One of the great things about working as an entry level maintenance technician is that there are plenty of opportunities for growth. By gaining experience and certifications, you can move up the ladder to higher-paying positions like lead technician or maintenance manager. Additionally, you can explore industries that offer higher salaries, such as healthcare or manufacturing.

How to Increase Your Entry Level Maintenance Technician Salary

If you're looking to increase your entry level maintenance technician salary, there are a few things you can do. First, consider gaining additional certifications or specialized training in areas like HVAC or electrical systems. Additionally, gaining experience in a niche industry like healthcare or manufacturing can also increase your earning potential.

Networking for Entry Level Maintenance Technician Salary

Networking is another important factor in increasing your entry level maintenance technician salary. Attend industry events and conferences to meet other professionals in the field and learn about new opportunities. Additionally, consider joining professional organizations like the National Maintenance Agreements Policy Committee or the International Maintenance Institute to connect with other professionals and stay up-to-date on industry trends.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the average salary for an entry level maintenance technician in the manufacturing industry?

A: The average salary for an entry level maintenance technician in the manufacturing industry is around $40,000 per year, but can vary depending on experience and location.

Q: Is it necessary to have certifications to earn a higher entry level maintenance technician salary?

A: While not always necessary, gaining additional certifications or specialized training can increase your earning potential as an entry level maintenance technician.

Q: Can networking help increase your entry level maintenance technician salary?

A: Yes, networking can be an important factor in increasing your entry level maintenance technician salary. Attending industry events and joining professional organizations can help you connect with other professionals and learn about new opportunities.

Q: Is it possible to earn a high salary as an entry level maintenance technician?

A: While entry level positions may start at a lower salary, with experience and additional certifications, you can earn a competitive salary as an entry level maintenance technician, with some technicians earning up to $60,000 per year.

Conclusion of Entry Level Maintenance Technician Salary

In conclusion, entry level maintenance technician salary can vary widely depending on a variety of factors, including experience, industry, and location. However, with dedication and hard work, you can earn a competitive salary in this field. By gaining experience, certifications, and networking with other professionals, you can increase your earning potential and take your career to new heights.
