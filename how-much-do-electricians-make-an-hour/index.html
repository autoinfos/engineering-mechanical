How Much Do Electricians Make An Hour A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Frank Nov 19, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career as an electrician, but wondering about the potential earning potential? Or perhaps you're an electrician yourself, looking to benchmark your salary against industry standards. In either case, you've come to the right place. In this post, we'll explore the topic of how much do electricians make an hour, and provide you with an in-depth guide to understanding this important aspect of the profession.

Being an electrician can be a challenging and rewarding career path. However, one of the most common concerns among those considering a career in this field is how much they can expect to earn. The good news is that electricians are generally well paid, with a median hourly wage of $26.01 as of May 2020, according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS).

There are a number of factors that can influence how much an electrician makes per hour. Some of these include their level of experience and education, the type of employer they work for, and the geographic location of their work. In the following paragraphs, we'll explore each of these factors in more detail.

The Role of Experience and Education in Electrician Salaries

One of the most important factors that can influence how much an electrician makes per hour is their level of experience and education. Generally speaking, the more experience and education an electrician has, the higher their hourly wage is likely to be.

For example, an entry-level electrician with less than one year of experience can expect to earn an hourly wage of around $14.50, while a mid-career electrician with 5-10 years of experience can expect to earn an hourly wage of around $22.50. A highly experienced electrician with 20+ years of experience can expect to earn an hourly wage of around $32.50. Additionally, electricians with specialized certifications or training in areas like solar or wind energy may be able to command even higher salaries.

The Impact of Employer Type on Electrician Salaries

The type of employer an electrician works for can also have a significant impact on their hourly wage. For example, electricians who work for government or utilities companies tend to earn higher salaries than those who work for smaller private companies. Unionized electricians also tend to earn higher wages than non-unionized electricians.

Additionally, electricians who work for themselves as independent contractors may be able to earn higher hourly rates, but will also be responsible for securing their own work and paying for their own healthcare and retirement benefits.

The Influence of Geographic Location on Electrician Salaries

Another important factor that can impact how much an electrician makes per hour is their geographic location. Generally speaking, electricians who work in urban areas or areas with a high cost of living tend to earn higher salaries than those who work in rural or low cost of living areas.

For example, electricians who work in California, New York, or Alaska tend to earn some of the highest salaries in the country, with hourly wages ranging from $30-$35 per hour on average. Meanwhile, electricians who work in states like Montana, South Dakota, or Mississippi tend to earn lower salaries, with hourly wages ranging from $20-$25 per hour on average.

Questions and Answers About How Much Do Electricians Make an Hour

Q: Do electricians make more than plumbers?

A: Yes, electricians generally make more than plumbers on an hourly basis. According to the BLS, the median hourly wage for electricians as of May 2020 was $26.01, while the median hourly wage for plumbers was $25.28.

Q: Is it worth becoming an electrician?

A: Yes, becoming an electrician can be a lucrative and rewarding career path. In addition to potentially high hourly wages, electricians also have job stability, opportunities for advancement, and the ability to work in a variety of different settings.

Q: How long does it take to become an electrician?

A: The length of time it takes to become an electrician can vary depending on the specific requirements of your state or jurisdiction. Generally speaking, however, most electricians complete a four or five-year apprenticeship program and are required to pass a state licensing exam in order to work independently.

Q: Can electricians make six figures?

A: Yes, it is possible for electricians to make six-figure salaries with the right combination of experience, education, and specialization. According to the BLS, the top 10% of electricians earned an annual salary of $98,720 or more as of May 2020.

Conclusion of How Much Do Electricians Make an Hour

Overall, electricians are generally well-compensated for their skills and expertise, with a median hourly wage of $26.01 as of May 2020. However, this hourly wage can vary depending on a number of factors, including an electrician's level of experience and education, the type of employer they work for, and their geographic location. By understanding these factors and how they influence electrician salaries, you can make an informed decision about whether this career path is right for you.
