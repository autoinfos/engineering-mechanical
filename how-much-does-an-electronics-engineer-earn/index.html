How Much Does An Electronics Engineer Earn

Written by Bardi Jul 29, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you interested in pursuing a career as an electronics engineer but unsure about the earning potential? Well, you've come to the right place! In this blog post, we will be discussing how much an electronics engineer can earn and factors that affect their salary.

As with any profession, there are certain pain points that electronics engineers face when it comes to their salary. Some of these include the level of education and experience, the location of their job, and the industry they work in. Additionally, electronics engineers may be concerned about job security and the possibility of a stagnant salary as they progress in their career.

So, how much can an electronics engineer expect to earn? According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for electronics engineers in the United States was $107,540 in May 2020. However, this can vary depending on factors such as education level, industry, and experience. For example, electronics engineers working in the computer and electronic product manufacturing industry tend to earn higher salaries than those working in telecommunications.

In summary, electronics engineering is a lucrative career path with a median annual salary of over $100,000. However, factors such as education, experience, industry, and location can have an impact on salary.

Factors That Affect Electronics Engineer Earnings

Now, let's take a closer look at the factors that can affect an electronics engineer's salary:

Education and Experience

As with many professions, electronics engineers with higher levels of education and experience tend to earn higher salaries. Advanced degrees such as a Master's or Ph.D. in Electrical Engineering can lead to higher-paying job opportunities.

Industry

The industry that an electronics engineer works in can also impact their earnings. Those in the computer and electronic product manufacturing industry tend to earn higher salaries than those in telecommunications or engineering services.

My Personal Experience with Electronics Engineer Earnings

As a former electronics engineer myself, I can attest to the earning potential of this career path. After completing my Bachelor's in Electrical Engineering, I was able to secure a job in the computer and electronic product manufacturing industry with a starting salary of $80,000. Over the years, I was able to increase my salary through promotions and gaining more experience in the field.

Job Outlook for Electronics Engineers

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the employment of electronics engineers is projected to grow 3% from 2019 to 2029, which is about as fast as the average for all occupations. This growth is due to the continued demand for consumer electronics and advancements in technology.

FAQs About Electronics Engineer Earnings

Q: What is the average salary for an entry-level electronics engineer?

A: According to Glassdoor, the average salary for an entry-level electronics engineer in the United States is $73,167 per year.

Q: Do electronics engineers make more than software engineers?

A: It depends on the industry and level of experience. In general, software engineers tend to earn higher salaries. However, electronics engineers working in certain industries such as computer and electronic product manufacturing can earn comparable salaries to software engineers.

Q: Can electronics engineers work remotely?

A: Yes, many electronics engineering jobs can be done remotely. However, some positions may require on-site work or travel.

Q: How can I increase my earning potential as an electronics engineer?

A: Pursuing higher levels of education and gaining more experience in the field can lead to higher-paying job opportunities. Additionally, specializing in a certain area such as embedded systems or telecommunications can also make you a more valuable candidate.

Conclusion of How Much Does an Electronics Engineer Earn?

Overall, electronics engineering is a lucrative career path with a median annual salary of over $100,000. While factors such as education, experience, industry, and location can impact earnings, pursuing higher levels of education and gaining more experience in the field can lead to higher-paying job opportunities. With a growing demand for consumer electronics and advancements in technology, the job outlook for electronics engineers looks promising.
