How Much Does A Car Mechanic Make Find Out Here

Written by Daniel May 26, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you considering a career as a car mechanic? Do you want to know how much money you can make in this field? If so, you're in the right place. In this post, we'll explore the average salary of a car mechanic and discuss the factors that can impact your earnings.

Many people choose to become car mechanics because they love cars and enjoy working with their hands. However, the pay can be a concern for some. It's important to understand that the salary of a car mechanic can vary depending on several factors, such as experience, location, and type of employer.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for automotive service technicians and mechanics was $44,050 in May 2020. The lowest 10 percent earned less than $25,790, and the highest 10 percent earned more than $71,940. Keep in mind that these figures are for the entire country, and salaries can differ depending on where you live.

In conclusion, a car mechanic's salary can vary depending on several factors, such as experience, location, and type of employer. However, the median annual wage for automotive service technicians and mechanics was $44,050 in May 2020, according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics.

How Much Does a Car Mechanic Make in a Dealership?

When I first started working as a car mechanic, I worked in a dealership. I was excited to work with brand new cars and learn from experienced technicians. However, I quickly realized that the pay was lower than I expected.

Dealerships typically pay their mechanics an hourly rate, which can range from $15 to $30 per hour, depending on your experience and the dealership's location. In addition to your hourly rate, you may receive bonuses or commissions based on your performance.

How Much Does a Car Mechanic Make in an Independent Repair Shop?

After a few years of working in a dealership, I decided to switch to an independent repair shop. I found that the pay was higher than what I was making before, and I had more opportunities for growth.

Independent repair shops may pay their mechanics an hourly rate or a flat rate. Flat rate pay is based on the amount of work you complete, and can be a more lucrative option if you're a fast and efficient worker. The average hourly rate for a mechanic in an independent repair shop is around $20 to $30 per hour.

Factors That Impact a Car Mechanic's Salary

As we mentioned earlier, several factors can impact a car mechanic's salary. Here are some of the most important ones:

  • Experience: The more experience you have, the higher your salary can be.
  • Location: Salaries can differ depending on where you live. For example, a car mechanic in New York City may make more than one in a smaller town.
  • Type of Employer: Dealerships and independent repair shops may pay their mechanics differently.
  • Specialization: If you specialize in a particular area, such as diesel engines or high-performance cars, you may be able to command a higher salary.

How Can You Increase Your Salary as a Car Mechanic?

If you want to increase your salary as a car mechanic, there are several steps you can take:

  • Get certified: Earning certifications from organizations like the National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence (ASE) can help you stand out from other job candidates.
  • Specialize: If you become an expert in a particular area, such as hybrid vehicles or computer diagnostics, you may be able to earn a higher salary.
  • Gain experience: The more experience you have, the more valuable you are to an employer.
  • Consider moving: If you live in an area where salaries are lower, you may want to consider moving to a place where there is more demand for car mechanics.

Question and Answer

Q: How much does a beginner car mechanic make?

A: The salary of a beginner car mechanic can vary depending on the factors mentioned earlier. However, the average hourly rate for an entry-level mechanic is around $15 per hour.

Q: Can a car mechanic make six figures?

A: It's rare for a car mechanic to make six figures, but it's not impossible. If you specialize in a high-demand area, such as performance tuning, you may be able to earn a higher salary.

Q: What is the highest paying job in the automotive industry?

A: The highest paying job in the automotive industry is a chief mechanic, with a median salary of $83,930 per year.

Q: What is the job outlook for car mechanics?

A: The job outlook for car mechanics is good, with a projected growth rate of 4 percent from 2019 to 2029.

Conclusion of How Much Does a Car Mechanic Make

In conclusion, a car mechanic's salary can vary depending on several factors, such as experience, location, and type of employer. The median annual wage for automotive service technicians and mechanics was $44,050 in May 2020, according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics. If you're interested in becoming a car mechanic, it's important to research the job market in your area and consider the factors that can impact your salary.
