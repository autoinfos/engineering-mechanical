How Much Do Car Mechanics Make Per Hour

Written by William Jul 15, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you curious about how much car mechanics are getting paid per hour? Do you want to know if it's a lucrative career path to take? If so, then you're in the right place. In this blog post, we will discuss how much do car mechanics make per hour and what factors can affect their hourly rate.

Being a mechanic can be a tough job. They have to deal with dirty, greasy, and sometimes dangerous working conditions. They also have to deal with the pressure of fixing a car and making sure it's safe for the driver. Despite all these challenges, many people still choose to pursue this career path because of the potential to earn a good income.

The average hourly rate for a car mechanic in the United States is around $20. However, this can vary depending on several factors, such as experience level, location, and type of employer. Some mechanics can earn as much as $30 per hour or more, while others may only earn around $15 per hour.

In general, mechanics who work for dealerships or specialty repair shops tend to earn more than those who work for general repair shops. This is because dealerships and specialty repair shops often work on higher-end cars and have a higher level of expertise, which translates to higher pay.

Personal Experience with Car Mechanics' Hourly Rate

As a car owner, I have had to visit several mechanics over the years for various repairs and maintenance services. From my personal experience, I have noticed that the hourly rate for mechanics can vary greatly depending on where you go. For example, I once took my car to a dealership for a simple oil change, and I was charged twice as much as I would have paid at a general repair shop.

Factors Affecting Car Mechanics' Hourly Rate

Aside from the type of employer, experience level, and location, there are other factors that can affect how much a car mechanic makes per hour. One of these factors is certification. Mechanics who have certifications from the National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence (ASE) tend to earn more than those who don't. This is because ASE certification is a sign of expertise and professionalism, which many employers value.

Education and Training

Another factor that can affect a car mechanic's hourly rate is education and training. Mechanics who have completed formal training programs or have a degree in automotive technology may earn more than those who don't. This is because formal education and training can provide a more in-depth understanding of how cars work and how to fix them.

Negotiation Skills

Lastly, negotiation skills can also play a role in how much a car mechanic makes per hour. Mechanics who are good at negotiating their pay and benefits may be able to earn more than those who aren't. This is because they can advocate for themselves and make a compelling case for why they deserve higher pay.

Question and Answer

Q: Is being a car mechanic a good career choice?
A: Yes, being a car mechanic can be a good career choice for those who enjoy working with their hands and have a passion for cars. It can also be a lucrative career path with the potential to earn a good income.

Q: How much does a beginner car mechanic make per hour?
A: A beginner car mechanic can expect to earn around $11-$15 per hour. However, this can vary depending on the factors we discussed earlier.

Q: Can car mechanics earn overtime pay?
A: Yes, most car mechanics are eligible for overtime pay if they work more than 40 hours per week. This can significantly increase their hourly rate.

Q: What is the job outlook for car mechanics?
A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the job outlook for car mechanics is expected to grow by 4% from 2019 to 2029. This is about as fast as the average for all occupations.

Conclusion of How Much Do Car Mechanics Make Per Hour

In conclusion, the hourly rate for car mechanics can vary depending on several factors, such as experience level, location, type of employer, certification, education and training, and negotiation skills. Despite this variability, being a car mechanic can be a good career choice with the potential to earn a good income.
