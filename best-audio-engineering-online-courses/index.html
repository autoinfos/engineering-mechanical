The Best Audio Engineering Online Courses For Aspiring Sound Engineers

Written by Frank Oct 11, 2023 · 4 min read

Have you ever wondered what it takes to become a sound engineer? Do you want to learn how to create high-quality sound recordings for music, film, and television? If so, you're not alone. Many people are interested in pursuing a career in audio engineering, but they don't know where to start. One of the best ways to get started is to enroll in an online course. In this post, we'll explore the best audio engineering online courses available and how they can help you achieve your goals.

When it comes to learning about audio engineering, there are several pain points that students often face. These include a lack of access to quality instruction, the high cost of traditional education, and a lack of flexibility when it comes to scheduling. Fortunately, online courses can address all of these issues. They are typically more affordable than traditional courses, offer high-quality instruction, and allow students to learn at their own pace.

So, what should you look for in an audio engineering online course? First and foremost, you want to make sure that it covers the basics of sound engineering, such as microphone placement, signal flow, and EQ. You also want to choose a course that provides hands-on experience with industry-standard software and hardware. Finally, you want to make sure that the course is taught by experienced professionals who can provide you with valuable insights and feedback.

In summary, the best audio engineering online courses are affordable, flexible, and provide high-quality instruction. They cover the basics of sound engineering, provide hands-on experience with industry-standard software and hardware, and are taught by experienced professionals.

1. Introduction to Audio Engineering

One of the best audio engineering online courses available is Introduction to Audio Engineering from Berklee Online. This course is designed for beginners who want to learn the basics of sound engineering. The course covers microphone selection and placement, signal flow, EQ, dynamics processing, and basic mixing techniques. Students also get hands-on experience with Pro Tools, the industry standard digital audio workstation. I took this course myself and found it to be a great introduction to the world of audio engineering.

2. Mixing and Mastering

If you're looking for a course that focuses specifically on mixing and mastering, Mixing and Mastering from Coursera is a great option. This course covers advanced mixing techniques, including EQ, compression, and reverb. Students also learn about mastering, which is the final step in the production process. The course is taught by multi-platinum producer and engineer, Young Guru, who has worked with artists like Jay-Z and Beyonce.

3. Audio Production Techniques

For those who want to learn more advanced techniques, Audio Production Techniques from Lynda.com is a great choice. This course covers advanced topics like surround sound mixing, audio restoration, and mastering for different formats. It also provides hands-on experience with industry-standard software like Pro Tools, Logic Pro X, and Ableton Live. The course is taught by Bobby Owsinski, a veteran producer and engineer who has worked with artists like The Who and Willie Nelson.

4. Sound Design for Visual Media

Finally, if you're interested in creating sound effects and music for film, television, and video games, Sound Design for Visual Media from Udemy is a great option. This course covers the basics of sound design, including recording, editing, and processing sound effects. Students also learn about creating music for visual media, including composing for different genres and moods. The course is taught by an experienced sound designer who has worked on films like The Avengers and The Dark Knight.

Question and Answer

Q: How long do these courses typically take?

A: The length of each course varies, but most take between 4-12 weeks to complete.

Q: Do I need any prior experience to enroll in these courses?

A: No, most courses are designed for beginners and assume no prior experience.

Q: How much do these courses cost?

A: Prices vary, but most courses cost between $50-$500.

Q: Will I receive a certificate or credential upon completion of the course?

A: Yes, most courses offer a certificate of completion that you can add to your resume or LinkedIn profile.

Conclusion of Best Audio Engineering Online Courses

In conclusion, if you're interested in pursuing a career in audio engineering, enrolling in an online course is a great way to get started. The best audio engineering online courses provide high-quality instruction, hands-on experience with industry-standard software and hardware, and are taught by experienced professionals. Whether you're a beginner or looking to learn more advanced techniques, there's a course out there that's right for you.
