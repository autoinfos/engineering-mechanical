How Much Does A Cvs Pharmacy Tech Make In Florida

Written by Frank Jul 03, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you considering a career as a pharmacy technician in Florida? If so, you may be wondering how much you can expect to earn in this field. While pharmacy techs play an essential role in the healthcare industry, they are often overlooked when it comes to compensation. In this blog post, we'll explore the average salary of a CVS pharmacy tech in Florida and what factors can affect their earnings.

As with any job, there are both benefits and drawbacks to being a pharmacy technician. While you'll have the opportunity to work in a fast-paced and rewarding environment, you may also face challenges such as long hours and dealing with difficult customers. Additionally, the salary of a pharmacy tech can vary depending on several factors, including experience level, location, and education.

According to Glassdoor, the average base pay for a CVS pharmacy tech in Florida is around $14 per hour. However, this can range from $10 to $19 per hour depending on the factors mentioned above. For example, pharmacy techs with more experience may earn a higher salary, while those working in a high-cost-of-living area may earn more to offset expenses. Additionally, pharmacy techs who hold a certification or degree may be more marketable and able to negotiate a higher salary.

In summary, the average salary for a CVS pharmacy tech in Florida is around $14 per hour, but this can vary depending on various factors. While pharmacy techs may face challenges on the job, they play a crucial role in the healthcare industry and can find fulfilling careers in this field.

What is a Pharmacy Technician?

A pharmacy technician is a healthcare professional who works closely with pharmacists to ensure that patients receive the correct medications. They may perform a variety of tasks, including filling prescriptions, verifying insurance information, and answering customer questions. Pharmacy technicians may work in a variety of settings, including retail pharmacies, hospitals, and clinics.

My Experience as a CVS Pharmacy Tech in Florida

As a former CVS pharmacy tech in Florida, I can attest to both the benefits and challenges of this career. On the one hand, I enjoyed the fast-paced work environment and the opportunity to help people. However, I also faced challenges such as dealing with difficult customers and working long hours. Overall, I found the job to be rewarding and learned valuable skills that have helped me in my current career.

How to Become a Pharmacy Technician in Florida

If you're interested in becoming a pharmacy technician in Florida, there are several steps you'll need to take. First, you'll need to complete a pharmacy technician training program. This can be done through a vocational school, community college, or online program. Once you've completed your training, you'll need to pass a certification exam to become a licensed pharmacy technician.

Factors That Can Affect a Pharmacy Tech's Salary

As mentioned earlier, there are several factors that can affect a pharmacy tech's salary. These include:

  • Experience level
  • Location
  • Education and certifications
  • Type of employer (retail pharmacy, hospital, etc.)

How to Negotiate a Higher Salary as a Pharmacy Tech

If you're looking to negotiate a higher salary as a pharmacy tech, there are a few things you can do. First, make sure you have a clear idea of what the average salary is for your position in your area. You can use resources like Glassdoor or the Bureau of Labor Statistics to research this information.

Next, emphasize your education and certifications. If you have a degree or certification that sets you apart from other pharmacy techs, make sure to highlight this during negotiations. Finally, be confident and assertive during negotiations. Remember that you bring value to your employer and deserve to be compensated fairly for your work.

Conclusion of How Much Does a CVS Pharmacy Tech Make in Florida

In conclusion, the salary of a CVS pharmacy tech in Florida can vary depending on several factors, including experience level, location, and education. While the average base pay is around $14 per hour, pharmacy techs who hold certifications or degrees may be able to negotiate a higher salary. Despite the challenges of the job, being a pharmacy tech can be a rewarding career path for those interested in the healthcare industry.
