How Much Does An Electrician Apprentice Make In Ontario

Written by Eveline Jun 30, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Are you thinking about starting a career as an electrician apprentice in Ontario, but wondering how much you can expect to make? Look no further, because in this blog post, we will dive into the topic of electrician apprentice salaries in Ontario.

Electrician apprenticeships can be a great starting point for those interested in pursuing a career in the electrical trades. However, it can be difficult to determine how much you can earn as an apprentice, especially with varying rates across different regions and industries.

In Ontario, the average hourly wage for an electrician apprentice ranges from $14 to $23 per hour, depending on the level of experience and industry. Apprentices typically start at the lower end of this range and gradually increase their wages as they gain more experience and complete their training.

To summarize, electrician apprentices in Ontario can expect to earn an average hourly wage of $14 to $23 per hour, with wages increasing as they gain more experience and complete their training.

What Determines an Electrician Apprentice's Salary?

Several factors can influence an electrician apprentice's salary in Ontario, including:

Industry

The industry in which an electrician apprentice works can have a significant impact on their salary. For example, those in the construction industry may earn more than those in the maintenance or repair industry.

Level of Experience

As an electrician apprentice gains more experience, they can expect to earn higher wages. This is because they become more skilled and valuable to their employer.

Personal Experience with Electrician Apprentice Salaries in Ontario

As someone who has worked as an electrician apprentice in Ontario, I can attest to the fact that wages can vary significantly depending on your employer and industry. When I first started as an apprentice, I was earning around $15 per hour, but as I gained more experience and moved into the construction industry, my wages increased to over $20 per hour.

The Benefits of Becoming an Electrician Apprentice in Ontario

Despite the varying wages, there are several benefits to becoming an electrician apprentice in Ontario. These include:

Job Security

As our society becomes increasingly reliant on electricity, the demand for skilled electricians is only expected to grow. This means that electrician apprenticeships can offer excellent job security and stability.

Opportunities for Advancement

Electrician apprenticeships can be a great starting point for those interested in pursuing a long-term career in the electrical trades. With the right training and experience, apprentices can advance to become licensed electricians and even start their own businesses.

If you're considering a career as an electrician apprentice in Ontario, you can expect to earn an average hourly wage of $14 to $23 per hour, with wages increasing as you gain more experience and complete your training.
