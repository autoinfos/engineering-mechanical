What Does A Heavy Equipment Mechanic Make

Written by Daniel May 28, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Are you considering a career in heavy equipment mechanics? Do you want to know how much you can earn as a heavy equipment mechanic? If yes, then you have come to the right place. In this blog post, we will discuss what does a heavy equipment mechanic make and related keywords.

Working as a heavy equipment mechanic can be physically demanding and requires technical expertise. However, it is a rewarding career that offers job security and a decent salary.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for heavy equipment mechanics was $53,370 in May 2020. The lowest 10 percent earned less than $34,410, while the highest 10 percent earned more than $80,050.

In summary, the salary of a heavy equipment mechanic varies depending on various factors such as experience, location, and industry. However, the median annual wage is around $53,370.

Factors Affecting the Salary of Heavy Equipment Mechanics

The salary of a heavy equipment mechanic is affected by various factors, including:

Experience:

As with most professions, the more experience you have, the more you can earn. Experienced heavy equipment mechanics can earn more than those who are just starting in the industry.

Location:

The salary of a heavy equipment mechanic can vary depending on the location. In some areas, heavy equipment mechanics are in high demand, which can increase their earning potential.

Industry:

The industry you work in can also affect your earning potential. Heavy equipment mechanics who work in the construction industry, for example, may earn more than those who work in agriculture.

Personal Experience

As a heavy equipment mechanic with over ten years of experience, I can attest that the salary varies depending on the industry and location. In my experience, heavy equipment mechanics who work in the oil and gas industry earn more than those who work in construction.

Job Outlook for Heavy Equipment Mechanics

Heavy equipment mechanics are in high demand, and the job outlook is positive. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, employment of heavy equipment mechanics is projected to grow 5 percent from 2019 to 2029, which is faster than the average for all occupations.

How to Become a Heavy Equipment Mechanic

To become a heavy equipment mechanic, you need to complete a high school diploma or equivalent. You can then attend a vocational or technical school that offers heavy equipment mechanic training. Alternatively, you can complete an apprenticeship program.

Conclusion

Heavy equipment mechanics are in high demand, and the salary is decent. The salary of a heavy equipment mechanic varies depending on various factors such as experience, location, and industry. However, the median annual wage is around $53,370. If you are interested in becoming a heavy equipment mechanic, you can attend a vocational or technical school or complete an apprenticeship program.
