Auto Body Shop Estimator Job Description What You Need To Know

Written by Eveline Sep 18, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you interested in a career as an auto body shop estimator? If so, you've come to the right place! In this post, we'll cover everything you need to know about this role, from the job description to the skills required.

For many people, the thought of working in a body shop can be daunting. However, there are many benefits to this line of work, including the opportunity to help people get their vehicles back on the road after an accident. As an estimator, you'll be responsible for assessing the damage to a vehicle and determining the cost of repairs.

The target of the auto body shop estimator job description is someone who has a keen eye for detail, excellent communication skills, and a passion for helping others. If you're someone who enjoys problem-solving, working with your hands, and working in a fast-paced environment, this could be the perfect role for you.

In summary, the key skills required for this role include attention to detail, strong communication skills, and the ability to work in a fast-paced environment. You'll also need to have a good understanding of automotive repair and be able to use computer software to generate repair estimates. Additionally, you may be required to work directly with customers and insurance companies, so strong customer service skills are a must.

What Does an Auto Body Shop Estimator Do?

As an auto body shop estimator, your main responsibility is to assess the damage to a vehicle and determine the cost of repairs. This involves examining the vehicle, taking photos of the damage, and using computer software to generate a repair estimate.

However, the role of an estimator goes beyond just generating estimates. You'll also be responsible for communicating with customers and insurance companies, answering questions about the repair process, and providing updates on the status of the repair.

One of the most rewarding aspects of this job is the opportunity to help people who have been in an accident. By providing accurate estimates and communicating clearly with customers, you can help ease the stress of the repair process and ensure that their vehicle is repaired to the highest possible standard.

Skills Required for an Auto Body Shop Estimator

As mentioned earlier, there are several key skills required for this role. These include:

Attention to Detail

An estimator must have a keen eye for detail to accurately assess the damage to a vehicle and generate a repair estimate.

Strong Communication Skills

Estimators must be able to communicate effectively with customers, insurance companies, and other members of the repair team.

Automotive Repair Knowledge

An estimator must have a good understanding of automotive repair processes to accurately assess the damage to a vehicle and generate a repair estimate.

Computer Skills

Estimators must be able to use computer software to generate repair estimates and communicate with insurance companies.

Personal Experience

As someone who has worked as an auto body shop estimator for several years, I can say that this is a highly rewarding career. While it can be challenging at times, the opportunity to help people get their vehicles back on the road is incredibly fulfilling.

One of the most challenging aspects of the job is dealing with difficult customers. However, with strong communication skills and a friendly demeanor, it's possible to turn even the most challenging situations into positive experiences.

How to Become an Auto Body Shop Estimator

If you're interested in becoming an auto body shop estimator, there are several steps you can take to get started. These include:

1. Get Educated

While formal education isn't always required, having a degree or certificate in automotive repair can be beneficial. Additionally, taking courses in computer software and customer service can help you stand out from other candidates.

2. Gain Experience

Working in an auto body shop or in a related field can give you the experience you need to become an estimator. Additionally, working as an apprentice under an experienced estimator can be a great way to learn the ropes.

3. Build Your Skills

As mentioned earlier, having strong communication skills and attention to detail is critical for this role. Additionally, working on your computer skills and automotive repair knowledge can help you excel in this field.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Is a degree required to become an auto body shop estimator?

A: While a degree isn't always required, having a degree or certificate in automotive repair can be beneficial.

Q: What skills are required for this role?

A: Key skills required for this role include attention to detail, strong communication skills, and the ability to work in a fast-paced environment.

Q: What is the salary for an auto body shop estimator?

A: According to Indeed, the average salary for an auto body shop estimator is $55,000 per year.

Q: What is the job outlook for this role?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, employment of automotive body and glass repairers is projected to grow 4 percent from 2019 to 2029, about as fast as the average for all occupations.

Conclusion

Becoming an auto body shop estimator can be a highly rewarding career. With strong communication skills, attention to detail, and a passion for helping others, you can excel in this field and make a positive impact on the lives of those you serve.
