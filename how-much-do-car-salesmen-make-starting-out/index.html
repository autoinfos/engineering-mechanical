How Much Do Car Salesmen Make Starting Out

Written by Oliver Aug 21, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career in car sales but wondering how much you can expect to make starting out? It's a common question and one that can have a significant impact on your decision to pursue this career path. In this post, we'll explore the earning potential of car salesmen starting out and what factors can impact your salary.

One of the biggest pain points for those considering a career in car sales is the uncertainty around how much they can expect to earn. There's often a perception that car salesmen make a lot of money, but the reality can be quite different. It's important to understand the earning potential of this career path so you can make an informed decision about whether it's the right choice for you.

The answer to how much car salesmen make starting out is not straightforward. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for a retail salesperson in the United States is $25,440. However, salaries can vary widely depending on the type of dealership you work for, your location, and your individual sales performance.

In general, car salesmen starting out can expect to make around $30,000 to $40,000 per year. However, top performers can earn significantly more, with some earning six-figure salaries. Commission is a major factor in determining your earnings as a car salesman, and those who are skilled at closing deals and building relationships with customers can earn substantial commissions on top of their base salary.

My Experience with Starting Out as a Car Salesman

When I started out as a car salesman, I had little experience in sales and was nervous about how much I could earn. However, I quickly learned that my earnings were largely within my control. By focusing on building relationships with customers and providing excellent customer service, I was able to earn a good salary and even exceed my initial earnings expectations.

One thing to keep in mind is that it can take time to build up your customer base and establish yourself as a top performer. In the beginning, you may need to rely more heavily on your base salary while you work on developing your sales skills and building relationships with customers.

Factors That Can Impact Your Earnings as a Car Salesman

As mentioned earlier, your individual sales performance is a major factor in determining your earnings as a car salesman. However, there are a number of other factors that can impact your salary:

Type of Dealership

The type of dealership you work for can have a big impact on your earnings potential. Luxury car dealerships tend to offer higher salaries and commissions than lower-end dealerships.

Location

Your location can also impact your earnings as a car salesman. Dealerships in larger cities or more affluent areas may offer higher salaries and commissions than those in rural areas or less affluent regions.

How to Maximize Your Earnings as a Car Salesman

If you're looking to maximize your earnings as a car salesman, there are a number of things you can do:

Focus on Building Relationships with Customers

Building relationships with customers is key to success in car sales. By providing excellent customer service and building trust with your customers, you can increase your chances of closing deals and earning commissions.

Stay Up-to-Date on Industry Trends

The automotive industry is constantly evolving, and staying up-to-date on industry trends can give you an edge in the marketplace. Attend industry conferences and events, read industry publications, and stay informed about changes in the market.

Conclusion of How Much Do Car Salesmen Make Starting Out

While the answer to how much car salesmen make starting out is not straightforward, it's clear that there is significant earning potential in this career path. By focusing on developing your sales skills, building relationships with customers, and staying up-to-date on industry trends, you can maximize your earnings and build a successful career in car sales.
