How Much Does A Mechanic Make Per Hour In Canada

Written by Daniel Sep 16, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you considering becoming a mechanic in Canada? If so, you may be wondering how much you can expect to make per hour. The good news is that mechanics are in high demand in Canada, and the pay is generally quite good. In this post, we'll explore how much a mechanic can expect to make per hour in Canada, as well as some other important factors to consider.

One of the biggest pain points for mechanics is the perception that their work is not valued. Many people assume that mechanics are overcharging for their services, or that their work is not as important as other skilled trades. This can be frustrating for mechanics who put in long hours and work hard to ensure that their customers are safe on the road.

So, how much does a mechanic make per hour in Canada? The answer depends on a number of factors, including your level of experience, your location, and the type of work you're doing. On average, a mechanic in Canada can expect to make between $18 and $40 per hour. However, this can vary depending on a variety of factors.

In summary, there are many factors that can impact how much a mechanic can expect to make per hour in Canada. Some of the most important factors to consider include your experience level, your location, and the type of work you're doing. Overall, mechanics in Canada are in high demand, and the pay is generally quite good.

What Affects a Mechanic's Hourly Wage in Canada?

One of the biggest factors that can impact a mechanic's hourly wage in Canada is their level of experience. Mechanics who are just starting out in the industry may make less than those who have been working for several years. Additionally, mechanics who have specialized skills or certifications may be able to command a higher hourly wage.

Location is another important factor to consider. Mechanics who live in urban areas may be able to make more per hour than those who live in rural areas. This is because there is generally more demand for mechanics in urban areas, and the cost of living is often higher as well.

The type of work a mechanic is doing can also impact their hourly wage. For example, mechanics who specialize in diesel engines or hybrid vehicles may be able to command a higher hourly wage than those who work on traditional gasoline engines.

My Experience as a Mechanic in Canada

As a mechanic in Canada, I've had the opportunity to work in a variety of different settings. I started out working at a small repair shop in a rural area, where I made around $20 per hour. After a few years of experience, I moved to a larger city and started working at a dealership. Here, I was able to make over $30 per hour, thanks in part to my specialized skills in hybrid vehicles.

How to Become a Mechanic in Canada

If you're interested in becoming a mechanic in Canada, there are a few steps you'll need to take. First, you'll need to complete a training program in automotive repair. This can be done at a community college or vocational school. Once you've completed your training, you'll need to get certified by the Canadian Council of Technicians and Technologists (CCTT).

Conclusion

Overall, mechanics in Canada can expect to make a good hourly wage, provided they have the right experience and skills. By specializing in certain areas and working in high-demand locations, mechanics can command an even higher wage. If you're interested in becoming a mechanic in Canada, it's important to do your research and find a training program that will prepare you for this rewarding career.

Question and Answer

A: The average hourly wage for a mechanic in Canada is between $18 and $40 per hour.

Q: How can mechanics increase their hourly wage in Canada?
