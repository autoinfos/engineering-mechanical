How Much Does A New Software Engineer Make

Written by Daniel Aug 10, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Are you considering a career as a software engineer? Do you want to know how much you can expect to make in your first job? The answer may surprise you. While the tech industry is known for high salaries, the pay for entry-level positions can vary widely depending on a number of factors.

One of the biggest pain points for new software engineers is figuring out what they should be making. Without a lot of experience, it can be tough to know what a fair salary is. Plus, the tech industry is known for its secrecy around salaries, which can make it difficult to negotiate for better pay.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual salary for software developers was $107,510 in May 2019. However, this number can vary widely depending on factors like location, industry, and experience. In some areas, new software engineers might make closer to $60,000 or $70,000 per year, while in high-paying tech hubs like San Francisco, they could make upwards of $100,000 right out of college.

In conclusion, how much a new software engineer makes can vary greatly depending on a number of factors. However, the median salary for software developers is around $107,510 per year. Keep in mind that this number can vary depending on location, industry, and experience.

What is the Average Salary for a New Software Engineer?

When I started my first job as a software engineer, I was offered a salary of $75,000 per year. While this might seem like a lot of money to some people, it's important to remember that the cost of living in the San Francisco Bay Area, where I live, is extremely high. In fact, $75,000 per year is considered low for a software engineer in this area.

So, how much should you expect to make in your first job as a software engineer? The answer depends on a number of factors, including your location, the industry you're working in, and your level of experience. However, according to Glassdoor, the national average salary for a software engineer with less than one year of experience is around $69,000 per year.

How Can You Negotiate a Higher Salary?

Negotiating your salary can be tough, especially when you're new to the industry. However, it's important to remember that you are valuable and that your skills are in high demand. When negotiating your salary, be sure to do your research and know what the going rate is for someone in your position and with your level of experience.

It's also important to be confident and assertive when negotiating your salary. Don't be afraid to ask for what you're worth and to explain why you think you deserve a higher salary. And remember, it's always okay to walk away from a job offer if it doesn't meet your salary expectations.

Location Matters

One of the biggest factors that can impact how much a new software engineer makes is location. Salaries can vary widely depending on where you live and work. For example, a software engineer working in San Francisco can expect to make significantly more than someone working in a smaller city or town.

It's also important to consider the cost of living in your area when looking at salaries. While a software engineer in a smaller city might make less than someone in a larger city, they might also have a lower cost of living, which can make up for the difference in salary.

Experience Matters Too

Another factor that can impact how much a new software engineer makes is their level of experience. Someone with more experience is likely to make more money than someone who is just starting out. This is because experienced software engineers have more skills and are able to take on more complex projects.

However, don't let your lack of experience hold you back from negotiating for a higher salary. If you can show that you have valuable skills and are eager to learn, you may be able to negotiate a higher starting salary.

My Personal Experience

When I graduated from college with a degree in computer science, I was offered a job as a software engineer at a small startup. The starting salary was $60,000 per year, which was lower than what many of my classmates were making in larger tech companies. However, I was excited about the opportunity to work at a startup and to gain valuable experience.

Over the next year, I worked hard and proved myself to be a valuable member of the team. When it came time for my annual review, I was able to negotiate a higher salary based on my performance and the skills I had developed over the past year.
