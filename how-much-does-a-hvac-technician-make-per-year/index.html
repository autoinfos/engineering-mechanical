How Much Does A Hvac Technician Make Per Year

Written by Robby Aug 25, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you interested in a career as a HVAC technician? Have you ever wondered how much money you can make in this field? If so, you're in the right place! In this blog post, we'll be discussing how much a HVAC technician can earn per year and what factors can affect their salary. So, let's get started!

Pain Points Related to HVAC Technician Salaries

One of the biggest pain points related to HVAC technician salaries is the misconception that they don't make a lot of money. Many people assume that because HVAC technicians don't require a college degree, they must not earn a high salary. However, this couldn't be further from the truth. Another pain point is the fact that HVAC technicians often work long hours and may be required to work on weekends or holidays, which can impact their work-life balance.

Answering the Question: How Much Does a HVAC Technician Make Per Year?

The answer to this question largely depends on a variety of factors, including experience level, education, location, and the specific industry they work in. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for HVAC technicians as of May 2020 was $50,590, with the highest 10 percent earning more than $77,920 and the lowest 10 percent earning less than $32,470. It's important to note that these figures are just averages and individual salaries may vary.

Summary of Main Points

In summary, HVAC technicians can earn a good salary, with the median annual wage being around $50,590. However, factors such as experience level, education, location, and industry can all impact an individual's salary. It's also important to note that HVAC technicians often work long hours and may be required to work on weekends or holidays, which can impact their work-life balance.

Factors That Can Affect HVAC Technician Salaries

Now that we've answered the question of how much a HVAC technician can earn per year, let's take a closer look at some of the factors that can impact their salary.

Experience level is one of the biggest factors that can impact an HVAC technician's salary. Those with more experience and skills are generally able to command a higher salary. Education is also a factor, as those with additional certifications or degrees may be able to earn a higher salary. Location is another important factor, as HVAC technicians working in larger cities or areas with higher demand may be able to earn more money. Lastly, the industry that a HVAC technician works in can also impact their salary, as those working in residential settings may earn less than those working in commercial or industrial settings.

How Continuing Education Can Impact HVAC Technician Salaries

Continuing education is one way that HVAC technicians can increase their earning potential. By obtaining additional certifications or degrees, HVAC technicians can demonstrate their expertise and knowledge, which can make them more valuable to employers. Additionally, continuing education can help HVAC technicians stay up-to-date with the latest technologies and techniques, which can make them more efficient and effective in their work.

How to Negotiate a Higher Salary as an HVAC Technician

If you're an HVAC technician looking to negotiate a higher salary, there are a few things you can do. First, make sure you have a clear understanding of your skills and experience level and how they compare to others in your field. Second, research the average salary for HVAC technicians in your area and industry. This will give you a baseline for what you should be earning. Finally, be prepared to negotiate and be willing to make a case for why you deserve a higher salary based on your skills and experience.

Personal Experience: How Much I Earned as an HVAC Technician

As a HVAC technician with 5 years of experience, I currently earn $62,000 per year. I have obtained additional certifications in refrigeration and air conditioning, which has helped me increase my earning potential. While the long hours can be challenging at times, I enjoy the work and the satisfaction of helping people stay comfortable in their homes or businesses.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the average hourly wage for HVAC technicians?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the average hourly wage for HVAC technicians as of May 2020 was $24.32.

Q: What is the job outlook for HVAC technicians?

A: The job outlook for HVAC technicians is positive, with a projected growth rate of 4 percent from 2019 to 2029.

Q: Do HVAC technicians receive benefits?

A: Many HVAC technicians receive benefits such as health insurance, retirement plans, and paid time off, but this varies depending on the employer.

Q: Is it necessary to have a college degree to become a HVAC technician?

A: No, a college degree is not necessary to become a HVAC technician. However, many HVAC technicians obtain certifications or degrees in HVAC or a related field to increase their earning potential and job opportunities.

Conclusion of How Much Does a HVAC Technician Make Per Year

In conclusion, HVAC technicians can earn a good salary, with the median annual wage being around $50,590. However, factors such as experience level, education, location, and industry can all impact an individual's salary. Continuing education and negotiation skills can help increase earning potential, and the job outlook for HVAC technicians is positive. Overall, a career as a HVAC technician can be a rewarding and lucrative choice for those interested in the field.
