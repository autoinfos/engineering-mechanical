How Much Does A Parts Person Make At A Dealership

Are you considering a career as a parts person at a dealership? One question that may be on your mind is how much money you can expect to make in this position. It's important to know what you can expect in terms of salary and benefits before making a decision about your career path.

As with any job, there are pros and cons to being a parts person at a dealership. One of the biggest pain points for many people is the starting salary. While you may be able to work your way up to a higher salary over time, the starting pay for this position can be lower than what you would earn in other entry-level positions.

On average, a parts person at a dealership can expect to make around $35,000 to $45,000 per year. However, this can vary depending on the dealership and your level of experience. Some dealerships may offer higher salaries to attract top talent, while others may offer lower salaries to save money.

In addition to your salary, you may also be eligible for benefits such as health insurance, retirement savings plans, and paid time off. These benefits can vary depending on the dealership and your employment status (full-time vs. part-time).

To summarize, a parts person at a dealership can expect to make around $35,000 to $45,000 per year, with the potential for higher earnings over time. While the starting salary may be lower than other entry-level positions, you may be eligible for benefits such as health insurance and paid time off.

Potential for Advancement in Parts Person Position

While the starting salary for a parts person may be lower than what you would earn in other entry-level positions, there is potential for advancement in this position. As you gain more experience and demonstrate your value to the dealership, you may be eligible for promotions and raises.

Some dealerships may also offer training programs and career development opportunities to help you advance your career. This can include courses on customer service, inventory management, and other skills that are important for a successful parts person.

Factors That Affect Parts Person Salary

There are several factors that can affect the salary of a parts person at a dealership. These include:

  • Location: Salaries may be higher in areas with a higher cost of living
  • Experience: Those with more experience may be eligible for higher salaries
  • Dealership Size: Larger dealerships may have more money in their budget for salaries
  • Job Performance: Those who perform well in their job may be eligible for raises and promotions

How to Negotiate a Higher Salary as a Parts Person

If you feel that your salary as a parts person is lower than what you deserve, there are several steps you can take to negotiate a higher salary. These include:

  • Researching the average salary for parts people in your area
  • Preparing a list of your accomplishments and contributions to the dealership
  • Scheduling a meeting with your manager to discuss your salary
  • Being willing to compromise and negotiate

Final Thoughts on Parts Person Salary

While the starting salary for a parts person at a dealership may be lower than what you would earn in other entry-level positions, there is potential for advancement and higher earnings over time.
