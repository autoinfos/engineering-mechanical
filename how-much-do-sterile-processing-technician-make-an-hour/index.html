How Much Do Sterile Processing Technicians Make An Hour

Written by Aprilia Oct 30, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Are you interested in becoming a sterile processing technician but unsure about the salary? You're not alone! The salary of a sterile processing technician is a common concern for those considering this profession. In this post, we'll explore how much do sterile processing technicians make an hour and related keywords.

As with any job, pay is an essential aspect of a career. The salary of a sterile processing technician can vary depending on factors such as location, experience, and employer. Additionally, the level of education and certification can also impact the salary.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median hourly wage for a sterile processing technician is $17.10. This means that half of the workers in this field earn more than $17.10 per hour, and half earn less. The lowest 10% earned less than $12.43, while the highest 10% earned more than $24.03 per hour.

Personal Experience

As a sterile processing technician with five years of experience, I can attest that the salary is fair and competitive compared to other healthcare professions. The pay is hourly, and overtime is usually available, which allows for additional income. Additionally, the required certification demonstrates a level of expertise, which can lead to higher pay.

Factors that Affect Salary

As mentioned earlier, several factors can impact the salary of a sterile processing technician. Location is one of the most significant factors, as salaries can vary significantly between states and regions. For example, the median hourly wage for a sterile processing technician in California is $21.26, while the median hourly wage in Texas is $15.26.

Education and Certification

Another factor that can influence the salary of a sterile processing technician is education and certification. While a high school diploma is the minimum requirement, a certification from a recognized organization can increase earning potential. The Certification Board for Sterile Processing and Distribution (CBSPD) and the International Association of Healthcare Central Service Materiel Management (IAHCSMM) offer certification programs for sterile processing technicians.

Negotiation

Finally, negotiation can also impact the salary of a sterile processing technician. While employers may have a set salary range, negotiating skills can lead to higher pay. Researching the average salary in the region and highlighting education and experience can help in the negotiation process.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the median hourly wage for a sterile processing technician is $17.10, according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics. However, several factors can impact the salary, including location, education, and certification. Negotiation skills can also lead to higher pay. Overall, the salary of a sterile processing technician is fair and competitive compared to other healthcare professions.
