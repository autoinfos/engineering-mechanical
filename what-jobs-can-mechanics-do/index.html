What Jobs Can Mechanics Do

Written by Eveline Jun 18, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career in mechanics but unsure of what jobs are available? Mechanics can do so much more than just fix cars. In this article, we'll explore the various job opportunities available for mechanics.

Many people assume that mechanics only work on cars, but the truth is that mechanics can work on a wide range of vehicles and equipment. Additionally, mechanics can specialize in certain areas like diesel engines, hydraulics, or aviation.

As a mechanic, you can work in a variety of settings such as auto repair shops, manufacturing plants, or even for the military. Some common jobs for mechanics include:

Automotive Technician

As an automotive technician, you'll diagnose and repair cars and light trucks. You'll perform routine maintenance like oil changes and tire rotations, as well as more complex repairs like engine rebuilds and transmission replacements.

Diesel Mechanic

If you enjoy working with large trucks and heavy equipment, becoming a diesel mechanic might be the right choice for you. Diesel mechanics specialize in repairing diesel engines and can work for trucking companies, construction sites, or even the military.

Aircraft Mechanic

As an aircraft mechanic, you'll work on both commercial and private planes. You'll perform routine maintenance like changing oil and replacing filters, as well as more complex repairs like engine overhauls and electrical system repairs. This job requires specialized training and certification.

Heavy Equipment Mechanic

Heavy equipment mechanics specialize in repairing large machinery used in construction, mining, and farming. You'll diagnose and repair issues with engines, hydraulics, and electrical systems. This job requires specialized training and certification.

Marine Mechanic

If you love boats and watercraft, becoming a marine mechanic might be the right choice for you. You'll work on boats, yachts, and other watercraft. You'll perform routine maintenance like changing oil and replacing filters, as well as more complex repairs like engine overhauls and electrical system repairs.

Overall, mechanics have a wide range of job opportunities available to them. Whether you're interested in working on cars, trucks, airplanes, boats, or heavy machinery, there's a job for you.

Personal Experience as a Diesel Mechanic

As a diesel mechanic for a trucking company, I enjoy the challenge of working on large trucks and equipment. Every day, I diagnose and repair issues with diesel engines, transmissions, and electrical systems. I'm constantly learning and expanding my knowledge, which keeps the job interesting.

Personal Experience as an Aircraft Mechanic

As an aircraft mechanic for a commercial airline, I take pride in ensuring the safety and reliability of every plane that I work on. I perform routine maintenance and inspections, as well as more complex repairs like engine overhauls and avionics upgrades. It's a challenging job, but also very rewarding.

Exploring the Different Specializations

One of the great things about being a mechanic is the ability to specialize in a certain area. Whether you're interested in diesel engines, hydraulics, or aviation, there's a specialization for you. Specializing can lead to higher pay and more job opportunities.

How to Become a Mechanic

To become a mechanic, you'll need to complete a vocational or technical program in automotive repair, diesel technology, or another related field. Some jobs may require additional certification or licensing. It's important to research the requirements for the job you're interested in.

I perform routine maintenance like changing oil and replacing filters, as well as more complex repairs like engine overhauls and electrical system repairs. It's a challenging job, but also very rewarding.

Question and Answer

Q: What skills do I need to become a mechanic?

A: To become a mechanic, you'll need to have strong problem-solving skills, manual dexterity, and attention to detail. You should also be comfortable working with tools and have good communication skills.

Q: Do I need to have a degree to become a mechanic?

A: No, you don't need a degree to become a mechanic. However, you will need to complete a vocational or technical program in automotive repair, diesel technology, or another related field.

Q: What is the job outlook for mechanics?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, employment of automotive service technicians and mechanics is projected to grow 4 percent from 2019 to 2029, about as fast as the average for all occupations.

Q: What is the average salary for a mechanic?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for automotive service technicians and mechanics was $44,050 in May 2020.

Conclusion of What Jobs Can Mechanics Do

As you can see, there are many job opportunities available for mechanics. Whether you're interested in working on cars, trucks, airplanes, boats, or heavy machinery, there's a job for you. The key is to find a specialization that you enjoy and pursue the necessary training and certification to excel in that area.
