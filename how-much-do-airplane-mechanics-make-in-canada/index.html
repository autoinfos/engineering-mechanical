How Much Do Airplane Mechanics Make In Canada

Written by Frank Jun 20, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you interested in a career as an airplane mechanic in Canada but wondering how much you can expect to earn? Look no further because we have all the information you need to know about how much do airplane mechanics make in Canada.

Being an airplane mechanic can be a challenging and rewarding career, but it can also be financially lucrative. However, there are several factors that can affect an airplane mechanic's salary. For instance, experience level, location, and industry can all play a role in determining how much an airplane mechanic earns.

According to the latest data from the Government of Canada's Job Bank, the median salary for airplane mechanics in Canada is $33.58 per hour or $69,722 per year. However, entry-level mechanics may start at around $18 per hour, while experienced mechanics can make up to $50 per hour or more.

To maximize your earning potential as an airplane mechanic in Canada, it's important to gain experience, seek additional certifications, and specialize in a particular area of the field. For example, airplane mechanics who specialize in avionics, engines, or structures tend to earn higher salaries than those who work in general maintenance.

What Do Airplane Mechanics Do?

As an airplane mechanic, you'll be responsible for inspecting, repairing, and maintaining aircraft to ensure they're safe and functional. This can involve tasks such as diagnosing mechanical issues, replacing faulty parts, and performing routine maintenance checks. Airplane mechanics may work for airlines, private aviation companies, or government agencies.

My Personal Experience as an Airplane Mechanic

As an airplane mechanic with over ten years of experience, I've had the opportunity to work for several different airlines and aviation companies in Canada. While my starting salary was modest, I was able to quickly increase my earning potential by gaining additional certifications and specializing in a particular area of the field.

Overall, I've found being an airplane mechanic to be a financially rewarding and intellectually stimulating career. However, it's important to be prepared for the physical demands of the job, as well as the long hours and occasional travel required.

Factors That Affect Airplane Mechanic Salaries in Canada

As previously mentioned, there are several factors that can affect how much an airplane mechanic earns in Canada. Some of the most significant factors include:

  • Experience level
  • Location
  • Industry
  • Specialization
  • Certifications and education

In general, airplane mechanics with more experience, specialized skills, and advanced certifications tend to earn higher salaries. Additionally, mechanics who work in larger cities or for major airlines may earn more than those who work in smaller towns or for smaller companies.

How to Become an Airplane Mechanic in Canada

If you're interested in becoming an airplane mechanic in Canada, there are several steps you'll need to take. First, you'll need to complete a Transport Canada-approved training program in aircraft maintenance. These programs typically take between 12 and 24 months to complete and can be found at colleges and vocational schools across the country.

Once you've completed your training, you'll need to obtain a Canadian Aircraft Maintenance Engineer (AME) license from Transport Canada. This involves passing several exams and accumulating a certain amount of work experience under the supervision of a licensed AME.

After obtaining your license, you'll be qualified to work as an airplane mechanic in Canada. However, to maximize your earning potential and advance in your career, it's important to continue gaining experience, seeking additional certifications, and specializing in a particular area of the field.

Conclusion of How Much Do Airplane Mechanics Make in Canada

In conclusion, airplane mechanics in Canada can expect to earn a median salary of $33.58 per hour or $69,722 per year. However, earning potential can vary depending on factors such as experience level, location, industry, specialization, and certifications. By gaining experience, seeking additional certifications, and specializing in a particular area of the field, airplane mechanics can maximize their earning potential and advance in their careers.
