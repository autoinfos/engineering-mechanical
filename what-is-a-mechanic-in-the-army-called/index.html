What Is A Mechanic In The Army Called

Have you ever wondered what the men and women who keep the army's vehicles running smoothly are called? Whether you're considering a career in the military or just curious, you've come to the right place. In this blog post, we'll answer the question of what is a mechanic in the army called in simple language, so you can understand it easily.

Being a mechanic in the army can be a challenging job. Not only do you have to fix complex machinery, but you also need to do it quickly and under pressure. Furthermore, you might be called upon to work in extreme weather conditions, such as in the desert or during a storm.

A mechanic in the army is called a "63B - Light-Wheel Vehicle Mechanic". This is a military occupational specialty (MOS) code assigned by the United States Army to those who repair and maintain military vehicles. They are responsible for troubleshooting and diagnosing problems, repairing or replacing damaged parts, and performing routine maintenance to keep the vehicles in top condition.

In summary, a 63B - Light-Wheel Vehicle Mechanic is someone who maintains and fixes military vehicles to ensure they are always ready for action.

More about 63B - Light-Wheel Vehicle Mechanic

When I was in the army, I had the opportunity to work with some of the most skilled and dedicated mechanics I've ever met. They were responsible for keeping our vehicles running smoothly, whether we were training at home or deployed overseas.

One of the most important skills a 63B - Light-Wheel Vehicle Mechanic needs is the ability to work under pressure. In the field, there is no time to waste, and every minute counts. They need to be able to diagnose and fix problems quickly, often without the use of advanced tools or equipment.

Becoming a 63B - Light-Wheel Vehicle Mechanic

If you're interested in becoming a 63B - Light-Wheel Vehicle Mechanic, you'll need to meet specific requirements. First and foremost, you must be a US citizen and meet the age and education requirements of the army. You'll also need to pass a physical fitness test and a medical exam.

Once you've met these requirements, you'll need to attend Basic Combat Training (BCT) and Advanced Individual Training (AIT) to learn the skills you'll need to be a 63B - Light-Wheel Vehicle Mechanic. During AIT, you'll learn about engine repair, suspension systems, and more.

The Role of a 63B - Light-Wheel Vehicle Mechanic

The role of a 63B - Light-Wheel Vehicle Mechanic is a critical one in the army. They are responsible for keeping the military's vehicles running smoothly, which is essential to the success of any mission. Without skilled mechanics, our troops would be unable to move quickly and efficiently, putting them at risk of injury or worse.

How to Become a 63B - Light-Wheel Vehicle Mechanic

If you're interested in becoming a 63B - Light-Wheel Vehicle Mechanic, the first step is to speak with a recruiter. They can provide you with more information about the requirements and help you get started on your journey to a rewarding career in the army.

Conclusion of What is a Mechanic in the Army Called

A 63B - Light-Wheel Vehicle Mechanic is a critical role in the army. They are responsible for maintaining and repairing military vehicles to ensure they are always ready for action.
