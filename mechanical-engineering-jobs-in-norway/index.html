Mechanical Engineering Jobs In Norway Opportunities And Challenges

Written by Petter Jun 26, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you a mechanical engineer looking for new opportunities abroad? Norway might be a great option for you. With a thriving economy and a strong focus on technology, this Scandinavian country offers many exciting job prospects for engineers. However, there are also some challenges to consider before making the move. In this post, we'll explore the world of mechanical engineering jobs in Norway and what you need to know before starting your search.

One of the biggest pain points for engineers looking for work in Norway is the language barrier. While many Norwegians speak English fluently, the language of business is still Norwegian. This means that to fully integrate into the workplace and build relationships with colleagues, you'll need to learn the language. Additionally, the cost of living in Norway is notoriously high, which can be a challenge for those used to more affordable living expenses.

If you're up for the challenge, however, there are many opportunities for mechanical engineers in Norway. The country is home to a thriving oil and gas industry, as well as a growing renewable energy sector. There are also opportunities in manufacturing, robotics, and other areas of engineering.

In summary, mechanical engineering jobs in Norway offer both opportunities and challenges. If you're willing to learn the language and adapt to a higher cost of living, there are many exciting prospects to explore. Keep reading for more information on specific areas of mechanical engineering in Norway.

Mechanical Engineering Jobs in Norway: Oil and Gas Industry

One area where mechanical engineers are in high demand in Norway is the oil and gas industry. Norway is one of the world's largest producers of oil and gas, and companies like Equinor, Aker BP, and Shell have a strong presence in the country. Mechanical engineers in this industry might work on projects like developing new drilling technologies, designing offshore platforms and pipelines, or improving the efficiency of existing equipment.

Personally, I know a mechanical engineer who worked for a major oil company in Norway. He was responsible for designing new drilling tools and equipment that could withstand the harsh conditions of the North Sea. While the work was challenging, he found it rewarding to contribute to the country's energy independence and to work with such a talented team of engineers.

Mechanical Engineering Jobs in Norway: Renewable Energy

Another area of mechanical engineering that is growing in Norway is renewable energy. As a country with abundant hydroelectric power and wind resources, Norway is well-positioned to be a leader in green energy. Mechanical engineers in this field might work on projects like designing wind turbines, optimizing hydroelectric dams, or developing new energy storage technologies.

A friend of mine who is a mechanical engineer recently started working for a renewable energy company in Norway. She is part of a team that is designing a new type of wave energy converter that can generate electricity from ocean waves. She loves the challenge of working on such a cutting-edge project and feels proud to be contributing to a more sustainable future.

Mechanical Engineering Jobs in Norway: Manufacturing and Robotics

While the oil and gas industry and renewable energy sectors might be the most well-known areas for mechanical engineering in Norway, there are also opportunities in manufacturing and robotics. Norway has a strong manufacturing industry, with companies like Kongsberg and Jotun producing everything from industrial equipment to consumer goods. There are also several companies working on robotics and automation technologies, which are becoming increasingly important in many industries.

For example, I have a colleague who works for a Norwegian robotics company. She is responsible for designing and programming robots that can perform tasks like welding, painting, and assembly. She enjoys the challenge of working on projects that require both creativity and technical skill, and feels like she is making a real difference in the world of manufacturing.

How to Find Mechanical Engineering Jobs in Norway

If you're interested in pursuing a career in mechanical engineering in Norway, there are several steps you can take to make your job search more successful. Here are a few tips:

  • Learn Norwegian: As we mentioned earlier, knowing the local language is key to success in Norway. Consider taking language classes or using online resources to improve your skills.
  • Network: Attend industry events and conferences, and try to connect with other engineers in your field. LinkedIn can also be a helpful tool for making professional connections.
  • Research companies: Look for companies that match your interests and skills, and learn as much as you can about their culture and values.
  • Consider a recruiter: Working with a recruiter who specializes in engineering can be a great way to find job opportunities that match your skills and experience.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the average salary for mechanical engineers in Norway?

A: According to the website Numbeo, the average monthly salary for a mechanical engineer in Norway is around 50,000 NOK (about $5,700 USD) before taxes.

Q: Do I need a visa to work in Norway as a mechanical engineer?

A: If you are a citizen of the EU/EEA or Switzerland, you do not need a visa to work in Norway. Citizens of other countries will need to apply for a work visa.

Q: What are the working hours like for mechanical engineers in Norway?

A: The standard working hours in Norway are 37.5 per week. However, many companies have flexible hours and offer options like working from home or a compressed workweek.

Q: What is the job outlook for mechanical engineers in Norway?

A: According to the Norwegian Labour and Welfare Administration, there is currently a shortage of qualified engineers in Norway. This means that there are many job opportunities for those with the right skills and experience.

Conclusion of Mechanical Engineering Jobs in Norway

If you're a mechanical engineer looking for new opportunities, Norway might be a great place to start your search. With a thriving economy and opportunities in areas like oil and gas, renewable energy, and manufacturing, there are plenty of exciting prospects to explore. Just be prepared for the challenges of learning a new language and adapting to a higher cost of living. With the right skills and attitude, however, you could find a rewarding career in one of the world's most beautiful countries.
