Question and Answer

Q: What qualifications do I need to become a diesel mechanic?

A: To become a diesel mechanic, you need a high school diploma or equivalent. You also need to complete a diesel technology program, which can take anywhere from six months to two years. Some employers also require certification from the National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence (ASE).

Q: What types of vehicles do diesel mechanics work on?

A: Diesel mechanics work on a variety of heavy-duty vehicles, including trucks, buses, construction equipment, and agricultural equipment.

Q: What is the average salary for diesel mechanics?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for diesel service technicians and mechanics was $48,500 as of May 2020.

Q: What are the working hours for diesel mechanics?

A: Diesel mechanics typically work full-time, and their schedules can vary depending on the employer. Some diesel mechanics work during regular business hours, while others work evenings, weekends, or holidays.
