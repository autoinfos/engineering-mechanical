How Much Do Mechanics Make Per Year

Written by Petter Aug 15, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Are you interested in pursuing a career as a mechanic? Or are you already a mechanic and wondering if you are being paid fairly? Either way, understanding how much mechanics make per year is crucial. Keep reading to learn more about this topic.

One pain point for mechanics is the perception that they are not paid enough for the amount of work they do. Many mechanics work long and irregular hours, and the job can be physically demanding. Additionally, some people may view mechanics as replaceable, leading to lower wages.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for automotive service technicians and mechanics was $44,050 in May 2020. This means that half of mechanics earned more than this amount, and half earned less. The lowest 10% of mechanics earned less than $25,760, while the highest 10% earned more than $68,880.

In summary, while being a mechanic can be a challenging job, the median annual wage of $44,050 is competitive compared to other industries. Additionally, with experience and additional certifications, mechanics can earn higher salaries.

Personal Experience

As a former mechanic, I can attest that the job can be physically demanding and requires attention to detail. However, I found the pay to be fair, especially with opportunities for overtime and bonuses. Additionally, I was able to earn a higher salary by becoming certified in specific automotive systems.

Factors that Affect Salary

Several factors can affect a mechanic's salary. Firstly, location plays a significant role. Mechanics in urban areas tend to earn more than those in rural areas. Additionally, the type of employer can affect salary. Mechanics who work for dealerships or larger repair shops may earn more than those who work for smaller independent shops.

Certifications and Experience

Earning additional certifications and gaining experience can also increase a mechanic's salary. Certifications from the National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence (ASE) are highly valued in the industry and can lead to higher pay. Additionally, mechanics who specialize in specific areas, such as diesel engines or transmission repair, may earn higher salaries.

Negotiating Salary

If you are a mechanic and feel that you are not being paid fairly, it may be worth negotiating your salary. Do research on the average salary for mechanics in your area and bring this information to your employer. Additionally, highlight any certifications or experience that make you a valuable employee.
