How Much Do Auto Glass Technicians Make

Written by William Jun 07, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career as an auto glass technician? Do you want to know how much you can expect to earn in this field? If so, keep reading!

Auto glass technicians are responsible for repairing and replacing windshields, windows, and other glass components on vehicles. While this can be a rewarding and interesting career, it's important to understand the earning potential before making a commitment.

What is the Average Salary for Auto Glass Technicians?

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for automotive glass installers and repairers was $36,660 as of May 2020. However, the lowest 10 percent of workers in this field earned less than $24,760, while the highest 10 percent earned more than $61,150.

It's important to note that salaries can vary depending on a number of factors, including location, level of experience, and employer. For example, auto glass technicians working in metropolitan areas or for large auto dealerships may earn more than those working in rural areas or for smaller companies.

My Personal Experience as an Auto Glass Technician

As a former auto glass technician with over 10 years of experience, I can attest to the fact that this can be a lucrative career for those who are willing to put in the work. When I first started out, I was earning around $15 per hour, but as I gained more experience and developed a reputation for quality work, my salary increased to over $30 per hour.

One thing to keep in mind is that this job can be physically demanding, as it often involves working on vehicles in less-than-ideal conditions. However, for those who are up for the challenge, it can be a rewarding and well-paying career.

How Can I Increase My Earning Potential as an Auto Glass Technician?

If you're interested in maximizing your earning potential as an auto glass technician, there are a few things you can do:

1. Get Certified

Many employers prefer to hire technicians who are certified by the National Glass Association or another recognized organization. Certification can demonstrate your knowledge and skills, which can lead to higher pay.

2. Develop a Reputation for Quality Work

If you consistently provide high-quality work and excellent customer service, your reputation will grow. This can lead to more business and higher pay.

3. Consider Specializing

Some auto glass technicians specialize in certain types of repairs or vehicles. By developing expertise in a particular area, you can become a sought-after specialist and command higher pay.

Question and Answer

Q: Is a college degree required to become an auto glass technician?

A: No, a college degree is not required. However, most employers prefer to hire technicians who have completed a postsecondary training program in automotive glass repair or a related field.

Q: Is there room for advancement in this field?

A: Yes, there are opportunities for advancement, such as becoming a supervisor or manager. Technicians who specialize in certain areas may also be able to command higher pay.

Q: What is the job outlook for auto glass technicians?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, employment of automotive glass installers and repairers is projected to grow 5 percent from 2019 to 2029, about as fast as the average for all occupations.

Q: What skills are important for auto glass technicians?

A: Important skills for auto glass technicians include manual dexterity, attention to detail, and good customer service skills. Technicians must also be able to stand for long periods of time and lift heavy objects.

Conclusion of How Much Do Auto Glass Technicians Make

While the earning potential for auto glass technicians may vary depending on a number of factors, it's clear that this can be a rewarding and well-paying career for those who are willing to put in the work. By developing your skills, specializing in certain areas, and providing excellent customer service, you can maximize your earning potential and achieve success in this field.
