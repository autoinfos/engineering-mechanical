How Much Does A Pharmacy Tech Make Per Hour

Written by Bardi Nov 05, 2023 · 3 min read

Are you considering a career as a pharmacy technician but wondering how much you can expect to earn per hour? It's a valid concern, as salary plays a significant role in career decisions.

Pharmacy techs are responsible for assisting pharmacists in dispensing medication and providing customer service. They work in retail pharmacies, hospitals, and other healthcare settings. A pharmacy tech's salary can vary based on location, experience, and education level.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median hourly wage for pharmacy technicians in May 2020 was $16.26. The lowest 10 percent earned less than $11.72 per hour, while the highest 10 percent earned more than $23.08 per hour.

Overall, becoming a pharmacy tech can be a lucrative career choice. However, it's essential to consider the cost of education and training, as well as the availability of jobs in your area.

What to Expect When Starting as a Pharmacy Tech

When starting as a pharmacy tech, it's common to earn a wage on the lower end of the spectrum. However, with experience and additional training, you can expect to earn a higher hourly wage. It's also important to note that working in a hospital or other medical facility may offer higher pay compared to a retail setting.

The Importance of Education and Certification

Education and certification can also impact a pharmacy tech's salary. Completing a formal training program or obtaining certification can increase your chances of earning a higher hourly wage. Additionally, some states require certification to work as a pharmacy tech.

Factors that Affect a Pharmacy Tech's Salary

Aside from experience and education, other factors can impact a pharmacy tech's salary. Location plays a significant role, with pharmacy techs in metropolitan areas typically earning higher wages compared to those in rural areas. The type of employer can also impact salary, with hospital pharmacy techs earning more compared to those in retail settings.

Negotiating Your Salary as a Pharmacy Tech

If you're starting as a pharmacy tech or looking for a new job, it's essential to negotiate your salary. Research the average salary for pharmacy techs in your area and come prepared with a salary range. Highlight your experience and education, and be willing to compromise if necessary.

My Experience as a Pharmacy Tech

As a pharmacy tech, I started on the lower end of the salary spectrum but was able to negotiate a higher wage after completing additional training and certification. Working in a hospital setting has also allowed me to earn a higher hourly wage compared to my previous retail pharmacy job.

Question and Answer

Q: How does location affect a pharmacy tech's salary?

A: Pharmacy techs in metropolitan areas typically earn higher wages compared to those in rural areas.

Q: Do pharmacy techs need certification?

A: Some states require certification to work as a pharmacy tech, and completing a formal training program or obtaining certification can increase your chances of earning a higher hourly wage.

Q: Can a pharmacy tech negotiate their salary?

A: Yes, it's essential to research the average salary in your area and come prepared with a salary range. Highlight your experience and education and be willing to compromise if necessary.

Q: What is the median hourly wage for pharmacy technicians?

A: The median hourly wage for pharmacy technicians in May 2020 was $16.26.

Conclusion

Overall, becoming a pharmacy tech can provide a stable and rewarding career. While salary is an essential consideration, it's crucial to also factor in the cost of education and training, job availability in your area, and the potential for career growth. With experience and additional training, pharmacy techs can earn a higher hourly wage and advance their careers.
