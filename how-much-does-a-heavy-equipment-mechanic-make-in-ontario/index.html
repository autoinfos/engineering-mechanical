How Much Does A Heavy Equipment Mechanic Make In Ontario

Written by Daniel Sep 10, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you considering a career as a heavy equipment mechanic in Ontario? If so, you may be wondering how much you can expect to make in this field. Many factors can impact a heavy equipment mechanic's salary, including education and experience, location, and the type of equipment they specialize in. In this post, we'll dive into the average salary for heavy equipment mechanics in Ontario and what you can expect from this career path.

According to the Government of Canada's Job Bank, the median salary for heavy equipment mechanics in Ontario is $33.65 per hour. This means that half of all heavy equipment mechanics earn more than this amount, and half earn less. However, this is just a median, and actual salaries can vary widely depending on several factors.

When it comes to experience, heavy equipment mechanics with more years of experience tend to earn higher salaries. Similarly, those with specialized skills such as welding or hydraulics may be able to command higher salaries. Location can also make a difference, with heavy equipment mechanics in larger cities generally earning more than those in small towns or rural areas.

In summary, the average salary for a heavy equipment mechanic in Ontario is around $33.65 per hour, but this can vary widely depending on factors such as experience, location, and specialization. If you're considering a career in this field, it's important to research salaries in your specific area and talk to other mechanics to get a better idea of what you can expect to earn.

How to Become a Heavy Equipment Mechanic in Ontario

As with many skilled trades, becoming a heavy equipment mechanic requires a combination of education and on-the-job training. Many mechanics start by completing a pre-apprenticeship program, which provides a foundation in the skills and knowledge needed for the job. These programs typically last around 8-12 weeks and cover topics such as safety, basic tool use, and equipment maintenance.

After completing a pre-apprenticeship program, aspiring mechanics can then begin an apprenticeship. In Ontario, this typically involves working for a licensed mechanic for a period of four years and completing a certain number of hours of on-the-job training and classroom instruction. Once the apprenticeship is complete, the mechanic can then take a certification exam to become a licensed mechanic.

What Skills Do I Need to Be a Heavy Equipment Mechanic?

In addition to the technical skills needed to diagnose and repair heavy equipment, there are several other skills that can be helpful in this field. These include:

  • Problem-solving skills:

    Heavy equipment mechanics must be able to diagnose and solve complex problems with machinery.

  • Physical fitness:

    This job can be physically demanding, and mechanics may need to lift heavy parts or equipment.

  • Attention to detail:

    Small mistakes can have big consequences in this field, so it's important to be detail-oriented.

  • Communication skills:

    Heavy equipment mechanics may need to communicate with other mechanics, customers, and supervisors, so strong communication skills are important.

What is the Job Outlook for Heavy Equipment Mechanics in Ontario?

Overall, the job outlook for heavy equipment mechanics in Ontario is positive. According to the Government of Canada's Job Bank, there is currently a shortage of qualified mechanics in this field, which means that job prospects are good. Additionally, many mechanics in this field are nearing retirement age, which is expected to create even more job openings in the coming years.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the demand for heavy equipment mechanics in Ontario?

A: There is currently a shortage of qualified heavy equipment mechanics in Ontario, which means that job prospects are good.

Q: What is the average salary for a heavy equipment mechanic in Ontario?

A: The average salary for a heavy equipment mechanic in Ontario is around $33.65 per hour, but this can vary depending on factors such as experience and location.

Q: What skills do I need to be a successful heavy equipment mechanic in Ontario?

A: In addition to technical skills, successful heavy equipment mechanics need problem-solving skills, physical fitness, attention to detail, and communication skills.

Q: How do I become a licensed heavy equipment mechanic in Ontario?

A: To become a licensed heavy equipment mechanic in Ontario, you must complete a pre-apprenticeship program, complete a four-year apprenticeship, and pass a certification exam.

Conclusion

Overall, becoming a heavy equipment mechanic in Ontario can be a rewarding career path with good job prospects and earning potential. Although salaries can vary widely, the average salary for this field is around $33.65 per hour. If you're interested in pursuing this career path, be sure to research salaries in your specific area and talk to other mechanics to get a better idea of what you can expect to earn.
