How To Write A Cover Letter For A Job You Really Want In Relaxed English Language

Written by Eveline Aug 04, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you tired of submitting job applications without getting any response? Do you feel like your cover letter is not doing its job? Writing a cover letter for a job you really want can be daunting, especially if you don't know how to make it stand out. But don't worry, we've got you covered! In this blog post, we'll give you some tips on how to write a cover letter for a job you really want in relaxed English language.

When it comes to writing a cover letter, most people tend to focus on their qualifications and experience. While these are important, it's also essential to think about the pain points of the employer. What are they looking for in a candidate? What problems are they trying to solve? By addressing these pain points, you'll be able to make a connection with the employer and show them that you're the right person for the job.

The first thing you need to do when writing a cover letter for a job you really want is to research the company and the position. Look at the job description and try to understand what the employer is looking for. Then, tailor your cover letter to address those specific needs. Use keywords from the job description to show that you understand the requirements of the job.

To make your cover letter stand out, start with a strong opening paragraph that grabs the reader's attention. This could be a personal story, a quote that resonates with you, or a bold statement that reflects your passion for the job. Then, in the body of the letter, highlight your relevant skills and experience. Use concrete examples to illustrate your achievements and demonstrate how you can contribute to the company's success.

In summary, when writing a cover letter for a job you really want, focus on the pain points of the employer, tailor your letter to the job description, and use concrete examples to demonstrate your skills and experience.

Target: Use Relaxed English Language

When writing a cover letter, it's important to use language that is clear, concise, and easy to understand. Avoid using technical jargon or complex sentences that may confuse the reader. Instead, use simple language and short sentences to convey your message.

For example, instead of saying "I possess a myriad of skills and competencies that make me uniquely qualified for this position," you could say "I have the skills and experience needed for this job." This way, you're using language that is more relaxed and easier to understand, while still getting your point across.

Target: Highlight Your Motivation

One of the most important things you can do when writing a cover letter for a job you really want is to show your motivation. Employers want to know that you're not just looking for any job, but that you're specifically interested in their company and the position they're offering.

For example, you could start your cover letter by saying "I was thrilled to see the job posting for [position] at [company name]." Then, explain why you're interested in the company and the job. Maybe you admire their mission, or you're excited about the prospect of working on a particular project. Whatever it is, make sure you're specific and genuine in your motivation.

Target: Use Active Voice

When writing a cover letter, it's important to use active voice instead of passive voice. Active voice makes your writing more engaging and helps you convey your message more clearly. Passive voice, on the other hand, can make your writing sound dull and impersonal.

For example, instead of saying "My skills and experience have been honed over the course of my career," you could say "I have honed my skills and experience over the course of my career." This way, you're using active voice to emphasize your achievements and demonstrate your value to the employer.

Target: Show Your Personality

While it's important to be professional when writing a cover letter, it's also important to show your personality. Employers want to know that you're not just a robot, but that you're a real person with interests, quirks, and a unique perspective.

For example, you could mention a hobby or interest that is relevant to the job. Maybe you're a graphic designer who loves to paint, or a writer who is passionate about social justice. Whatever it is, make sure you're showing your personality in a way that is relevant and appropriate for the job.

Question and Answer

Q: Should I include my salary requirements in my cover letter?

A: No, it's not necessary to include your salary requirements in your cover letter. Wait until you have an offer to negotiate salary.

Q: How long should my cover letter be?

A: Your cover letter should be no more than one page long. Keep it short and to the point.

Q: Should I include a photo with my cover letter?

A: No, it's not necessary to include a photo with your cover letter. Your qualifications and experience should speak for themselves.

Q: Can I use the same cover letter for multiple job applications?

A: It's not recommended to use the same cover letter for multiple job applications. Tailor your cover letter to each job description to show that you understand the specific needs of the employer.

Conclusion of How to Write a Cover Letter for a Job You Really Want

Writing a cover letter for a job you really want can seem intimidating, but with these tips, you'll be able to make a strong impression on the employer. Remember to focus on the pain points of the employer, use relaxed English language, highlight your motivation, use active voice, and show your personality. With these strategies, you'll be well on your way to landing your dream job!
