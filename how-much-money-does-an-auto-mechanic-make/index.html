How Much Money Does An Auto Mechanic Make

Written by Eveline Sep 27, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you considering a career as an auto mechanic but wondering how much money you can expect to make? It's a common concern for those thinking about entering the field. After all, you want to make sure that your chosen career path will provide you with a comfortable living. Let's explore the topic of how much money does an auto mechanic make and what factors can impact your earnings.

Auto mechanics are skilled professionals who work with different types of vehicles to diagnose and repair mechanical issues. As with any profession, the amount of money you can make as an auto mechanic can vary depending on several factors such as location, experience, education, and type of employer.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for automotive service technicians and mechanics was $44,050 as of May 2020. This means that half of all mechanics earned more than this amount, and half earned less. The lowest 10 percent of earners in this field made less than $25,000 per year, while the highest 10 percent earned more than $66,950.

In general, auto mechanics who work for dealerships or for companies that specialize in the repair of high-end vehicles tend to earn more than those who work for small independent repair shops. Additionally, mechanics who have advanced certifications and skills, such as those who are trained to work on electric or hybrid vehicles, can also command higher salaries.

In conclusion, the amount of money that an auto mechanic can make can vary depending on several factors. However, with the median annual wage for automotive service technicians and mechanics at around $44,050, it is a career path that can provide a comfortable living for those who are skilled and dedicated.

How Much Money Does an Auto Mechanic Make? My Personal Experience

When I was considering becoming an auto mechanic, I was worried about how much money I could make in the field. I had always loved working on cars, but I also wanted to make sure that I could support myself and my family. After doing some research, I learned that auto mechanics can earn a good living, especially if they work for a reputable company or dealership.

During my first few years as an auto mechanic, I worked at a small independent repair shop. While I enjoyed the work, I quickly realized that the pay was not as high as I had hoped. However, after gaining more experience and certifications, I was able to find a job at a dealership that specialized in luxury vehicles. The pay was much better, and I was able to support myself and my family comfortably.

What Affects How Much Money an Auto Mechanic Can Make?

As mentioned earlier, several factors can impact how much money an auto mechanic can make. One of the most significant factors is location. Mechanics who work in cities or areas with a higher cost of living tend to earn more than those who work in rural areas. Additionally, the type of employer can also impact earnings, with those who work for dealerships or companies that specialize in high-end vehicles earning more.

Experience and education are also essential factors that can impact how much money an auto mechanic can make. Mechanics who have been in the field for several years, and who have advanced certifications and skills, can command higher salaries than those who are just starting.

Education and Certification for Auto Mechanics

If you want to become an auto mechanic and earn a good living, you will need to obtain the necessary education and certifications. Most employers prefer to hire mechanics who have completed a formal training program and who have earned certification from organizations such as the National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence (ASE).

Training programs for auto mechanics are available at community colleges, vocational schools, and technical institutes. These programs typically last six months to a year and cover topics such as engine repair, brake systems, and electrical systems.

How to Increase Your Earnings as an Auto Mechanic

If you want to earn more money as an auto mechanic, there are several steps you can take. First, consider obtaining additional certifications and skills, such as those related to electric or hybrid vehicles. These specialized skills are in high demand and can result in higher pay.

You can also consider working for a dealership or a company that specializes in high-end vehicles. These employers tend to pay more than small independent repair shops.

Finally, consider starting your own auto repair business. While this can be challenging, it can also be very rewarding and can provide you with the opportunity to earn a higher income.
