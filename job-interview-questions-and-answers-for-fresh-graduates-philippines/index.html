Job Interview Questions And Answers For Fresh Graduates In The Philippines

Written by Aprilia Nov 09, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you a fresh graduate in the Philippines looking for a job? Congratulations! Landing your first job is an exciting milestone, but it can also be nerve-wracking. One of the most challenging parts of the job search process is the interview. It's where you have to prove yourself and show your potential employer why you're the best candidate for the job. But don't worry, we've got you covered. In this article, we'll provide you with job interview questions and answers for fresh graduates in the Philippines.

Job interviews can be stressful, especially for fresh graduates who don't have much experience. You might be worried about not knowing how to answer questions or not having enough experience to impress the interviewer. But keep in mind that everyone starts somewhere, and every interviewer understands that you're a fresh graduate. They want to see your potential and willingness to learn.

One of the most common interview questions for fresh graduates is "Tell me about yourself." This question is an opportunity for you to introduce yourself and highlight your relevant skills and experiences. Another common question is "Why should we hire you?" This question allows you to showcase your strengths and explain why you're the best fit for the job.

In summary, job interviews can be challenging, but don't let them discourage you. With the right preparation and mindset, you can ace your interview and land your dream job. Remember to research the company, practice answering common interview questions, and show your potential employer why you're the best candidate for the job.

How to Answer "Tell me about yourself."

When answering this question, keep in mind that the interviewer wants to know how your skills and experiences relate to the job. Start by introducing yourself briefly and highlighting your most relevant skills and experiences. For example: "My name is John, and I recently graduated from XYZ University with a degree in Marketing. During my time in college, I interned at ABC Company, where I gained experience in social media marketing and market research. I'm excited about this role because I believe my skills in digital marketing and data analysis would be an asset to the team."

How to Answer "Why should we hire you?"

When answering this question, focus on your strengths and how they relate to the job requirements. For example: "I believe you should hire me because I have a strong background in digital marketing and data analysis, which are essential skills for this role. Additionally, I'm a quick learner and thrive in fast-paced environments. I'm confident that I would be able to contribute to the team and help achieve your company's goals."

How to Prepare for Job Interviews

Preparing for a job interview is essential for success. Here are some tips to help you prepare:

  • Research the company and learn about their mission, values, and culture.
  • Review the job description and make a list of your relevant skills and experiences.
  • Practice answering common interview questions with a friend or family member.
  • Dress appropriately and arrive early to the interview.
  • Bring copies of your resume and any other relevant documents.

How to Follow Up After Job Interviews

Following up after a job interview is a great way to show your interest in the job and keep yourself top of mind for the interviewer. Here are some tips for following up:

  • Send a thank-you email or note within 24 hours of the interview.
  • Reiterate your interest in the job and why you're a good fit.
  • Ask about next steps and when you can expect to hear back.

Personal Experience: Overcoming Interview Nerves

When I was a fresh graduate, I was nervous about job interviews. I didn't know how to answer questions and was worried about not having enough experience. To overcome my nerves, I practiced answering common interview questions with a friend and researched the company to learn more about their values and culture. I also reminded myself that the interviewer wants to see my potential and willingness to learn. With these strategies, I was able to ace my interview and land my first job.

Personal Experience: Highlighting Relevant Skills

During a job interview for a marketing position, I was asked why I thought I was the best fit for the job. I highlighted my relevant skills in digital marketing and data analysis and explained how they related to the job requirements. I also mentioned my willingness to learn and adapt to new situations. This answer impressed the interviewer and helped me land the job.

Question and Answer:

Q: How do I answer "What are your salary expectations?"

A: Research the industry standard for the position and consider your experience and qualifications. Provide a range rather than a specific number and be open to negotiation.

Q: How do I answer "What are your biggest weaknesses?"

A: Be honest but focus on how you're working to improve your weaknesses. For example: "One of my weaknesses is public speaking, but I'm taking a course to improve my presentation skills."

Q: How do I answer "Describe a time when you faced a challenge and how you overcame it?"

A: Choose a specific example and explain the challenge, your actions, and the outcome. Focus on how you used your skills and experiences to overcome the challenge.

Q: How do I answer "Do you have any questions for me?"

A: Prepare a list of questions beforehand that show your interest in the company and the job. For example: "Can you tell me more about the company culture?" or "What are the next steps in the hiring process?"

Conclusion of Job Interview Questions and Answers for Fresh Graduates in the Philippines

Job interviews can be nerve-wracking, but with the right preparation and mindset, you can ace your interview and land your dream job. Remember to research the company, practice answering common interview questions, and show your potential employer why you're the best candidate for the job. Good luck!
