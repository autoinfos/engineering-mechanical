How Much Does A First Year Apprentice Diesel Mechanic Make

Written by Eveline Sep 06, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you considering a career as a diesel mechanic? If so, you may be wondering how much you can expect to earn as a first year apprentice. Money is an important factor in any career choice, and it's important to know what you can expect to earn before you make a decision.

As a first year apprentice diesel mechanic, your salary will vary depending on your location, employer, and experience level. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for diesel service technicians and mechanics was $49,150 as of May 2020. However, the lowest 10 percent of earners made less than $32,160, while the highest 10 percent made more than $76,940.

So, what does this mean for you as a first year apprentice? Typically, apprenticeships pay less than full-time positions, but they provide valuable on-the-job training and experience. As a first year apprentice diesel mechanic, you can expect to earn an hourly wage that is lower than the median annual wage. However, your pay will increase as you gain more experience and complete your apprenticeship. It's also important to note that some apprenticeships may offer additional benefits, such as paid time off and health insurance.

In summary, as a first year apprentice diesel mechanic, you can expect to earn an hourly wage that is lower than the median annual wage for diesel service technicians and mechanics. However, your pay will increase as you gain more experience and complete your apprenticeship. Additionally, some apprenticeships may offer additional benefits.

Personal Experience

When I started my apprenticeship as a diesel mechanic, I was making around $12 an hour. While this wasn't a lot of money, I was grateful for the opportunity to learn from experienced mechanics and gain hands-on experience. As I became more skilled and took on additional responsibilities, my pay increased. By the end of my apprenticeship, I was earning around $20 an hour.

Job Outlook

The job outlook for diesel service technicians and mechanics is positive. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, employment of diesel service technicians and mechanics is projected to grow 3 percent from 2019 to 2029, about as fast as the average for all occupations. This growth is due in part to the increasing demand for freight transportation and the need for diesel engines in construction and other industries.

Factors Affecting Pay

As mentioned earlier, your pay as a first year apprentice diesel mechanic will depend on a number of factors. Some of the factors that can affect your pay include:

  • Your location
  • The size and type of your employer
  • Your experience level
  • The type of equipment you work on

How to Increase Your Earnings

If you're interested in increasing your earnings as a diesel mechanic, there are a few things you can do. First, consider completing additional training and certifications. This can make you more valuable to employers and increase your earning potential. Second, consider specializing in a particular area, such as heavy equipment or marine engines. Specializing can also make you more valuable to employers and increase your earning potential.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Is it worth becoming a diesel mechanic?
A: Yes, becoming a diesel mechanic can be a rewarding career choice. Diesel mechanics are in high demand, and the job outlook is positive. Additionally, diesel mechanics can earn a good salary with opportunities for advancement.

Q: How long does it take to become a diesel mechanic?
A: The length of time it takes to become a diesel mechanic can vary. Some employers may require a high school diploma or equivalent, while others may require a postsecondary certificate or degree. Apprenticeships can take anywhere from one to four years to complete.

Q: Do diesel mechanics make good money?
A: Yes, diesel mechanics can make good money. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for diesel service technicians and mechanics was $49,150 as of May 2020.

Q: What skills do you need to be a diesel mechanic?
A: To be a successful diesel mechanic, you need a variety of skills, including strong problem-solving skills, attention to detail, manual dexterity, and the ability to work with complex machinery.

Conclusion

As a first year apprentice diesel mechanic, you can expect to earn an hourly wage that is lower than the median annual wage for diesel service technicians and mechanics. However, with hard work and dedication, you can increase your earning potential as you gain more experience and complete your apprenticeship. The job outlook for diesel mechanics is positive, and there are opportunities for advancement and specialization. If you're interested in becoming a diesel mechanic, it can be a rewarding and lucrative career choice.
