How Much Do Heavy Diesel Mechanics Make In Australia

Written by Eveline Sep 06, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you interested in pursuing a career as a heavy diesel mechanic in Australia, but unsure about the salary prospects? The answer to the question of how much do heavy diesel mechanics make in Australia is not a straightforward one as it depends on several factors, including location, experience, and industry sector. However, the good news for aspiring heavy diesel mechanics is that the demand for skilled workers in this field is high, and there is potential for career growth and earning potential.

One of the biggest pain points for individuals thinking about pursuing a career as a heavy diesel mechanic is the uncertainty surrounding salary prospects. Many people are unsure if the time and financial investment required to become a qualified mechanic is worth the potential earnings. Moreover, there is often a misconception that heavy diesel mechanics do not earn as much as other trade professions or that the job is physically demanding and not worth the effort.

The average salary for a heavy diesel mechanic in Australia is around AUD 72,000 per year. However, this figure can vary depending on various factors such as location, experience, and industry sector. For instance, heavy diesel mechanics working in Western Australia and Queensland tend to earn higher salaries due to the booming mining and resources sector. Similarly, experienced mechanics with ten or more years of experience can earn up to AUD 100,000 or more per year.

In summary, heavy diesel mechanics in Australia earn a respectable salary that is comparable to other trade professions. While it may not be the highest-paying job, it is a stable and well-paying career with potential for growth and advancement. Furthermore, the demand for skilled heavy diesel mechanics is high, and there are plenty of job opportunities available across various industry sectors.

How to Become a Heavy Diesel Mechanic in Australia

There are several pathways to becoming a qualified heavy diesel mechanic in Australia. The most common pathway is to complete an apprenticeship program that combines on-the-job training with classroom instruction. This typically takes four years to complete, and apprentices are paid a wage while they learn. Alternatively, individuals can complete a Certificate III in Heavy Commercial Vehicle Mechanical Technology, which takes around two years to complete and provides a more theoretical approach to learning.

I completed my apprenticeship as a heavy diesel mechanic, and it was one of the best decisions I ever made. The hands-on experience and knowledge gained during the apprenticeship provided a solid foundation for my career and opened up many doors of opportunity. I have worked in various industry sectors, including mining, agriculture, and transport, and the skills I learned during my apprenticeship have been invaluable.

How Much Do Heavy Diesel Mechanics Make in the Mining Industry?

The mining industry is one of the highest-paying sectors for heavy diesel mechanics in Australia. The remote locations, long hours, and physically demanding work make it a challenging but rewarding career. Heavy diesel mechanics working in the mining industry can earn up to AUD 150,000 or more per year, depending on their level of experience and qualifications.

Factors Affecting Salary for Heavy Diesel Mechanics in Australia

Several factors can affect the salary prospects for heavy diesel mechanics in Australia. These include:

Location: Heavy diesel mechanics working in remote or regional areas often earn higher salaries due to the demand for skilled workers.

Experience: Experienced mechanics with ten or more years of experience can command higher salaries due to their level of expertise.

Industry Sector: Heavy diesel mechanics working in the mining, resources, or transport sectors often earn higher salaries due to the demand for workers in these industries.

How to Increase Your Salary as a Heavy Diesel Mechanic

If you want to increase your earning potential as a heavy diesel mechanic, there are several ways to do so. These include:

Specializing in a Niche Area: By specializing in a niche area such as hydraulics, transmissions, or electrical systems, you can increase your level of expertise and command higher salaries.

Further Education and Training: Completing additional education and training, such as a Diploma in Heavy Vehicle Management or a Certificate IV in Automotive Mechanical Overhauling, can increase your knowledge and skills and open up new career opportunities.

Experience: By gaining more experience in the field, you can increase your expertise and command higher salaries.
