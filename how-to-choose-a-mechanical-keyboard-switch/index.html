How To Choose A Mechanical Keyboard Switch

Written by Petter Sep 04, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you tired of your old keyboard and looking for an upgrade? A mechanical keyboard may be the perfect solution for you. However, with so many different types of switches available, it can be overwhelming to choose the right one. In this blog post, we will guide you through the process of how to choose a mechanical keyboard switch.

Have you ever felt frustrated with your keyboard's unresponsive keys or loud noise? These are common pain points that mechanical keyboards can solve. Mechanical keyboards offer more tactile feedback and durability than traditional membrane keyboards. However, choosing the right switch can make a big difference in your typing experience.

When choosing a mechanical keyboard switch, it is essential to consider the following factors:

Actuation Force

Actuation force is the amount of pressure required to register a keystroke. It is measured in grams and ranges from 35g to 150g. If you prefer a lighter touch, a switch with a lower actuation force is ideal. However, if you prefer a more tactile response, switches with higher actuation forces are better.

Switch Type

There are three main types of mechanical keyboard switches: linear, tactile, and clicky. Linear switches have a smooth keystroke with no tactile bump or click sound. Tactile switches have a bump that you can feel when the key actuates. Clicky switches have both a tactile bump and an audible click sound. Your choice of switch type depends on your personal preference and typing style.

Durability

The durability of a mechanical keyboard switch is measured in keystrokes. Most switches have a lifespan of 50 million keystrokes, which is significantly higher than membrane keyboards. It is essential to choose a switch with a high durability rating to ensure a long-lasting keyboard.

Brand Reputation

When choosing a mechanical keyboard switch, it is essential to consider the reputation of the brand. Brands such as Cherry MX, Gateron, and Kailh are well-known for producing high-quality switches. It is also important to research the specific switch model to ensure it meets your typing needs.

Personal Experience

When choosing a mechanical keyboard switch, it is helpful to have a personal experience with different switches. Visit a store that sells mechanical keyboards and try out different switches to determine which one feels the best for you. You can also purchase switch tester kits that allow you to test out different switch types at home.

Online Reviews

Researching online reviews can also be helpful when choosing a mechanical keyboard switch. Look for reviews from trusted sources such as tech websites or YouTube channels. Reading reviews from other users can also provide valuable insight into the typing experience of a particular switch.

Conclusion

Choosing the right mechanical keyboard switch is crucial to ensure a comfortable and enjoyable typing experience. Consider factors such as actuation force, switch type, durability, brand reputation, personal experience, and online reviews when making your decision. With the right switch, you can elevate your typing experience to the next level.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the difference between a linear and tactile switch?

A: A linear switch has a smooth keystroke with no tactile bump or click sound. A tactile switch has a bump that you can feel when the key actuates.

Q: What is actuation force?

A: Actuation force is the amount of pressure required to register a keystroke. It is measured in grams and ranges from 35g to 150g.

Q: How do I know which switch type is best for me?

A: Your choice of switch type depends on your personal preference and typing style. Visit a store that sells mechanical keyboards and try out different switches to determine which one feels the best for you.

Q: Can I replace the switches on my mechanical keyboard?

A: It depends on the keyboard model. Some mechanical keyboards allow you to replace the switches, while others do not. It is best to research your keyboard model before attempting to replace the switches.

Conclusion of How to Choose a Mechanical Keyboard Switch

Choosing the right mechanical keyboard switch can be a daunting task, but considering factors such as actuation force, switch type, durability, brand reputation, personal experience, and online reviews can help you make an informed decision. Remember to test out different switches before making your purchase and prioritize your personal comfort and typing style. Happy typing!
