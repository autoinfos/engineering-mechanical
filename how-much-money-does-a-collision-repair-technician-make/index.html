How Much Money Does A Collision Repair Technician Make

Written by Eveline Nov 16, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Are you considering a career as a collision repair technician but wondering how much money you can make? If so, you're not alone. The salary of a collision repair technician is one of the biggest concerns for those entering the field.

Collision repair technicians, like many other trade workers, may face several challenges when it comes to earning a fair wage. For example, many technicians may work long hours or have to deal with physically demanding tasks, which can make it difficult to earn a decent living. Additionally, some technicians may not receive the necessary training to advance their careers, which can limit their earning potential.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for automotive body and glass repairers was $44,550 in May 2020. The lowest 10 percent earned less than $26,780, and the highest 10 percent earned more than $74,020.

In conclusion, the salary of a collision repair technician can vary depending on several factors, including experience, location, and employer. However, with the right training and experience, collision repair technicians can earn a decent living and enjoy a rewarding career.

My Personal Experience

When I first started as a collision repair technician, I was making close to minimum wage. However, as I gained more experience and skills, my salary increased significantly. I also had the opportunity to work at a high-end auto body shop, which paid me more than I would have made at a smaller, independent shop.

Factors Affecting a Collision Repair Technician's Salary

Several factors can affect a collision repair technician's salary, including:

Experience

The more experience a technician has, the higher their salary is likely to be. Experienced technicians have a better understanding of repair techniques and can work more efficiently, which can lead to higher pay.

Training and Certifications

Technicians who have completed formal training and earned certifications in collision repair may be more attractive to employers and may be able to command higher salaries.

Location and Employer

The location and employer can also play a significant role in a collision repair technician's salary. Technicians working in a high-cost-of-living area may earn more than those working in a lower-cost area. Additionally, technicians working for large, well-established automotive companies may earn more than those working for smaller, independent shops.

Question and Answer

Q: Is it necessary to have formal training to become a collision repair technician?

A: While formal training is not always required, it can help you gain the necessary skills and knowledge to succeed in this field. Many employers prefer to hire technicians who have completed formal training programs.

Q: Can a collision repair technician work part-time?

A: Yes, many collision repair technicians work part-time, especially those who are just starting in the field.

Q: Do collision repair technicians receive benefits?

A: Many collision repair technicians receive benefits, such as health insurance and retirement plans, from their employers.

Q: Is there room for advancement in the collision repair industry?

A: Yes, there are several opportunities for advancement in the collision repair industry. Technicians may become supervisors, managers, or even open their own repair shops.

Conclusion of How Much Money Does a Collision Repair Technician Make

In summary, collision repair technicians can earn a decent living, especially with the right training and experience. The salary of a collision repair technician can vary depending on several factors, including location, employer, and experience. However, with hard work and dedication, collision repair technicians can build a successful and rewarding career in this industry.
