What are A&P jobs in Orlando?

A&P stands for Airframe and Powerplant, which refers to the certification that mechanics receive after completing a rigorous training program. A&P mechanics are responsible for maintaining and repairing all aspects of an aircraft, including its engines, electrical systems, and avionics. They must be highly skilled and knowledgeable in order to ensure that aircraft are safe and ready to fly.

In Orlando, there are many companies that hire A&P mechanics to work on a variety of aircraft, including commercial airlines, private jets, and military aircraft. Some of the top employers in the area include Lockheed Martin, Delta Airlines, and JetBlue Airways.

How to Find A&P Jobs in Orlando

If you're interested in pursuing a career in A&P maintenance in Orlando, there are several steps you can take to increase your chances of success. Here are a few tips:

1. Get Certified

The first step to becoming an A&P mechanic is to get certified. You'll need to complete a training program that covers all aspects of aircraft maintenance, including airframe structures, systems and components, and powerplant systems. Once you've completed your training, you'll need to pass a series of exams to become certified.

2. Network

Networking is key in any industry, and aviation maintenance is no exception. Attend industry events and conferences to meet other professionals in the field. Join online forums and groups to connect with other mechanics and stay up-to-date on industry news and trends.

3. Apply to Top Employers

Research the top aviation companies in Orlando and apply to their open positions. Some of the top employers in the area include Lockheed Martin, Delta Airlines, and JetBlue Airways. Be sure to tailor your resume and cover letter to each job you apply for.

4. Consider Contract Work

Many aviation companies in Orlando offer contract work for A&P mechanics. While this type of work may not offer the same benefits as a full-time position, it can be a great way to gain experience and make connections in the industry.
