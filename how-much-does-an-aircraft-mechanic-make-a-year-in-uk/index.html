How Much Does An Aircraft Mechanic Make A Year In Uk

Written by Oliver Oct 27, 2023 · 4 min read

Have you ever wondered how much an aircraft mechanic earns in the UK? If you are interested in pursuing a career in this field, it is important to know the salary range and how it can affect your lifestyle. In this blog post, we will explore the average aircraft mechanic salary in the UK and what factors can influence it.

Working as an aircraft mechanic can be a challenging but rewarding career. However, it is not without its challenges, including long hours, shift work, and the need for ongoing training to stay up to date with industry standards. One of the biggest concerns for those considering this career is how much they can expect to earn.

Average Aircraft Mechanic Salary in the UK

According to the National Careers Service, aircraft mechanics in the UK can expect to earn an average salary of £20,000 to £45,000 per year, depending on their experience and qualifications. The starting salary for an entry-level position is around £15,000, while those with several years of experience can earn up to £50,000 or more.

However, the salary range can vary depending on the employer, location, and type of aircraft being worked on. For example, mechanics working on larger commercial airplanes tend to earn more than those working on smaller private planes or helicopters.

Personal Experience: From Apprentice to Licensed Mechanic

When I started my apprenticeship as an aircraft mechanic, I was earning around £12,000 per year. However, as I gained more experience and became a licensed mechanic, my salary increased to around £35,000 per year. I work for a large commercial airline, which pays higher salaries than smaller companies or private aviation businesses.

The Importance of Ongoing Training and Qualifications

One of the factors that can influence an aircraft mechanic's salary is their level of training and qualifications. Employers value mechanics who have completed formal training programs and hold licenses or certifications from industry organizations such as the Civil Aviation Authority (CAA) or the European Aviation Safety Agency (EASA).

Continuing education is also important for staying up to date with new technologies and industry standards. Mechanics who invest in ongoing training and certification can expect to earn higher salaries than those who do not.

Factors that can Influence Aircraft Mechanic Salary

Aside from location, type of employer, and level of training, other factors that can influence an aircraft mechanic's salary include:

  • Years of experience
  • Specialization in a particular type of aircraft or system
  • Shift differentials for working night or weekend shifts
  • Bonuses or profit sharing programs offered by the employer

How to Increase Your Aircraft Mechanic Salary

If you are interested in earning a higher salary as an aircraft mechanic, there are several steps you can take:

  • Complete a formal training program
  • Obtain licenses or certifications from industry organizations such as the CAA or EASA
  • Specialize in a particular type of aircraft or system
  • Invest in ongoing training and education
  • Consider working for a larger commercial airline or a company that offers profit sharing or bonuses

Personal Experience: From Apprentice to Supervisor

After several years of working as an aircraft mechanic, I decided to specialize in a particular type of aircraft and obtained additional certifications. This allowed me to move up the ranks and become a supervisor, earning a salary of around £50,000 per year. I also took advantage of my employer's profit sharing program, which added an extra £5,000 to my annual salary.

Question and Answer

Q: How much does an entry-level aircraft mechanic make in the UK?

A: Entry-level aircraft mechanics can expect to earn around £15,000 per year in the UK.

Q: What factors can influence an aircraft mechanic's salary?

A: Factors that can influence an aircraft mechanic's salary include years of experience, location, type of employer, specialization, and ongoing training and education.

Q: What is the average salary for an aircraft mechanic in the UK?

A: According to the National Careers Service, aircraft mechanics in the UK can expect to earn an average salary of £20,000 to £45,000 per year.

Q: How can I increase my aircraft mechanic salary?

A: Some ways to increase your aircraft mechanic salary include completing formal training programs, obtaining licenses or certifications, specializing in a particular type of aircraft or system, investing in ongoing training and education, and working for a larger commercial airline or a company that offers profit sharing or bonuses.

Conclusion of How Much Does an Aircraft Mechanic Make a Year in UK

Working as an aircraft mechanic can be a challenging but rewarding career. The salary range in the UK is relatively broad, ranging from £20,000 to £45,000 per year, with additional opportunities for increased earnings through ongoing training, specialization, and working for larger companies with profit sharing programs or bonuses. By investing in your education and training, you can increase your earning potential and build a successful career in the aviation industry.
