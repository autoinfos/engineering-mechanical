How Much Does A Sterile Processing Tech Make An Hour

Written by Christine Nov 01, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're considering a career as a sterile processing tech, you may be wondering how much you can expect to make. The answer depends on a variety of factors, including your location, experience level, and the type of facility where you work.

One of the pain points related to how much does a sterile processing tech make an hour is that the salary can vary widely depending on these factors. It can be difficult to know what to expect without doing some research and talking to professionals in the field.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median hourly wage for sterile processing technicians in the United States was $19.70 as of May 2020. However, this figure can range from around $14 per hour for entry-level positions to over $28 per hour for those with more experience or who work in certain specialized facilities.

In summary, the hourly wage for a sterile processing tech can vary widely depending on factors such as location, experience, and facility type. It's important to do your research and talk to professionals in the field to get a better idea of what you can expect to make.

What Factors Affect How Much a Sterile Processing Tech Makes?

Location is one of the biggest factors that can impact how much a sterile processing tech makes. In general, areas with higher costs of living tend to offer higher salaries to offset these expenses. Experience level is another important factor; those with more experience will typically earn more than entry-level technicians.

The type of facility where you work can also play a role in your earnings. Hospitals and large medical centers tend to pay more than smaller clinics or outpatient facilities. Finally, the demand for sterile processing techs in your area can impact your earning potential; areas with a high demand for these professionals may offer higher salaries to attract and retain top talent.

My Experience as a Sterile Processing Tech

As a sterile processing tech with over five years of experience, I can attest that the hourly wage can vary widely depending on where you work. When I started out in a small clinic, I earned around $15 per hour. However, after gaining more experience and moving to a larger hospital, my hourly wage increased to over $25 per hour.

One thing to keep in mind is that the work can be challenging and fast-paced, but it's also rewarding to know that you're helping to keep patients safe and healthy. If you're considering a career as a sterile processing tech, I would encourage you to do your research and talk to professionals in the field to get a better idea of what to expect.

How Can I Increase My Earnings as a Sterile Processing Tech?

If you're already working as a sterile processing tech and want to increase your earning potential, there are a few things you can do. One is to pursue additional certifications or training in specialized areas such as endoscopy or surgical instruments. This can make you more valuable to your employer and may lead to higher pay.

Another option is to seek out positions at larger or more prestigious medical facilities. These tend to offer higher salaries and better benefits packages. Finally, networking with other professionals in the field and staying up-to-date on industry trends and best practices can help you stay competitive and increase your earning potential over time.
